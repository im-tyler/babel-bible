Symbols are grouped by topic. Within each topic, listed in canonical order; LaTeX source on the left, meaning + first-introduction unit on the right.

## Sign-convention markers

The project follows the **Lawson-Michelsohn convention** $v^2 = -q(v)$ in Clifford algebras throughout. Where the physics literature uses $v^2 = +q(v)$, the translation $\mathrm{Cl}_{p,q}^{\mathrm{LM}} \cong \mathrm{Cl}_{q,p}^{\mathrm{phys}}$ is noted at the point of use. See `03.09.02` Counterexamples to common slips and `03.09.03` Historical & philosophical context.

For Riemannian / Lorentzian signature, the physics convention $\eta = \mathrm{diag}(+, -, -, -)$ (mostly-minus) is used in `03.09.02` §"Connection to physics"; mathematical units default to positive-definite Riemannian unless explicitly Lorentzian.

For the Hodge star $\ast$ on an oriented Riemannian $M^n$, the convention $\alpha \wedge \ast \beta = \langle\alpha, \beta\rangle\, d\mathrm{vol}$ for $\alpha, \beta \in \Omega^k$ is in force in `03.07.05`.

For Chern classes, the normalization $c(E) = \det(I + \tfrac{i}{2\pi}F)$ is used in `03.06.04`. Equivalent variants $[\Omega/(2\pi i)]$ appear when curvature is taken complex-valued.
