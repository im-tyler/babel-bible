## Summary

The first-pass audit (`plans/fasttrack/griffiths-harris-geometry-algebraic-curves.md`)
was written when the Brill-Noether spine was almost entirely absent. **The corpus
has since grown a complete Brill-Noether block in `04.04.*`** — Petri/Gieseker-Petri,

**Almost the entire priority-1/2/3 punch-list of the original audit is now stale.**

After grepping the live corpus, **3 genuine gaps remain** — none of them in the
Brill-Noether core. They are the classical *projective* curve-geometry topics ACGH-I
develops alongside Brill-Noether but which the corpus's intersection-theory-flavoured
units never anchored: Weierstrass points, the Castelnuovo genus bound for space
curves, and the Enriques-Babbage-Petri canonical-ideal structure theorem.

## Genuine gaps

### Gap 1 — Weierstrass points and gap sequences

- **id:** `06.06.09`
- **title:** Weierstrass points and gap sequences
- **spec:** For a compact curve of genus $g\ge2$, the gap sequence / Weierstrass
  semigroup at a point, the Wronskian construction giving the divisor of
  Weierstrass points of total weight $g^3-g$, the weight bound, ordinary vs.
  higher Weierstrass points, and the hyperelliptic characterisation
  (Weierstrass points = ramification of the $g^1_2$).
- **prereqs:** `06.04.01` (Riemann-Roch RS), `06.06.06` (Jacobi inversion),
  `04.04.01` (Riemann-Roch curves), `04.08.02` (canonical sheaf)
- **chapter dir:** `content/06-riemann-surfaces/06-jacobians/`
  · frontmatter `section: riemann-surfaces`, `chapter: jacobians`
- **why genuine:** "Weierstrass point" / "gap sequence" appear ONLY inside exercise
  prose and asides (`06.02.03` genus-2 example, `06.08.02` Andreotti-Mayer remark,
  `04.10.34` hyperelliptic-reconstruction remark). No unit states the Wronskian
  weight formula $\sum w_p = g^3-g$, the gap sequence, or the Weierstrass-semigroup
  structure. The `*weierstrass*` slugs in the corpus are all complex-analysis
  (preparation theorem, factorization, $\wp$-function) — unrelated. ACGH-I Ch. I
  / Exercise batches make this a first-class object.

### Gap 2 — Castelnuovo's genus bound for curves in $\mathbb{P}^r$ and extremal curves

- **id:** `04.04.04`
- **title:** Castelnuovo's bound and extremal curves
- **spec:** The maximal-genus bound $\pi(d,r)$ for a non-degenerate smooth curve of
  degree $d$ in $\mathbb{P}^r$ (Castelnuovo 1889), via the Uniform Position /
  general-hyperplane-section count of $h^0$ on the points; the extremal curves
  achieving the bound lie on rational normal scrolls / Veronese surfaces;
  application to bounding $g$ in terms of $(d,r)$.
- **prereqs:** `04.04.01` (Riemann-Roch curves), `04.05.05` (ample line bundle),
  `06.02.03` (Riemann existence / canonical embedding)
- **chapter dir:** `content/04-algebraic-geometry/04-curves/`
  · frontmatter `section: algebraic-geometry`, `chapter: curves`
- **why genuine:** the corpus has the **Castelnuovo-Severi inequality** (`04.04.02`,
  a covering-degree statement) and the **Castelnuovo number** (`04.10.29`, the
  enumerative count of $g^r_d$'s when $\rho=0$) — both *different* theorems. The
  genus bound $\pi(d,r)$ for space curves and the extremal-curve theory are
  genuinely absent (grep for `castelnuovo bound|extremal curve|pi(d,r)|maximal
  genus.*degree` returns nothing in the curve/RS chapters). ACGH-I Ch. III develops
  this as the classical degree-genus ceiling.

### Gap 3 — Enriques-Babbage-Petri theorem (structure of the canonical ideal)

- **id:** `04.04.14`
- **title:** Enriques-Babbage-Petri theorem on the canonical ideal
- **spec:** Beyond Max Noether's degree-2 surjectivity (already anchored), the full
  structure theorem: the homogeneous ideal of a canonically embedded
  non-hyperelliptic curve of genus $\ge4$ is generated by quadrics, *except* for
  trigonal curves and smooth plane quintics, where cubics are also needed; Petri's
  explicit quadric/cubic relations and the syzygy reformulation.
- **prereqs:** `04.04.08` (Petri map / Gieseker-Petri), `04.08.02` (canonical sheaf),
  `06.02.03` (canonical embedding)
- **chapter dir:** `content/04-algebraic-geometry/04-curves/`
  · frontmatter `section: algebraic-geometry`, `chapter: curves`
- **why genuine:** Max Noether's *degree-2* statement (surjectivity of
  $\mathrm{Sym}^2 H^0(\omega_C)\to H^0(\omega_C^{\otimes2})$) IS covered in `04.10.34`
  (Torelli, line 456). But the Enriques-Babbage-Petri *structure theorem* — quadric
  generation with the explicit trigonal / plane-quintic exceptions and Petri's
  relations — appears only as a one-line **citation** (`04.10.34` line 458,
  `04.10.29` line 588 attribute Petri's conjecture) and is never stated or proved.
  This is the lowest-confidence of the three gaps (the core degree-2 content is
  already covered); flag as a focused structure-theorem unit, not a foundational one.
  If the campaign wants to economise, this can instead be a Master-section deepening
  of `04.04.08`.
