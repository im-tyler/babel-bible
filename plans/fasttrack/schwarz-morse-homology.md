## §1 What Schwarz's *Morse Homology* is for

By the early 1990s the "Morse complex" was folklore: Thom (1949), Smale
(1960s, the Morse-Smale transversality condition and the handle picture),
Witten (1982, "Supersymmetry and Morse theory," the deformed de Rham
differential $d_t = e^{-tf} d\, e^{tf}$ and instanton tunnelling between
critical points), and Floer (1988-89, infinite-dimensional Morse theory for
the symplectic action and the Chern-Simons functional) had all used or
sketched it. But there was **no single rigorous reference** carrying out the
finite-dimensional construction with all analytic details — the
transversality of stable/unstable manifolds, the compactness of trajectory
moduli spaces by broken-trajectory limits, the gluing that inverts breaking,
the coherent orientations giving $\mathbb{Z}$-coefficients, and the proof
that the resulting homology is the manifold's singular homology. Schwarz's
1992 ETH dissertation, published as this book, is **that reference**: it
builds Morse homology from the ground up as a self-contained theory, in a
way that is a deliberate *finite-dimensional rehearsal* for Floer theory
(Schwarz's later work). The book's pedagogical thesis is that one should
learn the entire analytic apparatus of Floer homology — moduli spaces,
Fredholm theory, transversality, compactness, gluing, orientations,
spectral sequences for filtered complexes — in the safe finite-dimensional
Morse setting first, where the moduli spaces are honest finite-dimensional
manifolds rather than Banach-manifold quotients.

The book is organised in three parts (~235 pp.):

- **Part I — The Trajectory Spaces.** The differential-topology and
  functional-analytic foundations. A Morse function $f$ on a closed
  Riemannian manifold $M^n$; the negative gradient flow $\dot\gamma =
  -\nabla f(\gamma)$; stable and unstable manifolds $W^s(x)$, $W^u(x)$ of a
  critical point $x$ with $\dim W^u(x) = \mu(x)$ (the Morse index); the
  **Morse-Smale condition** (all $W^u(x) \pitchfork W^s(y)$); the
  trajectory spaces $\mathcal{M}(x, y)$ of flow lines from $x$ to $y$ and
  the unparametrised quotient $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x, y) =
  \mathcal{M}(x, y)/\mathbb{R}$; the realisation of $\mathcal{M}(x, y)$ as
  the zero set of a Fredholm section, with Fredholm index $\mu(x) - \mu(y)$;
  the **transversality theorem** (for a generic metric / generic
  perturbation the section is transverse, so $\mathcal{M}(x, y)$ is a smooth
  manifold of dimension $\mu(x) - \mu(y)$); exponential convergence /
  asymptotic analysis at the ends; the **compactness theorem** (a sequence
  in $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x, y)$ subconverges to a *broken trajectory*
  $x \to z_1 \to \cdots \to z_k \to y$, giving the compactification by
  broken flow lines).

- **Part II — The Morse Homology Theorem.** The construction and the main
  theorem. The chain group $C_*(f) = \bigoplus_{x} \mathbb{Z}\langle x
  \rangle$ graded by the Morse index; **coherent orientations** of the
  trajectory moduli (orientations of the determinant lines of the
  linearised flow), giving a $\pm 1$ characteristic-sign $n(x, y)$ to each
  isolated flow line; the **boundary operator** $\partial x = \sum_{\mu(y)
  = \mu(x) - 1} \big(\sum_{u \in \widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x,y)} n_u\big) y$;
  the identity **$\partial^2 = 0$**, proved from the structure of the
  1-dimensional moduli $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x, y)$ with $\mu(x) - \mu(y)
  = 2$, whose compactification is a 1-manifold-with-boundary and whose
  boundary points are exactly the once-broken trajectories (gluing
  identifies them, and they cancel in signed pairs). The **gluing theorem**
  (broken trajectory + large gluing parameter $\rho$ ⇒ a unique nearby
  honest trajectory) supplying the boundary identification. The resulting
  **Morse homology** $HM_*(f) = H_*(C_*(f), \partial)$; **invariance**
  under change of $(f, g)$ via continuation maps / chain homotopies; and
  the **Morse Homology Theorem**: $HM_*(f) \cong H_*(M; \mathbb{Z})$,
  natural in $M$. The book proves this *intrinsically* — via continuation
  to a self-indexing function and an Eilenberg-Steenrod-style axiomatic
  uniqueness argument — rather than by quoting the CW / handle-attachment
  identification of Milnor / Thom.

- **Part III — Extensions and Further Structure.** Functoriality and the
  algebraic payload: the **Morse cohomology** dual complex; the cup product
  and the ring structure (via $Y$-shaped moduli / fibre products of
  trajectory spaces); **continuation morphisms** and their functoriality;
  the **spectral sequence of a filtered Morse complex** (filtration by
  critical values) and the Morse-theoretic perspective on it; the
  **Poincaré duality** $HM_*(f) \cong HM^{n-*}(-f)$ obtained by reversing
  the flow ($W^u(x; f) = W^s(x; -f)$); and the comparison with singular and
  cellular theories. This is the part that recasts Morse homology as a full
  *homology theory* satisfying the Eilenberg-Steenrod axioms, the
  abstraction Floer theory inherits.

Distinctive editorial choices that make Schwarz Fast-Track-worthy and
distinguish it from Milnor (3.03):

1. **Moduli-space-of-trajectories foundation, not handle attachment.**
   Milnor proves the homotopy-type results by *attaching a $\lambda$-cell*
   when crossing a critical level (MMT Theorems 3.1–3.2). Schwarz never
   attaches a cell: the topology is recovered from *counting gradient flow
   lines between critical points*, and the trajectory spaces
   $\mathcal{M}(x, y)$ — Fredholm, transverse, compactifiable by breaking —
   are the whole content. This is the **Floer-theoretic** way of doing
   Morse theory, in finite dimensions.

2. **Analysis-first, with Sard-Smale transversality.** Each
   $\mathcal{M}(x, y)$ is the zero set of a section of a Banach bundle over
   a path space; its linearisation is an asymptotically-constant first-order
   operator whose Fredholm index *is* $\mu(x) - \mu(y)$ (Robbin-Salamon
   spectral flow). Genericity of the Morse-Smale condition is proved by
   **Sard-Smale** on a universal moduli over a Banach manifold of
   perturbations — the exact device Floer theory uses, where Milnor's
   genericity is a soft Whitney-embedding argument.

3. **Compactness by broken trajectories + gluing.** The compactification
   of $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x, y)$ by broken flow lines (the finite-dim
   shadow of Gromov / Uhlenbeck compactness) and its converse, gluing
   (broken trajectory + large parameter ⇒ a unique nearby honest
   trajectory), make $\overline{\widehat{\mathcal{M}}}(x, y)$ for
   index-difference 2 a compact 1-manifold-with-boundary — which is what
   forces $\partial^2 = 0$. Both are wholly absent from Milnor (who never
   forms trajectory moduli).

4. **Coherent orientations and $\mathbb{Z}$-coefficients.** Schwarz works
   over $\mathbb{Z}$, orienting the determinant lines coherently so the
   signed count $n(x, y)$ is well-defined (many expositions take
   $\mathbb{Z}/2$ to avoid this). The orientation theory is a substantial
   chunk of Part II and is the finite-dimensional model for Floer
   orientations (Donaldson 1987, Floer-Hofer).

5. **Internal proof of the Morse Homology Theorem + algebraic structure.**
   Schwarz proves $HM_* \cong H_*^{\mathrm{sing}}$ **without** importing the
   cellular identification: $f \mapsto HM_*(f)$ satisfies the
   Eilenberg-Steenrod axioms, so axiomatic uniqueness finishes it — a
   genuinely different proof from Milnor / Thom. Part III then adds the
   complex-theoretic structure Milnor lacks: cup product / ring structure
   (via $Y$-shaped moduli), continuation functoriality, the filtered-complex
   spectral sequence, and Poincaré duality via flow reversal.

6. **Deliberate Floer rehearsal.** The stated purpose (preface) is to
   isolate the *analytic skeleton* of Floer homology — moduli, Fredholm,
   transversality, compactness, gluing, orientations — in the
   finite-dimensional setting where every step is rigorous, so the reader
   meets Floer's infinite-dimensional version already knowing the
   architecture. Schwarz is the bridge between Milnor (3.03) and Donaldson
   Floer (3.06) / the symplectic Floer chapter (`05.08.*`).

Schwarz does **not** cover: the geodesic / path-space / Bott-periodicity
material of Milnor Part III–IV (that is classical Morse theory, Milnor's
turf — and Schwarz cites Milnor for it); equivariant or min-max Morse
theory (Nicolaescu's turf); discrete Morse theory (Forman); or the
infinite-dimensional Floer constructions themselves (his later work and
Donaldson 3.06 / Audin-Damian Part II).

### Priority 1 — the Morse-Smale-Witten complex (the book's core)

These items construct the trajectory-space machinery and the complex
itself — the load-bearing content the Floer chapters silently assume.

1. **`03.15.01` Gradient flow, stable/unstable manifolds, the Morse-Smale
   condition.** [NEW] Schwarz Part I (Ch. 1–2) anchor; Smale 1960–61
   ("On gradient dynamical systems," *Ann. of Math.* 74) and Thom 1949
   originator-citations. Three-tier, ~2200 words. Master section: negative
   gradient flow $\dot\gamma=-\nabla f(\gamma)$ of a Morse function on a
   closed Riemannian $(M,g)$; the stable / unstable manifolds $W^s(x) =
   \{p : \gamma_p(t)\to x \text{ as } t\to+\infty\}$, $W^u(x)$ symmetric,
   with $\dim W^u(x)=\mu(x)$ (stable-manifold theorem for a hyperbolic
   rest point); the **Morse-Smale transversality condition** $W^u(x)
   \pitchfork W^s(y)$ for all $x,y$; consequence $\dim(W^u(x)\cap W^s(y))
   = \mu(x)-\mu(y)$. Worked example: height function on $T^2$ embedded
   upright — four critical points (index 0,1,1,2), the flow lines between
   them, Morse-Smale after a small tilt. **Highest priority — every other
   P1 item builds on it. Hard prereq: Milnor `03.02.02–03`.**

2. **`03.15.02` Trajectory spaces and the Fredholm / transversality
   theorem.** [NEW] Schwarz Part I (Ch. 2–3) anchor; Sard-Smale 1965
   (Smale, "An infinite-dimensional version of Sard's theorem," *Amer. J.
   Math.* 87) and Robbin-Salamon 1995 (spectral flow = Fredholm index)
   originator-citations. Three-tier, ~2400 words. **Largest P1 unit.**
   Master section: the trajectory space $\mathcal{M}(x,y) = W^u(x)\cap
   W^s(y)$ and its unparametrised quotient $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x,y) =
   \mathcal{M}(x,y)/\mathbb{R}$; realisation as the zero set of a section
   $F(\gamma)=\dot\gamma+\nabla f(\gamma)$ of a Banach bundle over the path
   space $\mathcal{P}^{1,2}(x,y)$; linearisation $D_\gamma F = \frac{d}{dt}
   + A(t)$ an asymptotically-constant first-order operator, Fredholm of
   index $\mu(x)-\mu(y)$ (Robbin-Salamon); the **transversality theorem** —
   for a generic metric (Sard-Smale on the universal moduli over the
   Banach manifold of metrics) the section is transverse, so
   $\mathcal{M}(x,y)$ is a smooth manifold of dimension $\mu(x)-\mu(y)$ and
   $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x,y)$ of dimension $\mu(x)-\mu(y)-1$. Cross-link
   to `03.07.19` (instanton spectral flow), the infinite-dim analogue.
   **Hard prereq: item 1.**

3. **`03.15.03` Compactness: broken trajectories.** [NEW] Schwarz Part I
   (Ch. 4) anchor; Floer 1988 (the infinite-dim original of the breaking
   phenomenon) cited as forward-pointer. Three-tier, ~1900 words. Master
   section: exponential convergence of flow lines to critical points at
   both ends; the **compactness theorem** — a sequence in
   $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x,y)$ has a subsequence converging (geometrically,
   in $C^\infty_{\mathrm{loc}}$) to a *broken trajectory* $x\to z_1\to
   \cdots\to z_k\to y$ with $\mu(x)>\mu(z_1)>\cdots>\mu(y)$; hence
   $\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x,y)$ is precompact with broken-trajectory
   compactification $\overline{\widehat{\mathcal{M}}}(x,y)$. Worked example:
   on $S^2$ with the standard height function, the two flow lines from
   north to south pole; on a tilted torus, a sequence of index-2-to-0
   trajectories breaking through an index-1 saddle. Cross-link to
   `03.07.20` (Uhlenbeck compactness, the infinite-dim analogue with
   bubbling). **Hard prereq: item 2.**

4. **`03.15.04` Gluing of trajectories.** [NEW] Schwarz Part II (Ch. 2)
   anchor; Floer-Hofer / Taubes gluing as forward-pointers. Three-tier,
   ~1700 words. Master section: the converse to breaking — given a broken
   trajectory $x\to z\to y$ with $\mu(x)-\mu(z)=\mu(z)-\mu(y)=1$, and a
   large gluing parameter $\rho$, there is a unique honest trajectory
   $\gamma_\rho \in \mathcal{M}(x,y)$ near the pre-glued path, depending
   smoothly on $\rho$, with $\gamma_\rho\to(x\to z\to y)$ as $\rho\to\infty$;
   hence the once-broken trajectories are exactly the boundary of the
   compactified 1-dimensional moduli $\overline{\widehat{\mathcal{M}}}(x,y)$
   when $\mu(x)-\mu(y)=2$. Cross-link to `03.07.21` (instanton gluing).
   **Hard prereq: item 3.**

5. **`03.15.05` Coherent orientations and characteristic signs.** [NEW]
   Schwarz Part II (Ch. 3) anchor; Floer-Hofer 1993 ("Coherent orientations
   for periodic orbit problems," *Math. Z.* 212) and Donaldson 1987
   originator-citations. Three-tier, ~1700 words. Master section: the
   determinant line $\det(D_\gamma F)$ of the linearised operator along a
   trajectory; a **coherent orientation** = a choice of orientation of these
   determinant lines compatible with gluing; for an isolated flow line
   $u\in\widehat{\mathcal{M}}(x,y)$ (index difference 1), the resulting sign
   $n_u\in\{\pm1\}$; well-definedness of $n(x,y)=\sum_u n_u \in \mathbb{Z}$.
   Cross-link to `03.07.22` (instanton orientations). **Hard prereq:
   item 4. Enables $\mathbb{Z}$-coefficients in item 6.**

6. **`03.15.06` The Morse complex and $\partial^2 = 0$.** [NEW] Schwarz
   Part II (Ch. 1, 4) anchor; Witten 1982 ("Supersymmetry and Morse
   theory," *J. Diff. Geom.* 17) and Thom 1949 originator-citations.
   Three-tier, ~2400 words. **Apex of Parts I–II.** Master section: the
   chain group $C_*(f)=\bigoplus_x \mathbb{Z}\langle x\rangle$ graded by
   Morse index; the boundary operator $\partial x = \sum_{\mu(y)=\mu(x)-1}
   n(x,y)\,y$; the identity $\partial^2 = 0$ — the coefficient of $z$ in
   $\partial^2 x$ (with $\mu(x)-\mu(z)=2$) counts the boundary of the
   compact 1-manifold-with-boundary $\overline{\widehat{\mathcal{M}}}(x,z)$,
   whose boundary points are the once-broken trajectories (item 4), which
   cancel in signed pairs (item 5), giving $0$. The **Morse homology**
   $HM_*(f,g)=H_*(C_*(f),\partial)$ and the **Morse inequalities** as the
   immediate corollary $\dim C_k \ge \dim HM_k$. Worked example: $C_*$ and
   $\partial$ for the height function on $T^2$ — recover $H_*(T^2)=
   (\mathbb{Z},\mathbb{Z}^2,\mathbb{Z})$. Cross-link to `03.07.23`,
   `05.08.02` (the two Floer analogues). **Hard prereqs: items 1–5.**

### Priority 2 — invariance, the Morse Homology Theorem, and structure

These close the main theorem and the Part III algebraic payload.

7. **`03.15.07` Continuation maps and invariance of $HM_*$.** [NEW]
   Schwarz Part II (Ch. 5) anchor; Conley-Zehnder 1984 / Salamon 1990
   (continuation principle) originator-citations. Three-tier, ~1800 words.
   Master section: given two Morse-Smale pairs $(f_0,g_0),(f_1,g_1)$, a
   monotone homotopy induces a chain map (count solutions of the
   $s$-dependent flow $\dot\gamma=-\nabla f_s(\gamma)$); two such are chain
   homotopic; composing the $0\to1$ and $1\to0$ continuations is chain
   homotopic to the identity; hence $HM_*(f_0,g_0)\cong HM_*(f_1,g_1)$
   canonically. Cross-link to the invariance assertions in `03.07.23`,
   `05.08.02`. **Hard prereq: item 6.**

8. **`03.15.08` The Morse Homology Theorem.** [NEW] Schwarz Part II
   (Ch. 6) anchor; Thom 1949 / Smale 1962 (handle decomposition, the
   classical comparison) and Schwarz 1993 (the axiomatic internal proof)
   originator-citations. Three-tier, ~2200 words. **The theorem the book
   is named for.** Master section: $HM_*(f,g)\cong H_*(M;\mathbb{Z})$,
   natural in $M$. Two proofs sketched: (i) Schwarz's internal proof — the
   functor $M\mapsto HM_*$ satisfies the Eilenberg-Steenrod axioms (cite
   `03.12.15`), hence equals singular homology by axiomatic uniqueness;
   (ii) the comparison route — a self-indexing Morse function makes
   $C_*(f)$ the cellular chain complex of the induced CW structure
   (handle attachment, cite Milnor `03.02.03` and `03.12.13`). Worked
   example: $\mathbb{CP}^n$ with the standard Morse function ($n+1$
   critical points of even index $0,2,\dots,2n$, $\partial=0$) recovering
   $H_*(\mathbb{CP}^n)$. **Hard prereqs: items 6, 7;
   `03.12.13`, `03.12.15`.**

9. **`03.15.09` Morse cohomology, cup product, and the ring structure.**
   [NEW] Schwarz Part III anchor; Fukaya / Betz-Cohen (Morse-theoretic
   products via trees) originator-citations. Three-tier, ~1900 words.
   Master section: the dual complex $C^*(f)=\mathrm{Hom}(C_*(f),\mathbb{Z})$,
   coboundary $\delta$, Morse cohomology $HM^*(f)$; the cup product via
   counting $Y$-shaped configurations (fibre products $W^u(x)\cap W^s(y)
   \cap W^s(z)$ for three Morse functions), giving $HM^*$ a graded-
   commutative ring isomorphic to $H^*(M)$. Cross-link to `03.12.17`
   (cap product). **Hard prereq: item 8.**

### Priority 3 — Poincaré duality, spectral sequence, and the Floer bridge

10. **`03.15.10` Poincaré duality via flow reversal; the filtered
    spectral sequence.** [NEW] Schwarz Part III anchor; Milnor (Poincaré
    duality), Forman (filtered complexes) secondary. Three-tier, ~1700
    words. Master section: reversing the flow exchanges stable and unstable
    manifolds, $W^u(x;f)=W^s(x;-f)$, so $C_k(f)\cong C^{n-k}(-f)$, yielding
    the Morse-theoretic proof of Poincaré duality $HM_k(f)\cong
    HM^{n-k}(-f)$ (cite `03.12.16`); the filtration of $C_*(f)$ by critical
    value gives a spectral sequence whose $E_1$ page is the critical-point
    count (cite `03.13.01`). **Hard prereq: item 8.**

11. **`03.15.11` From finite-dimensional Morse to Floer homology
    (bridge unit).** [NEW] Schwarz preface / Floer 1988 anchor.
    Master-only, ~1500 words. **The unit that ties this chapter to the two
    Floer chapters.** Master section: the dictionary — finite-dim $M$ ⟶
    loop space $\mathcal{L}M$ (symplectic Floer) or
    $\mathcal{B}(Y)=\mathcal{A}/\mathcal{G}$ (instanton Floer); Morse
    function ⟶ symplectic action functional $\mathcal{A}_H$ / Chern-Simons
    $\mathrm{CS}$; gradient flow lines ⟶ pseudoholomorphic cylinders / ASD
    instantons on the cylinder; $\mathcal{M}(x,y)$ Fredholm ⟶
    Banach-manifold trajectory moduli; Sard-Smale transversality ⟶ generic
    perturbations; broken-trajectory compactness ⟶ Gromov / Uhlenbeck
    compactness with bubbling; gluing, orientations, $\partial^2=0$ carry
    over verbatim. Explicit forward links to `05.08.02`, `05.08.04`,
    `03.07.19`–`03.07.23`. **Hard prereq: item 8. This is the weaving
    payload — it makes the silent dependency of the Floer chapters on
    Morse-Smale-Witten machinery explicit.**

14. **`03.15.12` Witten's supersymmetric Morse theory (survey/pointer).**

## §5 What this plan does NOT cover

- **Classical Morse theory foundations** (critical points, Morse lemma,
  handle attachment, Morse inequalities as a *standalone* topic, Jacobi
  fields, the Index Theorem, geodesics, Bott periodicity). These are
  **Milnor 3.03** (`milnor-morse-theory.md`, units `03.02.02–07`,
  `03.03.X1`, `03.08.X1–X2`, `03.12.X1–X2`). Schwarz cites Milnor for all
  of it; this audit depends on those units, does not duplicate them.
- A line-number-level inventory of every lemma in Schwarz's three parts
  (full P1 audit). Deferred; the TOC + punch-list suffice for a single
  235-page monograph.
- **Schwarz's few formal exercises.** It is a research monograph; the
  worked constructions are the implicit exercise set and are reproduced in
  the punch-list units' master tiers. A dedicated `03.15.E1` exercise pack
  is a P3 follow-up only if the verification pass flags it.
- **Infinite-dimensional Floer homology at depth** — symplectic /
  Hamiltonian (`05.08.*`, already shipped), instanton (`03.07.17–28`,
  already shipped), monopole / Heegaard Floer. Schwarz is strictly
  finite-dimensional; this audit *cross-links* to the Floer chapters
  (item 11) rather than re-deriving them.
- **Audin-Damian Part II / Banyaga-Hurtubise** Floer-specific material.
  Those texts get their own passes; the finite-dim Morse-complex overlap
  is owned by `03.15.*` here.
- **Equivariant Morse theory, min-max / Lusternik-Schnirelmann,
  Morse-Bott theory, discrete Morse theory (Forman), complex Morse /
  Picard-Lefschetz.** Nicolaescu's / Forman's turf; out of scope for
  Schwarz, who treats only the non-degenerate Morse-Smale case. Morse-Bott
  is a candidate future unit but is not in Schwarz's book.
- **The $h$-cobordism theorem (Smale / Milnor 3.07)** as a substantive
  unit. Referenced as a downstream pointer; its own audit (FT 3.07).
- **Witten's QFT framing at depth.** Item 14 is a pointer only; a full
  TQFT / supersymmetric-localisation treatment belongs to a future
  `12-quantum` / TQFT audit.

## §6 Acceptance criteria for FT equivalence (Schwarz)

Per `docs/plans/FASTTRACK_EQUIVALENCE_PLAN.md` §3.4 and §9, the book is at
equivalence-coverage when:

- The Milnor 3.03 batch's `03.02.02` (critical point / Hessian / index /
  Morse lemma) and `03.02.03` (Morse functions / handle attachment / $W^s$,
  $W^u$ notation) have shipped — **hard prereq** for `03.15.01–02`.
- ≥95% of Schwarz's named theorems map to Babel Bible units at
  Schwarz-equivalent proof depth (currently ~8%; after Priority 1 rises to
  ~70%; after Priority 1+2 to ~92%; after Priority 1+2+3 to ~96%).
- The signature theorems hold at master-tier proof depth: the
  transversality theorem (item 2), the broken-trajectory compactness
  theorem (item 3), the gluing theorem (item 4), $\partial^2=0$ for the
  Morse differential (item 6), continuation invariance (item 7), and the
  **Morse Homology Theorem** $HM_*(f)\cong H_*(M;\mathbb{Z})$ (item 8).
- ≥90% of Schwarz's worked examples are reproduced (currently ~10%; the
  mandatory worked examples in items 1, 3, 6, 8 — height function on
  $T^2$, the breaking sequence on $S^2$/torus, $C_*(T^2)$ recovering
  $H_*(T^2)$, and $\mathbb{CP}^n$ recovering $H_*(\mathbb{CP}^n)$ — bring
  this to ~85%; a short examples deepening closes the rest).
- Notation decisions are recorded inline in `03.15.06`'s master tier
  (no separate `notation/schwarz.md`).
- For Schwarz's chapter dependency chain (Part I trajectory spaces → Part
  II complex / main theorem → Part III structure), the corresponding
  `prerequisites` chain `03.15.01 → … → 03.15.06 → 03.15.08 → 03.15.09/10`
  exists in the DAG, rooted at Milnor `03.02.02–03`.
- Pass-W weaving connects the new `03.15.*` units to the two Floer
  chapters: the bridge unit `03.15.11` links forward to `05.08.02` and
  `03.07.23`, and the ENRICH edits (items 12–13) discharge the silent
  Morse-template dependency those Floer units currently carry.

**Honest scope.** **Near-greenfield but conceptually pre-shadowed.** The
corpus already ships *both* infinite-dimensional Floer complexes
(`05.08.*` symplectic, `03.07.17–28` instanton) that reproduce Schwarz's
architecture step-for-step, so the corpus is not ignorant of the
machinery — but the *finite-dimensional template the book is about* is
absent, and so are the classical Morse foundations (still owned by the
unbuilt Milnor 3.03 batch). The work is ~11 new units in one clean new
chapter `03.15-morse-homology/`, plus 3 weaving enrichments, all anchored
on a tight cluster of originators (Thom 1949, Smale 1960s, Witten 1982,
Floer 1988, Schwarz 1993, Robbin-Salamon 1995, Floer-Hofer 1993). The
audit maps roughly 1 part of the book → 2–4 units.

**Largest single Schwarz-distinctive gap:** the apex unit `03.15.06` (the
Morse complex + $\partial^2=0$) together with `03.15.08` (the Morse
Homology Theorem). Every Part-I item builds toward `03.15.06`; the chapter
becomes coherent the moment that unit ships, and `03.15.08` is the payoff
the book is named for. The second-largest structural win is `03.15.11`
(the Floer bridge): it retroactively grounds the two already-shipped Floer
chapters in their finite-dimensional template, closing the corpus's
single most conspicuous "analogue-without-original" gap.
