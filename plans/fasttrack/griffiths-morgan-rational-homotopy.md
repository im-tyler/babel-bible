# Griffiths-Morgan — *Rational Homotopy Theory and Differential Forms* (Fast Track 1.19) — Audit + Gap Plan

**Book:** Phillip A. Griffiths and John W. Morgan, *Rational Homotopy
Theory and Differential Forms*. Progress in Mathematics 16,
Birkhäuser, Boston 1981 (xi + 242 pp.); second edition Birkhäuser
2013 (xxxiv + 224 pp., ISBN 978-1-4614-8467-7). The 2013 edition adds
a substantial introduction by Loring Tu surveying developments since
1981, an expanded preface by Morgan, an appendix by Morgan on the
formality of Kähler manifolds in the language of cohomological-Hodge
theory, and corrections to the 1981 text; the chapter-numbering and
core pedagogical apparatus are unchanged. The book originates as
**John Morgan's 1972-74 Harvard course notes** taken under Griffiths'
direction, written up jointly with Griffiths for the *Rational
Homotopy Theory* summer school in Cortona (Italy) 1979 and the
Birkhäuser series in 1981. It is the **differential-forms-flavoured
canonical reference** for Sullivan's rational homotopy theory, sitting
in deliberate complement to **Quillen's 1969 *Annals* paper**
(*Rational homotopy theory*, Annals 90, 205-295) on the DG-Lie-algebra
side, **Sullivan's 1977 *Publ. IHÉS* paper** (*Infinitesimal
computations in topology*, IHÉS 47, 269-331) which is the originator
text, and **Félix-Halperin-Thomas's 2001 *Rational Homotopy Theory***
(GTM 205, Springer) which is the modern encyclopaedic reference. The
book's distinctive role on the Fast Track is to deliver the
**differential-form construction of the minimal model from
$\Omega^*(M)$ on a smooth manifold** at graduate-textbook tempo, in
~240 pp., with the **Deligne-Griffiths-Morgan-Sullivan 1975
formality theorem** for compact Kähler manifolds as the load-bearing
application that exhibits the apparatus's geometric content. The book
also covers — uniquely among introductory texts — the **Hodge-theoretic
mixed-Hodge-structure refinement** of rational homotopy theory for
smooth complex projective varieties, drawing on Morgan's 1978 *Publ.
IHÉS* 48 paper (the Morgan extension of DGMS 1975 from compact Kähler
to smooth quasi-projective varieties via Deligne's mixed Hodge
structures).

theory: a smooth-manifold reader (a differential geometer, gauge

**Summary.** Griffiths-Morgan covers, across its ten chapters in the
1981 edition: (Ch I) review of differential forms on a smooth manifold
and the de Rham complex with smooth-manifold conventions; (Ch II)
Sullivan's $A_{PL}$ functor — the **piecewise-polynomial de Rham
complex** on a simplicial set, with the comparison theorem
$A_{PL}(X) \otimes_\mathbb{Q} \mathbb{R} \xrightarrow{\sim} \Omega^*(|X|)$
for a smooth manifold realising $X$; (Ch III) commutative differential
graded algebras (CDGAs) over $\mathbb{Q}$, free CDGAs $\Lambda V$,
Sullivan algebras, the minimal-model existence and uniqueness theorem;
(Ch IV) **the main theorem of rational homotopy theory** identifying
indecomposables of the minimal model in degree $n$ with the dual of
$\pi_n(X) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$, plus the Sullivan spatial realisation
$|\Lambda V|$; (Ch V) the rational Hurewicz theorem and Whitehead-product
description as the quadratic part of the differential; (Ch VI)
**Halperin-Stasheff perturbation algorithm** for the minimal model of
a fibration $F \to E \to B$, with the perturbation $\tau$ encoding the
Leray-Serre transgression; (Ch VII) formal spaces, the formality
condition, and counterexamples (nilmanifolds, generic symplectic
manifolds, Massey-product detectors); (Ch VIII) **the
Deligne-Griffiths-Morgan-Sullivan 1975 formality theorem** for
compact Kähler manifolds, proved via the $dd^c$-lemma and the zigzag
$\Omega^*(X) \xleftarrow{} \ker d^c \cap \ker d \xrightarrow{}
H^*(X; \mathbb{R})$; (Ch IX) the **Morgan 1978 mixed-Hodge-structure
extension** to smooth complex projective varieties — the rational
homotopy type of a smooth quasi-projective variety carries a canonical
mixed Hodge structure with the weight filtration determined by the
divisor at infinity; (Ch X) applications and computations —
$\pi_*(S^n) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$ recovering Serre 1953, minimal models
of Lie groups and homogeneous spaces, the Sullivan-Friedlander 1982
profinite-completion refinement. **The 2013 second edition** adds
Loring Tu's introduction (~30 pp.) surveying post-1981 developments
including operadic formality (Tamarkin 1998, Kontsevich 1999), formality
of configuration spaces (Kontsevich 1994), the rational-formality of
classifying spaces of compact Lie groups, and the connection to
deformation quantization and $L_\infty$-algebras.

## §1 Chapter map of *Rational Homotopy Theory and Differential Forms* (1981 / 2013)

The Birkhäuser 1981 first edition is organised into **ten chapters
totalling ~240 pages**; the 2013 second edition adds a ~30-pp.
introduction by Loring Tu and a ~10-pp. Morgan appendix on the modern
mixed-Hodge view of formality, with the ten-chapter core unchanged.

1. **Chapter I — Introduction; differential forms and the de Rham
   complex.** ~15 pp. Recap of the smooth-manifold de Rham complex
   $(\Omega^*(M), d, \wedge)$ as a commutative differential graded
   algebra (CDGA) over $\mathbb{R}$; statement of the de Rham theorem
   $H^*_{dR}(M) \cong H^*_{sing}(M; \mathbb{R})$; brief preview of
   the chapter's main goal — to lift the smooth de Rham apparatus to
   a $\mathbb{Q}$-coefficient theory amenable to rational-homotopy
   computation. **[upstream-covered by `03.04.02-differential-forms`,
   `03.04.04-exterior-derivative`, `03.04.06-de-rham-cohomology` at
   Master tier]**.

2. **Chapter II — Piecewise-polynomial de Rham complex ($A_{PL}$).**
   ~25 pp. Definition of $A_{PL}^n(K)$ on a simplicial set $K$ as
   compatible families $\{\omega_\sigma\}_{\sigma \in K_p}$ of
   polynomial $n$-forms on each simplex $\sigma$ with $\mathbb{Q}$
   coefficients, satisfying face / degeneracy compatibility (the
   simplicial-form analogue of the de Rham complex). $A_{PL}$ is a
   contravariant functor $\mathbf{sSet}^{op} \to \mathbf{CDGA}_\mathbb{Q}$.
   **Comparison theorem** (Sullivan 1977, Bott-Tu §19, Griffiths-Morgan
   Ch II Thm 2.1): $A_{PL}(\mathrm{Sing}(X)) \otimes_\mathbb{Q}
   \mathbb{R} \xrightarrow{\sim} \Omega^*(X)$ as CDGAs over
   $\mathbb{R}$ for a smooth manifold $X$ with $\mathrm{Sing}(X)$ the
   singular complex. **Integration map** $\int : A_{PL}(K) \to C^*(|K|;
   \mathbb{Q})$ as a quasi-isomorphism (the rational de Rham theorem,
   Bott-Tu §19 Thm 19.1). **[Covered by `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-
   models` §"Formal definition" + §"Full proof set" at Master tier;
   the smooth-manifold comparison $A_{PL} \otimes \mathbb{R}
   \xrightarrow{\sim} \Omega^*(M)$ is stated as Bridge but not
   developed in detail at Beginner / Intermediate tier]**.

3. **Chapter III — Commutative differential graded algebras (CDGAs)
   and Sullivan algebras.** ~25 pp. Definition of a CDGA over $\mathbb{Q}$:
   a non-negatively-graded vector space $A = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} A^n$
   with associative graded-commutative multiplication and differential
   $d : A^n \to A^{n+1}$ with $d^2 = 0$ and Leibniz rule. **Free
   graded-commutative algebra $\Lambda V$** on a graded vector space
   $V = V^{\geq 1}$: tensor algebra modulo graded commutativity
   $v \otimes w = (-1)^{|v||w|} w \otimes v$. **Sullivan algebra**:
   $(\Lambda V, d)$ where $V$ admits an increasing filtration $V(0)
   \subseteq V(1) \subseteq \cdots$ with $d V(k) \subseteq \Lambda V(k-1)$.
   **Minimal Sullivan algebra**: a Sullivan algebra with $d V \subseteq
   \Lambda^{\geq 2} V$ (no linear part). **Existence and uniqueness
   of the minimal model** (Sullivan 1977 Thm; Griffiths-Morgan Ch III
   Thm 3.4): every simply-connected CDGA over $\mathbb{Q}$ of finite
   type admits a quasi-isomorphism $\varphi : (\Lambda V, d) \to (A, d)$
   from a minimal Sullivan algebra, unique up to isomorphism over
   $A$. The proof is by induction on cohomological degree, alternating
   "add generators in $V^n$ to lift $H^n$" and "kill spurious closed
   classes in $H^{n+1}(\Lambda V^{<n})$." **Lifting lemma**: any
   minimal Sullivan algebra has the left lifting property against any
   quasi-isomorphism of CDGAs. **[Covered by `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-
   models` at Master tier with full inductive proof; the lifting lemma
   is covered in Exercise 4 with full proof]**.

4. **Chapter IV — The main theorem of rational homotopy.** ~25 pp.
   Statement (Sullivan 1977 main theorem; Griffiths-Morgan Ch IV Thm
   4.1): for a simply-connected space $X$ of finite rational type with
   minimal model $M_X = (\Lambda V_X, d)$, the indecomposables in
   degree $n$ are canonically dual to the rational homotopy in
   degree $n$:
   $$ V_X^n \cong \mathrm{Hom}_\mathbb{Z}(\pi_n(X), \mathbb{Q}). $$
   The differential $d : V_X \to \Lambda^{\geq 2} V_X$ encodes
   **rational Whitehead products** and **higher Massey products** in
   $\pi_*(X) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$. **Sullivan spatial realisation**:
   for a minimal Sullivan algebra $(\Lambda V, d)$, there exists a
   simplicial set $|\Lambda V|$ (the **Sullivan realisation**) whose
   geometric realisation is the **rationalisation** $X_\mathbb{Q}$ of
   $X$ with $\pi_n(X_\mathbb{Q}) = \pi_n(X) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$. **Worked
   example**: minimal model of $S^n$ — even-dimensional spheres give
   $\Lambda(x, y)$ with $\deg x = n, \deg y = 2n-1, dy = x^2$;
   odd-dimensional spheres give $\Lambda(x)$ with $dx = 0$. **Worked
   example**: minimal model of $\mathbb{C}P^n$ — $\Lambda(x, y)$ with
   $\deg x = 2, \deg y = 2n+1, dy = x^{n+1}$. **[Covered by
   `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-models` at Master tier (main theorem
   stated and proved) + `03.12.E1-rational-homotopy-exercises`
   Exercise 1 ($S^{2k}$) + Exercise 2 ($S^{2k+1}$) + Exercise 3
   ($\mathbb{C}P^n$) with full solutions; Sullivan spatial realisation
   covered in `03.12.06` §"Advanced results" with $X_\mathbb{Q}$
   discussion]**.

5. **Chapter V — Rational Hurewicz theorem and Whitehead products.**
   ~20 pp. **Rational Hurewicz** (Griffiths-Morgan Ch V Thm 5.1): for
   a simply-connected $X$ with $\pi_i(X) \otimes \mathbb{Q} = 0$ for
   $1 \leq i \leq n-1$, the rational Hurewicz map $h_\mathbb{Q} :
   \pi_n(X) \otimes \mathbb{Q} \xrightarrow{\sim} H_n(X; \mathbb{Q})$
   is an isomorphism, and $H_i(X; \mathbb{Q}) = 0$ for $1 \leq i \leq
   n - 1$. The proof reads off both sides from the indecomposable
   layer of the minimal model. **Whitehead product** $[\alpha, \beta]
   \in \pi_{p+q-1}(X)$ for $\alpha \in \pi_p(X), \beta \in \pi_q(X)$
   defined via the attaching map of $S^p \wedge S^q$; the rational
   Whitehead product corresponds to the **quadratic part of the
   differential** $d_2 : V \to \Lambda^2 V$ on the minimal model.
   **Worked example**: the rational Whitehead bracket $[\iota_n,
   \iota_n] \in \pi_{2n-1}(S^n)$ is non-zero rationally iff $n$ is
   even, exactly when the differential of the second generator of the
   minimal model is non-zero. **[Covered by `03.12.07-whitehead-tower`
   at Master tier (rational Hurewicz stated and proved) + `03.12.06-
   sullivan-minimal-models` Master section "Loop space cohomology" and
   exercise solutions covering the Whitehead-product-as-quadratic-
   differential identification]**.

6. **Chapter VI — Fibrations and the Halperin-Stasheff perturbation
   algorithm.** ~30 pp. **Sullivan model of a fibration** $F \to E
   \to B$ of simply-connected spaces of finite rational type: form
   the underlying graded algebra $M_B \otimes \Lambda V_F$ as a free
   $M_B$-CDGA, and define a perturbed differential $d_E$ by
   $d_E(b \otimes 1) = d_B(b) \otimes 1$ for $b \in M_B$, and
   $d_E(1 \otimes v) = 1 \otimes d_F(v) + \tau(v)$ for $v \in V_F$,
   where $\tau : V_F \to M_B \otimes \Lambda V_F$ is a **perturbation
   of degree $+1$** encoding the Leray-Serre transgression. The
   condition $d_E^2 = 0$ forces $\tau$ to satisfy a Maurer-Cartan-type
   equation. **Halperin-Stasheff 1979 theorem** (*Trans. AMS* 244):
   such a $\tau$ exists, the resulting $(M_B \otimes \Lambda V_F,
   d_E)$ is a Sullivan model of $E$, and minimality is preserved if
   both $M_B$ and $M_F$ are minimal and the **monodromy action** of
   $\pi_1(B)$ on $H^*(F; \mathbb{Q})$ is trivial. **Worked example**:
   the Hopf fibration $S^1 \to S^3 \to S^2$ — minimal model of $S^2$
   is $\Lambda(x, y)$ with $\deg x = 2, \deg y = 3, dy = x^2$;
   minimal model of $S^1$ is $\Lambda(t)$ with $\deg t = 1, dt = 0$
   (but $S^1$ is not simply-connected, so the simply-connected version
   uses the path-fibration $\Omega S^2 \to PS^2 \to S^2$ instead).
   **Worked example**: principal $G$-bundle $G \to P \to B$ with
   simply-connected $G$ has $M_P = M_B \otimes \Lambda V_G$ with
   transgression $\tau$ determined by the characteristic classes in
   $H^*(B; \mathbb{Q})$. **[PARTIAL coverage: `03.12.06-sullivan-
   minimal-models` Exercise 6 sketches the Halperin-Stasheff
   construction in ~200 words with the perturbation formula; the
   Hopf-fibration worked example is referenced in `03.12.E1` Exercise
   5 (Bott-Samelson on $\Omega S^n$) but not the principal-bundle
   version; **the algorithm as a standalone unit is missing**]**.

7. **Chapter VII — Formal spaces and the formality condition.** ~20 pp.
   A simply-connected CDGA $(A, d)$ is **formal** if its minimal model
   $M_A$ is isomorphic to the minimal model of its cohomology ring
   $(H^*(A), 0)$ viewed as a CDGA with zero differential. Equivalent
   characterisations: (a) there exists a chain of quasi-isomorphisms
   $A \xleftarrow{} \cdot \xrightarrow{} H^*(A)$; (b) all Massey
   products of $A$ vanish at the level of cohomology with a coherent
   choice of representatives. **Massey products** $\langle \alpha,
   \beta, \gamma \rangle \in H^{p+q+r-1}(A)$ for cohomology classes
   $\alpha, \beta, \gamma$ with $\alpha \beta = 0 = \beta \gamma$,
   defined as the cohomology class of $u \gamma + (-1)^{|u|} \alpha
   v$ where $du = \alpha \beta$ and $dv = \beta \gamma$, with
   indeterminacy in the ideal $\alpha \cdot H^* + H^* \cdot \gamma$.
   **Worked example**: the **Borromean-rings complement** $\mathbb{R}^3
   \setminus L$ where $L$ is the Borromean link has a nontrivial
   triple Massey product on three $H^1$-classes (one for each ring),
   witnessing non-formality. **Worked example**: the **Heisenberg
   nilmanifold** $H_3(\mathbb{Z}) \backslash H_3(\mathbb{R})$ has a
   nontrivial triple Massey product on three $H^1$-classes, witnessing
   non-formality (and consequently non-Kähler — see Ch VIII).
   **[PARTIAL coverage: `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-models` Master
   section "Formality and the rational dichotomy" defines formality
   and lists counterexamples in one paragraph; **explicit Massey-product
   definition with the Borromean-rings or Heisenberg-nilmanifold
   worked computation is absent at all tiers**; the DGMS formality
   theorem itself is covered (see Ch VIII below); a **standalone unit
   on Massey products and formal spaces with worked computations** is
   missing]**.

8. **Chapter VIII — The Deligne-Griffiths-Morgan-Sullivan formality
   theorem for compact Kähler manifolds.** ~25 pp. The load-bearing
   geometric application of the apparatus. **Statement** (DGMS 1975
   *Inventiones* 29; Griffiths-Morgan Ch VIII Thm 8.1): the de Rham
   CDGA $(\Omega^*(X; \mathbb{R}), d, \wedge)$ of smooth real-valued
   differential forms on a compact Kähler manifold $X$ is **formal**:
   there is a zigzag of quasi-isomorphisms of CDGAs connecting
   $(\Omega^*(X; \mathbb{R}), d)$ to its cohomology ring $(H^*(X;
   \mathbb{R}), 0)$ equipped with zero differential. **Proof
   structure**: (i) Hodge theory on a compact Kähler manifold gives
   the **$dd^c$-lemma**: every $d$-closed and $d^c$-closed form that
   is either $d$-exact or $d^c$-exact equals $dd^c \beta$ for some
   $\beta$; (ii) define the subcomplex $A := \ker d^c \cap \Omega^*$
   of $d^c$-closed forms; (iii) the inclusion $A \hookrightarrow
   \Omega^*$ is a quasi-isomorphism (every de Rham class has a
   $d^c$-closed harmonic representative); (iv) the projection
   $A \to H^*(X; \mathbb{R})$ is a quasi-isomorphism (the kernel
   $d$-exact $\cap$ $d^c$-closed $= dd^c(\Omega^{*-2})$ by the
   $dd^c$-lemma); (v) the zigzag $\Omega^* \xleftarrow{} A
   \xrightarrow{} H^*$ exhibits formality. **Corollary** (DGMS 1975
   Cor. 8.2): two simply-connected compact Kähler manifolds with
   isomorphic rational cohomology rings are rationally homotopy
   equivalent. **Application** (DGMS 1975 §6): the **Iwasawa
   manifold** $I = H_3(\mathbb{Z}[i]) \backslash H_3(\mathbb{C})$ is
   compact complex but **not** Kähler — DGMS detect this via a non-zero
   triple Massey product on three $H^1$-classes. **[FULLY COVERED:
   `04.09.05-ddbar-lemma` at Master tier (~764 lines) with the
   $dd^c$-lemma, the formality zigzag, the formal proof, the Iwasawa
   counterexample, the corollary on Kähler-rational-homotopy rigidity,
   plus extended historical context. Also covered in `03.12.06-
   sullivan-minimal-models` Master section and Exercise 8 with the
   full DGMS proof. **This chapter has the strongest existing
   coverage in the book.**]**.

9. **Chapter IX — Mixed Hodge structures and rational homotopy of
   smooth complex algebraic varieties.** ~25 pp. The Morgan 1978
   *Publ. Math. IHÉS* 48 extension. For a smooth complex projective
   variety $X$ (compact, with a Kähler metric), DGMS gives formality
   directly. For a smooth quasi-projective variety $U = X \setminus
   D$ (where $X$ is smooth projective and $D \subset X$ is a normal
   crossings divisor), the de Rham CDGA $\Omega^*(U; \mathbb{C})$
   is **not** generally formal, but its minimal model carries a
   canonical **mixed Hodge structure** in the sense of Deligne 1971-74
   (*Théorie de Hodge II, III*, IHÉS 40 and 44). **Theorem** (Morgan
   1978; Griffiths-Morgan Ch IX Thm 9.3): the minimal model of a
   smooth quasi-projective variety $U$ admits a functorial mixed Hodge
   structure compatible with the differential and the algebra
   structure; the weight filtration is determined by the order of
   poles along $D$. **Application** (Morgan 1978 §10): the rational
   homotopy groups $\pi_n(U) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$ inherit a mixed Hodge
   structure, recovering Deligne's mixed Hodge structure on the
   homology of $U$ and extending it to homotopy. **Worked example**:
   the complement $\mathbb{P}^1 \setminus \{0, 1, \infty\}$ has a
   nontrivial mixed Hodge structure on $\pi_1$ (free on two generators
   with appropriate weights), connecting to the Drinfeld-Ihara
   pro-unipotent fundamental group and modern motivic theory of
   $\zeta(n)$. **[GAP: NO COVERAGE. The Morgan 1978 mixed-Hodge-
   structure-on-rational-homotopy extension is entirely absent from
   the corpus. This is the second-largest Griffiths-Morgan-
   distinctive gap.]**.

10. **Chapter X — Applications and computations.** ~30 pp. Three
    extended worked-application sections.
    - **§10.1 Sullivan's solution to Serre's question on $\pi_*(S^n)
      \otimes \mathbb{Q}$.** Recovers Serre's 1953 theorem that
      $\pi_k(S^n) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$ is non-zero only when $k = n$
      (all $n$) or $n = 2j$ even and $k = 4j - 1$. The proof reads
      directly off the minimal models of $S^n$. **[Covered:
      `03.12.E1-rational-homotopy-exercises` Exercise 7 (hard, full
      proof) covers Sullivan-Serre]**.
    - **§10.2 Minimal models of compact Lie groups and homogeneous
      spaces.** For a compact connected Lie group $G$, $H^*(G;
      \mathbb{Q}) = \Lambda(x_1, \ldots, x_r)$ is an exterior algebra
      on odd-degree generators (Hopf's theorem); the minimal model of
      $G$ is precisely this exterior algebra with zero differential.
      Lie groups are **formal**. For a homogeneous space $G/H$,
      Cartan's algorithm computes the minimal model from the
      classifying maps $BH \to BG$. **[PARTIAL coverage: `03.12.06`
      Master section "The minimal model of a Lie group" covers the
      exterior-algebra statement; **the Cartan algorithm for $G/H$
      is not stated**]**.
    - **§10.3 Sullivan-Friedlander 1982 profinite-completion
      refinement.** The minimal Sullivan model captures the rational
      homotopy type; the **Friedlander-Sullivan profinite completion**
      $X^\wedge$ captures the profinite homotopy type, with the
      arithmetic square $X = X_\mathbb{Q} \times_{X^\wedge \otimes
      \mathbb{Q}} X^\wedge$ reassembling the integral homotopy type
      from rational and profinite halves. **[PARTIAL coverage:
      `03.12.45-arithmetic-square-integral-fracture` covers the
      arithmetic-square framework; Sullivan-Friedlander profinite
      completion as a standalone unit is absent]**.

    approach, but technically forbidding for newcomers), (c)
    Bott-Tu §19 (the single-chapter summary, but compressed), (d)
    Félix-Halperin-Thomas 2001 *Rational Homotopy Theory* GTM 205
    (the modern encyclopaedic reference, ~535 pp., but too long for
    a first reading), and (e) Hess 2007 *Rational Homotopy Theory: A
    Brief Introduction* (Contemp. Math. 436; the shortest possible
    introduction, but pointing rather than developing):
    - **Differential-form-flavoured presentation.** Every construction
      is stated first with smooth differential forms $\Omega^*(M)$
      on a smooth manifold, then lifted to the rational simplicial
      $A_{PL}$ via the comparison theorem. A reader who knows the
      de Rham complex can pick up the book without rebuilding the
      foundations. Sullivan 1977 and Quillen 1969 do not have this
      on-ramp; Bott-Tu §19 has it but in 20 pp. only.
    - **DGMS formality as the geometric payoff.** The book's Ch VIII
      is essentially the DGMS paper recast as a textbook chapter,
      with the $dd^c$-lemma developed in full and the formality
      zigzag spelled out diagrammatically. This is the single most
      important geometric application of the Sullivan apparatus, and
      Griffiths-Morgan is the canonical pedagogical reference for it.
    - **Morgan 1978 mixed-Hodge extension as the modern frontier.**
      Ch IX develops Morgan's extension of DGMS to smooth quasi-
      projective varieties via mixed Hodge structures. This material
      is **not** covered in Sullivan 1977, Bott-Tu, Félix-Halperin-
      Thomas (which mentions it but does not develop it), or May
      *Concise*. Griffiths-Morgan is the unique introductory text
      that delivers it.
    - **Halperin-Stasheff fibration models in extended form.** Ch VI
      develops the perturbation algorithm with multiple worked
      examples (Hopf fibrations, principal $G$-bundles, sphere
      fibrations). Bott-Tu §19 sketches the algorithm in one
      paragraph; Félix-Halperin-Thomas treats it in encyclopaedic
      depth across multiple chapters. Griffiths-Morgan is the middle
      ground: enough detail to compute, not so much that the reader
      drowns.
    - **Stops cleanly before the operadic / $L_\infty$ refinement.**
      The 1981 edition explicitly says "the formality of the
      configuration spaces and the operadic refinement of Sullivan's
      theory are deferred to future work." The 2013 Loring Tu
      introduction surveys these post-1981 developments (Tamarkin
      1998, Kontsevich 1999, Kontsevich 2003) but does not develop
      them. Griffiths-Morgan is **not** trying to be Félix-Halperin-
      Thomas Vol II or Loday-Vallette *Algebraic Operads*; it is
      trying to deliver the Sullivan minimal model with formality and
      mixed Hodge as its capstones, then hand off to the operadic
      literature.

The book sits as the **graduate-textbook differential-geometric
on-ramp to rational homotopy theory**, deliberately complementing
Sullivan's research paper, Bott-Tu's single-chapter summary,
Quillen's DG-Lie dual, and Félix-Halperin-Thomas's encyclopaedic
reference. **Distinctive editorial contribution: the
differential-form construction + DGMS formality + Morgan 1978 mixed-
Hodge extension as a single coherent pedagogical arc.**

1. **`03.12.50` Halperin-Stasheff perturbation algorithm for the
   minimal model of a fibration.** [NEW] Anchor Griffiths-Morgan Ch
   VI + Halperin-Stasheff 1979 *Trans. AMS* 244 + Félix-Halperin-
   Thomas 2001 §15 + Bott-Tu §19 (fibration model sketch). Three-tier

   **Sullivan model of a fibration** $F \to E \to B$ of simply-connected
   spaces of finite rational type as the twisted-tensor-product CDGA
   $(M_B \otimes \Lambda V_F, d_E)$ with perturbation $\tau : V_F \to
   M_B^+ \otimes \Lambda V_F$ of degree $+1$ encoding the Leray-Serre
   transgression. Prove (Halperin-Stasheff 1979 Thm 2.2) that such a
   $\tau$ exists, the result is a Sullivan model of $E$, and
   minimality is preserved if both $M_B, M_F$ are minimal and the
   monodromy $\pi_1(B) \to \mathrm{Aut}(H^*(F; \mathbb{Q}))$ is
   trivial. The condition $d_E^2 = 0$ unfolds to the Maurer-Cartan
   equation $d_B \tau + \tau d_F + \frac{1}{2}[\tau, \tau] = 0$ (with
   the bracket the natural one on the perturbation Lie algebra).
   Worked examples in Intermediate tier:

     \otimes \Lambda V_{\Omega X}$ with $\tau$ chosen so that
     $d_E$-cohomology equals $H^*(PX; \mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}$. From
     this read off $H^*(\Omega X; \mathbb{Q}) \cong (\Lambda s^{-1}
     V_X, 0)$ — the Bott-Samelson loop-space cohomology.

     $\Lambda(x, y) \otimes \Lambda(\tilde x)$ with $\deg \tilde x =

     desuspension; spell out in the unit). Use this to compute
     $H^*(\Omega S^2; \mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}[\tilde x]$, the
     divided-power algebra on a degree-1 generator.

     connected Lie group: model $M_B \otimes \Lambda(x_1, \ldots, x_r)$
     with $\tau(x_i) = c_i \in M_B$ the rational characteristic class
     transgressing to the universal class $\tilde x_i \in H^*(BG;
     \mathbb{Q})$ via the classifying map $B \to BG$. The
     transgression $\tau$ is exactly the **Chern-Weil image** of the
     characteristic class. **Bridges** `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-
     models`, `03.13.02-leray-serre`, `03.08.04-classifying-space`,
     `03.06.04-chern-class`. **The single most-cited Griffiths-Morgan-
     distinctive pedagogical contribution beyond what `03.12.06` already
     ships.**

2. **`03.12.51` Massey products and the formality condition.** [NEW]
   Anchor Griffiths-Morgan Ch VII + Massey 1958 (originator, *Bull.
   AMS* 64) + Stasheff 1963 (associahedra and $A_\infty$ structures)
   + Sullivan 1977 + DGMS 1975. Three-tier with **Intermediate-tier
   dominant**; ~2200 words. Define the **triple Massey product**
   $\langle \alpha, \beta, \gamma \rangle \in H^{p+q+r-1}(A) /
   (\alpha \cdot H^{q+r-1}(A) + H^{p+q-1}(A) \cdot \gamma)$ for
   cohomology classes $\alpha \in H^p, \beta \in H^q, \gamma \in H^r$
   with $\alpha \beta = 0 = \beta \gamma$ via the construction: pick
   cocycle representatives $a, b, c$; pick $u, v$ with $du = ab$ and
   $dv = bc$; set $\langle \alpha, \beta, \gamma \rangle = [uc + (-1)^p
   av]$ modulo the indeterminacy ideal. Independence-of-choice via
   the standard $\langle \alpha, \beta, \gamma \rangle$ argument
   (Massey 1958). **Higher Massey products** $\langle \alpha_1,
   \ldots, \alpha_n \rangle$ as $A_\infty$-structure data on
   cohomology. **Formality**: a CDGA $(A, d)$ is **formal** iff its
   minimal model is isomorphic to the minimal model of $(H^*(A), 0)$;
   equivalent characterisations include (a) chain of quasi-isos
   $A \xleftarrow{} \cdot \xrightarrow{} H^*(A)$; (b) all Massey
   products (with coherent choices) vanish. Worked examples:
   - **Borromean-rings complement** $\mathbb{R}^3 \setminus L$ where
     $L$ is the Borromean link: three meridian classes $\alpha,
     \beta, \gamma \in H^1$ with $\alpha \beta = \beta \gamma =
     \gamma \alpha = 0$ but $\langle \alpha, \beta, \gamma \rangle
     \neq 0$ in $H^2$, witnessing non-formality. The Massey product
     is the **linking-triple invariant** of the Borromean link.
   - **Heisenberg nilmanifold** $H_3(\mathbb{Z}) \backslash
     H_3(\mathbb{R})$: explicit computation of a non-trivial triple
     Massey product on three $H^1$-classes, witnessing non-formality.
     The nilmanifold is a fibre bundle $T^2 \to H_3 \to S^1$ with
     non-trivial monodromy, and the Massey product detects the
     Heisenberg commutator structure.
   - **Compact Kähler manifolds**: all Massey products vanish (DGMS
     1975 corollary of formality). This is the formality-as-rigidity
     statement.
   Master tier: connection to $A_\infty$-structures on cohomology and
   to Kadeishvili's $A_\infty$-transfer theorem; formality of operads
   (Tamarkin 1998, Kontsevich 1999) as a structural enhancement.
   **Bridges** `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-models`, `04.09.05-ddbar-
   lemma`, `03.12.49-bialgebra-hopf-milnor-moore`. **The standalone
   home for the Massey-product apparatus that is currently scattered
   across the corpus as one-line mentions.**

3. **`03.12.52` Differential forms on a smooth manifold and the
   rational de Rham theorem: smooth-manifold on-ramp to rational
   homotopy.** [NEW] Anchor Griffiths-Morgan Ch I-II + Bott-Tu §19.
   Three-tier with **Beginner-tier dominant**; ~1800 words. Frame the
   transition from the smooth de Rham complex $\Omega^*(M)$ to
   Sullivan's $A_{PL}$ for the **smooth-manifold reader** (differential
   geometer, gauge theorist, Kähler-geometer) who knows Bott-Tu
   Chs I-III but not Sullivan. **Comparison theorem** (Sullivan 1977;
   Griffiths-Morgan Ch II Thm 2.1): for a smooth manifold $M$ with
   simplicial structure on a CW model (e.g., a smooth triangulation),
   $A_{PL}(\mathrm{Sing}(M)) \otimes_\mathbb{Q} \mathbb{R} \xrightarrow{\sim}
   \Omega^*(M)$ as CDGAs over $\mathbb{R}$. The comparison says: the
   smooth de Rham complex is the real extension of the rational
   piecewise-polynomial de Rham complex, and computes the same
   cohomology. Consequence: any rational-homotopy computation can be
   performed using $\Omega^*(M) \otimes \mathbb{R}$ — there is no
   information loss when tensoring with $\mathbb{R}$ except the
   loss of rational structure, which is **exactly** what one **wants
   to keep** for rational-homotopy purposes. **Bridge** the Bott-Tu
   reader: every minimal-model computation done with $\Omega^*(M)$ in
   real-coefficient form descends to a rational-coefficient
   computation by the comparison theorem; the descent is the
   load-bearing reason the apparatus is "rational" rather than just
   "real." Worked example (Beginner tier): the minimal model of
   $S^{2k}$ via the smooth de Rham complex — choose a volume form
   $\omega_{2k}$ on $S^{2k}$ with $\omega_{2k}^2 = 0$ (degree-reasons,
   $4k > 2k$); the formal model of $\Omega^*(S^{2k})$ is $\Lambda(x,
   y)$ with $dy = x^2$, recovering the Bott-Tu / Sullivan computation
   without ever leaving the smooth-manifold setting. Master tier:
   note that the comparison theorem fails for spaces without a smooth
   structure (e.g., generic finite CW complexes); the simplicial
   $A_{PL}$ is the **canonical** rational refinement, and the smooth
   $\Omega^*$ is its real extension on manifolds. **Bridges**
   `03.04.06-de-rham-cohomology`, `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-models`.
   **The on-ramp unit that lets a differential geometer enter
   rational-homotopy theory without leaving the smooth-manifold
   setting.**

4. **`03.12.53` Cartan algorithm: minimal models of homogeneous
   spaces $G/H$.** [NEW] Anchor Griffiths-Morgan Ch X §10.2 + Cartan
   1950 *Colloque de Topologie* (Bruxelles) + Borel 1953 thesis +
   Félix-Halperin-Thomas §15.10. Three-tier with **Intermediate-tier
   dominant**; ~2000 words. For $H \subseteq G$ a closed connected
   subgroup of a compact connected Lie group $G$, the homogeneous
   space $G/H$ has minimal model computed by **Cartan's algorithm**:
   start with the inclusion $i : BH \to BG$ of classifying spaces;
   the induced map on rational cohomology $H^*(BG; \mathbb{Q}) \to
   H^*(BH; \mathbb{Q})$ is a map of polynomial algebras
   $\mathbb{Q}[y_1, \ldots, y_r] \to \mathbb{Q}[z_1, \ldots, z_s]$;
   the Sullivan model of $G/H$ is the **Koszul complex** of this map,
   namely $\mathbb{Q}[z_1, \ldots, z_s] \otimes \Lambda(\bar y_1,
   \ldots, \bar y_r)$ with $\deg \bar y_i = \deg y_i - 1$ and
   differential $d \bar y_i = i^*(y_i)$. Worked examples in
   Intermediate tier:
   - **$SU(2)/T = S^2$** where $T = U(1) \subset SU(2)$ is the maximal
     torus: $H^*(BSU(2)) = \mathbb{Q}[c_2]$ with $\deg c_2 = 4$;
     $H^*(BT) = \mathbb{Q}[t]$ with $\deg t = 2$; the map sends $c_2
     \mapsto t^2$. Cartan algorithm gives $\mathbb{Q}[t] \otimes
     \Lambda(\bar c_2)$ with $\deg \bar c_2 = 3$ and $d \bar c_2 =
     t^2$, recovering the minimal model of $S^2$.
   - **$\mathbb{C}P^n = U(n+1)/(U(n) \times U(1))$**: sanity-check
     reproduction of the direct minimal model $\Lambda(x, y), dy =
     x^{n+1}$.
   - **Stiefel manifold $V_k(\mathbb{C}^n) = U(n)/U(n-k)$**: minimal
     model as exterior algebra on $k$ odd-degree generators (Stiefel
     manifolds are **formal** for the same reason Lie groups are).
   - **Flag manifold $\mathrm{Fl}(\mathbb{C}^n) = U(n)/T^n$**:
     minimal model via the Borel presentation $\mathbb{Q}[t_1,
     \ldots, t_n] \otimes \Lambda(\bar c_1, \ldots, \bar c_n)$ with
     $d \bar c_k = e_k(t_1, \ldots, t_n)$ the $k$-th elementary
     symmetric polynomial. Formal.
   Master tier: connection to Borel's 1953 theorem $H^*(BG;
   \mathbb{Q}) = $ Weyl-invariant polynomials on the Cartan
   subalgebra; **formality of homogeneous spaces** $G/H$ for $G$
   compact connected (a theorem of Borel-Cartan 1953 / Sullivan
   1975; see Notbohm-Ray 1990 for the full classification).
   **Bridges** `03.12.06-sullivan-minimal-models`, `03.08.04-
   classifying-space`, `03.08.05-borel-cohomology` (if shipped),
   `03.06.04-chern-class`.

6. **`03.12.54` Mixed Hodge structures on rational homotopy of smooth
   complex algebraic varieties (Morgan 1978).** [NEW, GATED] Anchor
   Griffiths-Morgan Ch IX + Morgan 1978 *Publ. Math. IHÉS* 48 + Deligne
   1971-74 *Théorie de Hodge II, III* (*Publ. Math. IHÉS* 40, 44) +
   Voisin Vol II Ch 8. Three-tier with **Master-tier dominant**;
   ~2800 words. **PREREQUISITE GATING:** This unit depends on a
   shipped mixed-Hodge-structure unit (currently absent; flagged for
   the Voisin Vol II / Deligne audit). Until that prerequisite ships,
   this unit is **deferred**. State Morgan's 1978 extension: for a
   smooth complex quasi-projective variety $U = X \setminus D$ (where
   $X$ is smooth projective and $D$ is a normal-crossings divisor),
   the minimal model $M_U$ of $A_{PL}(U) \otimes \mathbb{C}$ carries
   a canonical functorial mixed Hodge structure in the sense of
   Deligne, with the weight filtration determined by the order of
   poles along $D$. Consequence: $\pi_n(U) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$ inherits
   a mixed Hodge structure, recovering Deligne's mixed Hodge structure
   on $H^*(U)$ and extending it to homotopy. Worked example: $\mathbb{P}^1
   \setminus \{0, 1, \infty\}$ — fundamental group is free on two
   generators with the mixed Hodge structure giving weights $1$ on
   each generator; the pro-unipotent completion $\pi_1^{\mathrm{un}}
   \otimes \mathbb{Q}$ recovers the Drinfeld-Ihara $\mathrm{GT}$-group
   and connects to motivic theory of $\zeta(n)$ values. Master tier:
   connection to mixed Tate motives and to the Kontsevich-Zagier
   period conjecture. **Bridges** to the (future) mixed-Hodge unit,
