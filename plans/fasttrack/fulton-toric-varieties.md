## §1 What ITV is for

ITV is the canonical short introduction to **toric varieties** — algebraic
varieties admitting an action of an algebraic torus $(\mathbb{C}^*)^n$ with
an open dense orbit. It is the combinatorial bridge between algebraic
geometry and convex polytope theory: every toric variety is encoded by a
**fan** of rational polyhedral cones in a lattice $N$, and every property
of the variety (smoothness, projectivity, divisors, line bundles,
cohomology, intersection numbers) translates to a property of the fan or
its dual polytope.

Distinctive contributions of ITV, in the order Fulton develops them
(Chapters 1–5 + appendices):

1. **Cones, fans, and lattices** (Ch. 1). The basic combinatorial data:
   strongly convex rational polyhedral cone $\sigma \subset N_\mathbb{R}$,
   its dual cone $\sigma^\vee \subset M_\mathbb{R}$, the semigroup algebra
   $\mathbb{C}[\sigma^\vee \cap M]$ producing the affine toric variety
   $U_\sigma = \operatorname{Spec} \mathbb{C}[\sigma^\vee \cap M]$. A fan
   $\Sigma$ glues these into the toric variety $X_\Sigma$.
2. **Smoothness, completeness, orbits** (Ch. 2). $X_\Sigma$ is smooth iff
   every cone is regular (generated by part of a $\mathbb{Z}$-basis of $N$);
   complete iff $|\Sigma| = N_\mathbb{R}$. The orbit-cone correspondence:
   orbits of the torus on $X_\Sigma$ are in bijection with cones of
   $\Sigma$, with $T$-orbit $O_\sigma$ of codimension $\dim \sigma$.
   Star, link, refinement, and the orbit-closure stratification.
3. **Resolution of singularities and toric birational geometry**
   (Ch. 2–3). Any toric variety admits an equivariant resolution by
   refining the fan — Hironaka's theorem becomes combinatorial in the toric
   case. Star subdivision = toric blowup; the toric MMP.
4. **Divisors, line bundles, and the Picard group** (Ch. 3). Torus-
   invariant Weil divisors are $T$-Cartier iff they come from a piecewise-
   linear function on $|\Sigma|$ that is linear on each cone. The Picard
   group is the quotient of such PL functions modulo globally linear
   functions. For a fan from a polytope $P$, the polarisation line bundle
   $L_P$ has global sections $H^0(X, L_P) = \bigoplus_{m \in P \cap M}
   \mathbb{C} \cdot \chi^m$ — lattice points of $P$ are a basis for
   sections.
5. **Moment map and the polytope picture** (Ch. 4). For a projective toric
   variety $X_P$ with polarisation polytope $P$, the moment map of the
   compact $T_\mathbb{R}^n$-action sends $X_P$ surjectively onto $P$, and
   the polytope-to-fan duality (face fan / normal fan) provides the
   algebraic counterpart of the Delzant correspondence on the symplectic
   side.
6. **Cohomology and intersection theory** (Ch. 5). Toric varieties have
   cohomology rings with purely combinatorial descriptions: for $X_\Sigma$
   smooth complete, $H^*(X_\Sigma; \mathbb{Q})$ is the Stanley-Reisner
   ring of $\Sigma$ modulo the linear relations from $M$, and the rational
   Chow ring agrees with rational cohomology. Intersection numbers of toric
   divisors are mixed volumes of polytopes — the bridge to the
   Bernstein-Kushnirenko theorem and to combinatorial intersection theory.
7. **Reflexive polytopes and the entry point to mirror symmetry** (Ch. 4
   notes / appendix-level pointers). A reflexive polytope is a lattice
   polytope $P$ with $0 \in \mathrm{int}(P)$ whose polar dual $P^\circ$ is
   also a lattice polytope. Batyrev 1994 proved that reflexive polytopes
   come in mirror pairs whose associated Calabi-Yau hypersurfaces are
   topologically mirror-dual; this is the polytope-combinatorial origin of
   string-theoretic mirror symmetry.

Cited peer expositions confirm this is the canonical content list and the
standard order:

- **Cox-Little-Schenck (CLS), *Toric Varieties* (AMS GSM 124, 2011)** —
  the modern definitive textbook on the subject; explicitly written as the
  "expanded successor" to Fulton 1993. CLS chapters 1–4 cover Fulton
  chapters 1–3 verbatim; CLS chapters 5–6 expand Fulton chapter 3;
  CLS chapters 8–9 cover Fulton chapter 4; CLS chapters 12–13 cover
  Fulton chapter 5. CLS is the natural cross-reference for any
  Fulton-anchored Codex unit.
- **Oda, *Convex Bodies and Algebraic Geometry* (Springer Ergebnisse 15,
  1988)** — the predecessor monograph (in print before Fulton), with a
  more thorough treatment of the lattice-and-polytope side and explicit
  attention to the historical Demazure-Mumford-Danilov line. Oda's
  notation and conventions are the ones Fulton adopts and CLS continues.
- **Audin, *Topology of Torus Actions on Symplectic Manifolds* (Progress
  in Mathematics 93, Birkhäuser 1991)** — the symplectic-side companion
  to Fulton. Already cited in
  `content/05-symplectic/moment-reduction/05.04.04-delzant-theorem.md` as
  the Master-tier anchor. Audin Ch. VII–VIII give the bridge from Delzant
  polytopes (symplectic toric manifolds) to fans (algebraic toric
  varieties) that Fulton Ch. 4 develops on the algebraic side.
- **Danilov, *The geometry of toric varieties* (*Russian Math. Surveys* 33,
  1978)** — the canonical pre-Fulton survey article and originating
  exposition of the fan-and-cone formalism in English; explicitly cited
  by Fulton in the preface as the immediate predecessor. Already cited in
  the existing `05.04.04` Master Historical section.

ITV is **not** a first introduction to algebraic geometry. It assumes
basic scheme theory (affine and projective schemes; sheaves and
$\operatorname{Spec}$ of a ring), elementary commutative algebra (integrally
closed semigroups, Hilbert basis for cones), and a working understanding of
projective varieties. The Codex prereq surface for Fulton-anchored units is
`04-algebraic-geometry/02-schemes/` (affine + projective schemes) and
`04-algebraic-geometry/05-divisors/` (Weil + Cartier divisors, Picard
group, line bundles).

## §3 Gap punch-list (P3-lite — units to write, priority-ordered)

**Priority 0 — prereqs already in place.** Affine and projective schemes
(`04.02.01`–`04.02.03`), Weil and Cartier divisors (`04.05.01`,
`04.05.04`), Picard group (`04.05.02`), line bundles (`04.05.03`),
projective space (`04.07.01`), blow-up (`04.07.02`), moment map
(`05.04.01`), AGS convexity (`05.04.03`), Delzant theorem (`05.04.04`).
No upstream blockers.

**Priority 1 — high-leverage, captures ITV's central content:**

1. **`04.11.01` Algebraic torus and character/cocharacter lattices.**
   $T = (\mathbb{C}^*)^n$, character lattice $M = \mathrm{Hom}(T, \mathbb{C}^*)
   \cong \mathbb{Z}^n$, cocharacter lattice $N = \mathrm{Hom}(\mathbb{C}^*, T)
   \cong \mathbb{Z}^n$, perfect pairing $M \times N \to \mathbb{Z}$. Fulton §1.1
   anchor; CLS §1.1 anchor. Three-tier, ~1500 words. Foundational.
2. **`04.11.02` Rational polyhedral cone and dual cone.** Strongly convex
   rational polyhedral cone $\sigma \subset N_\mathbb{R}$; dual cone
   $\sigma^\vee = \{m \in M_\mathbb{R} : \langle m, n\rangle \ge 0 \text{ for all }
   n \in \sigma\}$; Gordan's lemma (the semigroup $\sigma^\vee \cap M$ is
   finitely generated). Fulton §1.2 anchor; CLS §1.2 anchor. Three-tier,
   ~1500 words.
3. **`04.11.03` Affine toric variety $U_\sigma$.** Construction
   $U_\sigma = \operatorname{Spec}\,\mathbb{C}[\sigma^\vee \cap M]$;
   functoriality on the cone category; key examples ($U_{\{0\}} = T$,
   $U_{\sigma}$ for $\sigma = \mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}^n$ giving $\mathbb{A}^n$,
   the quadric cone $xy = zw$ from a non-smooth cone). Fulton §1.3 anchor;
   CLS §1.3 anchor. Three-tier, ~2000 words. **Central ITV building
   block.**
4. **`04.11.04` Fan and the toric variety $X_\Sigma$.** Definition of a
   fan (collection of cones closed under face and intersection); the
   variety $X_\Sigma$ obtained by gluing the affine $U_\sigma$'s; standard
   examples (the fan for $\mathbb{P}^n$, for $\mathbb{P}^1 \times
   \mathbb{P}^1$, for the Hirzebruch surface $\mathbb{F}_n$, for the blow-up
   of $\mathbb{A}^2$ at the origin). Fulton §1.4 anchor; CLS §3.1 anchor.
   Three-tier, ~2500 words. **Central ITV object.**
5. **`04.11.05` Smoothness and completeness via fans.** $X_\Sigma$ is
   smooth iff every cone is generated by a subset of a $\mathbb{Z}$-basis
   of $N$; $X_\Sigma$ is complete iff $|\Sigma| = N_\mathbb{R}$; $X_\Sigma$
   is projective iff $\Sigma$ is the normal fan of a polytope. Fulton §2.1
   anchor; CLS §3.1, §6.1 anchors. Three-tier, ~1500 words.
6. **`04.11.06` Orbit-cone correspondence.** $T$-orbits of $X_\Sigma$
   in bijection with cones of $\Sigma$; orbit closures are themselves toric
   varieties (of star fans); the open orbit is $T \subset X_\Sigma$.
   Fulton §3.1 anchor; CLS §3.2 anchor. Three-tier, ~1500 words.
7. **`04.11.07` Toric resolution of singularities.** Any fan $\Sigma$
   admits a regular refinement $\widetilde\Sigma$; the induced map
   $X_{\widetilde\Sigma} \to X_\Sigma$ is a resolution. Star subdivisions
   = toric blowups; toric resolution is canonical and algorithmic
   (in contrast to Hironaka's general theorem). Fulton §2.6 anchor;
   CLS §11.1 anchor. Three-tier, ~1500 words.

**Priority 2 — divisors, line bundles, and the polytope bridge:**

8. **`04.11.08` Toric divisor and support function.** Torus-invariant
   prime divisors $D_\rho$ in bijection with rays $\rho \in \Sigma(1)$;
   the divisor $\sum a_\rho D_\rho$ is Cartier iff the piecewise-linear
   function $\varphi$ on $|\Sigma|$ with $\varphi(v_\rho) = -a_\rho$ on the
   primitive generator $v_\rho$ is linear on each cone. Fulton §3.3 anchor;
   CLS §4.0 anchor. Three-tier, ~2000 words. Cross-points
   `04.05.01`/`04.05.04`.
9. **`04.11.09` Toric Picard group.** $\mathrm{Pic}(X_\Sigma)$ = (PL
   functions on $|\Sigma|$ that are linear on each cone) / (globally
   linear functions on $|\Sigma|$). Computation for the standard
   examples. Fulton §3.4 anchor; CLS §4.2 anchor. Intermediate + Master.
   ~1500 words.
10. **`04.11.10` Polytope ↔ fan dictionary; the line bundle $L_P$.** Given
    a lattice polytope $P$, define the normal fan $\Sigma_P$ and the
    polarisation line bundle $L_P$ on $X_{\Sigma_P}$. Then
    $H^0(X_{\Sigma_P}, L_P) = \bigoplus_{m \in P \cap M} \mathbb{C} \cdot
    \chi^m$ — the lattice points of $P$ are an explicit basis of global
    sections. Ample iff $P$ has full $n$-dimensional interior. Fulton §3.4
    anchor; CLS §4.3, §6.1 anchors. Three-tier, ~2500 words.
    **Distinctive content; bridge to representation theory and physics.**
11. **`04.11.11` Algebraic moment map and the polytope.** For a projective
    toric variety $X_P$ with the compact torus $T_\mathbb{R}^n$ acting,
    the moment map sends $X_P$ onto $P$. Explicit formula via $L_P$ and
    the Fubini-Study form pulled back from the projective embedding.
    Fulton §4.2 anchor; Audin Ch. VII–VIII parallel. Intermediate + Master.
    ~1500 words. **Explicit cross-pointer to `05.04.04` Delzant theorem.**

**Priority 3 — cohomology and intersection theory:**

12. **`04.11.12` Cohomology of a smooth complete toric variety.**
    $H^*(X_\Sigma; \mathbb{Q})$ is the Stanley-Reisner ring of $\Sigma$
    modulo the linear relations from $M$. Includes the proof for the
    projective case (Jurkiewicz / Danilov). Fulton §5.2 anchor;
    CLS §12.4 anchor. Master-tier; Intermediate gives the statement and
    the computation for $\mathbb{P}^n$ and $\mathbb{P}^1 \times \mathbb{P}^1$.
    ~2500 words.
13. **`04.11.13` Toric intersection theory and mixed volumes.** For a
    smooth complete toric variety with ample divisors $L_{P_1}, \ldots,
    L_{P_n}$ from polytopes $P_1, \ldots, P_n$, the top intersection
    number is $n! \cdot \mathrm{MV}(P_1, \ldots, P_n)$, the mixed volume.
    Fulton §5.3–5.4 anchor; CLS §13.4 anchor. Master-tier. ~2000 words.
14. **`04.11.14` Bernstein-Kushnirenko theorem.** The number of solutions
    in $(\mathbb{C}^*)^n$ to a system of $n$ Laurent polynomial equations
    with generic coefficients and Newton polytopes $P_1, \ldots, P_n$ is
    $n! \cdot \mathrm{MV}(P_1, \ldots, P_n)$. Toric Bezout. Originator
    citations: Kushnirenko 1976, Bernstein 1975. Fulton §5.5 anchor (brief);
    CLS §13.5 anchor. Master-tier. ~1500 words. **Distinctive content; the
    classical Bezout reduces to this for $P_i$ = scaled simplex.**

**Priority 4 — Cox-Demazure construction; reflexive polytopes; pointers:**

15. **`04.11.15` Cox homogeneous coordinate ring; Demazure quotient
    construction.** $X_\Sigma = (\mathbb{C}^{|\Sigma(1)|} \setminus Z(\Sigma)) /\!/
    G$, where $G$ is the kernel of the torus map $(\mathbb{C}^*)^{|\Sigma(1)|}
    \to T$ dual to the linear map sending the $i$-th standard generator to
    $v_{\rho_i}$. The total coordinate ring is $\mathbb{C}[x_\rho :
    \rho \in \Sigma(1)]$. Cox 1995 originator citation (the construction
    sharpens Demazure 1970). Fulton's preface mentions this; CLS §5 is
    devoted to it. Master-tier; ~2500 words. **Explicit cross-pointer to
    `05.04.04` Delzant: the symplectic-quotient $\mathbb{C}^d /\!/ K$ in
    the Delzant construction is the topologists' shadow of the Cox
    construction.**
16. **`04.11.16` Reflexive polytope and Batyrev mirror duality (pointer).**
    Lattice polytope $P$ with $0 \in \mathrm{int}(P)$ is reflexive iff its
    polar dual $P^\circ$ is also a lattice polytope. Batyrev 1994:
    reflexive polytopes come in mirror pairs whose anti-canonical Calabi-
    Yau hypersurfaces in the associated Gorenstein toric Fano varieties
    are topologically mirror-symmetric. Pointer unit at FT-equivalence:
    statement + one worked pair (the quintic / mirror quintic via the
    standard 4-simplex). Master-only, ~1500 words. **Deferred deep content
    on mirror symmetry — see §5.**

  unit needs cone-side, polytope-side, and variety-side examples).
- 11 priority-1+2 units × ~3.5 hours = ~38–40 hours. Plus 3 priority-3
  units (~12 hours) and 2 priority-4 units (~8 hours) to reach
  ≥95% equivalence: total ~58–60 hours, a focused 7–8 day window.
- 7 priority-1 units alone (~25 hours) lift coverage from ~5% to ~50%;
  adding priority-2 (~13 hours) reaches ~80%; priority-3 reaches ~92%;
  priority-4 closes the residual.

**Originator-prose target.** ITV's content was originated by a small set
of authors with clear attributions per
`docs/plans/FASTTRACK_EQUIVALENCE_PLAN.md` §10:

- **Demazure 1970** (*Sous-groupes algébriques de rang maximum du groupe
  de Cremona*, *Ann. Sci. ENS* 4) — **originator of toric varieties** as
  smooth completions of $(\mathbb{C}^*)^n$ via fan-and-cone data,
  motivated by classifying maximal-rank subgroups of $\mathrm{Cr}_n$.
- **Mumford 1973** (Kempf-Knudsen-Mumford-Saint-Donat, *Toroidal
  Embeddings I*, Lecture Notes in Math 339, Springer) — generalised the
  toric construction to toroidal embeddings of arbitrary varieties along
  divisors; this is the algebraic-geometry-of-singularities incarnation
  of the toric story.
- **Danilov 1978** (*The geometry of toric varieties*, *Russian Math.
  Surveys* 33, pp. 97–154) — the canonical pre-Fulton survey;
  introduced the cohomology computation now associated with the
  Stanley-Reisner ring.
- **Fulton 1993** — the book itself; consolidated the working
  combinatorial-algebraic treatment used in the field today.
- **Cox 1995** (*The homogeneous coordinate ring of a toric variety*,
  *J. Algebraic Geom.* 4, 17–50) — the explicit quotient construction
  unifying the Demazure / GIT pictures.
- **Batyrev 1994** (*Dual polyhedra and mirror symmetry for Calabi-Yau
  hypersurfaces in toric varieties*, *J. Algebraic Geom.* 3, 493–535) —
  reflexive-polytope mirror duality; entry point to the toric side of
  mirror symmetry.

Each anchor unit's Master Historical section should carry at least one
of these originator citations; `04.11.03–04` (affine and gluing) should
cite Demazure 1970 explicitly; `04.11.12` (cohomology) should cite
Danilov 1978; `04.11.15` (Cox / Demazure quotient) should cite Cox 1995
and Demazure 1970 together; `04.11.16` (reflexive polytopes) should cite
Batyrev 1994.

**Notation crosswalk.** ITV uses $N$ for the cocharacter lattice and $M$
for the character lattice, with $N_\mathbb{R} = N \otimes \mathbb{R}$ and
similarly for $M_\mathbb{R}$; cones live in $N_\mathbb{R}$ and polytopes in
$M_\mathbb{R}$. CLS adopts the same convention. The Codex notation
decision (per `docs/specs/UNIT_SPEC.md` §11) should adopt
$(N, M, N_\mathbb{R}, M_\mathbb{R})$ throughout the `04.11.*` series, with
$\sigma$ for cones, $\Sigma$ for fans, $X_\Sigma$ for the toric variety,
$U_\sigma$ for affine pieces. Note the Delzant unit `05.04.04` uses
$\mathfrak{t} \cong \mathbb{R}^n$ for the torus Lie algebra and $\Lambda
= \mathbb{Z}^n \subset \mathfrak{t}$ for the integer lattice — these
identify with $N_\mathbb{R}$ and $N$ respectively, and the cross-pointer
notes in `04.11.11` and `04.11.15` should make this identification
explicit.

equivalence-coverage when:

- ≥95% of ITV's named theorems in chapters 1–5 map to Codex units.
  Currently ~5% (Delzant + general Picard partial coverage). After
  priority-1 ships: ~50%. After priority-1+2: ~80%. After priority-1+2+3:
  ~92%. After priority-1+2+3+4: ≥95%.
- ≥90% of ITV's worked computations have a direct unit or are referenced
  from a unit that covers them. The standard examples (affine toric
  varieties $\mathbb{A}^n$, the quadric cone, $\mathbb{P}^n$, $\mathbb{P}^1
  \times \mathbb{P}^1$, the Hirzebruch surfaces $\mathbb{F}_n$, blow-up of
  $\mathbb{A}^2$, the toric resolution of $A_n$ singularities) are
  distributed across priority-1 and priority-2 units' worked-example
  sections.
- Notation decisions are recorded (see §4 above).
- Cross-pointers to `05.04.04` Delzant theorem are explicit in
  `04.11.04`, `04.11.10`, `04.11.11`, and `04.11.15`. Reciprocal back-
  pointer in `05.04.04` Master section's "Algebraic-geometric
  counterpart" paragraph is updated to cite the new units.
- Pass-W weaving connects the new `04-algebraic-geometry/11-toric/`
  chapter to `05-symplectic/moment-reduction/`, to
  `04-algebraic-geometry/05-divisors/`, to
  `04-algebraic-geometry/07-projective/`, and (for the cohomology
  units) to `04-algebraic-geometry/03-cohomology/` and to
  `07-representation-theory/`.

The 7 priority-1 units close the foundational gap. Priority-2 closes the
divisor / polytope-bridge gap and supplies the explicit reciprocal
pointer to Delzant. Priority-3 closes the cohomology / intersection-
theory gap. Priority-4 is the Cox construction and the reflexive-polytope
pointer to mirror symmetry. Once all four priority bands are shipped, ITV
is at the FT-equivalence threshold.
