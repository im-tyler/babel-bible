## Critical notes for producers

1. **Prose-first contract**: biology units explain mechanisms and cite experimental evidence.
   "Key theorem" becomes "Key mechanism" or "Key experimental result". The Bridge paragraph
   must end the Key mechanism section and still hit the continuity patterns (`builds toward`,
   `appears again in`, plus ≥2 more). The historical-philosophical section should cite the
   original paper (e.g., Watson-Crick 1953 for DNA, Mendel 1866 for genetics).

2. **Biochemistry overlap**: units 17.01.02–04 (protein/carb/lipid chemistry) overlap with
   organic chemistry (15.12 amino acids, 15.13 nucleic acids). The 17.* versions focus on
   *biological function* — folding, membrane structure, signaling — not synthetic organic
   chemistry. Cross-ref 15.12 and 15.13 as the chemical-structure anchors.

3. **Section 18 physiology** relies heavily on 17.* for molecular-level explanations. Producers
   of 18.05.* should cross-ref 17.09.01–02 (action potential) and say "for the ionic mechanism
   see [17.09.01]"; the 18.05 units give anatomical organization and integrative physiology.

4. **Section 19 quantitative evolution**: units 19.02.* (population genetics), 19.04.03
   (coalescent), and 19.09.03 (Leslie matrix) are mathematical enough to cross-ref 37.* 
   (probability). Producers should cite 37.05.01 (Markov chains) for the coalescent and
   43.06.12 (matrix eigenvalue algorithms) for the Leslie matrix.
