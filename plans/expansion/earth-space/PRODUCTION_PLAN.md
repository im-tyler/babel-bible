## SECTION 27 — Earth Science

Existing: 27.01.01–27.08.01 (one per chapter).

### New units

**ch01 plate-tectonics**
`27.01.02` Plate boundaries: divergent, convergent, transform; seafloor spreading and Wilson cycles
`27.01.03` Mantle convection and driving forces: slab pull, ridge push, hot spots

**ch02 minerals-rocks**
`27.02.02` Rock cycle and igneous processes: crystallization, Bowen's reaction series
`27.02.03` Metamorphic and sedimentary rocks: facies, diagenesis, classification

**ch03 earthquakes-volcanoes**
`27.03.02` Seismic waves: P, S, surface waves; seismograph interpretation and Earth's interior
`27.03.03` Volcanic hazards: eruption styles, pyroclastic flows, volcanic risk assessment

**ch04 atmosphere-weather**
`27.04.02` Atmospheric circulation: Hadley, Ferrel, Polar cells; jet streams and Coriolis effect
`27.04.03` Weather systems: fronts, cyclones, severe weather formation

**ch05 oceanography**
`27.05.02` Ocean circulation: thermohaline conveyor, gyres, upwelling zones
`27.05.03` Ocean chemistry: salinity, carbonate system, ocean acidification

**ch06 hydrology**
`27.06.02` Groundwater systems: aquifers, Darcy's law, recharge and depletion
`27.06.03` Surface hydrology: watershed dynamics, flood frequency analysis

**ch07 climate-change**
`27.07.02` Radiative forcing and feedback loops: greenhouse gases, ice-albedo, water vapor
`27.07.03` Climate proxies and paleo-climate: ice cores, tree rings, ocean sediment records
`27.07.04` Impacts and adaptation: sea-level rise, extreme events, mitigation scenarios

**ch08 earth-history**
`27.08.02` Geologic time scale: radiometric dating, stratigraphy, and mass extinction boundaries
`27.08.03` Evolution of the atmosphere and biosphere: Great Oxidation Event, Snowball Earth

## SECTION 28 — Astronomy

Existing: 28.01.01–28.06.01 (one per chapter).

### New units

**ch01 solar-system**
`28.01.02` Formation of the solar system: nebular hypothesis, accretion, late heavy bombardment
`28.01.03` Planetary interiors and surfaces: comparative planetology, magnetic fields

**ch02 stars**
`28.02.02` Stellar structure: hydrostatic equilibrium, nuclear burning, the PP chain and CNO cycle
`28.02.03` Stellar evolution: main sequence to red giant, HR diagram tracks
`28.02.04` Stellar endpoints: white dwarfs (Chandrasekhar limit), neutron stars, pulsars, black holes

**ch03 galaxies**
`28.03.02` Galaxy classification and structure: Hubble sequence, bulge/disk/halo, dark matter halos
`28.03.03` Active galactic nuclei and quasars: accretion onto supermassive black holes

**ch04 cosmology**
`28.04.02` Big Bang nucleosynthesis: primordial abundances of H, He, Li
`28.04.03` Cosmic microwave background: temperature fluctuations, acoustic peaks, ΛCDM parameters
`28.04.04` Dark matter and dark energy: evidence, candidates, and the accelerating expansion

**ch05 exoplanets**
`28.05.02` Detection methods: radial velocity, transit photometry, direct imaging
`28.05.03` Exoplanet demographics: occurrence rates, habitability zone, atmospheric characterization

**ch06 space-exploration**
`28.06.02` Space telescope science: multiwavelength astronomy, gravitational-wave observatories
