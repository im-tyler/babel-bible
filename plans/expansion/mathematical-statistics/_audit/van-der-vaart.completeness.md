## GAP 1 — Simple linear rank statistics and locally-most-powerful rank tests

- **id:** `45.05.07`
- **title:** Simple Linear Rank Statistics and Locally Most Powerful Rank Tests
- **chapter dir:** `content/45-mathematical-statistics/05-empirical-processes-nonparametric/`
  (frontmatter `section: mathematical-statistics`, `chapter: empirical-processes-nonparametric`)
- **spec:** The general simple linear rank statistic $T_n = \sum_{i=1}^n c_{ni}\,a_n(R_i)$
  with regression constants $c_{ni}$ and scores $a_n(\cdot)$ generated by a
  score-generating function $\phi$ (Wilcoxon $\phi(u)=u-\tfrac12$, normal-scores /
  van der Waerden $\phi=\Phi^{-1}$, median/sign scores). Hájek's projection of
  $T_n$ onto sums of single-coordinate functions; asymptotic normality of the
  standardized statistic under the null via the projection method plus the CLT;
  the limit under contiguous local alternatives obtained by transporting the null
  limit with Le Cam's third lemma (the shift $\rho\,\sigma_\phi$); the resulting
  Pitman-ARE / local-power calculus and the optimal-score result that the rank
  test with $\phi$ matched to the underlying density's score function is locally
  most powerful (Chernoff–Savage / Hájek–Šidák optimality), recovering the
  Wilcoxon $3/\pi$ and the asymptotic equivalence of normal-scores to the t-test.
- **prereqs (verified to exist):**
  - `45.05.05` (u-statistics — Hájek projection method, Wilcoxon-as-U-statistic special case)
  - `45.04.07` (contiguity-le-cam-lemmas — Le Cam's third lemma, local alternatives)
  - `45.04.05` (asymptotic-relative-efficiency — Pitman ARE framework)
  - `45.04.01` (consistency-modes-of-convergence — stochastic-order calculus, Slutsky)
- **why a genuine gap:** 45.05.05 develops the Hájek *projection method* and treats
  Wilcoxon/Mann-Whitney/Kendall only as fixed degree-$m$ U-statistic kernels; it
  explicitly defers ("it appears again in the asymptotics of rank statistics").
  The legacy 26.08.01 gives the *applied* Wilcoxon/signed-rank procedures and
  states the $3/\pi$ ARE as a fact but proves no general linear-rank limit theorem
  and has no score-generating-function / contiguous-alternative / LMP-rank theory.
  45.04.07's own sources name the third lemma as "the engine for the asymptotic
  distribution theory of rank statistics under alternatives," but no unit executes
  that. The general statistic $\sum c_{ni}a_n(R_i)$ with arbitrary $c_{ni}$ and
  the optimal-score theorem are absent corpus-wide (the only "linear rank
  statistic" filename/body hit is the Hájek 1968 entry in 45.05.05's bibliography).

## GAP 2 — Cube-root asymptotics (Kim–Pollard) and Chernoff's distribution

- **id:** `45.04.11`
- **title:** Cube-Root Asymptotics and Non-Standard M-Estimator Rates
- **chapter dir:** `content/45-mathematical-statistics/04-asymptotic-statistics/`
  (frontmatter `section: mathematical-statistics`, `chapter: asymptotic-statistics`)
- **spec:** M-estimators whose criterion is not smooth enough for the $\sqrt n$
  delta-method theory and instead converge at rate $n^{1/3}$ to a non-Gaussian
  limit. The empirical-process / argmax-continuous-mapping route (vdV Ch. 5
  §5.8 with the maximal-inequality and rate machinery of Ch. 19): local
  empirical-process fluctuations of order $n^{-2/3}$ in level, a quadratic-drift
  + two-sided-Brownian-motion local limit, and the conclusion
  $n^{1/3}(\hat\theta_n-\theta_0)\xrightarrow{d}\operatorname*{argmax}_h\{aW(h)-bh^2\}$,
  i.e. (after rescaling) Chernoff's distribution = argmax of two-sided Brownian
  motion minus a parabola. Canonical examples: the empirical mode / shorth, the
  Manski maximum-score estimator, and the monotone-density (Grenander) NPMLE
  evaluated at a point. Contrast with the $\sqrt n$ Z-estimator regime to make the
  smoothness/curvature dichotomy explicit.
- **prereqs (verified to exist):**
  - `45.04.04` (m-estimators-z-estimators — standard $\sqrt n$ M/Z theory it departs from)
  - `45.05.03` (donsker-classes-empirical-process — empirical-process weak convergence)
  - `45.05.04` (entropy-vc-maximal-inequalities — maximal inequalities giving the rate)
  - `45.04.02` (delta-method — the smooth regime whose hypotheses fail here)
- **why a genuine gap:** zero filename/body hits for cube-root, Chernoff
  distribution, argmax-of-Brownian, monotone-density NPMLE, or Manski max-score
  anywhere in `content/`. 45.04.04 establishes only the standard asymptotically-
  normal M/Z theory; the non-standard $n^{1/3}$ rate with a non-Gaussian limit is
  a distinct, named van der Vaart result (Kim–Pollard 1990) with a clear
  theorem-level payoff and the empirical-process prereqs already in place.

## COVERED (not gaps) — due-diligence evidence

- **Semiparametric efficiency / efficient influence function / tangent spaces** —
  COVERED, and not "a brief mention." `45.04.08` (convolution-theorem-lam-bound)
  carries a full **Definition** of the tangent set / tangent space /
  pathwise-differentiable functional / gradient / efficient influence function as
  the orthogonal projection of a gradient onto the tangent space; a dedicated
  **Theorem 5 (semiparametric efficiency bound)** with the asymptotic-linearity
  characterization of efficiency; the **efficient score / nuisance-projection /
  efficient-information** theory ($\tilde\ell=\dot\ell_\theta-\Pi[\dot\ell_\theta\mid\dot\ell_\eta]$,
  bound $\tilde I^{-1}$); and two worked exercises (parametric EIF $a^\top I^{-1}\dot\ell$;
  semiparametric bound with a nuisance). vdV Ch. 25 + BKRW are its named sources.
  21 body occurrences of tangent/efficient-influence/semiparametric. A dedicated
  unit would duplicate this.
- **Rank statistics — Wilcoxon / signed-rank / Mann-Whitney / Kendall asymptotics
  via projection** — COVERED as U-statistic instances in `45.05.05` (Wilcoxon-Mann-
  Whitney kernel $\mathbf 1\{x\le y\}$, Kendall's tau worked end-to-end with the
  $4/9$ limit, the Hájek projection Theorem 2). Applied procedures + Pitman ARE
  $3/\pi$ + Hodges-Lehmann in legacy `26.08.01`. (The *general linear* rank
  statistic with score functions and contiguous-alternative/LMP theory is the
  residual carved out as GAP 1 — strictly more than these instances.)
- **Functional / process CLT for empirical vs partial-sum processes** — COVERED:
  Donsker's empirical-process invariance principle and weak convergence in $\ell^\infty$
  in `45.05.03`; the partial-sum / Brownian functional CLT in the probability spine
  (`37.03.03`); Glivenko-Cantelli + empirical CDF in `45.05.02`.
- **Delta method, including the functional/Hadamard form** — `45.04.02` and
  `45.05.06` (functional-delta-method-hadamard). Argmax/argmin *continuous mapping*
  in its standard $\sqrt n$ use is implicit here; only its cube-root use is new (GAP 2).
- **Consistency / modes of convergence, Slutsky, stochastic order** — `45.04.01`.
- **MLE asymptotic normality & efficiency; M/Z-estimators; ARE; LAN; contiguity /
  Le Cam's three lemmas; convolution & LAM bound; LR/Wald/score asymptotics &
  chi-squared trinity; bootstrap consistency** — `45.04.03`–`45.04.10`, all present.
- **Bernstein-type tails, VC/entropy & maximal inequalities, Donsker classes** —
  `45.05.01`, `45.05.04`, `45.05.03`. Glivenko-Cantelli `45.05.02`. U-statistics
