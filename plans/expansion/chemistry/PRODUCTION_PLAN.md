1. **Prose-first contract**: chemistry units justify mechanisms and use experimental evidence.
   The "Key theorem" section becomes "Key mechanism" or "Key result" — a derivation of a mechanism,
   thermodynamic identity, or spectroscopic selection rule. Bridge and Synthesis must still hit
   the continuity patterns.

2. **lean_status: none** on all chemistry units. The lean_mathlib_gap note (≥30 words) should
   explain what formalization would require (e.g., for DFT units: a formal proof of Hohenberg-Kohn
   requires measure-theoretic functional analysis not in Mathlib; for MO theory: linear algebra
   of the secular determinant is formalizable but the physical content is not).

3. **Cross-reference physics sections**: 14.04.* (quantum chemistry) cross-refs `12.*` (quantum
   mechanics) for the theoretical basis. 14.07.* (statistical mechanics of chemistry) cross-refs
   `11.*` (stat-mech-physics) for ensemble foundations. Do not re-derive quantum mechanics or stat
   mech — cite the parent spine and focus on the chemical application.

4. **Spectroscopy units (14.12.*)** cross-ref `12.*` (quantum mechanics) for selection rules and
   `15.11.*` (organic NMR) for practical chemical structure determination.

5. **Section 15.09 Pd catalysis** (15.09.02–03): these are among the most important modern synthetic
   tools. The Worked Example should walk through a real retrosynthetic disconnection using a Suzuki
   coupling or RCM to highlight why the catalytic cycle matters.
