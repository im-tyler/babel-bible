## Genuine gaps (propose to write)

### 1. `39.03.01` — The bicommutant theorem and the operator topologies on B(H)
- **title:** Von Neumann algebras, the bicommutant theorem, and the weak/strong operator topologies
- **spec:** Define a von Neumann algebra as a unital *-subalgebra M ⊆ B(H) closed in the weak operator topology; introduce the weak-, strong-, σ-weak (ultraweak)- and σ-strong (ultrastrong) operator topologies on B(H) and the relations among them (σ-weak ⊆ weak on bounded sets coincide; the σ-topologies are the ones intrinsic to M). Prove **von Neumann's bicommutant theorem**: for a unital *-subalgebra M ⊆ B(H), M = M″ ⇔ M is weakly closed ⇔ M is strongly closed (the density step: M is σ-strongly dense in M″). State the equivalent **abstract characterisation** (Sakai): M is a vN algebra iff it is a C*-algebra possessing a predual (a W*-algebra), deferring the predual existence to 39.03.02. Commutant, center Z(M) = M ∩ M′, abelian/maximal-abelian (MASA) subalgebras as L^∞(X,μ).
- **prereqs:** `02.11.08` (Hilbert space), `02.11.01` (bounded linear operators, B(H)), `39.01.*` **[sibling, may be co-produced]** (C*-algebra, *-algebra, states, continuous functional calculus)
- **chapter:** `03-von-neumann-algebras` · `section: operator-algebras` · `source_book: takesaki-von-neumann` · `source_curriculum: operator-algebras`
- **why a gap:** `grep -riE "bicommutant|double commutant|weak operator topology|von neumann algebra"` over `content/` → 0 genuine hits (only the physics-application files above, which take "the bicommutant" as given). The defining theorem of the subject is absent. This is Takesaki Ch. II §3.

### 2. `39.03.02` — The predual, normal states, and the σ-weak topology
- **title:** The predual of a von Neumann algebra, normal states/weights, and normality vs. σ-weak continuity
- **spec:** Construct the **predual** M_* (the unique, up to isometry, Banach space whose dual is M) as the σ-weakly continuous functionals; M = (M_*)* recovers the σ-weak topology as the weak-* topology. Characterise **normal states/positive functionals** on M three equivalently: σ-weakly continuous ⇔ completely additive on projections (ω(Σp_i) = Σω(p_i) for orthogonal families) ⇔ of the form ω(x) = Σ⟨xξ_n,ξ_n⟩ (normal form). Normal *-homomorphisms; the fact that a normal homomorphism has σ-weakly closed range. The universal enveloping von Neumann algebra A** = the bidual of a C*-algebra A as the σ-weak closure of the universal representation (the "W*-envelope"). Weights as the unbounded analogue (semifinite, faithful, normal weights) — the objects modular theory needs.
- **prereqs:** `39.03.01` (vN algebra, σ-weak topology), `02.11.04` (Banach spaces, duality), `02.11.02` (Hahn-Banach), `39.01.*` **[sibling, may be co-produced]** (states, GNS, the bidual A**)
- **chapter:** `03-von-neumann-algebras` · `section: operator-algebras` · `source_book: takesaki-von-neumann` · `source_curriculum: operator-algebras`
- **why a gap:** `grep -riE "predual|normal state|σ-weak|sigma-weak|completely additive"` over `content/` → 0 hits. The predual is the feature that distinguishes W*-algebras from general C*-algebras and is the technical heart of Takesaki Ch. III; absent. (Davidson's C*-spine builds states/GNS but not the predual / normality theory, which is a vN-specific object.)

### 3. `39.03.03` — The Kaplansky density theorem
- **title:** The Kaplansky density theorem
- **spec:** **Kaplansky's theorem:** if a *-subalgebra A ⊆ B(H) is σ-strongly (equivalently strongly) dense in a vN algebra M, then the unit ball of A is σ-strongly dense in the unit ball of M, and the self-adjoint part of A's unit ball is σ-strongly dense in M_sa's unit ball (and likewise for the positive part / unitaries). Proof via the continuity of the operator function t ↦ (1+t²)^{-1}·2t turning a strongly convergent net of self-adjoints into a *bounded* one. Corollary: density results can always be taken with norm control, the workhorse lemma for nearly every later vN argument (e.g. constructing the modular objects, approximating in II_1 factors).
- **prereqs:** `39.03.01` (operator topologies, bicommutant), `39.03.02` (normal functionals), `02.11.03` (functional calculus for self-adjoint operators)
- **chapter:** `03-von-neumann-algebras` · `section: operator-algebras` · `source_book: takesaki-von-neumann` · `source_curriculum: operator-algebras`
- **why a gap:** `grep -riE "kaplansky"` over `content/` → 0 hits. Standalone theorem, Takesaki Ch. II §4; absent.

### 4. `39.03.04` — Comparison of projections and the Murray-von Neumann type classification
- **title:** The comparison theory of projections and the Murray-von Neumann type I/II/III classification
- **spec:** **Murray-von Neumann equivalence** of projections p ~ q ⇔ p = v*v, q = vv* for a partial isometry v ∈ M; the order p ≼ q; the **comparison theorem** (for any p,q there is a central projection z with zp ≼ zq and (1−z)q ≼ (1−z)p) via the halving/exhaustion (Zorn) argument. Finite / infinite / properly infinite / purely infinite projections; **abelian** and **minimal** projections. The **type decomposition**: every vN algebra splits uniquely by central projections into type I (has an abelian projection of central support 1), type II (no abelian, has a finite projection: II_1 finite, II_∞), and type III (no nonzero finite projection); the I_n / I_∞ / II_1 / II_∞ / III refinement. **Factors** (Z(M) = ℂ) as the building blocks; type I_n factor = M_n(ℂ) or B(H). Halving lemma; the dimension/equivalence lattice of projections.
- **prereqs:** `39.03.01` (vN algebra, center, commutant), `01.01.13` (spectral theorem / projections, finite-dim orientation), `02.11.03` (projections via spectral theorem for self-adjoint operators)
- **chapter:** `03-von-neumann-algebras` · `section: operator-algebras` · `source_book: takesaki-von-neumann` · `source_curriculum: operator-algebras`
- **why a gap:** `grep -riE "murray.?von neumann|comparison theory of projections|type ii|type iii|abelian projection|partial isometry equivalence"` over `content/` → 0 genuine hits. ### 5. `39.03.05` — Traces, dimension, and the type II_1 trace; finite factors
- **title:** Traces on von Neumann algebras, the dimension function, and the canonical trace on a II_1 factor
- **spec:** Normal (semifinite, faithful) **traces** τ; the **dimension function** d(p) = τ(p) on projections; the **uniqueness** of the trace on a factor up to scaling, giving the continuous range of d: {0,1,…,n} (I_n), [0,∞] (I_∞), **[0,1]** (II_1, the "continuous dimension"), [0,∞] (II_∞), and {0,∞} (III — no trace, the reason III is invisible to this method). A factor is **finite** ⇔ it carries a faithful normal *tracial* state ⇔ 1 is a finite projection. The **center-valued (canonical) trace** on a finite vN algebra. The hyperfinite II_1 factor R as the σ-weak closure of ∪ M_{2^n}(ℂ) with the normalised trace (uniqueness of the AFD II_1 factor stated; full uniqueness/Connes deferred). The coupling constant / the trace as the source of continuous-dimension geometry. This is the theorem that makes "non-integer dimension" precise and is the gateway to subfactors, L²-Betti numbers, and free probability.

### 6. `39.04.01` — The GNS standard form and the cyclic-separating vector

### 7. `39.04.02` — Tomita's theorem: the modular operator Δ and the modular conjugation J

### 8. `39.04.03` — The modular automorphism group and the KMS condition
- **title:** The modular automorphism group σ_t and its characterisation by the KMS condition
- **spec:** The **modular automorphism group** σ_t^φ = Ad(Δ^{it}) ∈ Aut(M) attached to a faithful normal state/weight φ. The **KMS condition** at β = 1 (Takesaki's convention): φ is a (σ_t, β=1)-KMS state, i.e. for x,y ∈ M there is a function F holomorphic on the strip 0 < Im z < 1, continuous on its closure, with F(t) = φ(σ_t(x)y), F(t+i) = φ(yσ_t(x)). **Takesaki's theorem:** σ^φ is the *unique* one-parameter automorphism group for which φ satisfies the KMS condition — so the modular group is canonically determined by the state, with no Hamiltonian chosen. Consequences: the modular group fixes the centre pointwise; φ is a trace ⇔ σ_t^φ = id (recovering 39.03.05 — finite/trace ⇔ trivial modular flow). The KMS condition as the algebraic definition of thermal equilibrium (the bridge the statistical-mechanics and AQFT units consume).
- **prereqs:** `39.04.02` (modular operator Δ, Tomita's theorem), `39.03.05` (traces — the σ_t = id ⇔ trace dichotomy), `08.10.07` (Wightman/algebraic framework — cross-link, *reference only*: KMS as thermal equilibrium)
- **chapter:** `04-tomita-takesaki-modular` · `section: operator-algebras` · `source_book: takesaki-von-neumann` · `source_curriculum: operator-algebras`
- **why a gap:** `grep -riE "modular automorphism|kms condition|kms state"` over `content/` → only the physics consumers 13.09.09 / 12.14.01, which *state* the KMS boundary condition and cite Bratteli-Robinson for the proof that the modular group satisfies it. The abstract σ_t ⇔ KMS uniqueness theorem (Takesaki Ch. VIII, the LNM 128 core) is never proved in the corpus; absent.

### 9. `39.04.04` — Connes' classification of type III factors (S- and T-invariants)
- **title:** The Connes cocycle, the S- and T-invariants, and the type III_λ classification
- **spec:** **Connes' cocycle (Radon-Nikodym) theorem:** for two faithful normal weights φ,ψ there is a σ-strongly continuous family of unitaries (Dψ : Dφ)_t ∈ M (the cocycle derivative) intertwining σ_t^ψ and σ_t^φ; consequently the modular automorphism group is **canonical modulo inner automorphisms** — the map t ↦ σ_t^φ into Out(M) = Aut(M)/Inn(M) is independent of φ. This produces the two invariants: the **T-invariant** T(M) = {t : σ_t^φ is inner} and the **Connes spectrum / S-invariant** S(M) = ∩_φ spec(Δ_φ) ⊆ [0,∞). **Connes' classification of type III factors:** III_λ for λ ∈ [0,1] according to S(M) ∩ (0,∞) = {1} (III_0), = λ^ℤ (III_λ, 0<λ<1), or = (0,∞) (III_1); the type III_λ (0<λ<1) factors as crossed products / the discrete decomposition by the flow of weights (III_0). This is what makes type III — invisible to traces (39.03.05) — classifiable, and earned Connes the Fields Medal; it closes the Murray-von Neumann program that 39.03.04 opened.
- **prereqs:** `39.04.03` (modular automorphism group, KMS), `39.03.04` (type classification, type III), `39.04.02` (modular operator spectrum)
- **chapter:** `04-tomita-takesaki-modular` · `section: operator-algebras` · `source_book: takesaki-von-neumann` · `source_curriculum: operator-algebras`
- **why a gap:** `grep -riE "connes invariant|S-invariant|T-invariant|connes spectrum|III_lambda|III_1|flow of weights|cocycle derivative|radon.?nikodym.*weight"` over `content/` → 0 hits. The type III_λ classification is absent. Strictly this is Takesaki Vol. II / Connes' thesis material, but it is the natural terminus of the modular-theory chapter and the prompt explicitly scopes it to me ("Connes' classification of type III factors via the S- and T-invariants"); the *spectral-triple/NCG* side of Connes is owned by the separate Connes audit (39.06/07), so this unit stays in 39.04 as the modular-theory capstone. Coordinate with the Connes audit to avoid duplicating the cocycle theorem.
