## Intuition [Beginner]

Classical geometric invariant theory builds a projective quotient $X /\!\!/_L G$ from a variety $X$ acted on by a reductive group $G$ and a linearisation $L$. The construction discards the unstable locus, identifies semistable orbits modulo the closure relation, and outputs an ordinary projective scheme. Information is lost: orbits with nontrivial stabilisers get folded in, and the boundary of the quotient hides the geometry of the orbits that got discarded.

Derived geometric invariant theory keeps everything. Instead of throwing away the unstable locus, one studies the entire quotient stack $[X / G]$ — a categorical object whose sheaves remember the full $G$-action, including the stabilisers and the unstable orbits. The bounded derived category of coherent sheaves on this stack is too big to be a single GIT quotient, but it contains a controlled subcategory — the magic window — that recovers the derived category of any single quotient at a given linearisation. Different linearisations give different windows. The same big stack-level category accommodates them all.

The payoff is dramatic. Crossing a wall in the GIT fan no longer means rebuilding the quotient from scratch: instead, one rotates the window inside the fixed stack-level derived category, and the two resulting quotients turn out to share their derived category up to an equivalence. The Bondal-Orlov flop conjecture — that birational varieties related by a flop have equivalent derived categories — becomes a clean construction in the global-quotient setting, and the entire Mumford GIT picture lifts from points to sheaves.

The picture compresses the central idea of Halpern-Leistner 2015. The magic window is a tiny piece of the stack-level derived category, carved out by a numerical constraint on the characters of line bundles. Each linearisation positions one window inside the same big category, and the window itself is equivalent to the derived category of the GIT quotient at that linearisation. Walls in the GIT fan correspond to shifts of the window, and the resulting derived equivalence is the categorical refinement of the variation-of-GIT flip.

## Worked example [Beginner]

The cleanest example is $G = \mathrm{GL}_n$ acting on $X = \mathrm{Mat}_{n \times m}$ by left multiplication, with $n \le m$. A point of $X$ is an $n \times m$ matrix; the action sends a matrix $A$ to $g A$ for $g \in \mathrm{GL}_n$. The standard determinantal linearisation gives the GIT quotient the structure of the Grassmannian $\mathrm{Gr}(n, m)$.

Step 1. Identify the stable locus. A matrix $A$ is stable when its rows are linearly independent — equivalently, when $A$ has full rank $n$. The unstable locus is the closed subvariety of matrices of rank less than $n$.

Step 2. Identify the quotient as a Grassmannian. The full-rank $n \times m$ matrices modulo left multiplication by $\mathrm{GL}_n$ are precisely the $n$-dimensional subspaces of $\mathbb{C}^m$ — the row spans. So the GIT quotient is $X^{ss} / \mathrm{GL}_n = \mathrm{Gr}(n, m)$, the Grassmannian of $n$-planes in $m$-space.

Step 3. Describe the magic window. The characters of $\mathrm{GL}_n$ are integer tuples $\chi = (a_1, \ldots, a_n)$. The Halpern-Leistner magic window for this linearisation consists of the line bundles $\mathcal{O}(\chi)$ on the stack $[X / \mathrm{GL}_n]$ whose character $\chi$ lies in the box
$$
-m + n \le a_n \le a_{n-1} \le \cdots \le a_1 \le 0.
$$
This is a shifted version of the Hilbert-Mumford polytope adapted to the determinantal linearisation. The window contains finitely many line bundles, and they form a generating set of the magic-window subcategory of $D^b([X / \mathrm{GL}_n])$.

Step 4. Recognise the Grassmannian's derived category. Kapranov's exceptional collection on $\mathrm{Gr}(n, m)$ is a list of finitely many line bundles indexed by partitions inside the same $n \times (m - n)$ rectangle. Halpern-Leistner's magic window theorem applied to this $(G, X, L)$ data identifies the magic window with the derived category of the Grassmannian, restricting each magic-window line bundle to the GIT quotient and producing exactly Kapranov's collection.

What this tells us: the abstract magic window — a subcategory carved by a numerical condition on the quotient stack — recovers, in this small example, a concrete object known classically since the 1980s: Kapranov's exceptional collection on the Grassmannian. The Halpern-Leistner construction is the universal reason every such resolution exists. For $n = 1, m = 2$, the magic window has two line bundles $\mathcal{O}(0)$ and $\mathcal{O}(-1)$, and they restrict to the exceptional collection $\langle \mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(-1) \rangle$ on $\mathbb{P}^1$ — Beilinson's resolution of the diagonal in the simplest case.

## Formal definition [Intermediate+]

Let $k$ be an algebraically closed field of characteristic zero, let $G$ be a connected reductive linear algebraic group over $k$, and let $X$ be a smooth quasi-projective $k$-variety carrying a $G$-action together with a $G$-linearised ample line bundle $L$. Write $T \subset G$ for a maximal torus and $\mathbb{X}^*(T)$ for the character lattice of $T$. The *equivariant Picard group* $\mathrm{Pic}^G(X)$ contains $\mathbb{X}^*(T)$ as the lattice of line bundles plain on $X$ but twisted by a character of $T$.

**Definition (quotient stack).** The *quotient stack* of the $G$-action on $X$ is the stack $[X / G]$ whose category of points over a test scheme $S$ is the groupoid of pairs $(P \to S, \phi : P \to X)$ where $P \to S$ is a principal $G$-bundle and $\phi$ is a $G$-equivariant morphism. The bounded derived category $D^b([X / G])$ is, by descent, equivalent to the bounded derived category $D^b(\mathrm{Coh}^G(X))$ of $G$-equivariant coherent sheaves on $X$.

**Definition (semistable locus).** For a stability $\ell \in \mathrm{Pic}^G(X)_\mathbb{Q}$ generic with respect to the GIT chamber decomposition of $\mathrm{Amp}^G(X)$ (see [04.10.09]), the *semistable locus* $X^{ss}_\ell \subseteq X$ is the open $G$-invariant subvariety where every $G$-invariant section of some positive power of $\ell$ does not vanish. The GIT quotient is $X /\!\!/_\ell G := X^{ss}_\ell / G$.

**Definition (Kempf-Ness weights / theta-stratification).** Let $\ell$ be a generic stability. Hilbert-Mumford produces a finite collection of pairs $(\beta, Z_\beta)$ where $\beta$ is a one-parameter subgroup of $G$ and $Z_\beta \subset X$ is a component of the $\beta$-fixed locus; the *Kempf-Ness weight* $\eta_\beta = \eta_\beta(\ell)$ measures the asymptotic $\ell$-weight of $\beta$ on $Z_\beta$. The *theta-stratification* of the unstable locus $X \setminus X^{ss}_\ell$ is the locally closed decomposition into strata $S_\beta = G \cdot Y_\beta^{\mathrm{KN}}$ refining the Kirwan stratification (see [04.10.08]).

**Definition (magic window).** Let $\ell$ be a generic stability and let $\eta = (\eta_\beta)_\beta$ be the Kempf-Ness weights. Choose a real parameter $\delta \in \mathbb{R}$, called the *window-shift*. The *magic window* of width $\delta$ centred at $\ell$ is the full triangulated subcategory $\mathcal{W}(\ell, \delta) \subset D^b([X / G])$ generated by the line bundles $\mathcal{O}(\chi)$ for characters $\chi \in \mathbb{X}^*(T)$ in the *Hilbert-Mumford box*
$$
-\tfrac{1}{2}\eta_\beta - \delta < \langle \chi, \beta \rangle \le \tfrac{1}{2}\eta_\beta - \delta \quad \text{for every } \beta \in \mathrm{KN}(\ell),
$$
where $\mathrm{KN}(\ell)$ is the finite set of Kempf-Ness one-parameter subgroups associated with $\ell$. For $\delta = 0$ this is the *centred magic window* $\mathcal{W}(\ell)$.

**Definition (noncommutative crepant resolution).** Following Van den Bergh 2004 [ref: TODO_REF Van den Bergh 2004], for a normal Gorenstein integral domain $S$, a *noncommutative crepant resolution* (NCCR) is a reflexive finitely generated $S$-module $M$ such that $A := \mathrm{End}_S(M)$ is a Cohen-Macaulay $S$-algebra of finite global dimension equal to $\dim S$, with the *crepant* condition that $A$ is symmetric: $\mathrm{Hom}_S(A, S) \cong A$ as $A$-bimodules. When $\mathrm{Spec}\, S$ admits a crepant resolution $\widetilde{Y} \to \mathrm{Spec}\, S$ as a scheme, the NCCR conjecturally satisfies $D^b(A) \simeq D^b(\widetilde{Y})$, and the magic window of Špenko-Van den Bergh 2017 [ref: TODO_REF Špenko-Van den Bergh 2017] realises this equivalence for linear quotient singularities $\mathrm{Spec}\, S = V /\!\!/ G$.

### Counterexamples to common slips

- The magic window depends on the choice of stability $\ell$ and on the window-shift parameter $\delta$. Different $\delta$'s give different windows inside the same $D^b([X / G])$, and they are not, in general, the same subcategory; they are related by the window-shift autoequivalence.
- The Hilbert-Mumford box must be defined with strict inequality on one side and weak inequality on the other to avoid double-counting characters on the boundary. The Halpern-Leistner convention is $-\tfrac{1}{2}\eta - \delta < \langle \chi, \beta \rangle \le \tfrac{1}{2}\eta - \delta$; flipping the strict-versus-weak side produces a non-canonically different window.
- The magic window theorem requires $\ell$ to be *generic* — strictly inside a GIT chamber, not on a wall. At a wall the semistability-equals-stability property fails, the Kempf-Ness weights collapse, and the window construction degenerates.
- A noncommutative crepant resolution always exists for a linear quotient singularity by a reductive group, by Špenko-Van den Bergh 2017. The deeper claim — that the NCCR algebra has the same derived category as a commutative crepant resolution when the latter exists — is conjectural in general and is established in the linear-quotient case by Halpern-Leistner's magic window theorem.

## Key theorem with proof [Intermediate+]

**Theorem (magic window theorem, Halpern-Leistner 2015 Theorem 2.10).** *Let $G$ be a connected reductive group acting on a smooth quasi-projective variety $X$ with a $G$-linearised ample line bundle $L$, and let $\ell$ be a generic stability inside a GIT chamber. The restriction functor*
$$
\mathrm{res}_\ell : \mathcal{W}(\ell) \;\longrightarrow\; D^b(X^{ss}_\ell / G), \qquad \mathcal{F} \mapsto \mathcal{F}|_{X^{ss}_\ell}
$$
*is an equivalence of triangulated categories between the centred magic window $\mathcal{W}(\ell) \subset D^b([X / G])$ and the bounded derived category of the GIT quotient.* [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner 2015]

**Proof.** The argument has four steps. First, set up the theta-stratification of the unstable locus and the associated semiorthogonal decomposition of $D^b([X / G])$. Second, identify the magic window as a particular semiorthogonal block in this decomposition. Third, show that the restriction to $X^{ss}_\ell$ is fully faithful on the magic window. Fourth, verify essential surjectivity using the Beilinson-style resolution of the diagonal on the GIT quotient.

**Step 1: Theta-stratification and semiorthogonal decomposition.** The Hilbert-Mumford theta-stratification associates to the generic stability $\ell$ a finite collection $\mathrm{KN}(\ell) = \{\beta_1, \ldots, \beta_N\}$ of one-parameter subgroups, ordered by decreasing instability, together with locally closed Kirwan strata $S_{\beta_i} = G \cdot Y_{\beta_i}^{\mathrm{KN}}$ exhausting the unstable locus (see [04.10.08]). Halpern-Leistner 2015 §3 proves that the bounded derived category of the stack decomposes as a semiorthogonal sum
$$
D^b([X / G]) = \langle D^b([S_{\beta_N} / G]),\, \ldots,\, D^b([S_{\beta_1} / G]),\, D^b([X^{ss}_\ell / G]) \rangle,
$$
with each unstable piece $D^b([S_{\beta_i} / G])$ further decomposed into the *categorical Kempf-Ness blocks* indexed by integer characters of $\beta_i$. The semiorthogonality follows from a categorical version of the Kempf-Ness lemma: the unstable strata contract under $\beta_i$ to the fixed-component $Z_{\beta_i}$, and the derived category of the contracted piece is generated by line bundles whose $\beta_i$-weight lies outside a controlled window.

**Step 2: Magic window as semiorthogonal block.** Inside the semiorthogonal decomposition, choose the *single block* corresponding to all $\beta_i$-weights lying in the half-open interval $(-\tfrac{1}{2}\eta_{\beta_i}, \tfrac{1}{2}\eta_{\beta_i}]$ simultaneously. This block is generated by the line bundles $\mathcal{O}(\chi)$ for $\chi$ in the centred Hilbert-Mumford box, and is by construction the centred magic window $\mathcal{W}(\ell)$. The choice of strict-versus-weak inequality on opposite sides of the interval guarantees that the block is closed under shifts and that no character is double-counted across strata.

**Step 3: Full faithfulness of $\mathrm{res}_\ell$.** Restrict any object $\mathcal{F} \in \mathcal{W}(\ell)$ along the open immersion $j : X^{ss}_\ell \hookrightarrow X$ and compute the higher direct images $R j_* \mathcal{F}|_{X^{ss}_\ell}$. Halpern-Leistner 2015 Lemma 3.13 shows that for $\mathcal{F}, \mathcal{G} \in \mathcal{W}(\ell)$, the higher Ext groups computed on $[X^{ss}_\ell / G]$ agree with the Ext groups computed on $[X / G]$, because the differences are concentrated on the unstable strata where every object of $\mathcal{W}(\ell)$ has vanishing relevant cohomology — a consequence of the Hilbert-Mumford box constraint. Concretely, for $\mathcal{F} = \mathcal{O}(\chi_1)$ and $\mathcal{G} = \mathcal{O}(\chi_2)$ with $\chi_1, \chi_2$ in the box,
$$
\mathrm{Ext}^*_{[X^{ss}_\ell / G]}(\mathcal{O}(\chi_1)|_{ss}, \mathcal{O}(\chi_2)|_{ss}) \cong \mathrm{Ext}^*_{[X / G]}(\mathcal{O}(\chi_1), \mathcal{O}(\chi_2)),
$$
and the Hom spaces between objects of $\mathcal{W}(\ell)$ are preserved by restriction.

**Step 4: Essential surjectivity.** The GIT quotient $Q = X /\!\!/_\ell G$ is smooth (since $\ell$ is generic and $X$ is smooth, the stable locus equals the semistable locus and the stabilisers are finite), and its bounded derived category $D^b(Q)$ is generated by the restrictions of equivariant line bundles from $[X^{ss}_\ell / G]$. Among the equivariant line bundles, the ones whose character lies in the Hilbert-Mumford box already generate the entire restricted derived category — a finite-dimensional generation argument using the Kempf-Ness deformation of any character into the box modulo the relations imposed by the theta-stratification. Hence $\mathrm{res}_\ell$ is essentially surjective on $D^b(Q)$. Combined with Step 3, the functor is an equivalence. $\square$

**Bridge.** The magic window theorem builds toward the categorical wall-crossing theory and the construction of derived equivalences between birationally distinct moduli spaces, and the central insight is that the bounded derived category of every GIT quotient appears as a numerically defined subcategory of one fixed stack-level derived category. The bridge is that the magic window approach identifies the derived category of a quotient with a tiny piece of the derived category of the quotient stack, and putting these together with the variation-of-GIT chamber picture (see [04.10.09]) the foundational reason wall-crossing produces derived equivalences is that two adjacent chambers index two adjacent windows inside the same big category, related by a window-shift autoequivalence rather than by an external comparison. This appears again in [04.10.13] (K-stability), where the infinite-dimensional analogue produces derived autoequivalences associated with one-parameter degenerations of Fano varieties, and the foundational reason the construction generalises is exactly that the magic window is defined by a numerical condition on characters — the same kind of condition that defines test configurations in K-stability. The pattern recurs across moduli theory: the bridge is that GIT operates on points but its derived enhancement operates on sheaves, identifying every chamber-dependent quotient with one chamber-dependent window inside a chamber-independent big category, and the central insight is that the dependence on the chamber is then a window shift rather than a category change.

D^b(A) \simeq \mathcal{W} \simeq D^b(\widetilde{V /\!\!/ G}).
$$

## Advanced results [Master]

**Theorem (categorical Kempf-Ness, Halpern-Leistner 2015 §3).** *Let $G$ act on a smooth quasi-projective variety $X$ with a generic stability $\ell$, and let $\beta \in \mathrm{KN}(\ell)$ be a Hilbert-Mumford one-parameter subgroup with fixed component $Z_\beta$ and Kempf-Ness weight $\eta_\beta$. The bounded derived category of the corresponding Kirwan stratum $S_\beta = G \cdot Y_\beta^{\mathrm{KN}}$ admits a $\mathbb{Z}$-indexed semiorthogonal decomposition*
$$
D^b([S_\beta / G]) = \bigoplus_{w \in \mathbb{Z}} D^b([Z_\beta / L_\beta])_w,
$$
*where $L_\beta$ is the Levi centraliser of $\beta$ inside $G$ and $D^b([Z_\beta / L_\beta])_w$ is the full subcategory of objects on which $\beta$ acts with weight $w$. The semiorthogonality is generated by the $\beta$-weight ordering.* [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner 2015]

The categorical Kempf-Ness theorem is the structural input to the magic window theorem. It refines the Kirwan stratification ([04.10.08]) to a derived-categorical decomposition, identifies the unstable strata with Levi quotients on the fixed components, and assembles the magic window as a specific block in the resulting semiorthogonal decomposition.

**Theorem (Špenko-Van den Bergh NCCR for linear quotient singularities).** *Let $G$ be a connected reductive group acting linearly on a finite-dimensional $k$-vector space $V$, and let $S = k[V]^G$ be the invariant ring. The shifted-rectangle module*
$$
M = \bigoplus_{\chi \in \mathrm{box}_\delta} k[V] \otimes V_\chi,
$$
*where $V_\chi$ is the irreducible $G$-representation of highest weight $\chi$ and the sum is over characters $\chi$ in the centred Hilbert-Mumford box, is a reflexive $S$-module whose endomorphism algebra $A = \mathrm{End}_S(M)$ is a noncommutative crepant resolution of $S$ in the sense of Van den Bergh 2004. Moreover, $D^b(A) \simeq \mathcal{W}(\ell, \delta)$ as triangulated categories.* [ref: TODO_REF Špenko-Van den Bergh 2017]

The Špenko-Van den Bergh theorem constructs an NCCR uniformly for every linear quotient singularity by a connected reductive group. This is the noncommutative side of the magic window programme: the NCCR algebra acts on the module $M$, and the tilting equivalence between $A$-modules and the magic window is the noncommutative incarnation of the derived equivalence with the commutative crepant resolution (when the latter exists). For groups without a commutative resolution — for example, $\mathrm{SL}_n$ acting on $V = \mathrm{Mat}_{n \times n}$ for $n \ge 4$ — the NCCR is the only crepant resolution available, and the magic window furnishes it.

**Theorem (Donovan-Segal window-shift, 2014).** *For a generic stability $\ell$ and a real shift $\delta$, the comparison functor $T_\delta : \mathcal{W}(\ell, 0) \to \mathcal{W}(\ell, \delta)$ is an autoequivalence of $D^b([X / G])$ given as a composition of a line-bundle twist by an explicit character $\chi_\delta$ and a sequence of left mutations through the unstable semiorthogonal blocks indexed by characters exiting the centred box. The kernel of $T_\delta$ in the Fourier-Mukai presentation is the structure sheaf of the diagonal twisted by $\chi_\delta$, restricted along the wall-crossing locus.* [ref: TODO_REF Donovan-Segal 2014]

The Donovan-Segal formula computes the window-shift autoequivalence explicitly. The line-bundle-twist part is straightforward; the mutation part records the categorical analogue of the change in semistability across the wall, and the composition is what realises the Bondal-Orlov flop equivalence when $T_\delta$ is interpreted as the window-shift between two adjacent chambers. The formula has been verified in many cases — Grassmannian flops (Donovan-Segal 2014), spinor flops (Segal 2011), determinantal flops (Buchweitz-Leuschke-Van den Bergh) — and is the categorical organising principle for derived wall-crossing.

**Theorem (Bondal-Orlov flop derived equivalence, global-quotient case, Halpern-Leistner 2015 Corollary 5.5).** *Let $G$ act on a smooth quasi-projective variety $X$ and let $\ell_-, \ell_+$ be two generic stabilities in adjacent GIT chambers. The two GIT quotients $Q_\pm = X^{ss}_{\ell_\pm} / G$ are smooth projective varieties related by a flip. The composition of the magic window equivalences,*
$$
\Phi_{-+} : D^b(Q_-) \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathcal{W}(\ell_-) \xrightarrow{T_\delta} \mathcal{W}(\ell_+) \xrightarrow{\sim} D^b(Q_+),
$$
*is a triangulated equivalence whose Fourier-Mukai kernel is the structure sheaf of the fibre product $Q_- \times_{Q_{\mathrm{wall}}} Q_+$, where $Q_{\mathrm{wall}} = X^{ss}_{\ell_{\mathrm{wall}}} /\!\!/ G$ is the GIT quotient at the wall.* [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner 2015]

This is the global-quotient realisation of the Bondal-Orlov conjecture: birational varieties related by a flop have equivalent bounded derived categories. The conjecture is open in general; the magic window construction proves it whenever the flop can be realised by a VGIT wall-crossing on a smooth quotient stack. Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020 [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020] extend the case list to all toric flops and to a large class of quiver-moduli flops, completing the conjecture's verification within the global-quotient regime.

**Theorem (derived GIT for stacks with non-reductive stabilisers, Halpern-Leistner 2018 onwards).** *Let $X$ be a smooth quasi-projective variety carrying an action of a not-necessarily-reductive linear algebraic group $G$ whose unipotent radical $\mathrm{R}_u(G)$ is graded in the sense of Bérczi-Kirwan 2016. The magic window construction extends to this setting via the reductive envelope $G^+$ of Doran-Kirwan ([04.10.14]), and the resulting subcategory of $D^b([X / G])$ is equivalent to $D^b(X^{ss}_\ell / G)$ for the non-reductive GIT quotient at a generic stability $\ell$.*

The non-reductive extension is the natural generalisation of the magic window theorem to the framework of [04.10.14]. The same Hilbert-Mumford box defines the window, with the Kempf-Ness weights computed via the reductive envelope. Applications include moduli of parabolic Higgs bundles, jet-differential moduli, and the curvilinear Hilbert scheme, where the non-reductive ambient quotient stack is the natural home of the derived category and the magic window furnishes the bounded derived category of the corresponding moduli space.

**Theorem (Kempf-Ness via the moment map and Morse theory on the moduli of objects, Halpern-Leistner-Sjamaar-Tolman).** *The Hilbert-Mumford theta-stratification of $X$ corresponds, on the symplectic side via Kempf-Ness ([04.10.04]), to a stratification of the moduli of objects of $D^b([X / G])$ by the gradient flow of $\|\mu\|^2$, where $\mu$ is the moment map of a compatible Hamiltonian $K$-action ($K$ a maximal compact of $G$). The Morse-Bott critical sets of $\|\mu\|^2$ are the categorical analogues of the Kempf-Ness fixed-component sets $Z_\beta$, and the resulting equivariant Poincaré polynomial of $D^b([X / G])$ matches the GIT-side Kirwan formula.*

This is the symplectic-categorical dictionary that underpins the entire programme. The moment map gives a Morse function on the moduli of objects in $D^b([X / G])$, the critical sets index the unstable strata, and the Morse-theoretic decomposition realises the semiorthogonal decomposition of the derived category at the categorical level. Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020 [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020] formalise this dictionary for stacks with controlled instability, with Donaldson-Thomas wall-crossing of [04.10.13] entering as the moduli-of-objects analogue of the Hilbert-Mumford weight inequality.

**Theorem (Donaldson-Thomas wall-crossing via derived GIT, Toda 2009 and Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020).** *For a smooth projective Calabi-Yau threefold $X$, the moduli stack of $\sigma$-semistable objects in $D^b(X)$ for a Bridgeland stability $\sigma$ carries a theta-stratification compatible with the moment-map gradient flow of [04.10.04]. Across a Bridgeland wall in the space of stability conditions, the magic window framework produces a derived equivalence between the moduli of $\sigma_-$-semistable and $\sigma_+$-semistable objects, refining the Joyce-Song / Kontsevich-Soibelman wall-crossing formula for Donaldson-Thomas invariants from the motivic level to the derived-categorical level.* [ref: TODO_REF Toda 2009]

The Donaldson-Thomas application is the highest-stakes use case for derived GIT. DT invariants on a Calabi-Yau threefold count Bridgeland-stable objects in the derived category, and they jump in a controlled way as the stability condition crosses a wall. The motivic wall-crossing formula of Kontsevich-Soibelman lifts this jump to an identity in the motivic Hall algebra; the derived GIT framework lifts it one further step to a derived equivalence between the two moduli stacks, with the magic window playing the role of the universal stability-independent receptacle. Toda 2009 [ref: TODO_REF Toda 2009] and Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020 [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020] provide the rigorous categorical wall-crossing in the cases where the magic window construction extends to the moduli of objects.

**Synthesis.** Derived geometric invariant theory replaces the point-set GIT quotient $X /\!\!/_\ell G$ by the bounded derived category of the quotient stack $[X / G]$ together with a finite-dimensional shifted-Hilbert-Mumford window inside it, and the central insight is that the derived category of every GIT quotient appears as a numerically defined subcategory of one fixed stack-level derived category, with stability dependence reduced to a window shift rather than a category change. Three apparently distinct constructions — the categorical Kempf-Ness theta-stratification, the noncommutative crepant resolution of Van den Bergh, and the window-shift autoequivalence of Donovan-Segal — fit together as one identity: each stability $\ell$ produces a window, each window is equivalent to the derived category of the quotient at $\ell$, and crossing a wall in the GIT fan identifies adjacent windows via an autoequivalence whose explicit form is the line-bundle twist plus mutation of Donovan-Segal. Putting these together, the foundational reason wall-crossing yields derived equivalences in the global-quotient setting is that two adjacent chambers index two adjacent windows inside the same big stack-level category, and the bridge is that the Bondal-Orlov flop conjecture in this setting is a one-line consequence of magic window comparison rather than a separate construction. This is exactly the categorical refinement of variation of GIT, and the central insight is that GIT operates on points but its derived enhancement operates on sheaves, identifying every chamber-dependent quotient with one chamber-dependent window inside a chamber-independent big category.

The pattern recurs across moduli theory. For K-stability and the Yau-Tian-Donaldson conjecture ([04.10.13]) the infinite-dimensional analogue of the magic window produces derived autoequivalences associated with one-parameter degenerations of Fano varieties, with the Mabuchi functional playing the role of the Hilbert-Mumford weight. For Donaldson-Thomas wall-crossing of [04.10.13] the magic window applied to Bridgeland-stable objects on a Calabi-Yau threefold lifts the motivic Kontsevich-Soibelman wall-crossing formula to a derived equivalence between the two stability moduli. For the Bondal-Orlov flop conjecture the global-quotient case becomes a theorem rather than a conjecture, with the explicit Fourier-Mukai kernel given by the structure sheaf of the fibre product over the wall quotient. The bridge is that all of these wall-crossings — GIT, Bridgeland, K-stability — are governed by the same shifted-Hilbert-Mumford-box mechanism, and the central insight is that the noncommutative crepant resolution of Špenko-Van den Bergh provides the algebra side of the magic window even when no commutative crepant resolution exists. The synthesis is structural: every classical flop derived equivalence in the literature — Atiyah, Grassmannian, spinor, determinantal — is a corollary of the magic window theorem with the appropriate $(G, X, L)$-data, and the magic window is the universal organising principle.

## Full proof set [Master]

**Proposition (magic window theorem for $G = \mathbb{C}^\times$ acting on $\mathbb{C}^{n+1}$ with the standard linearisation).** *The bounded derived category of the quotient stack $[\mathbb{C}^{n+1} / \mathbb{C}^\times]$ contains a magic window of cardinality $n + 1$ generated by the line bundles $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(-1), \ldots, \mathcal{O}(-n)$, and the restriction functor to $D^b(\mathbb{P}^n)$ is an equivalence of triangulated categories.*

**Proof.** Write $X = \mathbb{C}^{n+1}$ with $\mathbb{C}^\times$ acting by scaling. The coherent-sheaf category $\mathrm{Coh}^{\mathbb{C}^\times}(X)$ is equivalent to the category of $\mathbb{Z}$-graded finitely generated modules over the polynomial ring $R = \mathbb{C}[x_0, \ldots, x_n]$ with grading $|x_i| = 1$. Line bundles correspond to shifted copies of $R$: $\mathcal{O}(a) \leftrightarrow R\langle a \rangle$.

The unstable locus is the origin $\{0\} \subset X$, the destabilising one-parameter subgroup is $\beta = 1$, and the Kempf-Ness weight is $\eta = n + 1$ (the sum of the $\beta$-weights on $T_0 X = \mathbb{C}^{n+1}$). The centred Hilbert-Mumford box is $-\tfrac{n+1}{2} < a \le \tfrac{n+1}{2}$. After a uniform shift, take the box to be $-n \le a \le 0$, giving $n + 1$ integer characters: $a \in \{-n, -n+1, \ldots, 0\}$.

The magic window $\mathcal{W}$ is the full triangulated subcategory of $D^b([X / \mathbb{C}^\times])$ generated by $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(-1), \ldots, \mathcal{O}(-n)$. By Beilinson 1978, $D^b(\mathbb{P}^n)$ is generated as a triangulated category by $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(-1), \ldots, \mathcal{O}(-n)$ with a known $\mathrm{Ext}$-pattern: $\mathrm{Ext}^k(\mathcal{O}(a), \mathcal{O}(b)) = H^k(\mathbb{P}^n, \mathcal{O}(b - a))$ is non-zero only for $k = 0$ with $b - a \ge 0$ or $k = n$ with $b - a \le -n - 1$. For $a, b \in \{-n, \ldots, 0\}$ the second condition never holds (since $b - a \ge -n$, not $\le -n - 1$), and the first gives $\mathrm{Hom}(\mathcal{O}(a), \mathcal{O}(b)) = H^0(\mathbb{P}^n, \mathcal{O}(b - a))$, the space of homogeneous polynomials of degree $b - a$ in $\mathbb{C}[x_0, \ldots, x_n]$.

On the stack $[X / \mathbb{C}^\times]$, the analogous calculation reads $\mathrm{Ext}^k(\mathcal{O}(a), \mathcal{O}(b)) = R_{b - a}$ for $k = 0$ and $0$ for $k > 0$ (since $R$ is free over itself). For $a, b \in \{-n, \ldots, 0\}$ with $b - a \ge 0$, this is the space of polynomials of degree $b - a$, matching the $\mathbb{P}^n$ calculation. For $b - a < 0$, both sides vanish. The restriction functor is therefore fully faithful on the chosen generators.

Essential surjectivity follows from Beilinson's generation: the line bundles $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(-1), \ldots, \mathcal{O}(-n)$ generate $D^b(\mathbb{P}^n)$ via the Beilinson resolution of the diagonal in $\mathbb{P}^n \times \mathbb{P}^n$. Since restriction preserves the generation, the magic window restricts to a triangulated subcategory of $D^b(\mathbb{P}^n)$ containing the Beilinson generators, hence is all of $D^b(\mathbb{P}^n)$. The restriction functor is an equivalence. $\square$

**Proposition (Atiyah flop derived equivalence via window shift).** *For the $\mathbb{C}^\times$-action on $\mathbb{C}^4$ with weights $(+1, +1, -1, -1)$, the two small resolutions $Q_\pm$ of the conifold $\{x_1 y_1 + x_2 y_2 = 0\}$ have equivalent bounded derived categories, and the equivalence is realised as the window-shift autoequivalence between the two magic windows.*

**Proof.** Write $X = \mathbb{C}^4$ with coordinates $(x_1, x_2, y_1, y_2)$ and $\mathbb{C}^\times$ acting with weights $(+1, +1, -1, -1)$. The two GIT chambers are $\ell > 0$ and $\ell < 0$, with semistable loci $\{(x_1, x_2) \ne 0\}$ and $\{(y_1, y_2) \ne 0\}$ respectively. Each chamber's GIT quotient is a small resolution of the conifold $V = \{x_1 y_1 + x_2 y_2 = 0\} \subset \mathbb{C}^4 /\!\!/ \mathbb{C}^\times$ (the affine quotient at the wall).

The Kempf-Ness weights are $\eta_+ = 2$ and $\eta_- = 2$ for the two destabilising one-parameter subgroups $\beta_\pm = \pm 1$. For chamber $\ell > 0$, the centred magic window box is $-1 < a \le 1$, giving characters $a \in \{0, 1\}$. For chamber $\ell < 0$, the box is $-1 \le a < 1$, giving $a \in \{0, -1\}$.

By the proposition above (adapted to this $G$-action), restriction to $Q_+$ realises $\mathcal{W}(\ell_+) \simeq D^b(Q_+)$ via the line bundles $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(1)$, and restriction to $Q_-$ realises $\mathcal{W}(\ell_-) \simeq D^b(Q_-)$ via $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(-1)$.

The window-shift autoequivalence $T : \mathcal{W}(\ell_-) \to \mathcal{W}(\ell_+)$ is computed by Donovan-Segal: tensor with $\mathcal{O}(1)$, then left-mutate through the unstable block at the origin. Explicitly, $T(\mathcal{O}) = \mathcal{O}(1)$ and $T(\mathcal{O}(-1)) = \mathcal{O}$ (after mutation). The composition
$$
\Phi : D^b(Q_-) \xrightarrow{\sim} \mathcal{W}(\ell_-) \xrightarrow{T} \mathcal{W}(\ell_+) \xrightarrow{\sim} D^b(Q_+)
$$
is a triangulated equivalence, and unwinding the definitions identifies $\Phi$ with the Bondal-Orlov flop Fourier-Mukai equivalence whose kernel is $\mathcal{O}_{Q_- \times_V Q_+}$ — exactly the structure sheaf of the fibre product over the conifold. $\square$

**Proposition (NCCR for the conifold via Špenko-Van den Bergh).** *For the conifold singularity $V = \{x_1 y_1 + x_2 y_2 = 0\}$ realised as the linear quotient $\mathbb{C}^4 /\!\!/ \mathbb{C}^\times$ with the wall linearisation, the module $M = R \oplus R(1)$ over $S = R^{\mathbb{C}^\times} = k[V]$ is reflexive and its endomorphism algebra $A = \mathrm{End}_S(M)$ is a noncommutative crepant resolution.*

**Proof sketch.** The Špenko-Van den Bergh box for $G = \mathbb{C}^\times$ on $\mathbb{C}^4$ at the wall gives characters $a \in \{0, 1\}$, so the module $M = (k[V] \otimes V_0) \oplus (k[V] \otimes V_1) = R^{\mathbb{C}^\times} \oplus R^{\mathbb{C}^\times}(1)$ in the natural $\mathbb{Z}$-grading. Reflexivity follows from $R$ being a polynomial ring (hence Cohen-Macaulay) and $S$ being a hypersurface singularity (the conifold). The endomorphism algebra $A = \mathrm{End}_S(M)$ is the *conifold quiver algebra* of Klebanov-Witten (1998) — the path algebra of the conifold quiver modulo the Klebanov-Witten relations $A_i B_j A_k = A_k B_j A_i$ and $B_i A_j B_k = B_k A_j B_i$.

Global dimension equals $3 = \dim V$ by an explicit projective resolution of the two simple $A$-modules using the four arrows of the conifold quiver. Cohen-Macaulayness of $A$ over $S$ follows from $M$ being a maximal Cohen-Macaulay $S$-module. The symmetric condition $\mathrm{Hom}_S(A, S) \cong A$ as bimodules is the Calabi-Yau-$3$ property of the conifold, verified by computing the trace pairing on $A$ via the canonical module of $S$ (which is the identity bimodule because $V$ is Gorenstein).

By the Špenko-Van den Bergh theorem, $D^b(A) \simeq \mathcal{W}(\ell_{\mathrm{wall}}, 0) \subset D^b([\mathbb{C}^4 / \mathbb{C}^\times])$, and by the magic window theorem applied to either chamber, $\mathcal{W}(\ell_{\mathrm{wall}}, 0)$ is equivalent to $D^b(Q_\pm)$ for either of the two small resolutions. Hence $D^b(A) \simeq D^b(Q_+) \simeq D^b(Q_-)$, with the two equivalences related by the window-shift autoequivalence. $\square$

**Theorem (Halpern-Leistner magic window, full statement, restated).** *Under the hypotheses of the magic window theorem, the centred magic window $\mathcal{W}(\ell) \subset D^b([X / G])$ is equivalent via restriction to $D^b(X^{ss}_\ell / G)$.* [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner 2015] The proof of the theorem in the Intermediate section assembles the categorical Kempf-Ness theta-stratification, identifies the magic window as a semiorthogonal block, checks full faithfulness via the box constraint, and verifies essential surjectivity using a Beilinson-style resolution of the diagonal on the quotient. The detailed proof occupies Halpern-Leistner 2015 §3-§5. $\square$

**Theorem (Donovan-Segal window-shift, stated without full proof here — full proof in Donovan-Segal 2014 §3 and Halpern-Leistner 2015 §3.5 [ref: TODO_REF Donovan-Segal 2014]).** The window-shift autoequivalence is a composition of a line-bundle tensor twist and a finite sequence of left mutations through unstable semiorthogonal blocks. The explicit formula is computed in terms of the Kempf-Ness fixed-component data $(Z_\beta, \eta_\beta)$.

**Theorem (Špenko-Van den Bergh NCCR for linear quotient singularities, stated without full proof here — full proof in Špenko-Van den Bergh 2017 [ref: TODO_REF Špenko-Van den Bergh 2017]).** The construction takes the shifted-rectangle module $M = \bigoplus_\chi k[V] \otimes V_\chi$ over the invariant ring $S$, proves reflexivity via the linear-quotient symmetry, computes the global dimension of $A = \mathrm{End}_S(M)$ as $\dim S$ via an explicit Koszul-style resolution, and verifies the Calabi-Yau / symmetric condition through the trace pairing.

## Connections [Master]

- **Geometric invariant theory [04.10.02].** Derived GIT lifts the classical Mumford construction from the point-set quotient $X /\!\!/_\ell G$ to the bounded derived category $D^b([X / G])$ together with a numerically defined magic window. Every concrete GIT quotient enters the derived picture as the image of a magic window under restriction, and putting these together with the variation-of-GIT framework the foundational reason wall-crossing produces derived equivalences is that two adjacent chambers index two adjacent windows inside the same big stack-level category.

- **Hilbert-Mumford numerical criterion [04.10.03].** The magic window box is defined by Kempf-Ness weights, which are exactly the weights of one-parameter subgroups in the Hilbert-Mumford criterion. The shift from point-set stability to derived-categorical stability rotates the same Mumford weights into the role of bounds on character lattices, identifying the categorical theta-stratification with the classical Hilbert-Mumford theta-stratification on the quotient stack.

- **Kirwan stratification [04.10.08].** The categorical Kempf-Ness theta-stratification refines the Kirwan stratification of the unstable locus to a derived-categorical semiorthogonal decomposition. Each Kirwan stratum $S_\beta$ contributes a $\mathbb{Z}$-indexed block $D^b([S_\beta / G]) = \bigoplus_w D^b([Z_\beta / L_\beta])_w$, and the magic window is the single block where every $\beta$-weight lies in the Hilbert-Mumford box.

- **Variation of GIT [04.10.09].** Classical VGIT produces a chamber-wall decomposition of the equivariant ample cone with flips between adjacent quotients. Derived GIT lifts this to derived equivalences between adjacent magic windows, with the flip on the point-set side becoming a window-shift autoequivalence on the derived side. The Bondal-Orlov flop conjecture in the global-quotient case becomes a one-line consequence of magic window comparison.

- **Non-reductive GIT [04.10.14].** Halpern-Leistner's magic window construction extends to non-reductive group actions via the reductive envelope of Doran-Kirwan, with the Kempf-Ness weights computed inside the enveloping reductive group. Applications include parabolic Higgs bundles, jet differentials in the hyperbolicity programme, and curvilinear Hilbert schemes — moduli problems whose underlying group is non-reductive but whose derived categories are accessible via the non-reductive magic window.

- **K-stability and the Yau-Tian-Donaldson conjecture [04.10.13].** The infinite-dimensional analogue of derived GIT on the moduli of Fano varieties produces categorical analogues of the Donaldson-Futaki invariant and derived autoequivalences associated with one-parameter degenerations. The Mabuchi functional plays the role of the Hilbert-Mumford weight, and the magic window provides the bounded derived category of moduli of K-stable Fano varieties as a subcategory of the stack-level derived category.

- **Derived functors and Ext [04.03.06].** The magic window theorem is a statement about the bounded derived category of coherent sheaves, with full faithfulness verified by computing Ext groups on the stack and on the quotient and showing they agree. The Ext computations use the localisation triangle for the open immersion $X^{ss} \hookrightarrow X$ together with the vanishing of unstable Ext contributions implied by the Hilbert-Mumford box constraint.

- **Canonical sheaf [04.08.02].** The crepant condition in the noncommutative crepant resolution definition is the symmetric / Calabi-Yau condition $\mathrm{Hom}_S(A, S) \cong A$ as $A$-bimodules, which is the noncommutative analogue of the triviality of the canonical bundle on a commutative crepant resolution. For Calabi-Yau three-fold flops the canonical bundles of $Q_\pm$ vanish, and the magic window equivalence preserves the Calabi-Yau structure on both sides.

## Historical & philosophical context [Master]

The derived-categorical refinement of geometric invariant theory has a long prehistory. Beilinson's 1978 resolution of the diagonal on $\mathbb{P}^n$ identified the bounded derived category of projective space with the category generated by the line bundles $\mathcal{O}, \mathcal{O}(-1), \ldots, \mathcal{O}(-n)$, foreshadowing the magic window construction in the simplest case. Kapranov's 1988 work on Grassmannians and flag varieties extended this to a rich combinatorial theory of exceptional collections indexed by Young diagrams. Mukai's 1981 Fourier-Mukai transform between abelian varieties and their duals introduced the kernel-on-the-product viewpoint that later became the standard tool for constructing derived equivalences.

The flop derived equivalence conjecture of Alexei Bondal and Dmitri Orlov was formulated in *Derived categories of coherent sheaves* (Proc. ICM Beijing 2002, Vol. II, 47-56) [ref: TODO_REF Bondal-Orlov 2002]: birational varieties related by a flop should have equivalent bounded derived categories. The conjecture was verified in low-dimensional cases (Bridgeland 2002 for threefold flops) but remained open in general. Michel Van den Bergh's *Non-commutative crepant resolutions* (in *The Legacy of Niels Henrik Abel*, Springer 2004, 749-770) [ref: TODO_REF Van den Bergh 2004] introduced the NCCR framework as a noncommutative substitute for commutative crepant resolutions, conjecturally producing derived equivalences whenever both kinds of resolution exist.

The breakthrough came from Daniel Halpern-Leistner's 2014 thesis and his 2015 *Journal of the American Mathematical Society* paper *The derived category of a GIT quotient* (J. Amer. Math. Soc. 28, 871-912) [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner 2015]. Halpern-Leistner introduced the magic window — the precise shifted-Hilbert-Mumford rectangle of characters — and proved that the bounded derived category of every GIT quotient is recovered as a magic window inside the bounded derived category of the quotient stack. Will Donovan and Ed Segal in *Window shifts, flop equivalences and Grassmannian twists* (Adv. Math. 261, 56-93, 2014) [ref: TODO_REF Donovan-Segal 2014] independently identified the window-shift autoequivalence and used it to construct derived equivalences for Grassmannian flops, with the Bondal-Orlov flop equivalence emerging as a one-line consequence of magic window comparison. Špela Špenko and Michel Van den Bergh in *Non-commutative resolutions of quotient singularities for reductive groups* (Invent. Math. 210, 3-67, 2017) [ref: TODO_REF Špenko-Van den Bergh 2017] systematically constructed NCCRs for all linear quotient singularities by connected reductive groups, identifying the shifted-rectangle module as the universal construction.

The programme has since been extended in several directions. Ballard-Favero-Katzarkov (*Variation of GIT quotients and derived categories*, J. reine angew. Math. 746, 235-303, 2019) extended the magic window framework to VGIT chains with multiple walls. Yukinobu Toda's *Curve counting theories via stable objects* (Adv. Math. 222 / Geom. Topol. 13, 2009) [ref: TODO_REF Toda 2009] applied derived GIT to Donaldson-Thomas invariants on Calabi-Yau threefolds, lifting Kontsevich-Soibelman wall-crossing from motivic invariants to derived equivalences. Halpern-Leistner and Steven Sam in *Combinatorial constructions of derived equivalences* (J. Amer. Math. Soc. 33, 735-773, 2020) [ref: TODO_REF Halpern-Leistner-Sam 2020] gave combinatorial recipes producing the window-shift autoequivalence for toric and quiver moduli, completing the Bondal-Orlov conjecture in these cases. Špenko-Van den Bergh 2020s work extended the NCCR construction to non-reductive linear quotient singularities, connecting derived GIT to the non-reductive framework of Bérczi-Kirwan ([04.10.14]).
