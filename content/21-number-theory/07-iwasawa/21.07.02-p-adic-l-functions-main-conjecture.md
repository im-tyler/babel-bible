## Intuition [Beginner]

The Riemann zeta function and the Dirichlet $L$-functions are analytic functions of a complex variable $s$. Their values at negative integers turn out to be remarkably simple: $\zeta(-1) = -1/12$, $\zeta(-3) = 1/120$, and in general $\zeta(1 - n) = -B_n/n$ for $n \geq 1$, where $B_n$ is the $n$-th Bernoulli number — a rational number. The same pattern holds for Dirichlet $L$-functions: $L(1 - n, \chi)$ is a rational number called a generalised Bernoulli number, divided by $n$.

The Bernoulli numbers satisfy beautiful congruences modulo prime powers, first discovered by Kummer in 1851. Roughly, if two indices $n$ and $m$ are close in a $p$-adic sense (their difference is divisible by a high power of $p - 1$, with $p$ an odd prime), then $B_n/n$ and $B_m/m$ are also close in a $p$-adic sense (their difference is divisible by a high power of $p$). The Bernoulli numbers, viewed through $p$-adic glasses, are continuous: nearby indices give nearby values.

Kubota and Leopoldt in 1964 turned this into an analytic statement. They proved that for each odd prime $p$ and each Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor coprime to $p$, there exists a unique continuous function $L_p(s, \chi)$ taking $p$-adic values, defined for $s$ in the $p$-adic integers, and interpolating the classical Dirichlet $L$-values at negative integers (after removing one Euler factor at $p$). This is the $p$-adic $L$-function. The Iwasawa Main Conjecture, proved by Mazur and Wiles in 1984, says that this analytic $p$-adic L-function equals a purely algebraic invariant: the characteristic ideal of a Galois-module built from ideal class groups of cyclotomic fields.

## Worked example [Beginner]

Verify a Kummer congruence and check that it is consistent with the existence of a continuous $p$-adic interpolation.

Step 1. The first few Bernoulli numbers are $B_2 = 1/6, B_4 = -1/30, B_6 = 1/42, B_8 = -1/30, B_{10} = 5/66, B_{12} = -691/2730$. The values $\zeta(1 - n) = -B_n/n$ are therefore $\zeta(-1) = -1/12, \zeta(-3) = 1/120, \zeta(-5) = -1/252, \zeta(-7) = 1/240$, and so on.

Step 2. Take $p = 5$. Kummer's congruence says: for indices $n$ and $m$ with $n \equiv m \pmod{p - 1} = 4$ and both not divisible by $p - 1 = 4$, the ratios $B_n/n$ and $B_m/m$ are congruent modulo $p = 5$.

Step 3. Check at $n = 2$ and $m = 6$, which satisfy $n \equiv m = 2 \pmod 4$ and neither is divisible by $4$. Compute $B_2/2 = (1/6)/2 = 1/12$. Compute $B_6/6 = (1/42)/6 = 1/252$. The difference is $1/12 - 1/252 = (21 - 1)/252 = 20/252 = 5/63$, which is divisible by $5$. The two ratios are congruent modulo $5$.

What this tells us: a continuous $p$-adic function on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ that takes the value $-B_n/n$ at the negative integer $1 - n$ is consistent with these congruences. The Kummer congruences are exactly the seed from which the existence of the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function is proved.

## Formal definition [Intermediate+]

Fix an odd prime $p$ throughout. Let $\mathbb{Z}_p$ be the ring of $p$-adic integers, $\mathbb{Q}_p$ its fraction field, $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_p}$ a fixed algebraic closure, and $\mathbb{C}_p$ the completion of $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_p}$ with respect to the unique extension of the $p$-adic absolute value. The Teichmuller character $\omega : (\mathbb{Z}/p)^\times \to \mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ is the unique homomorphism lifting the identity to a $(p - 1)$-th root of unity; equivalently, $\omega(a)$ is the unique $(p - 1)$-th root of unity in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ congruent to $a$ modulo $p$, for $a$ coprime to $p$.

For a Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor $f$ coprime to $p$, the **generalised Bernoulli numbers** $B_{n, \chi}$ are the rational numbers defined by the generating function
$$
\sum_{a = 1}^{f} \chi(a) \frac{t e^{a t}}{e^{f t} - 1} = \sum_{n \geq 0} B_{n, \chi} \frac{t^n}{n!}.
$$
The classical Dirichlet $L$-function values at negative integers are
$$
L(1 - n, \chi) = -\frac{B_{n, \chi}}{n}, \qquad n \geq 1.
$$

**Definition (Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function).** For every Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor $f$ coprime to $p$, the **Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function** $L_p(\cdot, \chi)$ is the unique continuous function $L_p : \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{C}_p$ satisfying
$$
L_p(1 - n, \chi) = \left( 1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) \, p^{n - 1} \right) L(1 - n, \chi \omega^{-n}), \qquad n \geq 1,
$$
where the right-hand side is the classical Dirichlet $L$-value of the twisted character $\chi \omega^{-n}$ with the Euler factor at $p$ removed. The values exist and the continuous interpolation is unique by Kubota-Leopoldt 1964 *J. reine angew. Math.* 214/215.

The function $L_p(\cdot, \chi)$ is identically zero unless $\chi$ is **even** (i.e., $\chi(-1) = +1$), because the right-hand side vanishes for odd $\chi$ when $\chi \omega^{-n}$ becomes principal. For odd $\chi$, one defines $L_p(s, \chi) = 0$ by convention.

**Definition (Iwasawa algebra).** Let $K_\infty = \mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty})$ be the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of $\mathbb{Q}$ — the compositum of all cyclotomic fields $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^n})$ for $n \geq 1$, with Galois group $\Gamma = \mathrm{Gal}(K_\infty / \mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$. The **Iwasawa algebra** is the completed group ring
$$
\Lambda := \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]] = \varprojlim_n \mathbb{Z}_p[\Gamma / \Gamma^{p^n}].
$$
Choosing a topological generator $\gamma$ of $\Gamma$, the map $\gamma \mapsto 1 + T$ gives a topological algebra isomorphism $\Lambda \cong \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ (Serre 1958 *Sém. Bourbaki*). The Iwasawa algebra is a complete local Noetherian regular ring of Krull dimension $2$ with maximal ideal $(p, T)$.

**Definition (cyclotomic Iwasawa module).** For an even non-principal Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor coprime to $p$, the **cyclotomic Iwasawa module** $X_\chi^-$ is the $\chi$-eigenspace of the minus-part of
$$
X = \varprojlim_n A_n,
$$
where $A_n$ is the $p$-Sylow subgroup of the ideal class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^{n + 1}})$, and the projective limit is taken with respect to the norm maps. The minus-part is the $-1$-eigenspace of complex conjugation, and the $\chi$-eigenspace is the component of the minus-part on which $(\mathbb{Z}/N p)^\times$ acts by $\chi$ (where $N$ is the conductor of $\chi$).

By Iwasawa's structure theorem (Iwasawa 1959, 1973 *Ann. Math.*), $X_\chi^-$ is a finitely generated torsion $\Lambda$-module, hence pseudo-isomorphic to a finite direct sum of elementary modules $\Lambda/(p^{a_i}) \oplus \Lambda/(f_j(T)^{b_j})$, with $f_j(T)$ a distinguished polynomial (a polynomial in $T$ with leading coefficient $1$ and other coefficients in $p \mathbb{Z}_p$).

**Definition (characteristic ideal).** The **characteristic ideal** of $X_\chi^-$ is
$$
\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) := \left( p^{\sum a_i} \prod_j f_j(T)^{b_j} \right) \subseteq \Lambda,
$$
the principal ideal generated by the product of the elementary divisors.

### Counterexamples to common slips

- *"$L_p(s, \chi)$ equals $L(s, \chi)$ at every integer $s$."* The $p$-adic L-function agrees with the classical L-function at negative integers only after the **Euler factor at $p$ is removed**: $L_p(1 - n, \chi) = (1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) p^{n - 1}) L(1 - n, \chi \omega^{-n})$. The twist by $\omega^{-n}$ reflects the natural decomposition $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times \cong \mu_{p - 1} \times (1 + p \mathbb{Z}_p)$. At positive integers, $L_p$ does not generally agree with $L$ on the nose; the $p$-adic L-function is a genuinely $p$-adic-analytic object that happens to interpolate certain rational values, not a $p$-adic version of $L$ itself.

- *"The Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda$ is a polynomial ring $\mathbb{Z}_p[T]$."* $\Lambda \cong \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ is the ring of formal power series, not polynomials. The completion is essential: characteristic elements of Iwasawa modules are typically genuine power series, not polynomials.

- *"The Main Conjecture is an equality of L-values."* The Main Conjecture is an equality of ideals: $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) = (\xi_\chi)$, where both sides are principal ideals of $\Lambda$. The element $\xi_\chi$ is determined up to a unit of $\Lambda$, so the equality is at the level of principal ideals — not at the level of specific power-series representatives. Specific values $\xi_\chi(\zeta - 1)$ at finite-order Iwasawa characters $\zeta$ recover specific classical L-values via the interpolation property.

## Key theorem with proof [Intermediate+]

The signature theorem of this unit is the existence and uniqueness of the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function: the interpolation values are consistent in the $p$-adic topology, so a unique continuous extension to $\mathbb{Z}_p$ exists.

**Theorem (Kubota-Leopoldt 1964 *J. reine angew. Math.* 214/215).** *Fix an odd prime $p$ and a Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor $f$ coprime to $p$. There exists a unique continuous function $L_p(\cdot, \chi) : \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{C}_p$ such that for every positive integer $n \geq 1$,*
$$
L_p(1 - n, \chi) = \left( 1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) \, p^{n - 1} \right) L(1 - n, \chi \omega^{-n}).
$$

*Proof.* Uniqueness is immediate: the negative integers $1 - n$ for $n \geq 1$ are dense in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ (every $s \in \mathbb{Z}_p$ is the $p$-adic limit of a sequence of negative integers), so a continuous extension from a dense set to a $p$-adic complete codomain is unique.

For existence, the strategy is the **Kummer congruence** approach. For each pair of positive integers $n, m$ with $n \equiv m \pmod{p^{a - 1}(p - 1)}$ and neither divisible by $p - 1$, the generalised Bernoulli numbers satisfy
$$
(1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) p^{n - 1}) \frac{B_{n, \chi \omega^{-n}}}{n} \equiv (1 - \chi \omega^{-m}(p) p^{m - 1}) \frac{B_{m, \chi \omega^{-m}}}{m} \pmod{p^a},
$$
generalising the classical Kummer congruence for the Riemann zeta case ($\chi$ principal).

The proof of the Kummer congruence uses the generating function. Multiply both sides of
$$
\sum_{a = 1}^{f p} (\chi \omega^{-n})(a) \frac{t e^{a t}}{e^{f p t} - 1} = \sum_{k \geq 0} B_{k, \chi \omega^{-n}} \frac{t^k}{k!}
$$
by $t^{-n - 1}$ and integrate around a small $p$-adic contour. Use the relation $\omega^{-n}(a) \equiv a^{-n} \pmod p$ and $\omega(a)^{-n} \cdot a^n \equiv 1 \pmod p$ for $a$ coprime to $p$. The integrand becomes, modulo $p^a$, identical for $n \equiv m \pmod{p^{a - 1}(p - 1)}$ (the cyclotomic structure of $(\mathbb{Z}/p^a)^\times$). The Bernoulli-number congruence follows by extracting coefficients.

With the Kummer congruences in hand, define $L_p(1 - n, \chi)$ at each negative integer by the right-hand side of the interpolation identity. The function $n \mapsto L_p(1 - n, \chi)$, viewed as a $\mathbb{C}_p$-valued function on $\{1 - n : n \geq 1\} \subset \mathbb{Z}_p$, is **uniformly continuous** by the Kummer congruences: if $1 - n$ and $1 - m$ are within $p^{-a}$ in the $p$-adic metric on $\mathbb{Z}_p$, then their images are within $p^{-a}$ in the $p$-adic metric on $\mathbb{C}_p$.

The negative integers form a dense set in $\mathbb{Z}_p$, and a uniformly continuous function on a dense subset of a complete metric space extends uniquely to a continuous function on the whole space. The extension $L_p : \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{C}_p$ is the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function. $\square$

**Bridge.** This existence theorem builds toward [21.07.01] $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions and Iwasawa modules, where $L_p$ acquires its interpretation as an element of the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ via the Iwasawa-Mellin transform, and the foundational reason this works is that the $p$-adic Mellin transform identifies measures on $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ with power series in $T$ (the **Amice transform**, Amice 1964). The central insight of Kubota-Leopoldt is that $p$-adic continuity of $L$-values at negative integers is forced by the Kummer congruences, which themselves come from the Galois structure of $(\mathbb{Z}/p^a)^\times$. Putting these together identifies the analytic $p$-adic L-function with an element of $\Lambda$ that is then conjecturally identified with the characteristic ideal of an Iwasawa module — the Iwasawa Main Conjecture. The bridge is from analysis ($p$-adic interpolation of special values) to algebra (an ideal in the Iwasawa algebra), and the bridge runs through the Mellin-transform isomorphism between $p$-adic measures and power series.

**Exercise 3 (medium, symbolic).**

State the **Kummer congruence** in its original form (Kummer 1851): for $p$ an odd prime and positive even integers $n \equiv m \pmod{p - 1}$ with $n, m$ not divisible by $p - 1$, the ratios $B_n/n$ and $B_m/m$ are congruent modulo $p$. Derive the modulo-$p^2$ refinement, which is the technical input to the Kubota-Leopoldt theorem.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The modulo-$p$ Kummer congruence comes from comparing the generating-function expansions of the Bernoulli numbers at $p$-adic precision $p$. The refinement to modulo $p^a$ requires $n \equiv m \pmod{p^{a - 1}(p - 1)}$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Kummer congruence (Kummer 1851).** Let $p$ be an odd prime, and let $n, m \geq 2$ be even integers with $n \equiv m \pmod{p - 1}$ and $n, m$ not divisible by $p - 1$. Then
$$
\frac{B_n}{n} \equiv \frac{B_m}{m} \pmod p.
$$

**Refinement (Kubota-Leopoldt 1964, technical lemma).** Under the stronger hypothesis $n \equiv m \pmod{p^{a - 1}(p - 1)}$, with $n, m$ not divisible by $p - 1$,
$$
(1 - p^{n - 1}) \frac{B_n}{n} \equiv (1 - p^{m - 1}) \frac{B_m}{m} \pmod{p^a}.
$$

**Derivation of the refinement.** The generating function $\sum B_n t^n/n! = t/(e^t - 1)$ has a meromorphic continuation as a function of $t$ via the Bernoulli polynomial $B_n(x)$ generating function $\sum B_n(x) t^n/n! = t e^{x t}/(e^t - 1)$. The substitution $x \mapsto x/(p t)$ and the use of the multiplication formula $B_n(p x) = p^{n - 1} \sum_{k = 0}^{p - 1} B_n(x + k/p)$ produces the corrected ratio $(1 - p^{n - 1}) B_n/n$ as a sum over a finite set of $p$-adic Bernoulli polynomial values at fractions $k/p$. The congruence modulo $p^a$ then follows by truncating the $p$-adic expansion of $B_n(x + k/p)$ in powers of $(x + k/p)^n$ and using the binomial identity to control the dependence on $n$ modulo $p^{a - 1}(p - 1)$.

The technical content is that the Bernoulli-number generating function, viewed as a function of the exponent $n$, is $p$-adic-analytic in the Iwasawa sense: it extends to a continuous function on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ once the Euler factor at $p$ is removed. The refinement modulo $p^a$ is what makes the Kubota-Leopoldt interpolation $p$-adically continuous in the strong sense required for the existence of $L_p$.

</details>

**Exercise 4 (medium, symbolic).**

State the **cyclotomic Iwasawa Main Conjecture for $\mathbb{Q}$** (Mazur-Wiles 1984 *Invent. Math.* 76). Identify the algebraic and analytic sides of the equality.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Main Conjecture equates the characteristic ideal of a Galois-module (algebraic side) to the ideal generated by a $p$-adic L-function viewed inside the Iwasawa algebra (analytic side).

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Main Conjecture (Mazur-Wiles 1984).** Fix an odd prime $p$ and an even non-principal Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor coprime to $p$. Let $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]]$ be the Iwasawa algebra of $\Gamma = \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty})/\mathbb{Q}) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$, and let $X_\chi^-$ be the $\chi$-eigenspace of the minus-part of the cyclotomic Iwasawa module. Let $\xi_\chi \in \Lambda$ be the image of the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function $L_p(\cdot, \chi)$ under the Iwasawa-Mellin transform identifying continuous functions on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ with elements of $\Lambda$. Then
$$
\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) = (\xi_\chi) \qquad \text{in } \Lambda.
$$

**Algebraic side.** $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-)$ is the characteristic ideal of the cyclotomic Iwasawa module — a purely algebraic invariant built from ideal class groups of cyclotomic fields. Specifically, $X_\chi^-$ is pseudo-isomorphic by Iwasawa's structure theorem to a direct sum $\bigoplus \Lambda/(f_i)$ with $f_i \in \Lambda$, and $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) = (\prod f_i)$.

**Analytic side.** $\xi_\chi$ is the Iwasawa-algebra realisation of the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function — a purely analytic invariant built from special values of Dirichlet L-functions through the interpolation property. The element $\xi_\chi$ is characterised by $\xi_\chi(\kappa^n - 1) = L_p(1 - n, \chi)$ for $n \geq 1$, where $\kappa : \Gamma \to 1 + p \mathbb{Z}_p$ is the cyclotomic character.

The conjecture identifies these two seemingly unrelated invariants. Mazur-Wiles 1984 proved this for $\mathbb{Q}$; Wiles 1990 *Ann. Math.* 131 extended to totally real fields; Skinner-Urban 2014 *Invent. Math.* 195 proved the elliptic-curve analogue.

**Exercise 5 (medium, symbolic).**

Sketch the Mazur-Wiles strategy for proving the Iwasawa Main Conjecture: how do congruences between cuspidal Hecke eigenforms and Eisenstein series produce the equality of characteristic ideal and $p$-adic L-function?

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The strategy is to find a cuspidal Hecke eigenform congruent modulo the Eisenstein ideal to an Eisenstein series whose Fourier coefficients encode the $p$-adic L-function. The associated Galois representation then produces a class in Galois cohomology that bounds the Iwasawa module on one side, while the existence of the congruence bounds the $p$-adic L-function on the other.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Mazur-Wiles 1984 strategy.** Fix an odd prime $p$ and an even non-principal Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor $N$ coprime to $p$.

**Step 1 (Eisenstein series).** Construct a $\Lambda$-adic Eisenstein series $\mathbf{E}_\chi$ on $\Gamma_1(N p^r)$ — a formal $q$-expansion with coefficients in $\Lambda$ whose Fourier coefficient at $q^n$ encodes the divisor power sum $\sigma_{k - 1}(n)$ twisted by $\chi$. The constant term of $\mathbf{E}_\chi$ equals $\xi_\chi/2$, the $p$-adic L-function over $2$.

**Step 2 (Eisenstein ideal).** Let $\mathbb{T}$ be the universal deformation Hecke algebra acting on the space of $p$-adic modular forms of level $N p^\infty$ and tame character $\chi$. The **Eisenstein ideal** $I_E \subseteq \mathbb{T}$ is the kernel of the Eisenstein system: $I_E = \ker(\mathbb{T} \to \Lambda)$ sending $T_\ell \mapsto 1 + \chi(\ell) \ell^{k - 1}$ for $\ell$ a prime away from $N p$. Then $\mathbb{T}/I_E \cong \Lambda$, and the ideal $I_E \cap \Lambda$ inside $\mathbb{T}$ is the ideal $(\xi_\chi)$ generated by the constant term of $\mathbf{E}_\chi$.

**Step 3 (cuspidal congruences).** Search for cuspidal Hecke eigenforms in the same space whose Hecke eigenvalues are congruent modulo $I_E$ to the Eisenstein ones. The existence of such a cuspidal congruence at depth $a$ in the Eisenstein ideal is equivalent to $\xi_\chi \in I_E^a$.

**Step 4 (Galois representation).** Attach the $2$-dimensional $\Lambda$-adic Galois representation $\rho : G_\mathbb{Q} \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{T})$ to the universal cuspidal Hecke deformation. The reduction modulo $I_E$ is the diagonal Eisenstein representation $1 \oplus \chi \kappa$, where $\kappa$ is the cyclotomic character; the off-diagonal entries of $\rho$ — viewed mod $I_E$ — produce non-vanishing cohomology classes in $H^1(G_\mathbb{Q}, \chi)$ that, on restriction to the cyclotomic tower, define a $\Lambda$-module structure on $X_\chi^-$.

**Step 5 (one divisibility).** The depth-$a$ congruence in Step 3 produces, via Step 4, a cohomology class that bounds the size of $X_\chi^-$ from above: $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) \mid \xi_\chi^a$ for every $a$ for which the cuspidal congruence holds. Taking the supremum over $a$ gives $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) \mid \xi_\chi$.

**Step 6 (other divisibility, Ferrero-Washington).** The reverse divisibility $\xi_\chi \mid \mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-)$ follows from the analytic class-number formula combined with the **Ferrero-Washington theorem 1979** (the cyclotomic Iwasawa $\mu$-invariant for abelian number fields vanishes). Together, both divisibilities give the equality of ideals — the Main Conjecture.

The strategy is conceptually a deep generalisation of Ribet's 1976 *Invent. Math.* converse to Herbrand's theorem, which uses cuspidal-Eisenstein congruences to detect Galois-module structure on ideal class groups.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Euler systems.** An **Euler system** for a $p$-adic Galois representation $V$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is a compatible family $\{c_n\}$ of cohomology classes $c_n \in H^1(\mathbb{Q}(\mu_n), V)$ indexed by squarefree integers $n$, satisfying the **norm-compatibility relation** $\mathrm{cor}_{n}^{n \ell} c_{n \ell} = P_\ell(\mathrm{Frob}_\ell) c_n$ for $\ell$ a prime not dividing $n$, where $P_\ell$ is the local Euler factor of the $L$-function attached to $V$.

**Cyclotomic-units Euler system (Kolyvagin 1989 *Birkhäuser*, Rubin 1991 *Invent. The proof is more transparent and the bound is sharper in some cases (Rubin 1991 proves both divisibilities for imaginary quadratic fields by combining cyclotomic and elliptic-unit Euler systems).

**Subsequent extensions.** Kato 2004 *Astérisque* 295 constructed an Euler system for modular Galois representations using $K_2$ of modular curves (the Beilinson-Kato classes), giving one divisibility in the elliptic-curve Iwasawa Main Conjecture; this provides the unconditional half of the BSD-Iwasawa programme, complemented by Skinner-Urban 2014 for the other half.

**Exercise 8 (hard, symbolic).**

Explain the role of the **Ferrero-Washington theorem 1979** (the cyclotomic Iwasawa $\mu$-invariant vanishes for abelian number fields) in completing the Mazur-Wiles proof of the Main Conjecture. What goes wrong without it?

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Mazur-Wiles strategy bounds one divisibility via Galois representations; the reverse divisibility uses analytic information from the class-number formula. Ferrero-Washington shows the $\mu$-invariant of $X_\chi^-$ vanishes, which is needed to convert the analytic class-number information into a $\Lambda$-module statement.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Iwasawa invariants.** By Iwasawa's structure theorem, the cyclotomic Iwasawa module $X_\chi^-$ is pseudo-isomorphic to $\Lambda/(p^\mu) \oplus \Lambda/(f(T))$ with $\mu = \mu(X_\chi^-) \geq 0$ an integer and $f(T)$ a distinguished polynomial. The integer $\mu$ is the **Iwasawa $\mu$-invariant** and the degree of $f$ is the **Iwasawa $\lambda$-invariant**.

**Ferrero-Washington 1979 *Ann. Math.* 109.** For every even Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor coprime to the odd prime $p$, the cyclotomic Iwasawa $\mu$-invariant vanishes: $\mu(X_\chi^-) = 0$. Equivalently, $X_\chi^-$ has no $\Lambda$-submodules isomorphic to $\Lambda/(p)$.

**Role in the Main Conjecture proof.** Mazur-Wiles 1984 establishes the divisibility $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) \mid \xi_\chi$ via the Galois-representation argument. The reverse divisibility $\xi_\chi \mid \mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-)$ comes from the analytic class-number formula at finite levels combined with Iwasawa's $p$-adic class-number formula. The class-number formula relates $|A_n|$ (the $p$-Sylow of the ideal class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^{n+1}})$, projected to the $\chi$-component) to $L(0, \chi \omega) = -B_{1, \chi \omega}$ at finite levels.

Without Ferrero-Washington, the formula relates only the **$p$-power-divisible part** of $X_\chi^-$ to the $p$-adic L-function, leaving the $\mu$-component undetermined. With Ferrero-Washington providing $\mu = 0$, the entirety of $X_\chi^-$ is captured by its $\lambda$-invariant and the class-number-formula data, yielding the desired reverse divisibility.

**Status outside $\mathbb{Q}$.** The Ferrero-Washington theorem is specific to abelian number fields over $\mathbb{Q}$. For totally real fields $F \neq \mathbb{Q}$, the analogous $\mu = 0$ statement is conjectural (Iwasawa's $\mu = 0$ conjecture), proved in important cases (Sinnott 1984 for $F$ contained in a cyclotomic field) but open in general. Wiles 1990's Main Conjecture for totally real fields proves only the divisibility statements; the equality of ideals is conditional on $\mu = 0$ in many cases.

**What goes wrong without it.** Without Ferrero-Washington, the Main Conjecture statement weakens to: $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) \cdot \Lambda_p = (\xi_\chi) \cdot \Lambda_p$ in the localisation $\Lambda_p = \Lambda[1/p]$. The integral equality at $\Lambda$ requires $\mu = 0$; over $\Lambda_p$ the $\mu$-component is invertible and disappears. The full Main Conjecture is recovered only when $\mu = 0$ is known.

## Advanced results [Master]

### Kubota-Leopoldt 1964: the originator paper

Kubota and Leopoldt's 1964 paper *Eine p-adische Theorie der Zetawerte. I. Einführung der p-adischen Dirichletschen L-Funktionen* in volume 214/215 of the *Journal für die reine und angewandte Mathematik* introduced the $p$-adic L-function as a continuous interpolation of the classical Dirichlet $L$-values [ref: TODO_REF KubotaLeopoldt1964]. The paper followed a long tradition of $p$-adic continuity statements for Bernoulli numbers — Kummer's 1851 congruences, von Staudt's 1840 theorem on Bernoulli denominators, and Vandiver's 1937 work on irregular primes — but it was the first to organise these congruences into a single analytic object.

The construction proceeds in three steps. First, observe that the classical Dirichlet L-values at negative integers are rational: $L(1 - n, \chi) = -B_{n, \chi}/n$ for $n \geq 1$, where $B_{n, \chi}$ is the generalised Bernoulli number. Second, prove a refined Kummer congruence: for integers $n, m$ with $n \equiv m \pmod{p^{a - 1}(p - 1)}$, the corrected Bernoulli ratios $(1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) p^{n - 1}) B_{n, \chi \omega^{-n}}/n$ and $(1 - \chi \omega^{-m}(p) p^{m - 1}) B_{m, \chi \omega^{-m}}/m$ are congruent modulo $p^a$. The corrected ratios are the classical L-values with the Euler factor at $p$ removed. Third, observe that the negative integers $\{1 - n : n \geq 1\}$ are dense in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ and that the corrected L-values are uniformly continuous on this dense set; the unique continuous extension to $\mathbb{Z}_p$ is the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function $L_p(s, \chi)$.

The historical context was the post-Iwasawa transition. Iwasawa 1959 had introduced his theory of $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions and the projective limit of class groups, working in a purely algebraic setting. Kubota and Leopoldt's $p$-adic L-function provided the analytic counterpart that would, over the next two decades, allow Iwasawa to formulate his Main Conjecture relating the two sides. The 1964 paper does not yet state the Main Conjecture; that statement appears in Iwasawa 1969 *J. Math. Soc. Japan* 16 [ref: TODO_REF Iwasawa1969], after Iwasawa had absorbed the analytic existence theorem and connected it to his algebraic Iwasawa module via the Iwasawa-Mellin transform.

### The Iwasawa-Mellin transform and the Amice isomorphism

The bridge between the Kubota-Leopoldt analytic function and the Iwasawa-algebra element runs through the **Amice transform** (Amice 1964 *Bull. Soc. Math. France* 92). Let $\Gamma = 1 + p \mathbb{Z}_p \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$ be the multiplicative group of principal units, with topological generator $\gamma = 1 + p$. The Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda := \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]]$ is the completed group ring, and the isomorphism $\Lambda \cong \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ sends $\gamma \mapsto 1 + T$.

The **Amice isomorphism** identifies $p$-adic measures on $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ with their **moment generating power series** in $\Lambda$. Specifically, the algebra of continuous $\mathbb{C}_p$-valued measures on $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$, equipped with convolution, is isomorphic to $\Lambda \otimes \mathbb{C}_p$ via the moment map
$$
\mu \longmapsto \sum_{n \geq 0} \left( \int_{\mathbb{Z}_p^\times} \binom{x}{n} \, d\mu(x) \right) T^n.
$$
A continuous function $f : \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{C}_p$ is then identified with the Mellin transform of a unique measure, and the integral $\int f \, d\mu$ becomes a pairing inside $\Lambda$.

The Kubota-Leopoldt $L_p(s, \chi)$, viewed under the Amice transform, becomes an element $\xi_\chi \in \Lambda \otimes \mathbb{C}_p$. The fact that $L_p$ takes values in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ at the dense set of negative integers (with controlled denominators) shows that $\xi_\chi$ in fact lies in $\Lambda$ itself, not just $\Lambda \otimes \mathbb{C}_p$. The interpolation property reads, in this language,
$$
\xi_\chi(\kappa(\gamma)^n - 1) = L_p(1 - n, \chi) \qquad \text{for } n \geq 1,
$$
where $\kappa : \Gamma \to 1 + p \mathbb{Z}_p$ is the cyclotomic character. This is the recipe for going back and forth between the analytic and algebraic incarnations of the $p$-adic L-function.

### The cyclotomic Iwasawa module and its structure

Let $K_\infty = \mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty}) = \bigcup_n \mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^n})$ be the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension. The Galois group $G = \mathrm{Gal}(K_\infty / \mathbb{Q})$ decomposes as $G = \Delta \times \Gamma$ with $\Delta = \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\mu_p)/\mathbb{Q}) \cong (\mathbb{Z}/p)^\times$ and $\Gamma = \mathrm{Gal}(K_\infty/\mathbb{Q}(\mu_p)) \cong \mathbb{Z}_p$.

For each $n \geq 0$, let $A_n$ be the $p$-Sylow subgroup of the ideal class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^{n+1}})$. The norm maps make $\{A_n\}_n$ a projective system of finite abelian $p$-groups, with projective limit
$$
X := \varprojlim_n A_n.
$$
$X$ is a finitely generated $\mathbb{Z}_p$-module with a natural action of $G = \Delta \times \Gamma$, hence of $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]]$.

**The $\chi$-component.** For a Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor $p$, the $\chi$-isotypic component is
$$
X^{(\chi)} := \{x \in X : \delta x = \chi(\delta) x \text{ for all } \delta \in \Delta\}.
$$
The plus-and-minus decomposition under complex conjugation $c \in \Delta$ ($c = -1$ in $(\mathbb{Z}/p)^\times$) gives $X = X^+ \oplus X^-$, where $X^+$ is the $+1$-eigenspace and $X^-$ is the $-1$-eigenspace. By a theorem of Iwasawa 1973 *Ann. Math.* 98, $X^+$ is conjecturally vanishing (Greenberg's conjecture, known for many concrete examples) while $X^-$ is substantive and carries the arithmetic content. For an even character $\chi$ (so $\chi(-1) = +1$), the $\chi$-component sits inside the minus-part $X^-$, and one defines $X_\chi^- := X^{(\chi)} \cap X^-$.

**Iwasawa's structure theorem.** $X_\chi^-$ is a finitely generated torsion $\Lambda$-module, and there exists a $\Lambda$-module homomorphism with finite kernel and cokernel
$$
X_\chi^- \to \bigoplus_{i = 1}^r \Lambda / (p^{a_i}) \oplus \bigoplus_{j = 1}^s \Lambda / (f_j(T)^{b_j}),
$$
with $a_i, b_j \geq 1$ and $f_j(T)$ distinguished polynomials in $\mathbb{Z}_p[T]$ (i.e., monic with non-leading coefficients in $p \mathbb{Z}_p$). The map is called a **pseudo-isomorphism**, and the characteristic ideal of $X_\chi^-$ is
$$
\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) = \left( p^{\sum_i a_i} \prod_j f_j(T)^{b_j} \right) \subseteq \Lambda.
$$
The integer $\mu(X_\chi^-) := \sum_i a_i$ is the **Iwasawa $\mu$-invariant** and $\lambda(X_\chi^-) := \sum_j b_j \cdot \deg f_j$ is the **Iwasawa $\lambda$-invariant**.

### The Main Conjecture: statement and the Mazur-Wiles proof

**Iwasawa Main Conjecture (Iwasawa 1969 conjectured; Mazur-Wiles 1984 theorem).** Fix an odd prime $p$ and an even non-principal Dirichlet character $\chi$ of conductor coprime to $p$. Then
$$
\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-) = (\xi_\chi)
$$
inside the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$, where $\xi_\chi$ is the Amice-transform realisation of $L_p(\cdot, \chi)$.

**The Mazur-Wiles proof (1984).** The 1984 paper by Mazur and Wiles in *Inventiones Mathematicae* 76 [ref: TODO_REF MazurWiles1984] established the Main Conjecture for $\mathbb{Q}$ through a deep argument combining modular-form deformation theory with Galois cohomology. The strategy, outlined in Exercise 5 above, has four pillars.

**Pillar 1: $\Lambda$-adic modular forms.** Hida 1985 *Ann. Sci. ENS* 19 introduced the theory of $\Lambda$-adic modular forms — formal $q$-expansions with coefficients in $\Lambda$ — and proved that ordinary cuspidal eigenforms vary in $p$-adic families parameterised by $\Lambda$. Specialisation at points of $\Lambda$ corresponding to integers $k \geq 2$ recovers classical weight-$k$ eigenforms. The Eisenstein series $\mathbf{E}_\chi$ is a specific $\Lambda$-adic family whose constant term equals $\xi_\chi/2$.

**Pillar 2: the Eisenstein ideal.** The universal Hecke algebra $\mathbb{T}$ acting on the space of $\Lambda$-adic modular forms has a distinguished ideal $I_E$ — the **Eisenstein ideal** — characterised by $\mathbb{T}/I_E \cong \Lambda$. The depth of cuspidal-Eisenstein congruences (cuspidal Hecke eigensystems matching the Eisenstein system modulo $I_E^a$) is encoded in $I_E$, and the structure of $I_E$ determines the size of the cuspidal component of $\mathbb{T}$ modulo Eisenstein.

**Pillar 3: $\Lambda$-adic Galois representation.** Attach to the universal cuspidal Hecke deformation the $\Lambda$-adic Galois representation $\rho : G_\mathbb{Q} \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{T})$. The mod-$I_E$ reduction is the diagonal Eisenstein representation $\mathbf{1} \oplus \chi^{-1} \kappa^{1 - k}$, where $\kappa$ is the cyclotomic character. The off-diagonal entries of $\rho$ modulo $I_E$ produce non-vanishing $\Lambda$-adic Galois cohomology classes in $H^1(G_\mathbb{Q}, \chi^{-1})$ that, on restriction to the cyclotomic tower, define lifting maps from $\mathbb{T}/I_E$ to the Iwasawa module $X_\chi^-$.

**Pillar 4: class-number formula and Ferrero-Washington.** The reverse divisibility uses the analytic class-number formula at finite levels combined with Ferrero-Washington 1979 [ref: TODO_REF FerreroWashington1979] (the cyclotomic $\mu$-invariant vanishes for abelian fields). The class-number formula at level $n$ reads
$$
|A_n^\chi| = p^{e_n} \prod \chi(\text{cyclotomic units quotient}),
$$
and the Iwasawa $p$-adic class-number formula passes to the limit to give $\xi_\chi \mid \mathrm{char}_\Lambda(X_\chi^-)$ in $\Lambda \otimes \mathbb{Q}_p$. Ferrero-Washington upgrades this to a divisibility in $\Lambda$ itself by ruling out the $\mu$-component.

**Sources.** The full proof spans 150+ pages of Mazur-Wiles 1984. Subsequent expositions include Coates-Sujatha 2006 [ref: TODO_REF CoatesSujatha2006] (the canonical modern textbook), Washington 1997 [ref: TODO_REF Washington1997] Ch. 13, and Lang 1990 *Cyclotomic Fields I and II* (combined edition, GTM 121).

### Generalisations: Wiles for totally real, Skinner-Urban for elliptic

**Wiles 1990 (totally real fields).** Wiles 1990 *Ann. Math.* 131 extended the Main Conjecture to the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of a totally real number field $F$ [ref: TODO_REF Wiles1990]. The $p$-adic L-function in this setting is the **Deligne-Ribet $p$-adic L-function** (Deligne-Ribet 1980 *Invent. Math.* 59), interpolating values of $L(F, \chi, s)$ at negative integers for totally even characters $\chi$ of conductor coprime to $p$. The Iwasawa module is built from class groups of intermediate cyclotomic extensions. Wiles' proof uses **Hilbert modular forms** instead of classical modular forms — Hilbert eigenforms attached to the totally real field — and a generalisation of the Mazur-Wiles Eisenstein-ideal congruence method to the Hilbert setting. The proof is conditional on $\mu = 0$ for $F$, known in many cases but open in general.

**Skinner-Urban 2014 (elliptic curves).** Skinner and Urban 2014 *Invent. Math.* 195 proved the Iwasawa Main Conjecture for elliptic curves $E/\mathbb{Q}$ with good ordinary reduction at $p$ [ref: TODO_REF SkinnerUrban2014], under technical hypotheses including residual irreducibility of $\bar\rho_{E, p}$. The setting replaces the cyclotomic Iwasawa module with the Pontryagin dual of the $p$-primary Selmer group of $E$ along the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension, and the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function with the Mazur-Swinnerton-Dyer $p$-adic L-function of $E$ (Mazur-Swinnerton-Dyer 1974 *Invent. Math.* 25). The proof generalises Mazur-Wiles by working with $\mathrm{GL}_2$-Eisenstein series in the Hida-family-deformation framework and using Galois-cohomological arguments inspired by Wiles' Fermat-Last-Theorem techniques.

**BSD-Iwasawa application.** Combined with Kato's 2004 Euler-system theorem [ref: TODO_REF Kato2004], which provides the unconditional divisibility $\mathrm{char}_\Lambda(\mathrm{Sel}^\vee) \mid L_p(E, T)$ via Beilinson-Kato classes in motivic cohomology of modular curves, Skinner-Urban's reverse divisibility completes the Main Conjecture and yields the **$p$-part of the Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture** for elliptic curves of analytic rank zero. This is one of the deepest results in modern arithmetic geometry, and the closest a proof comes to BSD in full generality.

### The anticyclotomic Main Conjecture and Heegner points

Bertolini and Darmon 2005 *Ann. Math.* 162 proved an **anticyclotomic** Main Conjecture for elliptic curves over imaginary quadratic fields [ref: TODO_REF BertoliniDarmon2005]. The setting replaces the cyclotomic $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension with the **anticyclotomic** $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension of an imaginary quadratic field $K$ (the extension on which complex conjugation acts by $-1$). The $p$-adic L-function is the **Bertolini-Darmon-Prasanna $p$-adic L-function**, constructed from CM-period integrals of weight-$2$ eigenforms restricted to CM elliptic curves. The proof uses **Heegner-point Euler systems**: explicit cohomology classes built from CM-points on modular curves, via the Gross-Zagier formula 1986 *Invent. Math.* 84 (which identifies Heegner-point heights with central L-derivatives).

The anticyclotomic Main Conjecture, combined with the Gross-Zagier formula, addresses the **rank-one case** of BSD for elliptic curves over imaginary quadratic fields, complementing the rank-zero result of Kato 2004 + Skinner-Urban 2014. Together, the cyclotomic and anticyclotomic Main Conjectures form one of the two deepest known approaches to BSD.

**Synthesis.** The $p$-adic L-function and the Iwasawa Main Conjecture are the foundational analytic-algebraic bridge of modern arithmetic geometry, and the central insight of the Iwasawa programme is that classical L-values at negative integers interpolate to a $p$-adic-analytic object, and this object equals a characteristic invariant of a Galois-module built from arithmetic. The Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function is exactly the Mellin transform of a $p$-adic measure on $\mathbb{Z}_p^\times$ supplied by Kummer congruences on Bernoulli numbers, identifying the discrete (special values at negative integers) with the continuous ($p$-adic-analytic interpolation). The Mazur-Wiles proof identifies this analytic invariant with the characteristic ideal of the cyclotomic Iwasawa module via congruences between cuspidal Hecke eigenforms and Eisenstein series, generalising the Ribet 1976 converse to Herbrand's theorem.

Putting these together with Wiles 1990 (totally real fields), Rubin 1991 (alternative Euler-system proof for imaginary quadratic fields), Kato 2004 (Euler systems for modular forms via Beilinson-Kato classes in motivic cohomology), Skinner-Urban 2014 ($\mathrm{GL}_2$-Main Conjecture for elliptic curves), and Bertolini-Darmon 2005 (anticyclotomic Heegner-point Main Conjecture), the foundational reason that L-values control Galois-module invariants is the existence of Euler systems — coherent families of cohomology classes built from cyclotomic units, elliptic units, Beilinson-Kato classes, or Heegner points — whose norm-compatible behaviour under Galois reflects exactly the Euler-factor structure of the corresponding L-function. The pattern generalises through every motivic L-function via the Beilinson, Bloch-Kato, and Fontaine-Perrin-Riou conjectures, and builds toward [21.06.01] modularity and BSD where the Iwasawa Main Conjecture provides the deepest known approach.

## Full proof set [Master]

**Proposition (uniqueness of $L_p$).** *Let $L_p, L_p' : \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{C}_p$ be continuous functions satisfying the Kubota-Leopoldt interpolation property at every negative integer $1 - n$, $n \geq 1$. Then $L_p = L_p'$.*

*Proof.* The negative integers $\{1 - n : n \geq 1\} = \{0, -1, -2, -3, \ldots\}$ are dense in $\mathbb{Z}_p$: given any $s \in \mathbb{Z}_p$ and any $a \geq 1$, the negative integer $-N$ with $N \equiv -s \pmod {p^a}$ satisfies $|(-N) - (s - 1)|_p \leq p^{-a}$, so $-N \to s - 1$ in $\mathbb{Z}_p$ as $a \to \infty$. The set $\{1 - n\}$ is the translate of this set by $1$ and is also dense.

Since $L_p$ and $L_p'$ are continuous functions $\mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{C}_p$ agreeing on the dense subset of negative integers, they agree on all of $\mathbb{Z}_p$. $\square$

**Proposition (Kummer congruence yields uniform continuity).** *The corrected Bernoulli ratios $(1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) p^{n - 1}) B_{n, \chi \omega^{-n}}/n$, viewed as a function of $n \geq 1$ in $\mathbb{Z}_p$, are uniformly continuous in the $p$-adic metric.*

*Proof.* The Kummer congruence, in its refined form, states that for $n \equiv m \pmod{p^{a - 1}(p - 1)}$ with $n, m$ not divisible by $p - 1$,
$$
(1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) p^{n - 1}) \frac{B_{n, \chi \omega^{-n}}}{n} \equiv (1 - \chi \omega^{-m}(p) p^{m - 1}) \frac{B_{m, \chi \omega^{-m}}}{m} \pmod{p^a}.
$$
The condition $n \equiv m \pmod{p^{a - 1}(p - 1)}$ is implied by $|n - m|_p \leq p^{1 - a}$ when $n, m$ are not divisible by $p - 1$ (a finite number of residue classes to exclude per $a$), so the corrected ratios are uniformly continuous on the dense set of positive integers in $\mathbb{Z}_p$.

For the proof of the Kummer congruence itself: use the generating function $\sum B_{n, \psi} t^n/n! = \sum_a \psi(a) t e^{a t}/(e^{f t} - 1)$, multiply by $t^{-n - 1}$, and apply the residue theorem with the integration contour the small circle $|t|_p < 1$. The $\omega^{-n}$-twist is required to identify $\omega^{-n}(a) \equiv a^{-n} \pmod p$ for $a$ coprime to $p$, so the integrand depends only on $n$ modulo $p^{a - 1}(p - 1)$ (the cyclotomic structure). Extracting coefficients gives the Kummer congruence. $\square$

**Proposition (existence of $L_p$).** *Under the hypotheses of the Theorem, the continuous interpolation $L_p : \mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{C}_p$ exists.*

*Proof.* By the previous Proposition, the values $f(n) := (1 - \chi \omega^{-n}(p) p^{n - 1}) L(1 - n, \chi \omega^{-n})$ form a uniformly continuous function of $n$ on the dense subset $\{n \geq 1, n \not\equiv 0 \pmod{p - 1}\}$ of $\mathbb{Z}_p$ (the exceptional residue classes are handled by setting $L_p = 0$ on those classes for odd $\chi$, or by direct computation for $\chi$ even and $n$ in the exceptional class, which produces an identity in the limit).

A uniformly continuous function on a dense subset of a complete metric space extends uniquely to a continuous function on the whole space. The extension to $\mathbb{Z}_p$ via $s \mapsto L_p(s, \chi)$ is the Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function. $\square$

**Proposition (analyticity of $L_p$).** *The function $L_p(\cdot, \chi)$ extends to a $p$-adic-analytic function on the open disc $\{s \in \mathbb{C}_p : |s|_p < p^{(p - 2)/(p - 1)}\}$ (Iwasawa 1969 *J. Math. Soc. Japan* 16).*

*Proof.* Recall that the Amice transform identifies the Kubota-Leopoldt $L_p$ with an element $\xi_\chi \in \Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$. Choosing the topological generator $\gamma$ of $\Gamma = 1 + p \mathbb{Z}_p$ to satisfy $\kappa(\gamma) = 1 + p$, the interpolation property reads
$$
L_p(1 - n, \chi) = \xi_\chi((1 + p)^n - 1).
$$
Setting $u := (1 + p)^s - 1 = e^{s \log(1 + p)} - 1$, with the $p$-adic logarithm converging on $1 + p \mathbb{Z}_p$, gives $L_p(s, \chi) = \xi_\chi(u)$. The substitution $s \mapsto u$ is $p$-adic-analytic on $|s|_p < p^{(p - 2)/(p - 1)}$ — the radius is precisely the convergence radius of the $p$-adic exponential applied to $s \log(1 + p)$ — and the resulting function is given by the convergent power series $\xi_\chi(u)$. The function $L_p(s, \chi)$ is therefore $p$-adic-analytic on this open disc, with analytic continuation determined by the power series. $\square$

## Connections [Master]

- **$\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions and Iwasawa theory [21.07.01].** The sibling unit in this chapter, which provides the algebraic-side framework: the $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension $\mathbb{Q}(\mu_{p^\infty}) / \mathbb{Q}$, the Iwasawa algebra $\Lambda = \mathbb{Z}_p[[\Gamma]] \cong \mathbb{Z}_p[[T]]$ as the group ring of the Galois group, and the structure theorem for finitely generated torsion $\Lambda$-modules with the $\mu$ and $\lambda$ invariants. The $p$-adic L-function $L_p$ realises a continuous function on $\mathbb{Z}_p$, which becomes an element of $\Lambda$ via the Amice transform; the Main Conjecture identifies this $\Lambda$-element with the characteristic ideal of the cyclotomic Iwasawa module from $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extension theory.

- **Modularity and BSD [21.06.01].** The Iwasawa Main Conjecture for elliptic curves (Skinner-Urban 2014) provides the deepest known approach to the Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture. The $p$-part of BSD for elliptic curves of analytic rank zero follows from the Skinner-Urban Main Conjecture combined with Kato's Euler-system theorem; the anticyclotomic Bertolini-Darmon Main Conjecture combined with Gross-Zagier addresses the rank-one case. The bridge from the Iwasawa Main Conjecture to BSD is the **$p$-adic Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer formula** (Mazur-Tate-Teitelbaum 1986 *Invent. Math.* 84), which predicts the order of vanishing and leading coefficient of the Mazur-Swinnerton-Dyer $p$-adic L-function at $T = 0$ in terms of the Mordell-Weil rank and refined BSD data.

- **Riemann zeta and Dirichlet L-functions [21.03.01], [21.03.02].** The Kubota-Leopoldt $p$-adic L-function is the $p$-adic shadow of the classical Dirichlet L-function attached to the same character; for the principal character $\chi_0$, $L_p(s, \chi_0)$ is the $p$-adic interpolation of the Riemann zeta values at negative integers. The construction relies on the identity $\zeta(1 - n) = -B_n/n$ from the classical theory and on the Kummer congruences for Bernoulli numbers, which are themselves consequences of the functional equation of $\zeta$ and the von Staudt-Clausen theorem. The classical $L$-functions are inputs to the $p$-adic L-function construction; the $p$-adic L-function then carries additional algebraic content (the Iwasawa Main Conjecture) inaccessible to the classical L-function alone.

- **Modular forms and Galois representations [21.04.02], [21.05.01].** The Mazur-Wiles proof of the Main Conjecture for $\mathbb{Q}$ proceeds through $\Lambda$-adic modular forms (Hida 1985 *Ann. Sci. ENS* 19), the Eisenstein ideal in the universal Hecke algebra, and the $2$-dimensional $\Lambda$-adic Galois representation attached to the universal cuspidal deformation. The strategy generalises Ribet's 1976 *Invent. Math.* converse to Herbrand's theorem and forms a template for subsequent work on Iwasawa Main Conjectures for higher-rank automorphic forms (Urban 2013, Skinner-Urban 2014). The deep input is that congruences between modular forms encode information about Galois cohomology of the associated representations, and the depth of these congruences is measured by the $p$-adic L-function.

- **Euler systems and motivic L-functions.** The Rubin 1991 *Invent. Math.* 103 alternative proof of the Main Conjecture via cyclotomic-unit Euler systems, the Kato 2004 *Astérisque* 295 construction for modular Galois representations via Beilinson-Kato classes, and the Bertolini-Darmon 2005 *Ann. Math.* 162 Heegner-point Euler system together establish the Euler-system method as a general approach to Iwasawa Main Conjectures. The motivic L-function machinery — Beilinson conjectures (Beilinson 1985 *J. Soviet Math.* 30), Bloch-Kato conjectures (Bloch-Kato 1990 *Birkhäuser*), Fontaine-Perrin-Riou (Fontaine 1992) — provides the conjectural framework in which Iwasawa Main Conjectures for arbitrary motives are formulated, generalising the Dirichlet and elliptic-curve cases addressed by Mazur-Wiles and Skinner-Urban.

## Historical & philosophical context [Master]

Tomio Kubota and Heinrich-Wolfgang Leopoldt introduced the $p$-adic L-function in their 1964 paper *Eine $p$-adische Theorie der Zetawerte* in *Journal für die reine und angewandte Mathematik* 214/215 [ref: TODO_REF KubotaLeopoldt1964], building on the classical Kummer congruences from 1851 and the von Staudt-Clausen theorem from 1840 on Bernoulli-number denominators. The conceptual leap was to view the Kummer congruences as the seeds of a $p$-adic-analytic function on $\mathbb{Z}_p$ interpolating the classical Dirichlet L-values at negative integers. Kenkichi Iwasawa, who had introduced his algebraic theory of $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions and projective limits of class groups in 1959 *Bull. Amer. Math. Soc.* 65 and developed it through the 1960s, recognised in 1969 *J. Math. Soc. Japan* 16 that the Kubota-Leopoldt analytic object should equal a characteristic invariant of the Galois module on the algebraic side [ref: TODO_REF Iwasawa1969]. This identification, the **Iwasawa Main Conjecture**, was the founding statement of modern Iwasawa theory.

Barry Mazur and Andrew Wiles proved the Main Conjecture for $\mathbb{Q}$ in their 1984 *Inventiones Mathematicae* 76 paper [ref: TODO_REF MazurWiles1984], deploying $\Lambda$-adic modular forms, the Eisenstein ideal, and the associated $\Lambda$-adic Galois representation to bridge the algebraic and analytic sides. Wiles 1990 *Ann. Math.* 131 [ref: TODO_REF Wiles1990] extended this to totally real fields. Karl Rubin 1991 *Invent. Math.* 103 [ref: TODO_REF Rubin1991] gave an alternative proof for imaginary quadratic fields via cyclotomic-unit Euler systems, building on Kolyvagin's 1989 method. Ralph Greenberg 1989 *Astérisque* 165 [ref: TODO_REF Greenberg1989] formulated the Main Conjecture for arbitrary $p$-adic Galois representations, generalising the framework to motivic L-functions.

Bruno Ferrero and Lawrence Washington proved in 1979 *Ann. Math.* 109 [ref: TODO_REF FerreroWashington1979] that the cyclotomic Iwasawa $\mu$-invariant vanishes for abelian number fields, providing the technical input that converted the Mazur-Wiles divisibility into an equality of ideals. Kazuya Kato 2004 *Astérisque* 295 [ref: TODO_REF Kato2004] constructed the Euler system of Beilinson-Kato classes in motivic cohomology of modular curves, giving one half of the Iwasawa Main Conjecture for elliptic curves and a partial proof of BSD. Christopher Skinner and Eric Urban 2014 *Invent. Math.* 195 [ref: TODO_REF SkinnerUrban2014] proved the full $\mathrm{GL}_2$-Main Conjecture for elliptic curves under hypotheses, completing the Iwasawa-theoretic approach to the $p$-part of BSD for analytic rank zero. Massimo Bertolini and Henri Darmon 2005 *Ann. Math.* 162 [ref: TODO_REF BertoliniDarmon2005] proved the anticyclotomic Main Conjecture via Heegner-point Euler systems, addressing the rank-one case.

The canonical modern textbook treatments are Coates-Sujatha 2006 [ref: TODO_REF CoatesSujatha2006] (*Cyclotomic Fields and Zeta Values*, Springer Monographs) and Washington 1997 [ref: TODO_REF Washington1997] (*Introduction to Cyclotomic Fields*, GTM 83, 2nd edition). Manin-Panchishkin 2005 *Introduction to Modern Number Theory* (Springer EMS 49, 2nd edition) Ch. 7 [ref: TODO_REF ManinPanchishkin2005] provides the survey-level overview integrating $p$-adic L-functions with the broader Langlands programme.
