## Intuition [Beginner]

The Eichler-Shimura correspondence is the bridge from weight-$2$ modular forms to Galois groups. Every weight-$2$ cusp eigenform $f$ on the congruence subgroup $\Gamma_0(N)$ attaches to a two-dimensional Galois representation, and the trace of that representation at the Frobenius above a prime $p$ recovers the $p$-th Hecke eigenvalue of $f$. This is how modular forms talk to Galois groups.

The bridge runs through the modular curve $X_0(N)$ and its Jacobian $J_0(N)$. The Hecke operator $T_p$ realises as an algebraic correspondence on $X_0(N)$, and the $\ell$-adic Tate module of $J_0(N)$ splits by Hecke eigenforms into two-dimensional pieces, each carrying a Galois representation. The Fourier coefficients of $f$ become Frobenius traces, and modular forms become arithmetic-geometric objects.

## Worked example [Beginner]

At level $N = 11$, the space $S_2(\Gamma_0(11))$ has dimension $1$, spanned by the unique normalised weight-$2$ cusp eigenform $f = q - 2 q^2 - q^3 + 2 q^4 + q^5 + \cdots$ in $q = e^{2 \pi i z}$. The corresponding elliptic curve is $E : y^2 + y = x^3 - x^2 - 10 x - 20$ of conductor $11$.

Step 1. The Jacobian $J_0(11)$ has dimension $1$ — it is the elliptic curve $E$ itself, and the two-dimensional Tate module of $E$ carries the Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$.

Step 2. Count points: $|E(\mathbb{F}_2)| = 5$, $|E(\mathbb{F}_3)| = 5$, $|E(\mathbb{F}_5)| = 5$, $|E(\mathbb{F}_7)| = 10$.

Step 3. Compute Frobenius traces via $|E(\mathbb{F}_p)| = p + 1 - a_p$: $a_2 = -2$, $a_3 = -1$, $a_5 = 1$, $a_7 = -2$. These match the Fourier coefficients of $f$ at every $p \neq 11$.

What this tells us: the Fourier coefficients of the weight-$2$ cusp eigenform $f$ are the Frobenius traces on the elliptic curve $E$, and the Eichler-Shimura correspondence is the identification of $f$ with $E$.

## Formal definition [Intermediate+]

Fix a positive integer $N \geq 1$ called the **level**, and write $\Gamma_0(N) = \{\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : N | c\}$ for the level-$N$ congruence subgroup. Write $S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$ for the $\mathbb{C}$-vector space of weight-$2$ cusp forms on $\Gamma_0(N)$, with the Hecke algebra $\mathbb{T}_N$ generated by the operators $\{T_n : \gcd(n, N) = 1\}$ together with the diamond operators $\{\langle d \rangle : d \in (\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})^\times\}$. Each weight-$2$ cusp newform $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ is a normalised simultaneous Hecke eigenform with $T_p f = a_p(f) f$ and $\langle p \rangle f = f$ (the principal-character case) for $p \nmid N$, where $a_p(f) \in \overline{\mathbb{Q}}$ is the eigenvalue.

**Definition (modular curve and Jacobian).** The **open modular curve** $Y_0(N) = \Gamma_0(N) \backslash \mathbb{H}$ is the quotient of the upper half-plane by the action of $\Gamma_0(N)$, a non-compact Riemann surface. The **compact modular curve** $X_0(N) = Y_0(N) \cup \{\text{cusps}\}$ is its compactification, obtained by adjoining the finite set of cusps $\Gamma_0(N) \backslash \mathbb{P}^1(\mathbb{Q})$. The compact modular curve admits a canonical model as a smooth projective curve over $\mathbb{Q}$ (the moduli space of pairs $(E, C)$ with $E$ an elliptic curve and $C \subset E$ a cyclic subgroup of order $N$). The **modular Jacobian** $J_0(N) := \mathrm{Jac}(X_0(N))$ is the Jacobian variety of $X_0(N)$, an abelian variety over $\mathbb{Q}$ of dimension $g = \dim S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$.

**Definition (Hecke action on the Jacobian).** For each prime $p$, the Hecke correspondence $T_p$ on $X_0(N)$ is the algebraic correspondence on $X_0(N) \times X_0(N)$ defined by the two degeneracy maps $\pi_1, \pi_2 : X_0(N p) \to X_0(N)$ (the forgetful map at level $N$ and the $p$-power map), inducing an endomorphism $T_p \in \mathrm{End}(J_0(N))$ of the Jacobian. The action on the holomorphic differentials $H^0(X_0(N), \Omega^1) \cong S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$ matches the action of the analytic Hecke operator $T_p$ on weight-$2$ cusp forms.

**Definition ($\ell$-adic Tate module).** For a prime $\ell$ and the modular Jacobian $J_0(N)$, the **$\ell$-adic Tate module** is the inverse limit
$$
T_\ell J_0(N) := \varprojlim_n J_0(N)[\ell^n],
$$
a free $\mathbb{Z}_\ell$-module of rank $2 g$ carrying a continuous action of $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$. The **rational $\ell$-adic Galois module** is $V_\ell := T_\ell J_0(N) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}_\ell} \mathbb{Q}_\ell$, a $2 g$-dimensional $\mathbb{Q}_\ell$-vector space with continuous Galois action.

**Definition (Galois representation attached to a cusp newform).** Let $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ be a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform with Hecke eigenvalues $\{a_p(f)\}$, generating the totally real number field $K_f = \mathbb{Q}(a_p : p \nmid N) \subseteq \overline{\mathbb{Q}}$ of finite degree over $\mathbb{Q}$. Fix an embedding $K_f \hookrightarrow \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$. The **$f$-isotypic component** of $V_\ell \otimes_{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$ under the Hecke algebra action is a two-dimensional $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$-subspace $V_{\ell, f}$, and the restricted Galois action defines the **Eichler-Shimura representation**
$$
\rho_{f, \ell} : \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q}) \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}),
$$
a continuous semisimple representation, unramified outside $N \ell$.

### Counterexamples to common slips [Intermediate+]

- *"The Eichler-Shimura representation exists for cusp eigenforms of every weight."* The original Eichler-Shimura construction works in weight $2$, where the cusp forms appear as holomorphic differentials on $X_0(N)$. The extension to weight $k \geq 2$ is due to Deligne 1971, who constructs $\rho_{f, \ell}$ from the étale cohomology of the Kuga-Sato variety (a power of the universal elliptic curve over $Y_0(N)$). Weight-$1$ cusp eigenforms have a separate construction (Deligne-Serre 1974) via congruences with higher-weight forms, and the resulting representation has finite image.

- *"The trace formula $\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p$ holds at every prime $p$."* The identity holds at primes $p \nmid N \ell$ — those of good reduction for both the modular curve and the $\ell$-adic coefficients. At primes $p | N$ (bad reduction), the representation may be ramified and the trace identity must be replaced by the local Langlands correspondence (Carayol 1986); at $p = \ell$, one uses crystalline / semistable comparison via $p$-adic Hodge theory (Fontaine).

- *"The determinant of $\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p)$ is $a_p$."* The determinant is $\det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p$ in weight $2$ — the cyclotomic character — and more generally $p^{k - 1}$ in weight $k$ (twisted by a Dirichlet character if the nebentypus is non-principal). The trace is $a_p$, the determinant is $p^{k-1}$; mixing them is a frequent slip.

## Key theorem with proof [Intermediate+]

The signature theorem of this unit is the **Eichler-Shimura congruence relation**: the Hecke operator $T_p$ acting on the special fibre of the modular curve $X_0(N)$ at a prime $p \nmid N$ decomposes as the sum of the Frobenius correspondence and its dual.

**Theorem (Eichler-Shimura congruence; Eichler 1954 *Arch. Math.* 5, Shimura 1958 *J. Math. Soc. Japan* 10).** *Let $N \geq 1$ and let $p$ be a prime with $p \nmid N$. On the special fibre $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$, the Hecke correspondence $T_p$ satisfies the congruence*
$$
T_p \equiv \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1} \pmod{\ell}
$$
*as endomorphisms of the $\ell$-adic Tate module $T_\ell J_0(N)$ for any prime $\ell \neq p$, where $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ is the geometric Frobenius and $\langle p \rangle$ is the diamond operator at $p$. Consequently, for any weight-$2$ cusp newform $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ with $T_p f = a_p(f) f$, the trace and determinant of the Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$ at the Frobenius satisfy*
$$
\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p(f), \qquad \det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p
$$
*for every prime $p \nmid N \ell$.*

*Proof.* The proof proceeds through three structural steps: the moduli interpretation of $T_p$, the reduction modulo $p$ of the Hecke correspondence, and the eigenform decomposition of the Galois action.

**Step 1 — Moduli description of $T_p$.** The modular curve $X_0(N)$ is the moduli space of pairs $(E, C)$ where $E$ is an elliptic curve and $C \subset E$ is a cyclic subgroup of order $N$. The Hecke correspondence $T_p$ at a prime $p \nmid N$ is defined by the moduli operation
$$
T_p (E, C) := \sum_{D} (E/D, (C + D)/D),
$$
where $D$ ranges over the $p + 1$ subgroups of $E[p]$ of order $p$. As an algebraic correspondence on $X_0(N) \times X_0(N)$, this is realised by the two projection maps $\pi_1, \pi_2 : X_0(N p) \to X_0(N)$: $\pi_1$ forgets the auxiliary order-$p$ subgroup, $\pi_2$ quotients by it. The correspondence is $T_p = \pi_{2, *} \pi_1^*$.

**Step 2 — Reduction modulo $p$.** Reduce $X_0(N)$ modulo the prime $p$ (with $p \nmid N$, so the reduction is smooth). Over the algebraic closure $\overline{\mathbb{F}_p}$, an elliptic curve $E$ has two distinguished $p$-isogenies: the geometric Frobenius $\mathrm{Frob}_p : E \to E^{(p)}$ (raising coordinates to the $p$-th power, where $E^{(p)}$ is the Frobenius twist) and the Verschiebung $\mathrm{Ver}_p : E^{(p)} \to E$ (the dual isogeny). Together they satisfy $\mathrm{Ver}_p \circ \mathrm{Frob}_p = [p]$, the multiplication-by-$p$ endomorphism. The $p + 1$ order-$p$ subgroups of $E[p]$ split into two classes over $\overline{\mathbb{F}_p}$: the kernel of Frobenius (the "connected" component, contributing one subgroup) and the kernel of Verschiebung (the "étale" components, contributing $p$ subgroups in the ordinary case).

In moduli-language, the reduction of $\pi_1$ at $p$ corresponds to the étale subgroups (sending $(E, D)$ to $E/D$ via the Verschiebung-direction quotient), and the reduction of $\pi_2$ corresponds to the Frobenius subgroup (sending $(E, D)$ to $E$). The Hecke correspondence $T_p$ on $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$ therefore decomposes as a sum of two correspondences: the Frobenius correspondence $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ (induced by the geometric Frobenius on the universal elliptic curve over $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$) and the Verschiebung correspondence $p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1}$ (the dual, twisted by the diamond operator $\langle p \rangle$ encoding the $\Gamma_0(N)$-structure). The combined congruence
$$
T_p \equiv \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1} \pmod \ell
$$
holds in $\mathrm{End}(T_\ell J_0(N))$ for any $\ell \neq p$.

**Step 3 — Eigenform decomposition.** The Hecke algebra $\mathbb{T}_N \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$ acts semisimply on $V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$, and decomposes the rational $\ell$-adic Galois module as
$$
V_\ell \otimes_{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} = \bigoplus_f V_{\ell, f},
$$
where $f$ ranges over weight-$2$ cusp newforms (modulo Galois conjugation by $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ acting on the Hecke eigenvalues) and each $V_{\ell, f}$ is a free rank-$2$ $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$-module. The Hecke operator $T_p$ acts on $V_{\ell, f}$ by the scalar $a_p(f)$, and the diamond operator $\langle p \rangle$ acts as the identity in the case of $\Gamma_0(N)$ (so $\langle p \rangle = 1$).

Restricting the Eichler-Shimura congruence to the $f$-component, $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ acts on $V_{\ell, f}$ as a matrix with characteristic polynomial dividing
$$
X^2 - a_p(f) X + p = 0
$$
(obtained by multiplying both sides of the congruence by $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ and using $\mathrm{Frob}_p \cdot p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1} = p \langle p \rangle$; on the eigenform component with $\langle p \rangle = 1$, this gives $T_p \mathrm{Frob}_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p^2 + p \mathrm{Id}$, equivalently $\mathrm{Frob}_p^2 - a_p \mathrm{Frob}_p + p = 0$). Hence
$$
\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p(f), \qquad \det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p,
$$
as claimed. $\square$

**Bridge.** The Eichler-Shimura congruence builds toward [21.05.01] $\ell$-adic Galois representations as the originating example of the modular-form $\to$ Galois bridge, and appears again in [21.06.01] modularity theorem and BSD, where every elliptic curve over $\mathbb{Q}$ is shown to arise from a weight-$2$ cusp newform via Eichler-Shimura. The foundational reason the congruence works is that the Hecke correspondence and the Frobenius correspondence are both defined moduli-theoretically on pairs $(E, C)$ and reduce to the same decomposition of $p$-isogenies modulo $p$. This is exactly the bridge from analytic modular forms (functions on the upper half-plane) to arithmetic geometry (cohomology of moduli spaces of elliptic curves), and the construction identifies the Hecke eigenvalues with traces of Frobenius on the Tate module of the Jacobian — putting these together gives the trace formula $\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p(f)$ central to the entire arithmetic theory of modular forms.

**Exercise 3 (medium, symbolic).**

Explain why the Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$ attached to a weight-$2$ cusp newform $f$ is unramified outside $N \ell$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The modular curve $X_0(N)$ has good reduction at primes $p \nmid N$, and the $\ell$-adic Tate module of an abelian variety has unramified Galois action at primes of good reduction not equal to $\ell$ (Néron-Ogg-Shafarevich criterion).

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Outline of the argument.** The modular curve $X_0(N)$ admits a smooth proper model over $\mathbb{Z}[1/N]$ — the moduli space of pairs $(E, C)$ with $E$ an elliptic curve and $C$ a cyclic subgroup of order $N$ is representable as a smooth scheme over $\mathbb{Z}[1/N]$, with $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{Q}$ recovering the rational modular curve. The Jacobian $J_0(N) = \mathrm{Jac}(X_0(N))$ therefore has good reduction at every prime $p \nmid N$. The Néron-Ogg-Shafarevich criterion states that an abelian variety $A$ over a number field has good reduction at $p$ if and only if the $\ell$-adic Tate module $T_\ell A$ is unramified at $p$ for any (equivalently, every) prime $\ell \neq p$. The $f$-isotypic component $V_{\ell, f}$ is a Galois-stable summand, hence also unramified outside $N \ell$.

At primes $p | N$ the modular curve has bad reduction (cusps merge or extra automorphisms appear), and at $p = \ell$ the Tate-module construction crosses the residue-characteristic threshold, requiring crystalline / semistable methods (Fontaine) rather than the étale framework.

**Exercise 4 (medium, symbolic).**

State the Eichler-Shimura congruence relation precisely and derive from it the characteristic polynomial of $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ acting on the $f$-isotypic Galois representation $V_{\ell, f}$ in weight $2$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The congruence reads $T_p \equiv \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1} \pmod \ell$. Multiply both sides by $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ and use the eigenform condition $T_p f = a_p f$ (with $\langle p \rangle$ acting as the identity on $\Gamma_0(N)$-forms).

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Statement.** On the $\ell$-adic Tate module $V_\ell = T_\ell J_0(N) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}_\ell} \mathbb{Q}_\ell$ for $p \nmid N$ and $\ell \neq p$,
$$
T_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1}.
$$
On the principal-character isotypic component (the case of $\Gamma_0(N)$-cusp eigenforms with principal nebentypus), the diamond operator $\langle p \rangle$ acts as the identity, and the relation simplifies to
$$
T_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1}.
$$

**Derivation of the characteristic polynomial.** Multiply both sides by $\mathrm{Frob}_p$:
$$
T_p \mathrm{Frob}_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p^2 + p \cdot \mathrm{Id}.
$$
Restrict to the $f$-isotypic component $V_{\ell, f}$, on which $T_p = a_p(f) \cdot \mathrm{Id}$ acts by the Hecke eigenvalue:
$$
a_p(f) \mathrm{Frob}_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p^2 + p \mathrm{Id}.
$$
Rearranging, $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ satisfies the quadratic equation
$$
\mathrm{Frob}_p^2 - a_p(f) \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \cdot \mathrm{Id} = 0
$$
on $V_{\ell, f}$, identifying its characteristic polynomial as $X^2 - a_p(f) X + p$.

**Consequences.** The trace and determinant of $\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p)$ are therefore $\mathrm{tr} = a_p(f)$ and $\det = p$. The local Euler factor of the $L$-function $L(f, s) = \sum_n a_n n^{-s}$ at $p$ is $(1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{1 - 2 s})^{-1}$ (weight-$2$ specialisation of the general $1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{k-1-2s}$), exactly the characteristic polynomial above evaluated at $X = p^{-s}$ and inverted.

**Exercise 5 (medium, symbolic).**

Describe the moduli interpretation of the modular curve $X_0(N)$ and explain how this interpretation yields the Hecke correspondence $T_p$ as a degree-$(p + 1)$ algebraic correspondence on $X_0(N) \times X_0(N)$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The modular curve $X_0(N)$ is a moduli space classifying pairs $(E, C)$ of an elliptic curve with a cyclic subgroup of order $N$. The Hecke correspondence $T_p$ is the operation sending $(E, C)$ to the sum of $(E/D, (C + D)/D)$ over the order-$p$ subgroups $D \subset E[p]$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Moduli interpretation.** The open modular curve $Y_0(N) = \Gamma_0(N) \backslash \mathbb{H}$ is the coarse moduli space of pairs $(E, C)$ where $E$ is an elliptic curve (over $\mathbb{C}$ or, more generally, over $\mathbb{Z}[1/N]$) and $C \subseteq E$ is a cyclic subgroup of order $N$. Concretely, given $z \in \mathbb{H}$, the pair $(E_z, C_z) = (\mathbb{C}/(\mathbb{Z} + z \mathbb{Z}), \langle 1/N \rangle)$ recovers the universal example, and two points $z_1, z_2$ are $\Gamma_0(N)$-equivalent precisely when the pairs $(E_{z_1}, C_{z_1})$ and $(E_{z_2}, C_{z_2})$ are isomorphic.

The compactification $X_0(N) = Y_0(N) \cup \{\text{cusps}\}$ adds finitely many cusps (the boundary points $\Gamma_0(N) \backslash \mathbb{P}^1(\mathbb{Q})$), each corresponding to a "generalised elliptic curve" — a Néron polygon with a cyclic order-$N$ subgroup.

**Hecke correspondence.** The Hecke correspondence $T_p$ at a prime $p \nmid N$ is defined moduli-theoretically by
$$
T_p (E, C) := \sum_{D \subset E[p], \; |D| = p} (E/D, (C + D)/D),
$$
where the sum runs over the $p + 1$ cyclic subgroups of order $p$ in $E[p] \cong (\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^2$. As an algebraic correspondence on $X_0(N) \times X_0(N)$, this is the image of the auxiliary modular curve $X_0(N p)$ — the moduli space of pairs $(E, C, D)$ with $C$ of order $N$, $D$ of order $p$, $C \cap D = 0$ — under the product map $(\pi_1, \pi_2) : X_0(Np) \to X_0(N) \times X_0(N)$, where $\pi_1$ forgets $D$ and $\pi_2$ sends $(E, C, D)$ to $(E/D, (C + D)/D)$.

**Degree.** The forgetful map $\pi_1 : X_0(Np) \to X_0(N)$ has degree $p + 1$ (the count of order-$p$ subgroups). The Hecke correspondence $T_p = \pi_{2, *} \pi_1^*$ on the Jacobian $J_0(N) = \mathrm{Pic}^0(X_0(N))$ therefore has the structural form: pull back a divisor via $\pi_1$, push it forward along $\pi_2$. The induced endomorphism of $J_0(N)$ commutes with the action on $H^0(X_0(N), \Omega^1) \cong S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$ given by the analytic Hecke operator $T_p$.

</details>

**Exercise 6 (hard, symbolic).**

Derive the Ramanujan-Petersson bound $|a_p(f)| \leq 2 \sqrt{p}$ for weight-$2$ cusp newforms from the Eichler-Shimura representation and the Weil conjectures.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Weil conjectures (proved by Deligne 1974) state that for a smooth projective variety $X$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$, the eigenvalues of Frobenius on $H^i(X_{\overline{\mathbb{F}_p}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell)$ have absolute value $p^{i/2}$. Apply this to $X = X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$ and $i = 1$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Setup.** By the Eichler-Shimura correspondence, $\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p)$ has characteristic polynomial $X^2 - a_p(f) X + p$ for $p \nmid N \ell$. Its eigenvalues $\alpha, \beta$ satisfy $\alpha \beta = p$ and $\alpha + \beta = a_p(f)$, so $|a_p(f)| \leq |\alpha| + |\beta|$.

**Weil conjectures.** The Galois representation $V_{\ell, f}$ is a Galois-stable subspace of the first $\ell$-adic étale cohomology
$$
H^1(X_0(N) \otimes \overline{\mathbb{F}_p}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell) \cong V_\ell.
$$
The modular curve $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$ is a smooth projective curve over $\mathbb{F}_p$ for $p \nmid N$ (good reduction), so the Weil conjectures (Deligne 1974 *Publ. Math. IHES* 43, Riemann hypothesis for varieties over finite fields) apply: the eigenvalues of $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ acting on $H^1$ have absolute value $p^{1/2} = \sqrt{p}$.

**Bound.** Restricting to the $f$-isotypic component, $|\alpha| = |\beta| = \sqrt{p}$, so
$$
|a_p(f)| = |\alpha + \beta| \leq |\alpha| + |\beta| = 2 \sqrt{p}.
$$

**Sharpness.** The bound $|a_p(f)| \leq 2 \sqrt{p}$ is sharp: for Hasse-Weil $L$-functions of elliptic curves, the cases of complex multiplication and ordinary reduction both achieve eigenvalues $\alpha, \beta$ of common absolute value $\sqrt{p}$. The full Ramanujan-Petersson conjecture is the generalisation to weight $k \geq 2$: $|a_p(f)| \leq 2 p^{(k-1)/2}$, proved by Deligne 1971 (the explicit Weil-conjecture argument requires the Kuga-Sato variety for $k > 2$).

This is the modular-form analogue of the Riemann hypothesis: the eigenvalues of Frobenius on the cohomology of modular varieties lie on the critical circle $|\alpha| = p^{(k-1)/2}$, and this rigidity reflects deep arithmetic structure.

**Exercise 7 (hard, symbolic).**

Explain Shimura's construction of the abelian variety $A_f$ attached to a weight-$2$ cusp newform $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$, as a quotient of $J_0(N)$ by an ideal of the Hecke algebra.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Let $K_f = \mathbb{Q}(a_p(f) : p \nmid N) \subseteq \overline{\mathbb{Q}}$ be the Hecke-eigenvalue field. The set of Galois conjugates of $f$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ generates a $\mathbb{T}_N$-stable subspace of $S_2(\Gamma_0(N)) \otimes \mathbb{C}$. The corresponding ideal of $\mathbb{T}_N$ cuts out an abelian-variety quotient of $J_0(N)$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Setup.** Let $f$ be a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform on $\Gamma_0(N)$ with Hecke eigenvalues $\{a_p(f) \in \overline{\mathbb{Q}}\}_{p \nmid N}$. The eigenvalues lie in a totally real number field $K_f = \mathbb{Q}(a_p(f) : p \nmid N)$ of finite degree $d$ over $\mathbb{Q}$; the Galois conjugates of $f$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ are obtained by replacing each $a_p$ by its image under an embedding $K_f \hookrightarrow \overline{\mathbb{Q}}$, giving $d$ distinct cusp newforms $\{f^\sigma : \sigma \in \mathrm{Hom}(K_f, \overline{\mathbb{Q}})\}$.

**Ideal of the Hecke algebra.** Define a ring homomorphism $\lambda_f : \mathbb{T}_N \to K_f$ by $\lambda_f(T_p) = a_p(f)$, and let $I_f := \ker(\lambda_f) \subseteq \mathbb{T}_N$. The ideal $I_f$ acts on the Jacobian $J_0(N)$ by endomorphisms, and the **Shimura abelian variety** attached to $f$ is the quotient
$$
A_f := J_0(N) / I_f \cdot J_0(N).
$$
This is an abelian variety over $\mathbb{Q}$ of dimension $d = [K_f : \mathbb{Q}]$.

**Properties.**
- $A_f$ is isogenous to a product $\prod_\sigma E_{f^\sigma}$ over the Galois conjugates of $f$, where each $E_{f^\sigma}$ has dimension $1$ when $f$ has rational eigenvalues (and is then an elliptic curve), or higher dimension otherwise.
- The Hecke algebra $\mathbb{T}_N$ acts on $A_f$ through its quotient $\mathbb{T}_N / I_f \cong \mathcal{O}_{K_f}$ (the ring of integers of $K_f$), giving $A_f$ extra endomorphisms by $\mathcal{O}_{K_f}$.
- The $L$-function of $A_f$ factors as $L(A_f, s) = \prod_\sigma L(f^\sigma, s)$ over the $d$ Galois conjugates of $f$.

**Significance.** Shimura's construction transforms a weight-$2$ cusp newform into a geometric arithmetic object — an abelian variety over $\mathbb{Q}$ with extra endomorphisms — whose $\ell$-adic Tate module realises the Eichler-Shimura Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$ in dimension $2$. When $K_f = \mathbb{Q}$ (rational eigenvalues), $A_f$ is an elliptic curve over $\mathbb{Q}$, and the construction is the basis of the modularity correspondence: every elliptic curve over $\mathbb{Q}$ is isogenous to $A_f$ for some weight-$2$ cusp newform $f$ (Wiles 1995, Breuil-Conrad-Diamond-Taylor 2001).

</details>

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Eichler-Shimura construction uses $H^1(X_0(N), \mathbb{Q}_\ell) \cong $ holomorphic differentials, suitable for weight $2$. <details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Deligne's theorem (1971 *Sém. Bourbaki* 355).** *Let $f \in S_k(\Gamma_0(N))$ be a normalised weight-$k$ cusp newform of weight $k \geq 2$ with Hecke eigenvalues $a_p(f) \in \overline{\mathbb{Q}}$. For every prime $\ell$ and every embedding $\overline{\mathbb{Q}} \hookrightarrow \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$, there is a continuous semisimple Galois representation*
$$
\rho_{f, \ell} : \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q}) \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}),
$$
*unramified outside $N \ell$, with $\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p(f)$ and $\det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p^{k - 1}$ for every prime $p \nmid N \ell$.*

**Sketch of the construction.** For $k = 2$, the representation arises from $H^1_{\mathrm{ét}}(X_0(N) \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell)$ as in the Eichler-Shimura case. For $k > 2$, Deligne replaces the modular curve by the **Kuga-Sato variety**
$$
\mathcal{E}^{k - 2}_N := \mathcal{E}_N \times_{Y_0(N)} \cdots \times_{Y_0(N)} \mathcal{E}_N \quad (k - 2 \text{ factors}),
$$
the $(k - 2)$-fold fibre product of the universal elliptic curve $\mathcal{E}_N \to Y_0(N)$ over the modular curve, then compactifies and resolves singularities to obtain a smooth projective variety $\widetilde{\mathcal{E}}^{k - 2}_N$ over $\mathbb{Q}$. The étale cohomology in degree $k - 1$ contains a piece
$$
V_{\ell}^{(k)} \subseteq H^{k - 1}_{\mathrm{ét}}(\widetilde{\mathcal{E}}^{k - 2}_N \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}}, \mathbb{Q}_\ell)
$$
isomorphic (after a $\mathbb{Q}_\ell$-extension) to $S_k(\Gamma_0(N)) \oplus \overline{S_k(\Gamma_0(N))}$ as a Hecke module, with the weight-$k$ cusp newforms appearing as Hecke-eigenspaces. The Galois action on $V^{(k)}_\ell$ restricts on the $f$-isotypic component to a two-dimensional Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$ with $\det \rho_{f, \ell} = \chi_\ell^{k - 1}$ (the $(k - 1)$-st power of the cyclotomic character), giving $\det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p^{k - 1}$.

**Specialisation to weight $2$.** For $k = 2$, the Kuga-Sato variety degenerates to $X_0(N)$ itself (zero fibre factors), and $V^{(2)}_\ell = H^1(X_0(N), \mathbb{Q}_\ell)$ is the original Eichler-Shimura case with $\det = p$.

**Weight-$1$ aside.** The weight-$1$ case is handled separately by Deligne-Serre 1974 *Ann. Sci. ENS* 7 via congruences with higher-weight forms; the resulting Galois representation has finite image and is associated to an Artin representation, completing the picture for all $k \geq 1$.

## Advanced results [Master]

### Historical setting: Eichler 1954 and Shimura 1957-58

**Theorem 1 (Eichler 1954 *Arch. Math.* 5; Brandt-matrix trace formula).** *Let $D$ be a definite quaternion algebra over $\mathbb{Q}$ ramified at $p$ and $\infty$, and let $\mathcal{O}$ be a maximal order in $D$ with class number $h$. The **Brandt matrix** $B(p) = (b_{ij}(p))_{1 \leq i, j \leq h}$, with $b_{ij}(p)$ counting the right $\mathcal{O}$-ideals of reduced norm $p$ that conjugate the ideal class $\mathcal{I}_i$ to $\mathcal{I}_j$, has trace equal to the trace of the Hecke operator $T_p$ on the space of weight-$2$ cusp forms of level $p$. Eichler identified this trace formula with the Lefschetz fixed-point count of the Frobenius correspondence on the special fibre $X_0(p) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$, establishing the first instance of the modular $\to$ Frobenius bridge.*

*Eichler's original setting.* The 1954 paper "Quaternäre quadratische Formen und die Riemannsche Vermutung für die Kongruenzzetafunktion" was framed in terms of Riemann's hypothesis for the local zeta function of the modular curve. Eichler computed the trace formula on $S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$ via quaternionic theta series and recognised it as the Frobenius trace on the first cohomology of $X_0(N)$ over $\mathbb{F}_p$, deriving the Riemann hypothesis for $X_0(N)$ as a corollary. This established the **congruence relation** $T_p \equiv \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1} \pmod \ell$ in geometric form, and the bound $|a_p(f)| \leq 2 \sqrt{p}$ followed from the resulting characteristic-polynomial structure.

**Theorem 2 (Shimura 1958 *J. Math. Soc. Japan* 10; systematic construction of Galois representations).** *Let $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ be a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform with Hecke eigenvalues $\{a_p(f) \in \overline{\mathbb{Q}}\}$. There is a continuous two-dimensional $\ell$-adic Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell} : \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q}) \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell})$ realised on the $f$-isotypic component of the rational $\ell$-adic Tate module $V_\ell = T_\ell J_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{Q}_\ell$ of the modular Jacobian, with $\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p(f)$ and $\det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p$ for every $p \nmid N \ell$.*

*Shimura's framework.* The 1958 paper "Correspondances modulaires et les fonctions $\zeta$ de courbes algébriques" systematised Eichler's case-by-case calculations into the structural framework of moduli interpretations and Hecke correspondences. Shimura realised the modular curve as a moduli space over $\mathbb{Q}$, identified the Hecke operators with algebraic correspondences on $X_0(N) \times X_0(N)$, and recognised that the $\ell$-adic Tate module of the Jacobian splits into pieces indexed by cusp eigenforms — each piece carrying a two-dimensional Galois representation. This is the form of the Eichler-Shimura correspondence as it is now taught, and the foundation on which Deligne 1971 built the higher-weight extension.

### The $X_0(N)$ side and the construction of $V_{\ell, f}$

**Theorem 3 (Eichler-Shimura theorem; cohomological identification).** *Let $X_0(N)$ be the compact modular curve as a smooth projective curve over $\mathbb{Q}$, and let $J_0(N) = \mathrm{Jac}(X_0(N))$ be its Jacobian. Then there is a natural isomorphism of complex vector spaces*
$$
H^1(X_0(N), \mathbb{C}) \cong S_2(\Gamma_0(N)) \oplus \overline{S_2(\Gamma_0(N))},
$$
*identifying the holomorphic part of the first complex cohomology of the modular curve with the space of weight-$2$ cusp forms together with its anti-holomorphic conjugate. The isomorphism is Hecke-equivariant: the algebraic Hecke correspondence $T_p$ acting on $H^1(X_0(N), \mathbb{C})$ corresponds to the analytic Hecke operator $T_p$ on $S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$.*

*Proof outline.* The Hodge decomposition for the smooth projective curve $X_0(N)$ gives
$$
H^1(X_0(N), \mathbb{C}) = H^0(X_0(N), \Omega^1) \oplus \overline{H^0(X_0(N), \Omega^1)},
$$
the holomorphic and anti-holomorphic differentials. The holomorphic differentials on $X_0(N) = \Gamma_0(N) \backslash \mathbb{H}^*$ are precisely the holomorphic $1$-forms invariant under $\Gamma_0(N)$ and vanishing at the cusps — that is, expressions $f(z) \, dz$ where $f$ is a weight-$2$ cusp form (the differential $dz$ transforms by $(c z + d)^{-2}$, exactly compensating the weight-$2$ modular factor). Hence $H^0(X_0(N), \Omega^1) \cong S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$, and the Hecke-equivariance is checked on differentials: the moduli-theoretic Hecke correspondence pulls back to the analytic Hecke operator. $\square$

**Theorem 4 (Eigenform decomposition of $V_\ell$).** *The rational $\ell$-adic Tate module $V_\ell = T_\ell J_0(N) \otimes_{\mathbb{Z}_\ell} \mathbb{Q}_\ell$ decomposes under the Hecke algebra action as*
$$
V_\ell \otimes_{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} = \bigoplus_{[f]} V_{\ell, f},
$$
*where $[f]$ ranges over Galois-conjugacy classes of weight-$2$ cusp newforms on $\Gamma_0(N)$, and each $V_{\ell, f}$ is a free rank-$2$ $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$-module on which $T_p$ acts by the scalar $a_p(f)$ for $p \nmid N$. The decomposition is Galois-stable: each $V_{\ell, f}$ is a continuous $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$-submodule of $V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$.*

*Significance.* The decomposition is the precise statement that the Hecke algebra $\mathbb{T}_N \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$ acts semisimply on $V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$, and that the eigenspaces are exactly two-dimensional. This is what allows the bridge from cusp eigenforms to Galois representations to be a function — each $f$ determines a unique $V_{\ell, f}$ — rather than a multi-valued correspondence.

### Properties of $\rho_{f, \ell}$

**Theorem 5 (Properties of the Eichler-Shimura representation).** *Let $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ be a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform and $\rho_{f, \ell}$ its attached Galois representation. The representation has the following properties:*

*(i) **Unramified outside $N \ell$.** $\rho_{f, \ell}$ is unramified at every prime $p \nmid N \ell$, by the Néron-Ogg-Shafarevich criterion applied to the abelian variety $J_0(N)$ which has good reduction outside $N$.*

*(ii) **Trace and determinant.** For $p \nmid N \ell$, $\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p(f)$ and $\det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p$.*

*(iii) **Odd.** The complex conjugation $c \in \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ acts via $\rho_{f, \ell}(c)$ with determinant $-1$, eigenvalues $\pm 1$ (the representation is "odd").*

*(iv) **Irreducible.** $\rho_{f, \ell}$ is absolutely irreducible (as a $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$-representation) when $f$ is a cusp newform, by Ribet's irreducibility theorem.*

*(v) **Hodge-Tate weights.** At $p = \ell$, the local representation $\rho_{f, \ell} |_{\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}/\mathbb{Q}_\ell)}$ is crystalline (good reduction at $\ell \nmid N$) with Hodge-Tate weights $\{0, 1\}$, the analogue of weight-$2$ in the de Rham realisation.*

### Ramanujan-Petersson and the Weil bound

**Theorem 6 (Ramanujan-Petersson, weight-$2$ case; Deligne 1974 *Publ. Math. IHES* 43).** *For every weight-$2$ cusp newform $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ and every prime $p \nmid N$,*
$$
|a_p(f)| \leq 2 \sqrt{p}.
$$
*The roots $\alpha_p, \beta_p$ of the characteristic polynomial $X^2 - a_p(f) X + p$ have common absolute value $\sqrt{p}$, lying on the circle of radius $\sqrt{p}$ in $\mathbb{C}$. This is the modular-form analogue of the Riemann hypothesis, and it is the precise consequence of Deligne's proof of the Weil conjectures (Weil II 1974) applied to the étale cohomology of the modular curve.*

*Higher-weight version.* For weight $k \geq 2$ cusp newforms, Deligne 1971 + 1974 proves $|a_p(f)| \leq 2 p^{(k - 1)/2}$, with the bound arising from the same Weil-conjecture mechanism applied to the étale cohomology of the Kuga-Sato variety $\widetilde{\mathcal{E}}^{k - 2}_N$.

### Modular abelian varieties and the modularity bridge

**Theorem 7 (Shimura's modular abelian variety).** *Let $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ be a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform with eigenvalue field $K_f = \mathbb{Q}(a_p(f) : p \nmid N)$ of degree $d = [K_f : \mathbb{Q}]$. There is an abelian variety $A_f$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ of dimension $d$, constructed as the quotient $J_0(N) / I_f \cdot J_0(N)$ by the kernel $I_f$ of the Hecke-eigenvalue map $\mathbb{T}_N \to K_f$. The abelian variety $A_f$ satisfies $L(A_f, s) = \prod_{\sigma : K_f \hookrightarrow \overline{\mathbb{Q}}} L(f^\sigma, s)$, and its $\ell$-adic Tate module $T_\ell A_f$ realises the direct sum $\bigoplus_\sigma \rho_{f^\sigma, \ell}$ of Galois representations over the Galois conjugates of $f$.*

*Modularity (Wiles 1995, BCDT 2001; statement).* Conversely, every elliptic curve $E$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ of conductor $N_E$ is isogenous to $A_f$ for some weight-$2$ cusp newform $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N_E))^{\mathrm{new}}$ with rational eigenvalues $a_p(f) \in \mathbb{Q}$. The Hecke eigenvalues match the Frobenius traces of $E$ via $a_p(E) = p + 1 - |E(\mathbb{F}_p)| = a_p(f)$, and the $L$-functions coincide: $L(E, s) = L(f, s)$. This identification is the **modularity theorem**, and the Eichler-Shimura construction is exactly the bridge from the modular form $f$ to the elliptic curve $E = A_f$.

### Mod-$\ell$ behaviour and level lowering

**Theorem 8 (Carayol 1986 + Ribet 1990; mod-$\ell$ reduction).** *Let $f$ be a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform on $\Gamma_0(N)$, $\overline{\rho}_{f, \ell} : \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q}) \to \mathrm{GL}_2(\overline{\mathbb{F}_\ell})$ the residual mod-$\ell$ Galois representation obtained from $\rho_{f, \ell}$ by reduction modulo the maximal ideal of the ring of integers in $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$, and assume $\overline{\rho}_{f, \ell}$ is absolutely irreducible. Then $\overline{\rho}_{f, \ell}$ is **modular** in the sense that it arises from a weight-$2$ cusp newform $g$ on $\Gamma_0(M)$ for some $M | N$; under certain hypotheses on $N$ and the level structure at primes dividing $N$, the level $M$ can be strictly smaller than $N$ — the **level-lowering** theorem.*

*Carayol 1986* established the local Langlands compatibility at primes $p | N$, identifying the local ramification structure of $\rho_{f, \ell}$ at $p$ with the local automorphic representation of $\mathrm{GL}_2(\mathbb{Q}_p)$ attached to $f$. *Ribet 1990* proved the level-lowering theorem (epsilon conjecture) — that if $\overline{\rho}_{f, \ell}$ is unramified at a prime $q | N$ with $q \neq \ell$, then $\overline{\rho}_{f, \ell}$ arises from a cusp newform of level $N/q$. Together these results were essential inputs to Wiles' proof of modularity for semistable elliptic curves over $\mathbb{Q}$.

**Synthesis.** The Eichler-Shimura correspondence is the foundational reason that modular forms talk to Galois groups. The central insight is that the modular curve $X_0(N)$ is simultaneously a Riemann surface, an algebraic curve over $\mathbb{Q}$, and a moduli space of pairs $(E, C)$, and these three identifications interlock: the holomorphic differentials are weight-$2$ cusp forms, the Hecke correspondences are algebraic operations on the moduli space, and the special-fibre reduction at $p$ identifies the Hecke operator with the Frobenius correspondence. Putting these together, the $\ell$-adic Tate module of the Jacobian decomposes by Hecke eigenforms, and each $f$ produces a two-dimensional Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$ whose Frobenius trace recovers the Hecke eigenvalue $a_p(f)$.

This is exactly the bridge from the analytic modular world (functions on the upper half-plane) to the arithmetic-geometric world (cohomology of moduli spaces over $\mathbb{Q}$), and it generalises in three directions. Deligne 1971 extends to weight $k \geq 2$ via the Kuga-Sato variety; Deligne-Serre 1974 extends to weight $1$ via congruences; the pattern recurs throughout the Langlands programme as the model construction of Galois representations from automorphic forms. The bridge identifies the spectral theory of the Hecke algebra with the Frobenius spectrum on étale cohomology, and the Eichler-Shimura congruence relation is the precise algebraic statement of this identification.

The historical lineage runs from Eichler 1954, who computed the Brandt-matrix trace formula and recognised it as the Frobenius trace on $H^1(X_0(N))$, through Shimura 1958, who built the systematic moduli framework, to Deligne 1971, who extended to all weights $k \geq 2$ via étale cohomology. The downstream applications — Ramanujan-Petersson via Weil II (Deligne 1974), level-lowering (Ribet 1990), modularity (Wiles 1995 + BCDT 2001), and the Iwasawa main conjecture (Mazur-Wiles 1984) — all depend on the Eichler-Shimura framework as their structural foundation.

## Full proof set [Master]

**Proposition 9 (Eichler-Shimura congruence; detailed proof).** *Let $N \geq 1$ and $p$ a prime with $p \nmid N$. Then on the $\ell$-adic Tate module $T_\ell J_0(N)$ for $\ell \neq p$,*
$$
T_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1}.
$$

*Proof.* Consider the modular curve $X_0(N)$ as the moduli space of pairs $(E, C)$ with $E$ an elliptic curve and $C \subseteq E$ a cyclic subgroup of order $N$. Adjoin an auxiliary order-$p$ structure: $X_0(Np)$ is the moduli space of triples $(E, C, D)$ with $C$ of order $N$, $D$ of order $p$, and $C \cap D = 0$. The two degeneracy maps are
$$
\pi_1 : (E, C, D) \mapsto (E, C), \qquad \pi_2 : (E, C, D) \mapsto (E/D, (C + D)/D).
$$
Both have degree $p + 1$ over $X_0(N)$ (the count of order-$p$ subgroups in $E[p] \cong (\mathbb{Z}/p)^2$). The Hecke correspondence is $T_p = \pi_{2 *} \pi_1^*$ on the Jacobian $J_0(N) = \mathrm{Pic}^0(X_0(N))$.

Reduce $X_0(N)$ modulo $p$. Over $\overline{\mathbb{F}_p}$, the $p + 1$ order-$p$ subgroups of an elliptic curve split into two classes: the kernel of the relative Frobenius $\mathrm{Frob}_p : E \to E^{(p)}$ (one connected subgroup), and the kernels of the dual Verschiebung isogeny (the étale subgroups, generically $p$ of them in the ordinary case). The map $\pi_1$ restricted to the Frobenius-kernel subscheme of $X_0(Np) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$ is the identity on $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$ (the moduli point is unchanged by reduction, $D$ being the unique Frobenius kernel); the map $\pi_2$ restricted to the same subscheme sends $(E, C, \ker \mathrm{Frob})$ to $(E/\ker \mathrm{Frob}, (C + \ker \mathrm{Frob})/\ker \mathrm{Frob}) = (E^{(p)}, C^{(p)})$, which is the Frobenius image of $(E, C)$.

Hence the Frobenius-kernel branch of the correspondence $T_p$ on the special fibre is the Frobenius correspondence $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ on $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$. The Verschiebung-kernel branch is dual: by exchanging the roles of $\pi_1$ and $\pi_2$ and applying the diamond operator $\langle p \rangle$ to correct the level structure, one identifies the Verschiebung branch with $p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1}$ on $T_\ell J_0(N)$.

Adding the two branches yields $T_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \langle p \rangle \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1}$ on $T_\ell J_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_\ell$ (which, since both sides are integral operators on the Tate module, lifts to the rational statement). $\square$

**Proposition 10 (Trace-of-Frobenius identity).** *For $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$ a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform and $p \nmid N \ell$, $\mathrm{tr}\,\rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = a_p(f)$ and $\det \rho_{f, \ell}(\mathrm{Frob}_p) = p$.*

*Proof.* Restrict the Eichler-Shimura congruence to the $f$-isotypic component $V_{\ell, f} \subset V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$. The Hecke operator $T_p$ acts on $V_{\ell, f}$ by the scalar $a_p(f)$, and the diamond operator $\langle p \rangle$ acts as the identity (the case of $\Gamma_0(N)$-newforms with principal nebentypus). Multiplying both sides of the congruence $T_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \mathrm{Frob}_p^{-1}$ by $\mathrm{Frob}_p$:
$$
T_p \mathrm{Frob}_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p^2 + p \cdot \mathrm{Id}_{V_{\ell, f}}.
$$
Substituting $T_p = a_p(f) \mathrm{Id}$ on $V_{\ell, f}$ gives
$$
a_p(f) \mathrm{Frob}_p = \mathrm{Frob}_p^2 + p \mathrm{Id},
$$
equivalently $\mathrm{Frob}_p^2 - a_p(f) \mathrm{Frob}_p + p \mathrm{Id} = 0$ on $V_{\ell, f}$. The minimal polynomial of $\mathrm{Frob}_p$ on $V_{\ell, f}$ divides $X^2 - a_p(f) X + p$, and since $\dim V_{\ell, f} = 2$, the characteristic polynomial is exactly $X^2 - a_p(f) X + p$. The trace and determinant follow. $\square$

**Proposition 11 (Decomposition of $V_\ell$ by eigenforms).** *Let $V_\ell = T_\ell J_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{Q}_\ell$ and let $\mathbb{T}_N$ be the Hecke algebra acting on $V_\ell$. Then $V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$ decomposes as a direct sum of two-dimensional Hecke-isotypic components, each indexed by a Galois-conjugacy class of weight-$2$ cusp newforms on $\Gamma_0(N)$ and on $\Gamma_0(M)$ for $M | N$ (old-form contributions).*

*Proof.* The Hecke algebra $\mathbb{T}_N \subseteq \mathrm{End}_{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}(V_\ell)$ is finitely generated and commutative. The Petersson self-adjointness of the Hecke operators on $S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$ extends, via the Hodge decomposition (Theorem 3), to a perfect pairing on $V_\ell \otimes \mathbb{C} = H^1(X_0(N), \mathbb{C})$, with the Hecke algebra acting semisimply. After extending scalars to $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$, semisimplicity yields a direct-sum decomposition
$$
V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} = \bigoplus_\lambda V_{\ell, \lambda},
$$
where $\lambda$ ranges over ring homomorphisms $\mathbb{T}_N \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} \to \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$ and $V_{\ell, \lambda} = \{v \in V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell} : T_p v = \lambda(T_p) v \text{ for all } p \nmid N\}$.

Each such $\lambda$ is the eigenvalue system of a normalised weight-$2$ cusp newform $f$ on $\Gamma_0(M)$ for some $M | N$ (by the new/old decomposition of Atkin-Lehner 1970): the newforms at level $M$ contribute via the old-form lifts $\alpha_d : S_2(\Gamma_0(M)) \to S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$ for $d | (N/M)$.

The $\overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$-dimension of $V_{\ell, \lambda}$ is $2$ on the newform component (by the multiplicity-one theorem) and a multiple of $2$ on the old-form components. Restricting to the newform contributions gives the decomposition stated. $\square$

**Proposition 12 (Unramified outside $N \ell$).** *Let $\rho_{f, \ell}$ be the Eichler-Shimura representation attached to $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N))^{\mathrm{new}}$. For every prime $p \nmid N \ell$, $\rho_{f, \ell}$ is unramified at $p$ — that is, the inertia subgroup $I_p \subseteq \mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$ acts as the identity via $\rho_{f, \ell}$.*

*Proof.* The modular curve $X_0(N)$ admits a smooth proper integral model $\mathcal{X}_0(N)$ over $\mathbb{Z}[1/N]$ — the coarse moduli space of generalised elliptic curves with a cyclic order-$N$ subgroup is a smooth scheme over $\mathbb{Z}[1/N]$. The Jacobian $J_0(N) = \mathrm{Jac}(\mathcal{X}_0(N))$ then has good reduction at every prime $p \nmid N$ (the reduction is the Jacobian of the smooth special fibre $\mathcal{X}_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$).

By the **Néron-Ogg-Shafarevich criterion**: an abelian variety $A$ over a number field has good reduction at $p$ if and only if the $\ell$-adic Tate module $T_\ell A$ is unramified at $p$ for some (equivalently, every) prime $\ell \neq p$. Applied here: $T_\ell J_0(N)$ is unramified at every $p \nmid N \ell$, so the Galois action on $V_\ell$ factors through the unramified quotient $\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}_p^{\mathrm{ur}}/\mathbb{Q}_p)$ at such $p$.

The $f$-isotypic component $V_{\ell, f} \subseteq V_\ell \otimes \overline{\mathbb{Q}_\ell}$ is preserved by the Galois action (since the Hecke algebra commutes with the Galois action by Theorem 4), so the restriction $\rho_{f, \ell} = \rho |_{V_{\ell, f}}$ is also unramified at $p \nmid N \ell$. $\square$

## Connections [Master]

- **Modular forms on $\mathrm{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ [21.04.01].** Foundational unit defining the ambient space of modular forms and the Eisenstein-series / cusp-form decomposition. The present unit specialises to weight $2$ and level $N$, where the cusp forms acquire a cohomological interpretation as holomorphic differentials on the modular curve $X_0(N)$ and the Eichler-Shimura bridge connects them to Galois representations. The dimension formula $\dim S_2(\Gamma_0(N)) = g(X_0(N))$ recurs throughout the Eichler-Shimura construction.

- **Hecke operators and Hecke algebra [21.04.02].** Sibling unit on the operator-theoretic structure of $M_k(\Gamma)$. The Eichler-Shimura correspondence is the geometric realisation of the analytic Hecke operators as algebraic correspondences on the modular curve $X_0(N)$, identifying the Hecke eigenvalue $a_p(f)$ with the trace of Frobenius on the $\ell$-adic Tate module of the Jacobian. The Hecke algebra of [21.04.02] acts on the present unit's Jacobian $J_0(N)$ via algebraic correspondences whose $\ell$-adic realisation produces the Galois representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$.

- **$\ell$-adic Galois representations [21.05.01].** Sibling unit on the systematic framework of continuous $\ell$-adic Galois representations of $\mathrm{Gal}(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}/\mathbb{Q})$. The Eichler-Shimura representation $\rho_{f, \ell}$ is the originating example: a two-dimensional, continuous, semisimple, geometric representation arising from the cohomology of the modular curve, unramified outside $N \ell$, with prescribed Frobenius traces. The general theory of $\ell$-adic representations — Tate modules of abelian varieties, ramification, Hodge-Tate weights, crystalline representations — is built on the Eichler-Shimura case as the model construction.

- **Modularity theorem and BSD [21.06.01].** Sibling unit on the Wiles-Taylor-Breuil-Conrad-Diamond modularity theorem and the Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture. The modularity theorem states that every elliptic curve $E$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ of conductor $N_E$ is isogenous to the Shimura abelian variety $A_f$ attached to some weight-$2$ cusp newform $f \in S_2(\Gamma_0(N_E))^{\mathrm{new}}$ with rational eigenvalues. The Eichler-Shimura construction is exactly the bridge from $f$ to $A_f = E$: the Hecke eigenvalues $a_p(f)$ are the Frobenius traces $a_p(E)$, and the $L$-functions $L(f, s) = L(E, s)$ coincide.

- **Jacobian variety [06.06.03].** Prerequisite unit on the Jacobian $\mathrm{Jac}(C)$ of an algebraic curve as an abelian variety of dimension $g(C)$. The present unit specialises to $C = X_0(N)$, where the Jacobian $J_0(N) = \mathrm{Jac}(X_0(N))$ is the central geometric object carrying the Hecke action and the $\ell$-adic Galois representation. The general Jacobian theory (Abel-Jacobi map, polarisations, theta functions) underpins the modular case.

- **Elliptic curves [04.04.03].** Prerequisite unit on elliptic curves as smooth projective curves of genus $1$ with a marked point. The moduli interpretation of $X_0(N)$ uses pairs $(E, C)$ with $E$ elliptic and $C$ a cyclic subgroup; the Frobenius and Verschiebung isogenies on elliptic curves over $\mathbb{F}_p$ produce the Eichler-Shimura congruence relation. The simplest examples of modular abelian varieties $A_f$ are elliptic curves themselves (when the eigenvalue field $K_f = \mathbb{Q}$), giving the most direct modularity bridge: a weight-$2$ cusp newform with rational eigenvalues corresponds to an elliptic curve over $\mathbb{Q}$.

- **Dedekind zeta, Hecke $L$, Artin $L$ [21.03.03].** Sibling unit on higher-rank $L$-functions of number fields and Galois representations. The Hecke $L$-function $L(f, s) = \sum a_n(f) n^{-s}$ attached to a weight-$2$ cusp newform $f$ is, after Eichler-Shimura, equal to the Artin $L$-function $L(\rho_{f, \ell}, s)$ of the attached Galois representation (formally, after analytic continuation of the $\ell$-adic Galois representation to a complex $L$-function). The Eichler-Shimura correspondence is therefore the $\mathrm{GL}_2$-case of the Hecke-Artin / Langlands $L$-function correspondence.

- **Iwasawa $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions [21.07.01].** Sibling unit on Iwasawa theory of $\mathbb{Z}_p$-extensions of number fields. The Mazur-Wiles 1984 proof of the Iwasawa Main Conjecture for $\mathbb{Q}$ uses the Galois representations attached to weight-$2$ cusp eigenforms via the Eichler-Shimura framework as the key technical input: deformations of $\rho_{f, \ell}$ over the Iwasawa algebra produce the $p$-adic $L$-function whose Iwasawa-invariants match the class-group sides of the conjecture. The present unit's Galois representation is therefore the operator-theoretic substrate on which Iwasawa theory is built.

## Historical & philosophical context [Master]

Martin Eichler 1954 *Arch. Math.* 5 [ref: TODO_REF Eichler1954] computed the trace formula for the Hecke operator $T_p$ on $S_2(\Gamma_0(N))$ via the Brandt matrix associated to a definite quaternion algebra ramified at $p$ and $\infty$, and recognised this trace as the Lefschetz fixed-point count of the Frobenius correspondence on the special fibre $X_0(N) \otimes \mathbb{F}_p$. The title of the 1954 paper — "Quaternäre quadratische Formen und die Riemannsche Vermutung für die Kongruenzzetafunktion" — emphasises the connection to the Riemann hypothesis for the modular curve: the eigenvalues of Frobenius on the étale cohomology of $X_0(N)$ have absolute value $\sqrt{p}$, and Eichler's trace formula identification produced the bound $|a_p(f)| \leq 2 \sqrt{p}$ as a consequence. This was the first instance of the modular-form $\to$ Galois-representation bridge in its concrete shape: a Hecke trace identified with a Frobenius trace on cohomology.

Goro Shimura 1958 *J. Math. Soc. Japan* 10 [ref: TODO_REF Shimura1958] systematised Eichler's case-by-case calculations into a general framework. The 1958 paper "Correspondances modulaires et les fonctions $\zeta$ de courbes algébriques" recast the modular curve $X_0(N)$ as a moduli space of pairs $(E, C)$ over $\mathbb{Q}$, identified the Hecke operators with algebraic correspondences on $X_0(N) \times X_0(N)$, and constructed the two-dimensional $\ell$-adic Galois representations attached to weight-$2$ cusp eigenforms by decomposing the Tate module of the Jacobian $J_0(N)$ under the Hecke algebra. Shimura's *Introduction to the Arithmetic Theory of Automorphic Functions* (Princeton 1971) [ref: TODO_REF Shimura1971] codified this framework into the canonical book-form treatment, including the construction of the modular abelian variety $A_f$ as a quotient $J_0(N)/I_f \cdot J_0(N)$ by the Hecke-ideal of an eigenform.

Pierre Deligne 1969-71 [ref: TODO_REF Deligne1971] extended the Eichler-Shimura construction to cusp eigenforms of arbitrary weight $k \geq 2$ in the *Séminaire Bourbaki* report "Formes modulaires et représentations $\ell$-adiques," using the étale cohomology of the Kuga-Sato variety $\widetilde{\mathcal{E}}^{k-2}_N$ — a smooth compactification of the $(k-2)$-fold fibre product of the universal elliptic curve over $Y_0(N)$ — to realise the higher-weight Galois representations. The weight-$1$ case, completing the picture, was handled by Deligne-Serre 1974 *Ann. Sci. ENS* 7 [ref: TODO_REF DeligneSerre1974] via congruences with higher-weight forms; the resulting representation has finite image and is Artin-type. Deligne 1974 *Publ. Math. IHES* 43 [ref: TODO_REF Deligne1974] then established the Ramanujan-Petersson bound $|a_p(f)| \leq 2 p^{(k-1)/2}$ as a consequence of his proof of the Riemann hypothesis for varieties over $\mathbb{F}_q$ (the Weil conjectures), applied to the étale cohomology of the modular curve and its Kuga-Sato analogues.

The downstream applications run through three major theorems. Mazur-Wiles 1984 *Invent. Math.* 76 [ref: TODO_REF MazurWiles1984] proved the Iwasawa Main Conjecture for $\mathbb{Q}$, using deformations of Eichler-Shimura representations $\rho_{f, \ell}$ over the Iwasawa algebra to construct the $p$-adic $L$-function and match its Iwasawa invariants to class-group data. Carayol 1986 *Ann. Sci. ENS* 19 [ref: TODO_REF Carayol1986] established the local Langlands compatibility for $\rho_{f, \ell}$ at primes $p | N$, identifying the local ramification with the local automorphic representation. Ribet 1990 *Invent. Math.* 100 [ref: TODO_REF Ribet1990] proved the level-lowering theorem (epsilon conjecture), and Wiles 1995 *Ann. Math.* 141 combined these inputs to prove modularity for semistable elliptic curves over $\mathbb{Q}$; the full modularity theorem followed in Breuil-Conrad-Diamond-Taylor 2001. The Eichler-Shimura framework is therefore the operator-theoretic foundation on which the modularity theorem, Fermat's last theorem, and the Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture all rest.

Diamond-Shurman 2005 [ref: TODO_REF DiamondShurman2005] provides the canonical modern textbook account, integrating the moduli-theoretic, cohomological, and Galois-representation viewpoints. Manin-Panchishkin 2005 *Introduction to Modern Number Theory* [ref: TODO_REF ManinPanchishkin2005] codifies the Eichler-Shimura correspondence in its encyclopedia synthesis, placing it within the broader arithmetic geometry of $L$-functions and Galois representations.
