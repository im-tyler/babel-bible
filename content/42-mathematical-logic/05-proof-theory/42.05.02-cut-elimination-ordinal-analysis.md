## Intuition [Beginner]

The companion unit [42.05.01] treated a proof as a two-sided line and showed you can erase the detour rule called the *cut*. This unit takes the next step. When a theory also carries an induction rule, the finite erasing procedure breaks down, and the question becomes how much "transfinite climbing" is needed to finish the job. The amount of climbing turns out to measure exactly how strong the theory is.

Picture a proof as a route on a map. A cut is a detour: you drive out to a distant helper lemma, use it, then drive back, and the helper never appears at your destination. Erasing all cuts straightens every route into one that passes only through towns already named in your destination. For pure logic the straightening always finishes in finitely many steps. For arithmetic it does not, but it finishes if you are allowed to count down a tower of infinities.

That tower is the point of this unit. Each rung is an infinite number bigger than the one below: $\omega$, then $\omega$ to the power $\omega$, then that whole tower raised to the $\omega$ again, and so on. The limit of all these rungs is called $\varepsilon_0$. Gentzen's discovery is that Peano arithmetic is consistent exactly up to $\varepsilon_0$: the theory can climb any single rung but cannot reach the top. The height a theory can climb is its *proof-theoretic ordinal*, and that height is its consistency strength.

So erasing detours does two jobs at once. In pure logic it gives a normal form that makes consistency immediate. In arithmetic it converts the consistency question into an ordinal-measuring question, and the ordinal reached — $\varepsilon_0$, then the Feferman-Schütte ordinal $\Gamma_0$, then the Bachmann-Howard ordinal for stronger theories — becomes a ruler comparing theories. That ruler is what this unit builds.

## Visual [Beginner]

A sequent proof is a tree of two-sided lines, and the cut rule is the one move whose helper formula disappears. The picture below is the ordinal analogue: a tower of ever-larger infinite numbers, each rung earned by raising $\omega$ to the power of the rung below.

![The ordinal tower rising to epsilon-nought, the proof-theoretic ordinal of PA](/img/proof-theory-depth-placeholder.svg)

```
   THE ORDINAL TOWER  (each rung = omega raised to the rung below it)

           omega ^ ( omega ^ ( omega ^ ( ... ) ) )     <-- epsilon_0
           :                                           (PA cannot reach here)
           ^
           |
     omega ^ omega ^ omega                  rung 4
     omega ^ omega                          rung 3
     omega ^ omega                          wait: rung 2 = omega^omega
     omega                                  rung 1

   PA proves transfinite induction up to any FINITE rung,
   but NOT up to epsilon_0 itself.

   epsilon_0 = |PA|   (the proof-theoretic ordinal of Peano Arithmetic)
```

Read the tower from the bottom: $\omega$, then $\omega^\omega$, then $\omega^{\omega^\omega}$, and so on without end. The limit is $\varepsilon_0$. Each rung is strictly bigger than the one below, and only at the limit does the operation "raise $\omega$ to this power" settle — $\omega^{\varepsilon_0} = \varepsilon_0$.

| theory | proof-theoretic ordinal | what climbing that high buys |
|---|---|---|
| primitive recursive arithmetic (PRA) | $\omega^\omega$ | finitary consistency of PRA |
| Peano arithmetic (PA) | $\varepsilon_0$ | consistency of first-order arithmetic |
| $\mathsf{ATR}_0$ (predicative analysis) | $\Gamma_0$ | the Feferman-Schütte predicative limit |
| $\Pi^1_1\text{-}\mathsf{CA}_0$ | Bachmann-Howard ordinal | impredicative analysis |

Higher ordinal, stronger theory: the height a theory can climb is its consistency strength, made into a single comparable number.

## Worked example [Beginner]

Build $\varepsilon_0$ from below. Start with $\omega$, the first infinite number. Raise $\omega$ to its own power: $\omega^\omega$. Raise again: $\omega^{\omega^\omega}$. Each step stacks one more $\omega$ on top of the tower. The rungs are $\omega$, then $\omega^\omega$, then $\omega^{\omega^\omega}$, then $\omega^{\omega^{\omega^\omega}}$, and so on.

The limit of this endless tower is $\varepsilon_0$. Its defining trick is a fixed-point property: $\varepsilon_0$ is the first number $\alpha$ with $\omega^\alpha = \alpha$. Check the pattern by hand. For $\omega$ we have $\omega^\omega$ far bigger than $\omega$. For $\omega^\omega$ we have $\omega^{\omega^\omega}$ far bigger than $\omega^\omega$. Each rung sits strictly below the next. Only at the limit does the power settle: $\omega^{\varepsilon_0} = \varepsilon_0$.

Now the punch line. Gentzen proved that Peano arithmetic, PA, can prove transfinite induction up to every rung below $\varepsilon_0$ — $\omega$, $\omega^\omega$, $\omega^{\omega^\omega}$, each one — but cannot prove transfinite induction up to $\varepsilon_0$ itself. The height PA can climb is exactly $\varepsilon_0$. So $\varepsilon_0$ is called the *proof-theoretic ordinal* of PA, written $|PA| = \varepsilon_0$. A stronger theory climbs higher.

What this tells us: the consistency of PA is equivalent, over a weak base, to the well-foundedness of $\varepsilon_0$ — the claim that no endless descending chain of ordinals lives below $\varepsilon_0$. PA cannot see that well-foundedness itself (Gödel's second theorem [42.01.09]), but a metatheory that grants induction up to $\varepsilon_0$ can, and that single grant is exactly what Gentzen's consistency proof consumes.

## Formal definition [Intermediate+]

Recall from [42.05.01] the sequent calculus $\mathsf{LK}$: sequents $\Gamma \Rightarrow \Delta$, initial sequents $A \Rightarrow A$, the structural rules (weakening, contraction, exchange), the left and right logical rules with their eigenvariable conditions, and the cut rule. We do not redevelop the calculus here; this unit's object is the *ordinal layer* that the Hauptsatz leaves untouched once an induction rule is adjoined. Fix the language of first-order arithmetic with symbols $0, S, +, \times, =$ and the induction schema, giving the theory $\mathsf{PA}$.

**Cantor normal form (CNF).** Every nonzero ordinal $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ admits a unique expression

$$
\alpha = \omega^{\beta_1}\!\cdot c_1 + \omega^{\beta_2}\!\cdot c_2 + \cdots + \omega^{\beta_n}\!\cdot c_n,\qquad \alpha > \beta_1 \geq \beta_2 \geq \cdots \geq \beta_n,\quad 0 < c_i < \omega,
$$

where each exponent $\beta_i$ is itself in CNF [ref: TODO_REF Pohlers Ch. 3-4]. Iterating the exponent-descent terminates precisely because $\alpha < \varepsilon_0 = \sup\{\omega, \omega^\omega, \omega^{\omega^\omega}, \dots\}$. CNF makes the ordinals below $\varepsilon_0$ a primitive-recursive notation system: there is an algorithm deciding, for any two notations, which ordinal is smaller, and so transfinite induction up to $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ reduces to induction over a computable well-order on finite strings.

**Definition (proof-theoretic ordinal).** For a sound, recursively axiomatised theory $T \supseteq \mathsf{PRA}$, the *proof-theoretic ordinal* $\|T\|$ (also written $|T|$) is the following coincident countable ordinal:

- the supremum $\sup\!\big\{\operatorname{otp}(\prec) : \prec \text{ primitive-recursive well-ordering with } T \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\prec)\big\}$;
- equivalently, $\sup\{\alpha : F_\alpha \text{ is provably total in } T\}$ in the fast-growing hierarchy $(F_\alpha)$;
- equivalently, the least $\alpha$ along the canonical notation such that $T \nvdash \mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$.

The equivalence of the first and third is Gentzen-style (a theory proves $\mathsf{TI}$ up to exactly one bound); that of the first and second is the Wainer–Kreisel characterization of provable totality [ref: TODO_REF Pohlers]. Two theories with the same proof-theoretic ordinal have the same consistency strength.

**Veblen hierarchy.** Put $\varphi_0(\beta) = \omega^\beta$, and define $\varphi_{\alpha+1}$ to enumerate in increasing order the common fixed points of $\{\varphi_\gamma : \gamma \leq \alpha\}$; at limits set $\varphi_\lambda(\beta) = \sup_{\gamma < \lambda}\varphi_\gamma(\beta)$. Each $\varphi_\alpha$ is a continuous increasing (normal) function. The *Feferman-Schütte ordinal* $\Gamma_0$ is the least $\alpha$ with $\varphi_\alpha(0) = \alpha$; it bounds every predicatively definable ordinal. The *Bachmann-Howard ordinal* sits above $\Gamma_0$ and is reached only by impredicative constructions.

**Fast-growing hierarchy.** Set $F_0(n) = n+1$, $F_{\alpha+1}(n) = F_\alpha^{\,n+1}(n)$ (the $(n+1)$-fold self-composition), and $F_\lambda(n) = F_{\lambda[n]}(n)$ for a fixed fundamental sequence $\lambda[0] < \lambda[1] < \cdots$ with $\sup_n \lambda[n] = \lambda$. Then $F_\alpha$ dominates exactly the functions that a theory of proof-theoretic ordinal $> \alpha$ can prove total: $F_{\varepsilon_0}$ dominates the $\mathsf{PA}$-provably total functions, $F_{\Gamma_0}$ those provably total in predicative analysis.

### Counterexamples to common slips [Intermediate+]

- *"$\varepsilon_0$ is the largest ordinal PA can handle."* PA can *name* and reason about many ordinals larger than $\varepsilon_0$. What fails at $\varepsilon_0$ is specifically *transfinite induction*: PA proves $\mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$ for each $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ but not at $\varepsilon_0$. The proof-theoretic ordinal measures induction strength, not expressive reach.

- *"Ordinal analysis proves the theory true."* It does not. Ordinal analysis delivers *relative* consistency (the theory is consistent if a weak base plus $\mathsf{TI}(\|T\|)$ is) and a bound on the provably total functions. It is a calibration, not a soundness proof; a theory of ordinal $\varepsilon_0$ could still be unsound in a language it does not control.

- *"A larger proof-theoretic ordinal means strictly more theorems in the same language."* The ordinal compares *consistency strength* and *provable totality*, which is coarser than raw theorem-set inclusion. Two theories can be incomparable in theorem set yet have ordinals that line up, because the ordinal measures the transfinite-induction resources, one axis among several.

## Key theorem with proof [Intermediate+]

The load-bearing result of pure structural proof theory is the Hauptsatz; the load-bearing result of *this* unit is the ordinal-analysis theorem that calibrates $\mathsf{PA}$ at $\varepsilon_0$, which is what the Hauptsatz becomes when an induction rule blocks outright cut-elimination.

**Theorem (Cut-elimination, Gentzen's Hauptsatz — stated).** *Every $\mathsf{LK}$-derivation of a sequent $S$ transforms into a cut-free $\mathsf{LK}$-derivation of $S$; likewise for $\mathsf{LJ}$.* [ref: TODO_REF Gentzen 1934-35]

*Proof sketch.* Remove a topmost cut (more precisely a *mix*, which cuts all copies of the cut formula at once) by a double induction on the **grade** $g$ of the cut formula $A$ (its logical complexity) and on the **rank** $r$ (the combined heights of the chains of $A$-containing sequents above each premise). The **non-principal** case, where $A$ was carried passively by the rule above a premise, permutes the mix upward past that rule: $r$ drops, $g$ fixed, inner hypothesis applies. The **principal** case, where $A$ was just introduced on both sides, replaces the mix on $A$ by mixes on its immediate subformulas: $g$ drops, outer hypothesis applies. The $\forall$-case substitutes the eigenvariable by the witness term throughout, which is legitimate precisely because the eigenvariable condition kept that variable out of the side formulas. The full case analysis, with the mix reduction and the base cases against initial sequents and weakenings, is Proposition 1 of [42.05.01]. $\square$

The immediate corollary is the **subformula property**: every formula occurring in a cut-free derivation of $S$ is a subformula of a formula of $S$ (each non-cut rule has its premise-formulas among the subformulas of its conclusion-formulas), from which the consistency of pure $\mathsf{LK}$ follows as in [42.05.01]. The present unit's concern is what survives of this picture when the induction rule is adjoined.

**Theorem (Gentzen's ordinal analysis of $\mathsf{PA}$).** *$|\mathsf{PA}| = \varepsilon_0$: that is, $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$ for every $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ and $\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$. Consequently $\mathsf{Con}(\mathsf{PA})$ is equivalent over $\mathsf{PRA}$ to $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$.* [ref: TODO_REF Gentzen 1936] [ref: TODO_REF Takeuti Ch. 2]

*Proof.* Two halves.

*Lower bound ($\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$ for each $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$).* Induct on the CNF of $\alpha$. For a leading term $\omega^\beta \cdot c$ with $\beta < \alpha$, a primitive-recursive descent through $\omega^\beta$ decomposes (by the recursive CNF of $\beta$) into finitely many descents through strictly smaller ordinals, each dispatched by the inner induction hypothesis. The finite coefficient $c < \omega$ is absorbed by ordinary $\mathsf{PA}$-induction. Because the CNF exponent-descent terminates strictly below $\varepsilon_0$, every $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ is dispatched inside $\mathsf{PA}$, giving $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$.

*Upper bound ($\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$).* Assign to each $\mathsf{PA}$-derivation $D$ an ordinal $o(D) < \varepsilon_0$ in CNF, summing $\omega$-powers graded by cut-formula complexity and the depth of induction instances. Exhibit an effective reduction $D \mapsto D'$ acting on any derivation of the empty sequent $\Rightarrow$, with $D'$ again ending in $\Rightarrow$ and $o(D') < o(D)$: each maximal cut is pushed upward or eliminated (its grade drops), and each induction instance is unfolded one step (raising the local $\omega$-power but staying below $\varepsilon_0$).

If a derivation of $\Rightarrow$ existed, iterating the reduction would produce an infinite strictly descending chain $o(D) > o(D') > o(D'') > \cdots$ below $\varepsilon_0$, contradicting $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$. So no derivation of $\Rightarrow$ exists: $\mathsf{PA}$ is consistent. Now suppose for contradiction that $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$. Then the reduction argument above — which is primitive-recursive except for the single call to $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ — could be formalized *inside* $\mathsf{PA}$, yielding $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{Con}(\mathsf{PA})$, against Gödel's second incompleteness theorem [42.01.09]. Hence $\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$, and $|\mathsf{PA}| = \varepsilon_0$. $\square$

**Bridge.** The ordinal $|\mathsf{PA}| = \varepsilon_0$ is the foundational reason cut-elimination survives the move from pure logic to arithmetic: the grade-and-rank induction of the Hauptsatz, finite and bounded in [42.05.01], generalises here to a transfinite descent that consumes exactly the one principle — $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ — lying beyond $\mathsf{PA}$'s reach. This is exactly the calibrated face of Gödel's second theorem [42.01.09]: the missing consistency strength is not a vague gap but a single named ordinal. It builds toward the hierarchy below — $\Gamma_0$ for predicative theories, the Bachmann-Howard ordinal for impredicative $\Pi^1_1$-analysis — and it appears again in the fast-growing hierarchy, where $F_{\varepsilon_0}$ dominates exactly the $\mathsf{PA}$-provably total functions, turning ordinal strength into growth rate and into concrete independence results such as Goodstein's theorem. The central insight is that a theory's strength, its consistency, and the functions it proves total are three readings of one ordinal, and the bridge from a syntactic cut-elimination procedure to a semantic measure of strength is the proof-theoretic ordinal itself.

**Exercise 3 (medium, symbolic).**

From the definition $\varepsilon_0 = \sup\{\omega,\, \omega^\omega,\, \omega^{\omega^\omega},\, \omega^{\omega^{\omega^\omega}},\, \dots\}$, prove the fixed-point identity $\omega^{\varepsilon_0} = \varepsilon_0$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Exponentiation by $\omega$ is continuous (it commutes with suprema of increasing sequences). Apply it to the defining sequence and recognize the tail.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Let $\alpha_0 = \omega$ and $\alpha_{n+1} = \omega^{\alpha_n}$, so $\varepsilon_0 = \sup_n \alpha_n$. Continuity of $\beta \mapsto \omega^\beta$ on increasing suprema gives
$$\omega^{\varepsilon_0} = \omega^{\sup_n \alpha_n} = \sup_n \omega^{\alpha_n} = \sup_n \alpha_{n+1} = \sup_n \alpha_n = \varepsilon_0,$$
the last two suprema agreeing because the tail $(\alpha_{n+1})_n$ is the sequence $(\alpha_n)_n$ shifted by one. Rubric: full credit for using continuity of $\omega$-exponentiation and recognizing the shifted tail.

**Exercise 4 (medium, symbolic).**

A Goodstein sequence writes its current number in *hereditary base $b$* (every exponent also written in base $b$), changes every $b$ to $b+1$, then subtracts $1$. Translate the starting value $4$ to an ordinal by writing it in hereditary base $2$ and replacing every $2$ by $\omega$. Then show that after one Goodstein step the resulting natural number has *grown* while the associated ordinal has *shrunk*.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

$4 = 2^2$ is already hereditary base $2$. Replace the base $2$ by $\omega$ to get the ordinal. For the step, change base to $3$ (giving $3^3 = 27$), subtract $1$ to get $26$, rewrite $26$ in hereditary base $3$, replace $3$ by $\omega$, and compare ordinals.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Hereditary base $2$: $4 = 2^2$. Replacing $2$ by $\omega$ gives the ordinal $\omega^\omega < \varepsilon_0$. After one step: change base to $3$, obtaining $3^3 = 27$; subtract $1$, giving $26 = 2\cdot 3^2 + 2\cdot 3 + 2$ in hereditary base $3$. Replace $3$ by $\omega$: the ordinal is $\omega^2\cdot 2 + \omega\cdot 2 + 2$, which is *strictly smaller* than $\omega^\omega$ (lower leading exponent), even though $26 > 4$. This descent of the ordinal shadow at every step, while the number itself first balloons, is why transfinite induction up to $\varepsilon_0$ proves every Goodstein sequence terminates, and why $\mathsf{PA}$ — which lacks $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ — cannot prove it. Rubric: full credit for the ordinal $\omega^\omega$, the correct expansion of $26$, and the strict decrease.

</details>

An $\omega^k$-indexed descending chain projects, coordinate by coordinate through the CNF $\omega^{k-1}\cdot n_{k-1} + \cdots$, to finitely many $\omega^{k-1}$-indexed descents. Use induction on $k$.

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Rubric: full credit for the induction on $k$ and the leading-coefficient-plus-tail decomposition.

**Exercise 7 (hard, symbolic).**

Prove that $\mathsf{Con}(\mathsf{PA})$ and $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ are equivalent over the weak base theory $\mathsf{PRA}$: each is provable from the other inside $\mathsf{PRA}$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

**Exercise 8 (hard, symbolic).**

In the infinitary semi-formal calculus with the $\omega$-rule — from $\varphi(0), \varphi(1), \varphi(2), \dots$ infer $\forall x\, \varphi(x)$ — every true $\Pi^0_1$ sentence has a countably infinite cut-free derivation. Explain why cut-elimination for this infinitary calculus sends a derivation of height $\alpha$ to a cut-free derivation of height bounded by an $\omega$-tower in $\alpha$, and why this is the mechanism that produces the $\varepsilon_0$ bound for $\mathsf{PA}$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Eliminating a single cut of grade $g$ at height $h$ replaces it by cuts of lower grade repeated up to $\omega$ times; the height thus gets raised by one $\omega$-exponent per grade unit. Iterate over all grades and heights.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

In the $\omega$-rule calculus a derivation is a (possibly countably infinite) tree whose height is itself an ordinal. Eliminating a cut whose formula has grade $g$ and whose subderivations rise to height $h$ replaces it by countably many cuts of grade $g-1$ (one $\omega$-sequence of reductions), so the new derivation has height at most $\omega^h$-ish — formally, the height gets raised by one application of $\alpha \mapsto \omega^\alpha$. Eliminating cuts of grade $g$, then $g-1$, and so on down to grade $0$, composes this raising $g$ times, yielding a cut-free derivation whose height is an $\omega$-tower of depth $g$ in the original height. Embedding $\mathsf{PA}$ into the infinitary calculus (induction unfolds into an $\omega$-branching $\omega$-rule) gives finite $\mathsf{PA}$-proofs infinitary shadows of some bounded height $\alpha$; eliminating cuts raises the height below the least fixed point of $\alpha \mapsto \omega^\alpha$, namely $\varepsilon_0$. Hence a $\mathsf{PA}$-proof of a contradiction would yield a cut-free infinitary derivation of the empty sequent of height $< \varepsilon_0$, impossible by the subformula property at each ordinal level. The bound $\varepsilon_0$ is thus the cost, in transfinite height, of eliminating cuts from $\mathsf{PA}$. Rubric: full credit for the grade-by-grade $\omega$-exponent raising, the embedding of induction into the $\omega$-rule, and the identification of $\varepsilon_0$ as the resulting fixed point.

## Advanced results [Master]

Ordinal analysis past $\mathsf{PA}$ climbs through three calibrated regions — the predicative ($\leq \Gamma_0$), the impredicative countable (Bachmann-Howard and beyond), and the infinitary engine (the $\omega$-rule) — and each region has a complementary computability reading via the fast-growing hierarchy, plus Gödel's Dialectica as an alternative consistency route.

**Theorem 1 (predicative limit — Feferman-Schütte).** The Feferman-Schütte ordinal $\Gamma_0 = \min\{\alpha : \varphi_\alpha(0) = \alpha\}$ is the supremum of every ordinal that can be predicatively defined, in the precise sense of autonomous ramified progressions (Feferman 1964, Schütte 1965 independently) [ref: TODO_REF Pohlers]. It calibrates the proof-theoretic ordinal of the predicative systems of reverse mathematics: $|\mathsf{ATR}_0| = \Gamma_0$. The autonomy theorem says a predicative mathematician, starting from $\mathsf{PRA}$ and repeatedly adjoining well-orderings whose well-foundedness has already been established, can reach every ordinal below $\Gamma_0$ and none at or above; $\Gamma_0$ is thus the exact predicative ceiling.

**Theorem 2 (impredicative analysis and collapsing).** The system $\Pi^1_1\text{-}\mathsf{CA}_0$ of impredicative second-order arithmetic has proof-theoretic ordinal the *Bachmann-Howard ordinal*, lying strictly above $\Gamma_0$ [ref: TODO_REF Pohlers]. The ordinal-analytic engine here is *ordinal collapsing*: one works in a notation system built on an uncountable ordinal $\Omega$ (taken to name sets unavailable predicatively), performs cut-elimination in that enlarged system where the uncountable height supplies enough room, and then applies a collapsing function that maps the resulting notations back below $\Omega$, delivering a countable ordinal — the Bachmann-Howard ordinal — that records the impredicative strength. This *local predicativity* (Pohlers) is the modern form of Gentzen's reduction: the derivation is reduced transfinitely in an enlarged ordinal world, then collapsed to a countable measure.

**Theorem 3 (infinitary proof theory, the $\omega$-rule — Schütte).** Adjoin to a sequent calculus the *infinitary $\omega$-rule* — from the countably many premises $\Gamma \Rightarrow \Delta, \varphi(n)$ for every $n \in \mathbb{N}$, infer $\Gamma \Rightarrow \Delta, \forall x\, \varphi(x)$ [ref: TODO_REF Pohlers]. Derivations become countably infinite trees of some ordinal height. Cut-elimination for this semi-formal calculus terminates, raising the derivation height by one $\omega$-exponent per eliminated cut grade; embedding $\mathsf{PA}$ into it by unfolding induction into $\omega$-branching yields the bound $|\mathsf{PA}| = \varepsilon_0$ as the transfinite cost of cut-elimination, and analogously produces $\Gamma_0$ and the Bachmann-Howard ordinal for stronger theories. The infinitary calculus is the natural home of ordinal analysis: the ordinal a theory reaches is exactly the height its cut-elimination costs.

**Theorem 4 (provable totality and independence).** The Wainer–Kreisel characterization identifies the $\mathsf{PA}$-provably total recursive functions with those dominated by some $F_\alpha$, $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$, in the fast-growing hierarchy [ref: TODO_REF Pohlers]. Consequently the termination of the *Goodstein sequences*, of *Kirby-Paris's Hydra game*, and the strengthened finite Ramsey theorem of *Paris-Harrington* are each true $\Pi^0_2$ (or $\Pi^1_1$) statements unprovable in $\mathsf{PA}$: each encodes, at finite level, a descent that requires $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ to certify. The Goodstein theorem is particularly direct — Exercise 4 exhibits the ordinal shadow of each step — while Paris-Harrington reaches beyond $\varepsilon_0$ into mild fragments of $\Pi^1_1$-comprehension.

**Theorem 5 (Gödel's Dialectica — an alternative route).** Gödel's 1958 *Dialectica* interpretation interprets $\mathsf{PA}$ into the quantifier-free theory $T$ of primitive-recursive functionals of finite type (System $T$), reducing $\mathsf{Con}(\mathsf{PA})$ to the termination of $T$'s reduction sequences, proved by Tait-style computability [ref: TODO_REF Godel 1958]. This reaches the same consistency strength as Gentzen's $\varepsilon_0$ proof without ordinals, and Spector's 1962 extension via bar-recursion reaches $\Pi^1_1$-analysis — the same level Bachmann-Howard later pinned by ordinals. The Dialectica route and the Gentzen route are thus two complementary consistency proofs of identical strength: one syntactic-ordinal, one computational-functional.

**Synthesis.** Ordinal analysis is the central insight that a theory's consistency strength is a single ordinal, and putting these together the whole ladder — $|\mathsf{PRA}| = \omega^\omega$, $|\mathsf{PA}| = \varepsilon_0$, $|\mathsf{ATR}_0| = \Gamma_0$, $|\Pi^1_1\text{-}\mathsf{CA}_0| = $ Bachmann-Howard — is the foundational reason proof theory can compare theories that raw theorem-counting cannot distinguish. The Hauptsatz of [42.05.01] is dual to the infinitary $\omega$-rule cut-elimination that produces these bounds: one removes detours from finite proofs, the other from countably infinite ones, and the ordinal a theory reaches is exactly what its detour-elimination costs in transfinite height. This generalises from arithmetic into analysis via the Veblen hierarchy and ordinal collapsing, and the bridge is the fast-growing hierarchy, where $\varepsilon_0$, $\Gamma_0$, and the Bachmann-Howard ordinal each translate into a growth rate pinning which functions the theory proves total and which true statements (Goodstein, the Hydra, Paris-Harrington) it cannot reach. Ordinal analysis thus builds toward a single calibrated picture in which consistency, provable totality, and independence are three faces of one ordinal measure — the constructive complement to Gödel's abstract incompleteness, and exactly bounded by it.

## Full proof set [Master]

**Proposition 1 ($\varepsilon_0$ is the least fixed point of $\alpha \mapsto \omega^\alpha$).** *$\varepsilon_0 = \sup\{\omega, \omega^\omega, \omega^{\omega^\omega}, \dots\}$ satisfies $\omega^{\varepsilon_0} = \varepsilon_0$, and no smaller ordinal does.*

*Proof.* Let $\alpha_0 = \omega$ and $\alpha_{n+1} = \omega^{\alpha_n}$, so $\varepsilon_0 = \sup_n \alpha_n$. Continuity of $\beta \mapsto \omega^\beta$ (it carries suprema of increasing sequences to suprema) gives $\omega^{\varepsilon_0} = \omega^{\sup_n \alpha_n} = \sup_n \omega^{\alpha_n} = \sup_n \alpha_{n+1} = \sup_n \alpha_n = \varepsilon_0$, the last equality because $(\alpha_{n+1})_n$ is the sequence $(\alpha_n)_n$ shifted by one index. For minimality, let $\delta < \varepsilon_0$. Then $\delta < \alpha_N$ for some $N$, so $\omega^\delta \leq \omega^{\alpha_N} = \alpha_{N+1} < \varepsilon_0$; but also $\omega^\delta > \delta$ for every countable $\delta$ that is not already an $\varepsilon$-number (and $\delta < \varepsilon_0$ is not). Hence $\omega^\delta > \delta$, so $\delta$ is not a fixed point. Therefore $\varepsilon_0$ is the least fixed point. $\square$

**Proposition 2 ($\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$ for every $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$).** *For each ordinal $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ with its primitive-recursive CNF ordering, Peano arithmetic proves the transfinite-induction schema $\mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$.*

*Proof.* Induct on the CNF of $\alpha$. If $\alpha = 0$, $\mathsf{TI}(0)$ is vacuous. If $\alpha = \omega^{\beta_m}\!\cdot c_m + \cdots + \omega^{\beta_1}\!\cdot c_1$ (CNF, leading exponent $\beta_m$ with $\beta_m < \alpha$), suppose by the inner induction that $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\gamma)$ for every $\gamma < \alpha$, in particular $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\beta_m)$ and $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\omega^{\beta_m})$. A primitive-recursive descent through $\alpha$ projects, by stripping the leading $\omega^{\beta_m}\!\cdot c_m$ summand, to a finite descent of the natural-number coefficient $c_m$ (handled by ordinary $\mathsf{PA}$-induction) together with descents through the tail below $\omega^{\beta_m}$ (handled by $\mathsf{TI}(\omega^{\beta_m})$, itself reducible to $\mathsf{TI}(\beta_m)$ by the same argument iterated on $\beta_m$'s CNF). Composing these inductions is internal to $\mathsf{PA}$, so $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$. The CNF exponent-descent terminates strictly below $\varepsilon_0$, so the induction is well-founded and reaches every $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$. $\square$

**Proposition 3 (Goodstein termination is equivalent to $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ over $\mathsf{PRA}$).** *The statement "every Goodstein sequence terminates" is provable in $\mathsf{PRA} + \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ and, conversely, implies $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ over $\mathsf{PRA}$; hence it is unprovable in $\mathsf{PA}$.*

*Proof.* Associate to a natural number $n$ written in hereditary base $b$ the ordinal $\operatorname{ord}_b(n) < \varepsilon_0$ obtained by replacing the base $b$ everywhere by $\omega$; the result is in CNF below $\varepsilon_0$ because the hereditary exponent-depth is finite. At a Goodstein step, $n$ in hereditary base $b$ is rewritten in base $b+1$ then decreased by $1$. The replacement $b \mapsto b+1$ does not change $\operatorname{ord}_b(n) = \operatorname{ord}_{b+1}(n)$ (both map to the same $\omega$-expression). Subtracting $1$ from the base-$(b+1)$ hereditary form strictly *decreases* the ordinal: in CNF the unit coefficient at the bottom drops, and if it was already zero the descent cascades to a higher exponent at strictly smaller leading term — a strict ordinal decrease $\operatorname{ord}_{b+1}(n-1) < \operatorname{ord}_{b+1}(n) = \operatorname{ord}_b(n)$. Thus each step strictly lowers the ordinal shadow, giving a primitive-recursive descending chain below $\varepsilon_0$. By $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ no such infinite chain exists, so the sequence terminates: $\mathsf{PRA} + \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0) \vdash \mathsf{Goodstein}$.

For the converse, Kirby and Paris (1982) showed the Goodstein theorem is not provable in $\mathsf{PA}$ by an indicator-chain argument, and that over $\mathsf{PRA}$ the Goodstein theorem implies $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ (the descent certified by any putative failure of $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ can be encoded as a Goodstein sequence). Since $\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ (Prop. below), $\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \mathsf{Goodstein}$. $\square$

**Corollary ($|\mathsf{PA}| = \varepsilon_0$, restated).** *$\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$ for every $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ (Proposition 2) and $\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$; hence $|\mathsf{PA}| = \varepsilon_0$.*

*Proof of the upper bound.* If $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$, Gentzen's primitive-recursive reduction $D \mapsto D'$ (formalizable in $\mathsf{PA}$ save for the single call to $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$, now assumed) would internalize to $\mathsf{PA} \vdash \mathsf{Con}(\mathsf{PA})$, contradicting Gödel's second incompleteness theorem [42.01.09]. Hence $\mathsf{PA} \nvdash \mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$. Combined with Proposition 2, the supremum of provable transfinite inductions is exactly $\varepsilon_0$. $\square$

Computability and the halting problem [42.04.02] meet proof theory at the fast-growing hierarchy: the $\mathsf{PA}$-provably total recursive functions are exactly those dominated by some $F_\alpha$ with $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$, so the proof-theoretic ordinal is also a recursion-theoretic boundary, separating the total functions $\mathsf{PA}$ can certify from those (like $F_{\varepsilon_0}$ itself) it cannot. The three inductions — semantic, structural-finite, structural-transfinite — are three faces of the same control over what a theory can prove.

## Historical & philosophical context [Master]

David Hilbert's programme proposed to secure all of mathematics by finitary consistency proofs: formalize each branch as an axiomatic system, then prove, by utterly finite combinatorial means, that the system cannot derive a contradiction. Kurt Gödel's 1931 incompleteness theorems [ref: TODO_REF Godel 1931] showed no sufficiently strong, effectively axiomatized theory can prove its own consistency, blocking the programme in its naive form. Gerhard Gentzen, a student of Paul Bernays and Hermann Weyl, responded by isolating *exactly* which non-finitary principle a constructive consistency proof of arithmetic requires.

Gentzen introduced the sequent calculi $\mathsf{LK}$, $\mathsf{LJ}$ and the natural-deduction systems $\mathsf{NK}$, $\mathsf{NJ}$ in *Untersuchungen über das logische Schließen* (1934-35) and proved the cut-elimination Hauptsatz for both [ref: TODO_REF Gentzen 1934-35]. His 1936 *Die Widerspruchsfreiheit der reinen Zahlentheorie* and the cleaner 1938 *Neue Fassung* gave the consistency proof for first-order arithmetic by assigning ordinal notations below $\varepsilon_0$ to derivations and reducing them transfinitely [ref: TODO_REF Gentzen 1936]; the companion observation — $\mathsf{PA}$ proves $\mathsf{TI}(\alpha)$ for each $\alpha < \varepsilon_0$ but not $\mathsf{TI}(\varepsilon_0)$ — fixed $\varepsilon_0$ as the proof-theoretic ordinal of $\mathsf{PA}$. Gentzen died in 1945 in a Prague prison after the war, leaving much of the programme unfinished; his methods became the seed of all later ordinal analysis.

Oscar Veblen in 1908 had already introduced the continuous normal functions $\varphi_\alpha$ that enumerate fixed points, providing the raw material for the next rung. Solomon Feferman and Kurt Schütte, independently in 1964-65, proved $\Gamma_0$ — the least $\alpha$ with $\varphi_\alpha(0) = \alpha$ — to be the exact predicative ceiling, the *Feferman-Schütte ordinal* [ref: TODO_REF Pohlers]. Heinz Bachmann's 1950 system of ordinal notations built on uncountable ordinals, refined by Gerhard Jäger and Wolfram Pohlers in the 1970s and 80s via *ordinal collapsing functions* and *local predicativity*, pushed the analysis through the Bachmann-Howard ordinal into impredicative $\Pi^1_1$-analysis. Kurt Schütte's *Proof Theory* (1960; 2e 1977) systematized the $\omega$-rule and infinitary cut-elimination, and Pohlers's *Proof Theory: The First Step into Impredicativity* (2009) became the modern reference. Kurt Gödel's 1958 *Dialectica* paper offered a complementary, non-ordinal consistency proof via primitive-recursive functionals of finite type [ref: TODO_REF Godel 1958], extended by Clifford Spector's 1962 bar-recursion to $\Pi^1_1$-analysis — reaching, by a functional route, the same strength Gentzen and Bachmann-Howard reached by ordinals.
