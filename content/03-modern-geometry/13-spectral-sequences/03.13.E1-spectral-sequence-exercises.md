## Formal definition of the pack [Intermediate]

Bott-Tu Chapter III covers spectral sequences (§14), the Leray-Serre spectral sequence and its applications (§15–§18), and characteristic classes derived through them (§19–§23). Several Bott-Tu exercises and named computations cross-cut multiple Babel Bible units in `13-spectral-sequences/`, and several others are computations the main units state without working out in full (the cohomology of $\Omega S^n$, Serre's $\pi_4(S^3) = \mathbb{Z}/2$ truncation, Borel's $H^*(BU(n))$, Eilenberg-Moore on the path-loop fibration).

This pack collects eighteen such exercises — five easy, nine medium, four hard — each with a hint and full solution. It is read alongside its three prerequisite units rather than as a standalone development. The exercises are loosely grouped: easy ones at the start drill the abstract machinery (bidegree, convergence symbol, exact-couple from a SES); medium exercises run canonical computations (Hopf fibration via Serre, Gysin on $\mathbb{C}P^k$, Borel computation of $H^*(BU(n))$, Leray-Hirsch on $\mathbb{P}(E)$, Pontryagin via splitting); the hard ones at the end are the load-bearing computations a reader is most likely to need pen and paper for ($\pi_4(S^3) = \mathbb{Z}/2$ via Serre, Eilenberg-Moore on path-loop, transgression in a specific bundle, multiplicative structure on a Kähler-style spectral sequence).

The conventions follow Bott-Tu: cohomological grading throughout; $d_r$ of bidegree $(r, 1-r)$ (notation decision #15); convergence symbol $\Rightarrow$ (decision #30); two-filtration spectral sequences ${}_I E$ and ${}_{II} E$ (decision #29); Bott-Tu sign convention $d\psi = -\pi^* e(E)$ on the global angular form. Coefficients are integer unless otherwise specified.

## Key theorem with full solution [Intermediate]

Before the pack proper, we work one canonical exercise in full as an exemplar.

**Lead exercise.** *Verify that the Leray-Serre spectral sequence of the Hopf fibration $S^1 \to S^3 \to S^2$ collapses at $E_3$, with $E_3 = E_\infty$ matching $H^*(S^3)$.*

**Solution.** With $\mathbb{Z}$ coefficients,
$$
E_2^{p, q} = H^p(S^2; H^q(S^1)) = \begin{cases} \mathbb{Z} & (p, q) \in \{(0, 0), (0, 1), (2, 0), (2, 1)\}, \\ 0 & \text{else}. \end{cases}
$$
The non-zero entries form the corners of a $2 \times 1$ rectangle. The differential $d_2 : E_2^{p, q} \to E_2^{p + 2, q - 1}$ has bidegree $(2, -1)$. The only source-target pair where both are non-zero on $E_2$ is $E_2^{0, 1} \to E_2^{2, 0}$.

By the universal Euler-class transgression formula, $d_2$ on this column is multiplication by $e(\eta) \in H^2(S^2)$, where $\eta$ is the Hopf bundle. The Euler class of $\eta$ equals the generator of $H^2(S^2) = \mathbb{Z}$ (the Hopf bundle is the universal $U(1)$-bundle over $\mathbb{C}P^1$, with $c_1 = 1$). Hence $d_2 : \mathbb{Z} \to \mathbb{Z}$ is multiplication by $\pm 1$, an isomorphism. Both source and target are killed.

Surviving entries on $E_3 = E_\infty$: $E_\infty^{0, 0} = \mathbb{Z}$ (total degree $0$), $E_\infty^{2, 1} = \mathbb{Z}$ (total degree $3$). All other entries zero. Matching $H^0(S^3) = \mathbb{Z}$, $H^3(S^3) = \mathbb{Z}$, and $H^k(S^3) = 0$ otherwise. $\square$

This is the canonical computation — the *Hello, World* of spectral sequences. The same template (read off the $E_2$ grid; identify which differentials can be non-zero by bidegree; identify the values of those differentials by transgression; compute kernel and cokernel) recovers all eighteen exercises below in some form.

## Exercises [Intermediate]

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="easy" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 1 (easy, symbolic).** *Bidegree of $d_r$.*

In the cohomological convention, write down the bidegree of the differential $d_r$ on the $E_r$ page and verify that the total degree raises by $1$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The convention is $d_r : E_r^{p, q} \to E_r^{p + r, q - r + 1}$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

$d_r$ has bidegree $(r, 1 - r)$. The total degree shifts by $r + (1 - r) = 1$, consistent with $d_r$ being a cohomological differential. The square of $d_r$ has bidegree $(2r, 2 - 2r)$, but since $d_r^2 = 0$ this is moot. Notation decision #15.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="easy" data-type="multiple-choice" markdown="1">

**Exercise 2 (easy, multiple choice).** *Convergence symbol.*

The notation $E_2^{p, q} \Rightarrow H^{p+q}(X)$ asserts:

A. There is a graded isomorphism $E_2 \cong H^*(X)$
B. $E_\infty^{p, q}$ is the associated graded $F^p H^{p+q}(X) / F^{p+1} H^{p+q}(X)$ for some filtration on $H^*(X)$
C. The total dimensions of $E_2$ and $H^*(X)$ agree
D. The base ring is $\mathbb{Z}$

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Convergence is a statement about $E_\infty$ as the associated graded of a filtration on the abutment. $E_2$ may differ from $E_\infty$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**B.** The convergence symbol $\Rightarrow$ (notation decision #30) records the abutment of $E_\infty^{p, q}$ to $\operatorname{gr}^p H^{p+q}(X)$ for a filtration on $H^*(X)$. The filtration is recoverable from the construction. The $E_2$ page is the second page of the spectral sequence; it generally differs from $E_\infty$ by the cumulative effect of all $d_r$ for $r \geq 2$.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="easy" data-type="proof" markdown="1">

**Exercise 3 (easy, proof).** *Exact couple from a SES of complexes.*

Let $0 \to A^* \xrightarrow{f} B^* \xrightarrow{g} C^* \to 0$ be a short exact sequence of cochain complexes. Construct an exact couple whose associated spectral sequence has $E_1 = H^*(C)$ and $d_1 = $ a connecting morphism.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The long exact sequence in cohomology supplies the exactness data; take $A^{p, q} = H^{p+q}(B)$ and $E^{p, q} = H^{p+q}(C)$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

The long exact sequence
$$
\cdots \to H^n(A) \to H^n(B) \to H^n(C) \xrightarrow{\delta} H^{n+1}(A) \to \cdots
$$
unrolls into an exact triangle. Take $A = \bigoplus_n H^n(B)$ as the upper vertex (with bigrading absorbed into the filtration), $E = \bigoplus_n H^n(C)$ as the lower vertex, with maps:
- $i : A \to A$ the identity (no filtration shift) — for the simplest filtration the map is identity-shift
- $j : A \to E$ induced by $g_*$
- $k : E \to A$ the connecting morphism $\delta$.

The differential $d_1 := j \circ k$ on $E$ is the composition $g_* \circ \delta : H^*(C) \to H^*(A) \to H^*(C)$ (where the last map is $f_*$ pulled back), and squares to zero. The spectral sequence iterates from $E_1 = H^*(C)$. The Bockstein construction is the avatar.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="easy" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 4 (easy, symbolic).** *Two filtrations on a double complex.*

For a first-quadrant double complex $K^{p, q}$ with anticommuting differentials $d', d''$, write the $E_2$ pages of both spectral sequences ${}_I E$ and ${}_{II} E$ in terms of iterated cohomology.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Filter by columns ($p$): $E_1$ is $d''$-cohomology, then $E_2$ is $d'$-cohomology of that.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

$$
{}_I E_2^{p, q} = H^p_{d'}(H^q_{d''}(K)), \qquad {}_{II} E_2^{p, q} = H^q_{d''}(H^p_{d'}(K)).
$$
Both abut to $H^{p+q}(\operatorname{Tot}(K))$, but the iterated cohomologies $H_{d'} H_{d''}$ and $H_{d''} H_{d'}$ are generally not equal as bigraded objects. Comparing them is the workhorse technique of the subject (notation decision #29).

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="easy" data-type="proof" markdown="1">

**Exercise 5 (easy, proof).** *Collapse from a row hypothesis.*

Let $\{E_r^{p, q}\}$ be a first-quadrant cohomological spectral sequence. Show that if $E_2^{p, q} = 0$ for $q \geq 1$, then $E_2 = E_\infty$ and $H^n \cong E_2^{n, 0}$ via the bottom-edge homomorphism.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

$d_r$ from row $q = 0$ goes to negative $q$ (outside the first quadrant); $d_r$ landing in row $q = 0$ from the rest of the support is precluded if all higher rows vanish on $E_2$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Suppose $E_2^{p, q} = 0$ for all $q \geq 1$. The differential $d_r : E_r^{p, q} \to E_r^{p+r, q-r+1}$ has source in row $q = 0$ landing in row $q = 1 - r < 0$ for $r \geq 2$, outside the first quadrant. Hence $d_r$ is zero on row $q = 0$ at every page.

A differential landing in row $q = 0$ on page $r$ has source $(p - r, r - 1)$, in row $r - 1 \geq 1$. Since $E_2$ on this row vanishes, so do all later $E_r$ on this row, so the source is already zero. No differential lands in row $q = 0$ either.

Thus $E_2^{p, 0} = E_3^{p, 0} = \cdots = E_\infty^{p, 0}$, the only non-zero entries. The filtration on $H^n$ has only one non-zero graded piece (at $p = n$), so $H^n \cong E_\infty^{n, 0} = E_2^{n, 0}$, with the isomorphism the bottom-edge homomorphism. $\square$

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 6 (medium, symbolic).** *Hopf fibration via Serre.*

Run the Leray-Serre spectral sequence on the Hopf fibration $S^3 \to S^7 \to S^4$ to compute $H^*(S^7)$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The fibre $S^3$ has cohomology in degrees $0$ and $3$. The base $S^4$ has cohomology in degrees $0$ and $4$. The bidegree of $d_r$ matters.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

$E_2^{p, q} = H^p(S^4; H^q(S^3))$ has non-zero entries at $(0, 0), (0, 3), (4, 0), (4, 3)$, all $\mathbb{Z}$. The differential $d_r$ has bidegree $(r, 1-r)$. The only source-target pair on the first quadrant where both endpoints are non-zero is $E_2^{0, 3} \to E_2^{4, 0}$ via $d_4$. The differential $d_4$ in bidegree $(4, -3)$ acts as multiplication by the Euler class of the Hopf bundle $S^3 \to S^7 \to S^4$, which is the generator of $H^4(S^4)$ (this is the quaternionic Hopf bundle, with Euler class $\pm 1$).

Hence $d_4$ is an isomorphism and kills both endpoints. Surviving entries on $E_5 = E_\infty$: $E_\infty^{0, 0} = \mathbb{Z}$ (degree $0$) and $E_\infty^{4, 3} = \mathbb{Z}$ (degree $7$). Matching $H^*(S^7) = \mathbb{Z}$ in degrees $0$ and $7$ and zero elsewhere.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 7 (medium, symbolic).** *Gysin on $\mathbb{C}P^k$.*

Apply the Gysin sequence to the unit-circle bundle $S^1 \to S^{2k+1} \to \mathbb{C}P^k$. Use the resulting sequence to derive $H^*(\mathbb{C}P^k) = \mathbb{Z}[x] / (x^{k+1})$ with $\deg x = 2$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Euler class of $\mathcal{O}(1)$ over $\mathbb{C}P^k$ is the generator $x$ of $H^2(\mathbb{C}P^k)$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

The Gysin sequence with $r = 2$, $e = x$:
$$
\cdots \to H^{p-2}(\mathbb{C}P^k) \xrightarrow{\smile x} H^p(\mathbb{C}P^k) \to H^p(S^{2k+1}) \to H^{p-1}(\mathbb{C}P^k) \xrightarrow{\smile x} H^{p+1}(\mathbb{C}P^k) \to \cdots
$$
Since $H^*(S^{2k+1}) = \mathbb{Z}$ only in degrees $0$ and $2k+1$, multiplication by $x$ is an isomorphism on $H^p(\mathbb{C}P^k) \to H^{p+2}(\mathbb{C}P^k)$ for $0 < p < 2k$, and zero in degrees adjacent to $2k+1$. Inductively starting from $H^0 = \mathbb{Z}$: $H^{2j} = \mathbb{Z}$ generated by $x^j$ for $0 \leq j \leq k$, $H^{2j+1} = 0$. The relation $x^{k+1} = 0$ comes from the Gysin sequence in the top degree.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 8 (medium, symbolic).** *Borel computation of $H^*(BU(n))$.*

Use the Serre spectral sequence of the universal fibration $U(n) \to EU(n) \to BU(n)$ (with $EU(n)$ contractible) to derive $H^*(BU(n); \mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}[c_1, \ldots, c_n]$, $\deg c_i = 2i$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

$H^*(U(n); \mathbb{Q}) = \Lambda[x_1, x_3, \ldots, x_{2n-1}]$ exterior on odd-degree generators. Each $x_{2i-1}$ transgresses to $c_i$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

$H^*(U(n); \mathbb{Q})$ is the exterior algebra on generators $x_1, x_3, \ldots, x_{2n-1}$ in odd degrees (Hopf's theorem on cohomology of compact Lie groups). The Serre SS has $E_2 = H^*(BU(n); \mathbb{Q}) \otimes \Lambda[x_1, \ldots, x_{2n-1}]$ converging to $H^*(EU(n); \mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}$ in degree $0$.

Each generator $x_{2i-1}$ must die. The only way for $x_{2i-1}$ to be killed is by being transgressed to a class in the bottom row. Hence $d_{2i}(x_{2i-1}) = c_i$ for some class $c_i \in H^{2i}(BU(n); \mathbb{Q})$. By multiplicativity, $d_{2i}$ on $x_{2i-1} \cdot \alpha$ is $c_i \alpha \pm x_{2i-1} d_{2i}(\alpha)$, allowing one to inductively see that the bottom-row classes $c_i$ generate $H^*(BU(n); \mathbb{Q})$ and the kernel is exactly the polynomial algebra on $\{c_i\}$.

After all transgressions the entire $E_\infty = \mathbb{Q}$ in degree $0$. Hence $H^*(BU(n); \mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}[c_1, \ldots, c_n]$, with $c_i$ the transgressed images of the universal odd-degree generators of $H^*(U(n); \mathbb{Q})$ — these are the Chern classes of the universal bundle $EU(n) \to BU(n)$.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 9 (medium, symbolic).** *Cohomology of $\Omega S^n$ rationally.*

Use the Serre spectral sequence of the path-loop fibration $\Omega S^n \to PS^n \to S^n$ (with $PS^n$ contractible) to compute $H^*(\Omega S^n; \mathbb{Q})$ for $n \geq 2$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Reverse engineer: the abutment is concentrated in degree $0$, so all classes outside $(0, 0)$ must be killed by some differential. The only differential with non-vanishing range is $d_n$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

$E_2^{p, q} = H^p(S^n) \otimes H^q(\Omega S^n; \mathbb{Q})$. Non-zero columns at $p = 0$ and $p = n$ only. The single non-vanishing differential is $d_n : E_n^{0, q} \to E_n^{n, q - n + 1}$.

Let $\sigma \in H^{n-1}(\Omega S^n; \mathbb{Q})$ be the transgression source: $d_n(\sigma) = \iota_n \in H^n(S^n)$ the fundamental class. By multiplicativity,
$$
d_n(\sigma^k) = k \sigma^{k-1} \cdot d_n(\sigma) = k \sigma^{k-1} \iota_n.
$$
For this to kill all higher classes, $\sigma$ must generate $H^*(\Omega S^n; \mathbb{Q})$ as either a polynomial algebra (when $\sigma^k \neq 0$ for all $k$, i.e., $n$ odd, where $\sigma$ has even degree $n - 1$) or as polynomial in $\tau$ with exterior on $\sigma$ (when $n$ even, where $\sigma$ has odd degree). Computing: for $n = 2m+1$ odd, $\sigma$ has degree $2m$ (even) and $H^*(\Omega S^{2m+1}; \mathbb{Q}) = \mathbb{Q}[\sigma]$, polynomial. For $n = 2m$ even, $\sigma$ has degree $2m - 1$ (odd) and $\sigma^2 = 0$ rationally, so we need an additional even-degree generator $\tau = \sigma \cdot d_n(\sigma)$ of degree $4m - 2$, giving $H^*(\Omega S^{2m}; \mathbb{Q}) = \Lambda[\sigma_{2m-1}] \otimes \mathbb{Q}[\tau_{4m-2}]$.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 10 (medium, symbolic).** *Leray-Hirsch on the projectivisation.*

For a complex rank-$2$ bundle $E \to B$ with $H^*(B)$ concentrated in even degree, write $H^*(\mathbb{P}(E))$ as a quotient of $H^*(B)[x]$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Apply Leray-Hirsch with the basis $\{1, x\}$ where $x = c_1(L^*)$ is the Chern class of the dual tautological line.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

By Leray-Hirsch, $H^*(\mathbb{P}(E)) = H^*(B) \oplus H^*(B) \cdot x$ as a free module of rank $2$. The relation defining the cohomology ring is the Grothendieck relation:
$$
x^2 + c_1(E) x + c_2(E) = 0,
$$
hence $H^*(\mathbb{P}(E)) = H^*(B)[x] / (x^2 + c_1(E) x + c_2(E))$. Concretely, expanding via the splitting principle, $x^2 = -c_1(E) x - c_2(E)$.

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 11 (medium, symbolic).** *Splitting principle for Pontryagin classes.*

For a real vector bundle $E_{\mathbb{R}}$ of rank $2k$ with Chern roots $\pm \xi_1, \ldots, \pm \xi_k$ on the complexification, write the total Pontryagin class $p(E_{\mathbb{R}})$ in terms of $\xi_i$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

$p_i(E_{\mathbb{R}}) = (-1)^i c_{2i}(E_{\mathbb{R}} \otimes \mathbb{C})$ and the complexification has paired roots $\pm \xi_i$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

The complexification $E_{\mathbb{R}} \otimes \mathbb{C}$ has total Chern class
$$
c(E_{\mathbb{R}} \otimes \mathbb{C}) = \prod_{i=1}^k (1 + \xi_i)(1 - \xi_i) = \prod_{i=1}^k (1 - \xi_i^2).
$$
Expanding: $c_{2i}(E_{\mathbb{R}} \otimes \mathbb{C}) = (-1)^i e_i(\xi_1^2, \ldots, \xi_k^2)$, hence
$$
p_i(E_{\mathbb{R}}) = e_i(\xi_1^2, \ldots, \xi_k^2), \qquad p(E_{\mathbb{R}}) = \prod_{i=1}^k (1 + \xi_i^2).
$$
This is the splitting-principle formula for Pontryagin classes (decision: pair-symmetric in the squared roots).

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 12 (medium, symbolic).** *$H^*(\mathrm{Gr}(2, 4))$ via Borel presentation.*

Compute $H^*(\mathrm{Gr}(2, 4); \mathbb{Z})$ using the Whitney relation between the tautological subbundle $S$ and quotient $Q$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

$S \oplus Q = \mathbb{C}^4$ is the standard frame bundle, so $c(S) c(Q) = 1$. Both have rank $2$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Let $a, b$ be Chern classes of $S^*$ (dual subbundle), $a', b'$ of $Q^*$. Whitney: $(1 + a + b)(1 + a' + b') = 1$ modulo terms of degree $> 4$. Expanding:
- degree $2$: $a + a' = 0$, so $a' = -a$
- degree $4$: $b + a a' + b' = 0$, so $b' = -b + a^2$
- degree $6$: $a b' + a' b = 0$, gives $a^3 = 2 a b$
- degree $8$: $b b' = 0$, gives $b^2 = a^2 b$.

Hence $H^*(\mathrm{Gr}(2, 4); \mathbb{Z}) = \mathbb{Z}[a, b] / (a^3 - 2 a b, b^2 - a^2 b)$, Poincaré series $1 + t^2 + 2t^4 + t^6 + t^8$, total dimension $6$ (matching the count of Schubert cells: $\sigma_\emptyset, \sigma_1, \sigma_2, \sigma_{1,1}, \sigma_{2,1}, \sigma_{2,2}$).

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="symbolic" markdown="1">

**Exercise 13 (medium, symbolic).** *Chern character of $T\mathbb{C}P^n$.*

Use the splitting principle and the Euler exact sequence to compute $\mathrm{ch}(T\mathbb{C}P^n) \in H^*(\mathbb{C}P^n; \mathbb{Q})$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

$T\mathbb{C}P^n \oplus \mathcal{O} = \mathcal{O}(1)^{\oplus(n+1)}$, so $\mathrm{ch}(T\mathbb{C}P^n) + 1 = (n+1) \mathrm{ch}(\mathcal{O}(1))$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

The Euler sequence gives $T\mathbb{C}P^n \oplus \mathcal{O} = \mathcal{O}(1)^{\oplus(n+1)}$. Chern character is additive on direct sums and multiplicative on tensor products. Hence
$$
\mathrm{ch}(T\mathbb{C}P^n) = (n+1) \mathrm{ch}(\mathcal{O}(1)) - 1 = (n+1) e^x - 1,
$$
where $x \in H^2(\mathbb{C}P^n)$ is the Kähler class. Expanding: $\mathrm{ch}(T\mathbb{C}P^n) = (n+1)(1 + x + x^2/2 + \cdots) - 1 = n + (n+1) x + (n+1) x^2 / 2 + (n+1) x^3 / 6 + \cdots$, truncated at degree $2n$ (above which $x^k = 0$).

</details>

</aside>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="proof" markdown="1">

**Exercise 14 (medium, proof).** *Whitney via splitting on flag bundle.*

Use the splitting principle to prove the Whitney sum formula for Pontryagin classes: $p(E \oplus F) = p(E) p(F)$ modulo $2$-torsion.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Pull both bundles back to a flag bundle where each splits into lines, then use the Chern-root formula.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Pull back to $F\ell(E_{\mathbb{R}} \oplus F_{\mathbb{R}})$, where the bundle splits as a sum of real line bundles, and the complexification splits with paired roots $\pm \xi_i$ (from $E$) and $\pm \eta_j$ (from $F$). The total Pontryagin class on the flag bundle:
$$
\sigma^* p(E_{\mathbb{R}} \oplus F_{\mathbb{R}}) = \prod_i (1 + \xi_i^2) \cdot \prod_j (1 + \eta_j^2) = \sigma^* p(E_{\mathbb{R}}) \cdot \sigma^* p(F_{\mathbb{R}}).
$$
Since $\sigma^* : H^*(B) \to H^*(F\ell)$ is injective in characteristic $\neq 2$ (where the rank-$2$ flag bundle for an oriented bundle pair has Euler class $2 \xi$ — non-zero divisor away from $2$-torsion), the formula descends to $B$. The mod-$2$ caveat is a standard subtlety handled by passing to mod-$2$ characteristic classes (Stiefel-Whitney) on the real side. $\square$

</details>

</aside>

**Exercise 15 (hard, symbolic).** *$\pi_4(S^3) = \mathbb{Z}/2$ via Postnikov truncation.*

Compute $\pi_4(S^3)$ using the Postnikov truncation $K(\mathbb{Z}, 3) \to S^3 \langle 3 \rangle \to S^3$ and the Serre spectral sequence on the relevant fibration.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Replace $S^3$ with its Postnikov tower; $\pi_4(S^3) = \pi_4(S^3 \langle 3 \rangle)$ since $\pi_3$ is killed in the truncation. Compute $H^*(S^3 \langle 3 \rangle)$ using Serre on the path-loop fibration above $K(\mathbb{Z}, 3)$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

The Postnikov $3$-truncation $S^3 \to K(\mathbb{Z}, 3)$ has homotopy fibre $S^3 \langle 3 \rangle$, the $3$-connected cover of $S^3$. The fibration
$$
S^3 \langle 3 \rangle \to S^3 \to K(\mathbb{Z}, 3)
$$
has $\pi_4(S^3 \langle 3 \rangle) = \pi_4(S^3)$ (since $K(\mathbb{Z}, 3)$ has homotopy concentrated in degree $3$).

By Hurewicz on $S^3 \langle 3 \rangle$ (which is $3$-connected), $\pi_4(S^3 \langle 3 \rangle) = H_4(S^3 \langle 3 \rangle)$. Compute the latter using the Serre SS of the path-loop $\Omega K(\mathbb{Z}, 3) \to PK(\mathbb{Z}, 3) \to K(\mathbb{Z}, 3)$ in the relevant Whitehead-tower extension.

The cohomology of $K(\mathbb{Z}, 3)$ in low degrees: $H^3 = \mathbb{Z}$ (fundamental class $\iota_3$), $H^6 = \mathbb{Z}$ generated by $\iota_3^2$ (cup square; $K(\mathbb{Z}, 3)$ has divided power algebra structure rationally and a $\mathrm{Sq}^2$-connection mod $2$). The Serre SS of the path-loop fibration with fibre $\Omega K(\mathbb{Z}, 3) = K(\mathbb{Z}, 2)$ shows that the transgression $\tau(x) = \iota_3$ for $x$ the degree-$2$ generator of $K(\mathbb{Z}, 2)$, and $\tau(x^2) = 2 x \iota_3$ — so divided powers control the integer cohomology.

The mod-$2$ analysis: $\mathrm{Sq}^2 \iota_3 = $ a non-zero class in $H^5(K(\mathbb{Z}, 3); \mathbb{Z}/2)$, which detects the obstruction in the Whitehead extension. Working through Serre's argument (Bott-Tu §18 outline; full in Serre 1951 thesis), the result is $\pi_4(S^3) = \mathbb{Z}/2$, generated by the Hopf map $\eta : S^4 \to S^3$ (suspension of $S^3 \to S^2$).

This is one of the most consequential single computations in Serre's PhD thesis. The full argument requires the Steenrod algebra structure on $H^*(K(\mathbb{Z}, 3); \mathbb{Z}/2)$ via $\mathrm{Sq}^2 \iota_3$. $\square$

</details>

</aside>

**Exercise 16 (hard, symbolic).** *Eilenberg-Moore on the path-loop fibration.*

Use the Eilenberg-Moore spectral sequence to compute $H^*(\Omega S^n; \mathbb{Z})$ in a single page.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Eilenberg-Moore SS of the pullback $\Omega Y \to PY \to Y$ has $E_2 = \operatorname{Tor}_{H^*(Y)}^{*, *}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z})$. For $Y = S^n$, $H^*(S^n) = \mathbb{Z}[\iota_n]/\iota_n^2$, and $\operatorname{Tor}_{\mathbb{Z}[\iota]/\iota^2}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z})$ is computable.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

For $Y = S^n$, $H^*(Y) = \mathbb{Z}[\iota_n] / (\iota_n^2)$ — exterior on a single class of degree $n$. The Eilenberg-Moore SS of $\Omega S^n \to PS^n \to S^n$ has
$$
E_2 = \operatorname{Tor}_{H^*(S^n)}^{*, *}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}) = \operatorname{Tor}_{\mathbb{Z}[\iota_n] / (\iota_n^2)}(\mathbb{Z}, \mathbb{Z}).
$$
For $n$ odd: $\mathbb{Z}[\iota_n] / (\iota_n^2)$ is exterior on an odd-degree generator. The Koszul resolution of $\mathbb{Z}$ over this exterior algebra is the polynomial algebra $\mathbb{Z}[\sigma]$ on a class $\sigma$ of degree $n - 1$, so $\operatorname{Tor} = \mathbb{Z}[\sigma]$. The spectral sequence collapses and $H^*(\Omega S^n) = \mathbb{Z}[\sigma]$, polynomial on $\sigma$ of degree $n - 1$.

For $n$ even: the Koszul resolution of $\mathbb{Z}$ over $\mathbb{Z}[\iota_n] / (\iota_n^2)$ with $\iota_n$ in even degree gives $\operatorname{Tor} = \Lambda[\sigma_{n-1}] \otimes \mathbb{Z}[\tau_{2n-2}]$, exterior on an odd-degree class of degree $n - 1$ tensored with polynomial on an even-degree class of degree $2n - 2$. Hence $H^*(\Omega S^{2k}) = \Lambda[\sigma_{2k-1}] \otimes \mathbb{Z}[\tau_{4k-2}]$.

The Eilenberg-Moore SS collapses at $E_2$ for $Y = S^n$ because the $\operatorname{Tor}$ is concentrated in a single bigrading per total degree. The Serre SS computation of Exercise 9 gives the same answer with more pages of work.

</details>

</aside>

**Exercise 17 (hard, proof).** *Multiplicative structure on a Kähler-style spectral sequence.*

Let $K^*$ be a differential graded algebra with a multiplicative bounded filtration $F^*$ (i.e., $F^p \cdot F^q \subset F^{p+q}$). Show that each page $E_r$ of the associated spectral sequence is a bigraded ring, with $d_r$ a derivation, and $E_\infty$ matches the associated graded of the cup product on $H^*(K^*)$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Define the product on $E_r^{p, q} \otimes E_r^{p', q'} \to E_r^{p + p', q + q'}$ via cocycle representatives in $Z_r^{p, q} \cdot Z_r^{p', q'} \subset Z_r^{p+p', q+q'}$, then show $d_r$ is a derivation by Leibniz on $K^*$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Take cocycle representatives $x \in Z_r^{p, q}$ (i.e., $x \in F^p K^{p+q}$ with $dx \in F^{p+r} K^{p+q+1}$) and $y \in Z_r^{p', q'}$. By multiplicativity of the filtration, $xy \in F^{p+p'} K^{p+p'+q+q'}$, and by Leibniz on $K$:
$$
d(xy) = (dx) y + (-1)^{|x|} x (dy) \in F^{p+p'+r} K + F^{p+p'+r} K = F^{p+p'+r} K.
$$
Hence $xy \in Z_r^{p+p', q+q'}$. The class $[xy] \in E_r^{p+p', q+q'}$ is well-defined modulo $Z_{r-1}^{p+p'+1, q+q'-1} + B_{r-1}^{p+p', q+q'}$ (changing $x$ within its class by an element of $Z_{r-1}^{p+1, q-1} + B_{r-1}^{p, q}$ changes $xy$ by an element of the indicated submodule).

The differential $d_r$ on $E_r$ acts by $d_r [x] = [dx] \in E_r^{p+r, q-r+1}$, and the Leibniz rule on $K^*$ gives
$$
d_r ([x][y]) = [d(xy)] = [(dx) y + (-1)^{|x|} x (dy)] = (d_r [x])[y] + (-1)^{|x|} [x] (d_r [y]).
$$
So $d_r$ is a graded derivation. The page-advance to $E_{r+1}$ inherits this product. In the limit, $E_\infty = \operatorname{gr} H^*(K^*)$ as a bigraded ring, with the product matching the associated graded of the cup product on the abutment.

This is the structural reason for *multiplicative collapse* arguments: if the bottom-row classes (where $d_r$ acts by derivation) generate the entire $E_2$, no $d_r$ can be non-zero by Leibniz, hence collapse. $\square$

</details>

</aside>

**Exercise 18 (hard, symbolic).** *Transgression in a Stiefel manifold fibration.*

Compute the transgression for the fibration $V_{n, k}(\mathbb{C}) \to BU(n - k) \to BU(n)$ where $V_{n, k}(\mathbb{C}) = U(n) / U(n - k)$ is the complex Stiefel manifold of $k$-frames in $\mathbb{C}^n$. Express the result in terms of Chern classes.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The fibration is the universal $V_{n, k}$-bundle. The cohomology of $V_{n, k}(\mathbb{C})$ is exterior on classes $x_{2(n-k)+1}, \ldots, x_{2n-1}$ (Hopf's theorem applied to Lie groups). Transgression sends $x_{2j-1}$ to $c_j$ of the universal rank-$n$ bundle.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

$V_{n, k}(\mathbb{C}) = U(n) / U(n - k)$ has cohomology
$$
H^*(V_{n, k}(\mathbb{C}); \mathbb{Q}) = \Lambda[x_{2(n - k) + 1}, x_{2(n - k) + 3}, \ldots, x_{2n - 1}],
$$
exterior algebra on $k$ generators in odd degrees. The Serre SS of $V_{n, k} \to BU(n - k) \to BU(n)$ has $E_2 = H^*(BU(n)) \otimes H^*(V_{n, k})$, converging to $H^*(BU(n - k)) = \mathbb{Q}[c_1, \ldots, c_{n-k}]$.

The transgression of $x_{2j-1}$ (for $n - k < j \leq n$) lands in degree $2j$ on the base, where $H^{2j}(BU(n)) = $ degree-$2j$ part of $\mathbb{Q}[c_1, \ldots, c_n]$. By naturality with the universal classifying-space fibration $U(n) \to EU(n) \to BU(n)$ where each odd-degree generator transgresses to the corresponding Chern class:
$$
\tau(x_{2j - 1}) = c_j(\text{universal bundle})
$$
restricted to the appropriate quotient. Specifically the kernel of $H^*(BU(n)) \to H^*(BU(n - k))$ is generated by $c_{n-k+1}, \ldots, c_n$, exactly the Chern classes hit by the transgression of the new generators of $V_{n, k}$.

This computation is the structural reason classifying-space cohomology behaves well under quotients: the Serre SS of $G/H \to BH \to BG$ shows $H^*(G/H)$ as the *cokernel* of the natural map $H^*(BG) \to H^*(BH)$ in nice cases, with transgression supplying the explicit generators. It underlies the construction of the *characteristic classes of vector bundles with structure-group reduction*.

</details>

</aside>
