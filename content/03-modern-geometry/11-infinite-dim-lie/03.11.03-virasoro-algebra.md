## Intuition [Beginner]

Two-dimensional conformal symmetry has infinitely many local modes. The Witt algebra is the classical algebra of those modes. The Virasoro algebra is what appears after the quantum theory adds one central correction.

The correction is controlled by a number called the central charge. It commutes with every symmetry mode, but it changes the representation theory and the formulas for how fields transform.

CFT basics [03.10.02] uses the Virasoro algebra as the symmetry algebra of the stress tensor. This unit isolates the algebra itself.

## Visual [Beginner]

The Witt modes form an infinite row of symmetry generators. The Virasoro algebra adds one central direction that records the quantum correction.

![Witt modes with one added central direction forming the Virasoro algebra.](/img/virasoro-algebra-placeholder.svg)

The central direction does not move along the row. It labels the whole representation.

## Worked example [Beginner]

Imagine symmetry modes labeled by all integers:

$$
\ldots,L_{-2},L_{-1},L_0,L_1,L_2,\ldots
$$

The label tells which mode is acting. When two modes are compared, their bracket is mostly another mode. In the Virasoro algebra, some comparisons also produce a central term.

For example, the bracket of $L_2$ and $L_{-2}$ contains a multiple of $L_0$ and may also contain the central element. That extra part is the central charge contribution in a representation.

What this tells us: Virasoro is the Witt symmetry algebra with one central correction added.

## Formal definition [Intermediate+]

The **Witt algebra** has basis $\{\ell_n\}_{n\in\mathbb Z}$ and bracket

$$
[\ell_m,\ell_n]=(m-n)\ell_{m+n}.
$$

It is realized as the Lie algebra of Laurent polynomial vector fields on the punctured complex line by $\ell_n=-z^{n+1}\partial_z$ [ref: TODO_REF Kac §1.3].

The **Virasoro algebra** is the one-dimensional central extension with basis $\{L_n\}_{n\in\mathbb Z}$ and central element $C$, with bracket

$$
[L_m,L_n]=(m-n)L_{m+n}+\frac{m^3-m}{12}\delta_{m+n,0}C,\qquad [C,L_n]=0.
$$

In a representation where $C$ acts as $cI$, the scalar $c$ is the central charge [03.11.02].

## Key theorem with proof [Intermediate+]

**Theorem (The Virasoro bracket is a central extension of Witt).** The projection $L_n\mapsto \ell_n$ and $C\mapsto0$ defines a Lie algebra homomorphism from the Virasoro algebra to the Witt algebra whose kernel is the central line $KC$.

**Proof.** The projection is linear and sends

$$
[L_m,L_n]
$$

to $(m-n)\ell_{m+n}$ because the central term maps to zero. This is exactly $[\ell_m,\ell_n]$ in the Witt algebra. Also $[C,L_n]=0$ maps to zero.

The kernel is spanned by $C$: an element $aC+\sum b_nL_n$ maps to $\sum b_n\ell_n$, so it maps to zero precisely when all $b_n$ vanish. The kernel lies in the center because $C$ commutes with every generator. Thus the Virasoro algebra is a central extension of the Witt algebra. $\square$

## Advanced results [Master]

The central term in the Virasoro bracket is represented by the Gelfand-Fuchs 2-cocycle on the Lie algebra of vector fields on the circle. Algebraically, this cocycle spans the relevant one-dimensional central-extension class for the Witt algebra [ref: TODO_REF Kac §1.3].

Highest-weight modules are generated from a vector $v$ satisfying $L_0v=hv$, $Cv=cv$, and $L_nv=0$ for $n>0$. The descendants are obtained by applying negative modes. These modules supply the representation-theoretic language used in two-dimensional CFT [03.10.02].

**Synthesis.** The Virasoro algebra is the universal central extension of the Witt algebra of holomorphic vector fields on the circle: the Gelfand-Fuchs 2-cocycle $\omega(\ell_m, \ell_n) = \frac{m^3-m}{12}\delta_{m+n,0}$ generates $H^2(\mathfrak{W}; \mathbb{C})$, the central charge $c$ labels irreducible representations via Schur's lemma, highest-weight modules are built from a vacuum state annihilated by positive modes and graded by the energy operator $L_0$, and the descendant spaces produce the Verma modules whose structure encodes the operator algebra of two-dimensional conformal field theory [03.10.02]. The Virasoro bracket $[L_m, L_n] = (m-n)L_{m+n} + \frac{c}{12}(m^3-m)\delta_{m+n,0}C$ is the algebraic structure underlying the stress-tensor modes in CFT, connecting infinite-dimensional Lie theory to statistical mechanics, string theory, and the classification of two-dimensional critical phenomena.

## Full proof set [Master]

The theorem above proves that the displayed Virasoro bracket projects to the Witt bracket with central kernel. The Jacobi identity is equivalent to the 2-cocycle identity for

$$
\omega(\ell_m,\ell_n)=\frac{m^3-m}{12}\delta_{m+n,0}.
$$

This is the Gelfand-Fuchs cocycle in the Laurent basis, and its cocycle identity is the standard computation in the cohomology of the Witt algebra [ref: TODO_REF Kac §1.3]. The central-extension framework is exactly the one developed in [03.11.01].

For a representation with $C=cI$, Schur-type scalar central action is the mechanism described in [03.11.02]. The Virasoro bracket then acts on the representation by replacing the central generator with the scalar $c$.

## Connections [Master]

- Virasoro algebra depends on central extensions [03.11.01] and infinite-dimensional Lie algebra representations [03.11.02]. It refines the CFT symmetry discussion in [03.10.02], where the stress tensor has modes $L_n$ satisfying the Virasoro relations.

- The central-charge parameter also connects to anomalies in quantum field theory and to index-theoretic anomaly calculations, which later meet the Atiyah-Singer theorem [03.09.10].

## Historical & philosophical context [Master]

The Virasoro algebra was introduced in the study of dual resonance models and became central in two-dimensional conformal field theory through the representation theory used by Belavin, Polyakov, and Zamolodchikov [ref: TODO_REF Di Francesco-Mathieu-Sénéchal §6].

Kac presents the Virasoro algebra as a basic infinite-dimensional Lie algebra and central extension of the Witt algebra [ref: TODO_REF Kac §1.3]. Pressley and Segal connect it to loop groups, projective representations, and positive-energy representation theory [ref: TODO_REF Pressley-Segal §13].
