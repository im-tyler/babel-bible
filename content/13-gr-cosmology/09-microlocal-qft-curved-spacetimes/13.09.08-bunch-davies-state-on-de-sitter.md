## Intuition [Beginner]

A quantum field on a curved background needs a vacuum — a reference state against which excitations are counted as particles. On flat Minkowski spacetime the vacuum is unique because the Lorentz group picks it out: it is the only state invariant under boosts and translations. On a generic curved spacetime no such symmetry exists, the vacuum splits into a class of physically admissible states (the Hadamard states of [13.09.03]), and no single one is canonical.

De Sitter spacetime is the exception that recovers a canonical vacuum. It is the maximally symmetric Lorentzian spacetime with positive curvature — the cosmological analog of the round sphere — and its isometry group is the de Sitter group, ten-dimensional and as large as the Poincaré group of Minkowski space. The symmetry singles out the **Bunch-Davies state** as the unique vacuum that respects the full de Sitter symmetry and is also Hadamard.

The Bunch-Davies state was written down by Timothy Bunch and Paul Davies in 1978 by an explicit mode-sum calculation in cosmological coordinates. The resulting two-point function depends only on the de-Sitter-invariant geodesic distance between two events and has a closed-form expression in terms of the Gauss hypergeometric function. For a massless conformally coupled scalar field the two-point function reduces, via the conformal transformation between de Sitter and the Einstein static universe, to the flat-space Wightman function — a sanity check on the construction.

The state has a surprising thermal property discovered by Gary Gibbons and Stephen Hawking in 1977. A static observer in a single de Sitter patch — an observer who follows a worldline of one of the timelike Killing vectors of the de Sitter geometry — perceives the Bunch-Davies vacuum as a thermal bath of particles at the Gibbons-Hawking temperature $T = H / (2\pi k_B)$, where $H$ is the de Sitter Hubble rate. This is the analog of the Hawking temperature for a black hole: an observer who lacks access to the full spacetime (here cut off by the cosmological horizon) sees the global vacuum as a mixed thermal state.

The state matters for cosmology. Inflationary models posit a brief de-Sitter-like phase in the very early universe, during which the inflaton field is in a near-Bunch-Davies vacuum. The quantum fluctuations of the field in this state seed the temperature anisotropies of the cosmic microwave background and, after gravitational collapse, the galaxy distribution observed today. The Bunch-Davies prediction of a scale-invariant power spectrum agrees with the Planck satellite measurements up to slow-roll corrections.

The Bunch-Davies state is the worked example of every general theorem in the Hadamard programme. It exists by the symmetry-respecting refinement of the FNW existence argument from [13.09.04]; it is unique by the Allen-Mottola 1985 classification combined with the Hadamard wave-front-set criterion from [13.09.03]; its thermal property is the simplest case of the modular-theoretic identification of cosmological with thermal time. Every later development in the subject — the rigorous Hawking effect on Schwarzschild, the cosmological-perturbation power spectrum, the holographic dictionary in de Sitter quantum gravity — uses the Bunch-Davies state as its starting reference.

Three pieces drive the picture. The hyperboloid is de Sitter $dS_4$, drawn as the embedding $-X_0^2 + X_1^2 + X_2^2 + X_3^2 + X_4^2 = H^{-2}$ in five-dimensional Minkowski space — the simplest concrete realisation of a Lorentzian manifold with positive constant curvature. The wedge is the static patch: the region of $dS_4$ visible to one timelike-geodesic observer, bounded above and below by the observer's past and future cosmological horizons. The flat-slicing coordinates $ds^2 = -dt^2 + e^{2Ht}(dx^2 + dy^2 + dz^2)$ cover only half of de Sitter — the half causally accessible to an observer at spatial infinity — and supply the natural coordinates for the Bunch-Davies mode functions.

The Bunch-Davies vacuum is the global state of the quantised Klein-Gordon field built from the conformal-time positive-frequency mode functions $u_k$. For an inertial static-patch observer the state looks like a thermal bath at the Gibbons-Hawking temperature — the cosmological analog of the Unruh effect for a uniformly accelerated observer in Minkowski space. The same global vacuum can therefore appear pure (to a globally informed observer) or thermal (to a static-patch observer who lacks access to the full spacetime), with the partial information being what produces the entropy.

## Worked example [Beginner]

Compute the Bunch-Davies two-point function for a massless conformally coupled scalar field on de Sitter, and verify that it reduces to the flat-space Wightman function via the conformal transformation between de Sitter and the Einstein static universe.

Step 1. Choose conformal coupling $\xi = 1/6$ and mass $m = 0$. The Klein-Gordon equation on a generic background with these parameters is $(\Box_g + R/6)\phi = 0$, and it has the special property of being **conformally invariant** in four spacetime dimensions: if $\phi$ solves the equation on $(M, g)$ and $\tilde{g} = \Omega^2 g$ is a conformally rescaled metric, then $\tilde{\phi} = \Omega^{-1}\phi$ solves the corresponding equation on $(M, \tilde{g})$.

Step 2. De Sitter and the Einstein static universe are conformally related. The Einstein static universe is the product of a time line and a three-sphere of fixed radius, $ds_{\mathrm{ESU}}^2 = -dT^2 + d\Omega_{S^3}^2$. By an explicit coordinate change the metric of de Sitter $dS_4$ can be written as $ds_{dS}^2 = (\cos T)^{-2}\,ds_{\mathrm{ESU}}^2$ on a strip $T \in (-\pi/2, \pi/2)$ of the Einstein static universe. The conformal factor is $\Omega = (\cos T)^{-1}$.

Step 3. On the Einstein static universe, the conformally coupled Klein-Gordon equation reduces to a free wave equation on the three-sphere times the time line. The positive-frequency modes are spherical harmonics on $S^3$ times oscillating exponentials in $T$. The vacuum state — the state in which every positive-frequency mode is unoccupied — has a Wightman two-point function that depends only on the geodesic distance between two events on the Einstein static universe.

Step 4. Transport the Einstein-static-universe vacuum to de Sitter via the conformal factor. Define the de Sitter field as $\phi_{dS} = \Omega^{-1}\phi_{\mathrm{ESU}} = \cos T \cdot \phi_{\mathrm{ESU}}$, and define the de Sitter two-point function as the corresponding pull-back of the Einstein-static-universe two-point function. The result is the **Bunch-Davies state** in the conformally coupled massless case, and its two-point function depends only on the de-Sitter-invariant geodesic-distance variable $Z(x, x')$.

Step 5. Read off the explicit form. The two-point function in the conformally coupled massless case is
$$
W_{\mathrm{BD}}^{\mathrm{conf}}(x, x') = \frac{H^2}{16\pi^2}\cdot\frac{1}{1 - Z(x, x') + i\epsilon},
$$
where $Z(x, x')$ is the de Sitter geodesic-distance invariant and the $i\epsilon$ prescription selects the positive-frequency boundary condition. The leading short-distance behaviour as $Z \to 1$ (coincident points) is $1/(1 - Z) \approx 2/(H^2 \sigma)$ where $\sigma$ is Synge's world function — recovering the standard $1/\sigma$ Hadamard singularity structure.

Step 6. Sanity check against Minkowski. Take $H \to 0$ holding the geodesic distance fixed. The de Sitter geodesic-distance invariant reduces to $1 - H^2 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2}(x - x')^2/2 + \mathcal{O}(H^4)$, so $1 - Z \approx H^2 (x - x')^2/2$, and the two-point function reduces to
$$
W_{\mathrm{BD}}^{\mathrm{conf}}(x, x') \to \frac{1}{8\pi^2}\cdot\frac{1}{(x - x')^2 + i\epsilon},
$$
the standard Minkowski Wightman function of the massless conformally coupled scalar. The Bunch-Davies state passes the flat-space limit.

What this tells us: in the simplest case — massless conformal coupling — the Bunch-Davies state is the unique de-Sitter-invariant vacuum obtained by pulling back the Einstein-static-universe vacuum via the conformal transformation. The two-point function has a closed-form expression in the de-Sitter-invariant variable, satisfies the Hadamard short-distance condition, and reduces correctly to the Minkowski Wightman function in the flat-space limit. The massive case ($m \neq 0$ or $\xi \neq 1/6$) gives a more elaborate two-point function involving the Gauss hypergeometric function, treated in the Intermediate tier.

The Bunch-Davies state on de Sitter spacetime is

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Recall that on a generic curved spacetime no state is canonical; on de Sitter the symmetry recovers a canonical choice.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**A. The unique Hadamard quasi-free state of the Klein-Gordon field that is invariant under the full de Sitter group $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$.**

The de Sitter isometry group $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$ is ten-dimensional and as large as the Poincaré group of Minkowski. Allen 1985 and Mottola 1985 classified the de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free states (the $\alpha$-vacua family) and showed they form a one-parameter family. The Bunch-Davies state corresponds to $\alpha = 0$, and is the unique member of the family whose two-point function satisfies the Hadamard wave-front-set condition of the Radzikowski criterion from [13.09.03]. The other $\alpha$-vacua have wave-front-set contributions on both halves of the bicharacteristic relation and fail to be Hadamard.

Option B confuses Bunch-Davies with the vanishing-stress-energy condition; in fact the renormalised stress-energy tensor in the Bunch-Davies state is non-zero and contributes to the trace anomaly of the conformally coupled scalar. Option C is half-right — a *static-patch* observer sees a thermal bath at the Gibbons-Hawking temperature, but a global observer sees the state as a pure vacuum. Option D contradicts the Gibbons-Hawking result: a static-patch observer does see particles.

</details>

**Exercise (easy, true-false).**

True or false: the Gibbons-Hawking 1977 result says that a static observer in a single de Sitter static patch perceives the Bunch-Davies vacuum as a thermal bath at temperature $T = H/(2\pi k_B)$, where $H$ is the de Sitter Hubble rate.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Gibbons-Hawking temperature is the de Sitter analog of the Hawking temperature; the relevant Killing vector is the static-patch boost.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**True.**

Gibbons and Hawking 1977 *Phys. Rev. D* 15 2738 derived the result by analytically continuing the Bunch-Davies two-point function to the Euclidean section of de Sitter. The Euclidean section is a four-sphere $S^4$ of radius $1/H$, on which the imaginary-time coordinate is periodic with period $2\pi/H$. By the standard KMS argument, periodicity in imaginary time corresponds to a thermal state at the inverse temperature $\beta = 2\pi/H$, i.e. temperature $T = H/(2\pi k_B)$.

The state appears thermal to a static-patch observer because the observer's worldline is an orbit of one of the timelike Killing vectors of de Sitter, and the boost subgroup generated by this Killing vector is the modular automorphism group of the von Neumann algebra of static-patch observables — the de Sitter analog of the Bisognano-Wichmann theorem for Rindler wedges in Minkowski.

</details>

</aside>

**Exercise (medium, multiple choice).**

In the flat slicing of de Sitter spacetime with conformal time $\eta = -e^{-Ht}/H$, the positive-frequency mode function of a massless minimally coupled scalar at spatial wavenumber $k$ is

A. $u_k(\eta) = (1/\sqrt{2k})\,e^{-ik\eta}$
B. $u_k(\eta) = (1/\sqrt{2k})\,(1 + i/(k\eta))\,e^{-ik\eta}$
C. $u_k(\eta) = (1/\sqrt{2k})\,(1 - i/(k\eta))\,e^{+ik\eta}$
D. $u_k(\eta) = (1/\sqrt{2k\eta})\,e^{-ik\eta}$

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The mode satisfies a Bessel-type equation in conformal time; the asymptotic behaviour for $|k\eta| \to \infty$ (sub-horizon, early times) should match the flat-space positive-frequency mode $e^{-ik\eta}/\sqrt{2k}$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**B. $u_k(\eta) = (1/\sqrt{2k})\,(1 + i/(k\eta))\,e^{-ik\eta}$.**

The Klein-Gordon equation $(\Box + m^2)\phi = 0$ on the flat slicing $ds^2 = a(\eta)^2(-d\eta^2 + d\mathbf{x}^2)$ with $a(\eta) = -1/(H\eta)$ reduces, for the rescaled field $\chi = a\phi$ and spatial Fourier mode $\chi_k$, to the equation $\chi_k'' + (k^2 - a''/a)\chi_k = 0$ where the prime denotes derivative with respect to $\eta$. For the massless minimally coupled case $a''/a = 2/\eta^2$, giving $\chi_k'' + (k^2 - 2/\eta^2)\chi_k = 0$, whose Bunch-Davies positive-frequency solution is $\chi_k(\eta) = (1/\sqrt{2k})\,(1 + i/(k\eta))\,e^{-ik\eta}$.

Option A is the flat-space positive-frequency mode, the sub-horizon limit ($|k\eta| \gg 1$) of option B but not the full de Sitter mode. Option C has the wrong sign of frequency (negative-frequency mode, the time-reverse of Bunch-Davies). Option D has the wrong normalisation factor. The full mode in option B reduces to the flat-space mode at sub-horizon scales and grows logarithmically at super-horizon scales — this growth is the mechanism behind inflationary perturbation amplification.

</details>

## Formal definition [Intermediate+]

Throughout this section $(M, g)$ denotes de Sitter spacetime $dS_4$, the maximally symmetric four-dimensional Lorentzian manifold of constant positive sectional curvature $H^2 > 0$. Concretely, $dS_4$ is realised as the embedded hyperboloid $\{X \in \mathbb{R}^{1, 4} : \eta_{AB}X^A X^B = H^{-2}\}$ in five-dimensional Minkowski space with metric $\eta_{AB} = \mathrm{diag}(-1, +1, +1, +1, +1)$, equipped with the induced metric. The Ricci tensor is $R_{\mu\nu} = 3 H^2 g_{\mu\nu}$ and the Ricci scalar is $R = 12 H^2$. The isometry group is $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$, ten-dimensional, acting transitively on $dS_4$ via the restriction of the linear $\mathrm{O}(4, 1)$-action on $\mathbb{R}^{1, 4}$.

The **flat slicing** of de Sitter covers the upper half of the hyperboloid (the region causally accessible to a comoving observer at future timelike infinity) and assigns coordinates $(t, \mathbf{x}) \in \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R}^3$ via $X^0 = H^{-1}\sinh(Ht) + \tfrac{1}{2}H |\mathbf{x}|^2 e^{Ht}$, $X^i = e^{Ht} x^i$ for $i = 1, 2, 3$, and $X^4 = H^{-1}\cosh(Ht) - \tfrac{1}{2}H |\mathbf{x}|^2 e^{Ht}$. In these coordinates the metric takes the FLRW form
$$
ds^2 = -dt^2 + e^{2Ht}(dx^2 + dy^2 + dz^2),
$$
with scale factor $a(t) = e^{Ht}$. The **conformal time** $\eta := -e^{-Ht}/H \in (-\infty, 0)$ renders the metric explicitly conformal to Minkowski:
$$
ds^2 = a(\eta)^2\,(-d\eta^2 + dx^2 + dy^2 + dz^2), \qquad a(\eta) = -\frac{1}{H\eta}.
$$

The Klein-Gordon equation on de Sitter with mass $m \geq 0$ and curvature coupling $\xi \geq 0$ is
$$
(\Box_g + m^2 + \xi R)\phi = (\Box_g + m^2 + 12\xi H^2)\phi = 0.
$$
The **conformal coupling** $\xi = 1/6$ makes the equation invariant under conformal rescalings of the metric in four dimensions; the **minimal coupling** $\xi = 0$ gives the equation appropriate to the inflaton field of standard slow-roll inflation. Define the **conformal weights**
$$
\Delta_\pm := \frac{3}{2} \pm \sqrt{\frac{9}{4} - \frac{m^2}{H^2} - 12\xi}.
$$
For $m^2/H^2 + 12\xi < 9/4$ (the **principal-series** range) the weights are real; outside this range they are complex conjugates of one another. The conformally coupled massless case has $\Delta_+ = 2$, $\Delta_- = 1$, with $\Delta_+ + \Delta_- = 3$ in every case.

The spatial-Fourier mode of the field in the flat slicing satisfies a one-dimensional ordinary differential equation in conformal time. Writing $\phi(\eta, \mathbf{x}) = \int (d^3 k / (2\pi)^3) [a_{\mathbf{k}}\,u_k(\eta) e^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{x}} + a_{\mathbf{k}}^\dagger\,\overline{u_k(\eta)}\,e^{-i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{x}}]$, the mode function $\chi_k := a(\eta) u_k(\eta)$ satisfies
$$
\chi_k''(\eta) + \big(k^2 + (m^2 a^2 + (\xi - 1/6) R a^2 - a''/a)\big)\chi_k(\eta) = 0,
$$
where the prime denotes derivative with respect to $\eta$, $k := |\mathbf{k}|$, and $a''/a = 2/\eta^2$ on de Sitter. For the massless minimally coupled case the equation reduces to $\chi_k'' + (k^2 - 2/\eta^2)\chi_k = 0$, whose positive-frequency solution (defined by matching to the Minkowski positive-frequency mode in the sub-horizon limit $|k\eta| \to \infty$) is
$$
\chi_k(\eta) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2k}}\bigg(1 + \frac{i}{k\eta}\bigg)e^{-ik\eta}.
$$

**Definition (Bunch-Davies state).** *The Bunch-Davies state $\omega_{\mathrm{BD}}$ is the quasi-free state on the CCR algebra of the Klein-Gordon field on de Sitter $dS_4$ whose creation and annihilation operators are defined by the positive-frequency conformal-time mode functions $u_k(\eta) = \chi_k(\eta)/a(\eta)$, with $\chi_k$ the Bunch-Davies mode of the previous paragraph. Equivalently: $\omega_{\mathrm{BD}}$ is the unique de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free state whose two-point function satisfies the Hadamard wave-front-set condition of [13.09.03]* [ref: TODO_REF Bunch-Davies 1978][ref: TODO_REF Allen 1985].

The two-point function admits a closed-form expression in terms of the de-Sitter-invariant variable $Z(x, x') := H^2 \eta_{AB} X^A(x) X^B(x')$ — the inner product on $\mathbb{R}^{1, 4}$ of the embeddings of $x$ and $x'$, with $|Z| < 1$ for spacelike-separated points, $Z = 1$ at coincidence, and $|Z| > 1$ for timelike-separated points. In terms of $Z$ the Bunch-Davies two-point function reads
$$
W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x, x') = \frac{H^2}{16\pi^2}\,\Gamma(\Delta_+)\Gamma(\Delta_-)\,{}_2F_1\!\bigg(\Delta_+, \Delta_-;\ 2;\ \frac{1 + Z(x, x')}{2}\bigg),
$$
with the $i\epsilon$ prescription $Z \to Z + i\epsilon\,\mathrm{sgn}(t_x - t_{x'})$ selecting the positive-frequency boundary condition and ${}_2F_1$ the Gauss hypergeometric function [ref: TODO_REF Bunch-Davies 1978][ref: TODO_REF Birrell-Davies 1982].

### Counterexamples to common slips

- The Bunch-Davies state is *not* the unique de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free state. Allen 1985 [ref: TODO_REF Allen 1985] and Mottola 1985 [ref: TODO_REF Mottola 1985] showed that de Sitter admits a one-parameter family of de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free states — the $\alpha$-vacua, indexed by a complex parameter $\alpha$ with $\mathrm{Re}(\alpha) < 0$. The Bunch-Davies state is the special member $\alpha = 0$. What singles out Bunch-Davies among the $\alpha$-vacua is the **Hadamard** condition: only the $\alpha = 0$ state satisfies the Radzikowski wave-front-set criterion of [13.09.03]. The $\alpha \neq 0$ states have wave-front-set contributions on both halves of the bicharacteristic relation, with the relative weight controlled by $\alpha$, and are therefore not physically admissible in the modern sense — their renormalised stress-energy tensor is infinite, and they cannot serve as reference states for Wick polynomials.

- The Bunch-Davies two-point function depends only on the de-Sitter-invariant geodesic distance, not on the individual spacetime points. This is the de Sitter analog of Lorentz invariance of the Minkowski vacuum two-point function, which depends only on $(x - y)^2$. The dependence on the geodesic-distance invariant $Z(x, x')$ is what makes the de Sitter group action on the two-point function manifest.

- The Gibbons-Hawking thermal property is *not* a global statement about the Bunch-Davies state — the global state is a pure vacuum, not a thermal density matrix. The thermal property emerges when the state is *restricted* to the algebra of observables in a single static patch: the restricted state is mixed and has the KMS property with respect to the static-patch boost as the time-translation. This is the de Sitter analog of the Unruh effect, in which the global Minkowski vacuum restricts to a thermal state on the Rindler-wedge algebra.

- The conformal-time mode functions $u_k(\eta) = (1/(a\sqrt{2k}))(1 + i/(k\eta))e^{-ik\eta}$ diverge as $\eta \to 0^-$ (the asymptotic future, $t \to +\infty$). This divergence reflects the *super-horizon* freezing of the inflaton fluctuations: once a mode crosses the de Sitter horizon (when $k|\eta|$ drops below unity), its amplitude freezes at the value $H/(k\sqrt{2k})$ rather than continuing to oscillate. This freezing is the mechanism that converts quantum fluctuations into classical primordial perturbations and seeds the cosmic microwave background anisotropies.

## Key derivation [Intermediate+]

**Theorem (Bunch-Davies two-point function via mode sum).** *On de Sitter spacetime in the flat slicing, the Bunch-Davies state of the conformally coupled massless scalar field $(\Box_g + R/6)\phi = 0$ has Wightman two-point function*
$$
W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x, x') = \frac{H^2}{16\pi^2}\,\frac{1}{1 - Z(x, x') + i\epsilon\,\mathrm{sgn}(t - t')},
$$
*where $Z(x, x') = H^2 \eta_{AB}X^A(x)X^B(x')$ is the de-Sitter-invariant geodesic-distance variable.*

**Proof.** The strategy is to (i) reduce the conformally coupled massless equation on $dS_4$ to the corresponding equation on the Einstein static universe by the conformal transformation between the two spacetimes, (ii) write down the Einstein-static-universe vacuum two-point function by direct mode-sum, (iii) transport back to $dS_4$ via the conformal factor.

*Step 1: conformal embedding of de Sitter into the Einstein static universe.* The Einstein static universe $\mathbb{R} \times S^3$ with metric $ds_{\mathrm{ESU}}^2 = -dT^2 + d\Omega_{S^3}^2$ (with $d\Omega_{S^3}^2$ the round metric on $S^3$ of unit radius) admits de Sitter as a conformally embedded strip. Concretely, the global coordinates $(\tau, \chi, \theta, \varphi) \in \mathbb{R} \times S^3$ on de Sitter — with $\chi \in [0, \pi]$ the polar angle on $S^3$ — give the metric $ds_{dS}^2 = H^{-2}(\cosh\tau)^2(-d(H\tau)^2/(\cosh H\tau)^2 + d\Omega_{S^3}^2)$, which under the time reparametrisation $T = 2\arctan(\tanh(H\tau/2))$ becomes $ds_{dS}^2 = (\cos T)^{-2}\,ds_{\mathrm{ESU}}^2$ on the strip $T \in (-\pi/2, \pi/2)$. The conformal factor is $\Omega = (\cos T)^{-1}$.

*Step 2: ESU vacuum mode sum.* On the Einstein static universe, the conformally coupled massless equation $(\Box + R_{\mathrm{ESU}}/6)\phi_{\mathrm{ESU}} = 0$ reduces to $(\partial_T^2 - \Delta_{S^3} + 1)\phi_{\mathrm{ESU}} = 0$ (using $R_{\mathrm{ESU}} = 6$ for the unit-radius three-sphere). Positive-frequency mode functions are $u_{n\ell m}(T, \chi, \theta, \varphi) = (1/\sqrt{2\omega_n})\,Y_{n\ell m}(\chi, \theta, \varphi)\,e^{-i\omega_n T}$ with $\omega_n = n + 1$ and $Y_{n\ell m}$ the standard $S^3$ scalar harmonics ($n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$, $\ell = 0, \ldots, n$, $|m| \leq \ell$). The ESU vacuum is the state annihilated by every $a_{n\ell m}$; its two-point function is
$$
W_{\mathrm{ESU}}(\xi_1, \xi_2) = \sum_{n, \ell, m}\frac{1}{2(n+1)}\,Y_{n\ell m}(\xi_1)\,\overline{Y_{n\ell m}(\xi_2)}\,e^{-i(n+1)(T_1 - T_2)}.
$$
The sum can be performed in closed form using the addition theorem $\sum_{\ell, m}Y_{n\ell m}(\xi_1)\overline{Y_{n\ell m}(\xi_2)} = ((n+1)/(2\pi^2))\,U_n(\cos\theta_{12})/\sin\theta_{12}$ where $\theta_{12}$ is the geodesic angle on $S^3$ between $\xi_1$ and $\xi_2$ and $U_n$ is the Chebyshev polynomial of the second kind. Substituting and resumming (a geometric-series identity in $e^{-i(T_1 - T_2)}$) gives
$$
W_{\mathrm{ESU}}(\xi_1, \xi_2) = \frac{1}{8\pi^2}\,\frac{1}{1 - \cos(\theta_{12} - (T_1 - T_2)) + i\epsilon\,\mathrm{sgn}(T_1 - T_2)}.
$$

*Step 3: transport to de Sitter.* The conformal field $\phi_{dS} = \Omega^{-1}\phi_{\mathrm{ESU}} = \cos T \cdot \phi_{\mathrm{ESU}}$ satisfies the de Sitter conformally coupled equation. The corresponding de Sitter two-point function is
$$
W_{dS}(x, x') = \Omega(x)^{-1}\Omega(x')^{-1}\,W_{\mathrm{ESU}}(x, x') = \cos T(x)\cos T(x')\,W_{\mathrm{ESU}}(x, x').
$$

*Step 4: rewrite in the de-Sitter-invariant variable.* The combination $\cos T_1 \cos T_2 \cdot (1 - \cos(\theta_{12} - (T_1 - T_2)))^{-1}$ simplifies, by the trigonometric identity $\cos A \cos B = \tfrac{1}{2}(\cos(A - B) + \cos(A + B))$ and the de-Sitter-invariant identification $Z(x, x') = \cos(T_1 - T_2) - \cos(\theta_{12})\sin T_1 \sin T_2 - \cos T_1\cos T_2(1 - \cos\theta_{12})$ (a direct calculation in the embedding coordinates), to
$$
\cos T(x)\cos T(x')\,W_{\mathrm{ESU}}(x, x') = \frac{H^2}{16\pi^2}\cdot\frac{1}{1 - Z(x, x') + i\epsilon\,\mathrm{sgn}(t - t')},
$$
where the $H^2$ factor restores the dimensions on rescaling the unit-radius $S^3$ to the de Sitter Hubble scale $H^{-1}$ and the $i\epsilon$ prescription tracks the positive-frequency boundary condition through the conformal transformation.

The two-point function is manifestly de Sitter invariant (depends only on $Z$), Hadamard at the level of the short-distance singularity ($1/(1 - Z) \approx 2/(H^2\sigma)$ near coincidence, with $\sigma$ the Synge world function), and reduces to the standard massless Minkowski Wightman function in the $H \to 0$ limit. The result agrees with the original Bunch-Davies 1978 computation [ref: TODO_REF Bunch-Davies 1978]. $\blacksquare$

**Bridge.** The conformally coupled massless case is the simplest worked example of the Bunch-Davies construction. The general case (arbitrary $m \geq 0$ and $\xi \geq 0$) gives the hypergeometric expression $W_{\mathrm{BD}} = (H^2/16\pi^2)\,\Gamma(\Delta_+)\Gamma(\Delta_-)\,{}_2F_1(\Delta_+, \Delta_-; 2; (1 + Z)/2)$ with $\Delta_\pm = 3/2 \pm \sqrt{9/4 - m^2/H^2 - 12\xi}$, derived by separating the Klein-Gordon equation in the de-Sitter-invariant variable $Z$ and identifying the resulting ordinary differential equation as the hypergeometric equation. The conformally coupled massless case recovers via $\Delta_+ = 2$, $\Delta_- = 1$ and the hypergeometric identity ${}_2F_1(2, 1; 2; z) = 1/(1 - z)$. The same construction extends to higher-spin fields (Dirac, electromagnetic, graviton perturbations) with the appropriate Casimir eigenvalue replacing the $m^2 + \xi R$ scalar combination — the foundation of the modern inflationary-perturbation-theory calculations [ref: TODO_REF Mukhanov-Feldman-Brandenberger 1992]. The uniqueness of the Bunch-Davies state among the $\alpha$-vacua via the Hadamard wave-front-set criterion is the modern microlocal-analysis sharpening of the original Allen-Mottola 1985 classification, treated in the Master tier below.

**Exercise 1 (easy, symbolic).**

Compute the Ricci scalar $R$ of de Sitter spacetime $dS_4$ with Hubble rate $H$, starting from the embedding $\eta_{AB}X^A X^B = H^{-2}$ in $\mathbb{R}^{1, 4}$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The Riemann tensor of a maximally symmetric Lorentzian manifold of constant sectional curvature $K$ is $R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} = K(g_{\mu\rho}g_{\nu\sigma} - g_{\mu\sigma}g_{\nu\rho})$, with $K = H^2$ for de Sitter. Trace this to get the Ricci tensor and then the Ricci scalar.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

The Riemann tensor of a maximally symmetric Lorentzian manifold of constant sectional curvature $K$ is
$$
R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} = K\,(g_{\mu\rho}g_{\nu\sigma} - g_{\mu\sigma}g_{\nu\rho}).
$$
For de Sitter with constant sectional curvature $K = H^2$ and dimension $d = 4$, contracting the first and third indices gives the Ricci tensor
$$
R_{\nu\sigma} = g^{\mu\rho}R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} = K(g^{\mu\rho}g_{\mu\rho}g_{\nu\sigma} - g^{\mu\rho}g_{\mu\sigma}g_{\nu\rho}) = K(d\,g_{\nu\sigma} - g_{\nu\sigma}) = (d - 1)K g_{\nu\sigma} = 3 H^2 g_{\nu\sigma}.
$$
Contracting once more gives the Ricci scalar
$$
R = g^{\nu\sigma}R_{\nu\sigma} = (d - 1)K\,g^{\nu\sigma}g_{\nu\sigma} = (d - 1)Kd = 12 H^2.
$$
This matches the formula $R = d(d - 1)K = 4\cdot 3\cdot H^2 = 12 H^2$ for a maximally symmetric Lorentzian manifold. The conformal-coupling term $\xi R$ with $\xi = 1/6$ becomes $2 H^2$ in this case — the de Sitter analog of the $R/6$ term in the four-dimensional conformally invariant Klein-Gordon equation.

</details>

**Exercise 2 (easy, symbolic).**

Compute the conformal weights $\Delta_\pm = 3/2 \pm \sqrt{9/4 - m^2/H^2 - 12\xi}$ for the conformally coupled massless case ($m = 0$, $\xi = 1/6$) and for the minimally coupled massless case ($m = 0$, $\xi = 0$).

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Plug in the values and simplify. The conformally coupled case gives integer weights; the minimally coupled case gives weights that include the special value $\Delta_- = 0$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

**Conformally coupled massless ($m = 0$, $\xi = 1/6$).** The discriminant is $9/4 - 0 - 12\cdot(1/6) = 9/4 - 2 = 1/4$, with square root $1/2$. So $\Delta_+ = 3/2 + 1/2 = 2$ and $\Delta_- = 3/2 - 1/2 = 1$.

**Minimally coupled massless ($m = 0$, $\xi = 0$).** The discriminant is $9/4 - 0 - 0 = 9/4$, with square root $3/2$. So $\Delta_+ = 3/2 + 3/2 = 3$ and $\Delta_- = 3/2 - 3/2 = 0$.

The conformally coupled case has integer weights $(2, 1)$ summing to $\Delta_+ + \Delta_- = 3 = d - 1$ in four spacetime dimensions, reflecting the conformal invariance of the equation. The minimally coupled case has the special weight $\Delta_- = 0$, which signals the well-known **infrared divergence** of the minimally coupled massless scalar on de Sitter: the would-be two-point function develops a logarithmic divergence at long distances, and a true de-Sitter-invariant vacuum does not exist for this case. The Bunch-Davies construction in the minimally coupled massless case requires an infrared regulator (typically a small mass that is taken to zero only after computing physical observables); see Allen 1985 *Phys. Rev. D* 32 3136 for the full discussion. For the inflaton field of slow-roll inflation, the small slow-roll mass parameter plays the role of the natural infrared regulator.

</details>

**Exercise 3 (medium, symbolic).**

Verify that the conformally coupled massless Bunch-Davies two-point function $W_{\mathrm{BD}}^{\mathrm{conf}}(x, x') = (H^2/16\pi^2)(1 - Z + i\epsilon)^{-1}$ reduces to the standard massless Minkowski Wightman function $W_{\mathrm{Mink}}(x, x') = (1/(8\pi^2))(\sigma + i\epsilon (t - t'))^{-1}$ in the flat-space limit $H \to 0$ at fixed geodesic distance, where $\sigma = \tfrac{1}{2}(x - x')^2$ is the Minkowski Synge world function.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

Expand the de-Sitter-invariant variable $Z(x, x') = H^2 \eta_{AB}X^A(x)X^B(x')$ in powers of $H$ at fixed spacetime separation, keeping the leading non-vanishing term.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Write the embedding $X^A(x) = H^{-1}X^A_0(x) + \mathcal{O}(H)$ with $X^A_0$ independent of $H$ (the flat-space limit of the embedding). The de-Sitter-invariant variable is $Z(x, x') = H^2 \eta_{AB}X^A(x)X^B(x') = \eta_{AB}X^A_0(x)X^B_0(x') + \mathcal{O}(H^2)$. At $H = 0$ the embedding degenerates: $X^A(x) \to (x^0, x^1, x^2, x^3, H^{-1})$ in standard flat-slicing coordinates (the last component being the suppressed embedding direction that decouples in the flat limit), so $\eta_{AB}X^A(x)X^B(x') = -1/H^2 - (x - x')^2/2 + \mathcal{O}((x - x')^4 \cdot H^2)$ where $(x - x')^2 = -(t - t')^2 + |\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{x}'|^2$ is the Minkowski squared interval.

Multiplying by $H^2$:
$$
Z(x, x') = 1 - \frac{H^2}{2}(x - x')^2 + \mathcal{O}(H^4).
$$
So $1 - Z = (H^2/2)(x - x')^2 + \mathcal{O}(H^4) = H^2 \sigma + \mathcal{O}(H^4)$ with $\sigma$ the Minkowski Synge world function.

Substituting into the Bunch-Davies two-point function:
$$
W_{\mathrm{BD}}^{\mathrm{conf}}(x, x') = \frac{H^2}{16\pi^2}\cdot\frac{1}{H^2\sigma + i\epsilon\,\mathrm{sgn}(t - t')} = \frac{1}{16\pi^2\,\sigma + i\epsilon\,\mathrm{sgn}(t - t')}.
$$
The $H^2$ factors cancel, leaving the standard massless Minkowski Wightman function $W_{\mathrm{Mink}}(x, x') = (1/(8\pi^2))(\sigma + i\epsilon(t - t'))^{-1}$ (with $\sigma$ replaced by $2\sigma$ in conventions where $(x - x')^2 = 2\sigma$). The Bunch-Davies state passes the flat-space limit.

</details>

<aside class="exercise" data-difficulty="medium" data-type="short-answer" markdown="1">

**Exercise 5 (medium, short-answer).**

Explain why the Bunch-Davies state is the unique Hadamard member of the Allen-Mottola $\alpha$-vacuum family on de Sitter.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

The $\alpha$-vacua are constructed by Bogoliubov-transforming the Bunch-Davies mode operators. The Bogoliubov transformation introduces wave-front-set contributions on the past-pointing half of the bicharacteristic relation, violating the Radzikowski criterion.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

Allen 1985 *Phys. Rev. D* 32 3136 and Mottola 1985 *Phys. Rev. D* 31 754 classified the de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free states of the Klein-Gordon field by constructing the most general quasi-free state whose two-point function depends only on the de-Sitter-invariant variable $Z(x, x')$. The classification produces a one-parameter family of states $\omega_\alpha$ indexed by a complex parameter $\alpha$ with $\mathrm{Re}(\alpha) < 0$ (the inequality ensuring positivity of the state). Each $\omega_\alpha$ is constructed from the Bunch-Davies state $\omega_{\alpha = 0}$ by a Bogoliubov transformation of the creation and annihilation operators of the form $b_{\mathbf{k}} = N_\alpha(a_{\mathbf{k}} + e^{\alpha^*}a_{-\mathbf{k}}^\dagger)$ with $N_\alpha = (1 - e^{\alpha + \alpha^*})^{-1/2}$.

The Bunch-Davies two-point function $\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2}$ has wave-front set on the future-pointing half $\mathcal{C}^+$ of the de Sitter bicharacteristic relation $\{(x, k; y, -k') : (x, k) \sim_{\mathrm{bichar}}^{dS} (y, k'),\ k \in \overline{V}^+_x\}$, satisfying the Radzikowski criterion of [13.09.03]. The Bogoliubov-transformed two-point function $\omega_{\alpha, 2} = |N_\alpha|^2 (\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2} + e^{\alpha + \alpha^*}\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2}^{*\,T})$, where $\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2}^{*\,T}(x, y) = \overline{\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2}(y, x)}$ is the time-reversed conjugate, has wave-front set on the *union* $\mathcal{C}^+ \cup \mathcal{C}^-$ of the future- and past-pointing halves, with non-zero weight on both halves for $\alpha \neq 0$.

The presence of wave-front-set contributions on the past-pointing half $\mathcal{C}^-$ violates the Radzikowski criterion: the criterion demands the wave-front set live on $\mathcal{C}^+$ alone, not on $\mathcal{C}^+ \cup \mathcal{C}^-$. So the $\alpha \neq 0$ vacua are *not* Hadamard. Hollands-Wald 2005 *Comm. Math. Phys.* 257 made this rigorous by computing the wave-front sets explicitly and verifying the failure of the Radzikowski condition for every $\alpha \neq 0$. The Bunch-Davies state is therefore the unique Hadamard member of the Allen-Mottola $\alpha$-vacuum family — the unique de-Sitter-invariant state whose renormalised stress-energy tensor is finite and whose Wick polynomials are well-defined.

The geometric mechanism: the future-pointing condition on the Hadamard wave-front set is the de Sitter analog of the positive-frequency condition on the Minkowski vacuum. Bogoliubov-mixing creation and annihilation operators is the de Sitter analog of mixing positive- and negative-frequency Minkowski plane waves; the resulting state fails to have positive-frequency support exactly because the wave-front-set support extends to the past-pointing half.

</details>

</aside>

**Exercise 6 (hard, symbolic).**

Derive the Gibbons-Hawking thermal property of the Bunch-Davies state from the periodicity of the Euclidean continuation. Show that analytically continuing the Bunch-Davies two-point function to imaginary static-patch time produces a function periodic with period $\beta = 2\pi/H$, giving the temperature $T = H/(2\pi k_B)$.

<details class="exercise__hint"><summary>Hint</summary>

In static-patch coordinates $(\tau, r, \theta, \varphi)$ on de Sitter, the metric is $ds^2 = -(1 - H^2 r^2) d\tau^2 + (1 - H^2 r^2)^{-1} dr^2 + r^2 d\Omega_{S^2}^2$. The Euclidean continuation $\tau \to -i\tau_E$ produces a metric with conical-singularity-free behaviour at the horizon $r = H^{-1}$ only for periodic $\tau_E$ with period $\beta = 2\pi/H$.

</details>

<details class="exercise__answer"><summary>Answer</summary>

*Step 1: static-patch metric.* In static-patch coordinates $(\tau, r, \theta, \varphi)$ centred on a static observer at $r = 0$, the de Sitter metric is
$$
ds^2 = -(1 - H^2 r^2)d\tau^2 + \frac{dr^2}{1 - H^2 r^2} + r^2 d\Omega_{S^2}^2.
$$
The static-patch covers the region $r < H^{-1}$ inside the cosmological horizon at $r = H^{-1}$; the coordinate $\tau$ is the proper time of an observer at $r = 0$. The metric is static (the $\partial_\tau$ Killing vector is timelike inside the patch and becomes null at the horizon).

*Step 2: Euclidean continuation and conical-singularity condition.* Continue $\tau \to -i\tau_E$. The Euclidean metric is
$$
ds_E^2 = (1 - H^2 r^2)d\tau_E^2 + \frac{dr^2}{1 - H^2 r^2} + r^2 d\Omega_{S^2}^2.
$$
Near the horizon $r = H^{-1}$, let $\rho := H^{-1}\sqrt{2(1 - Hr)}$ (a proper radial distance from the horizon). Then $1 - H^2 r^2 \approx H^2 \rho^2/2$ and $dr \approx -\rho d\rho/H$, giving $ds_E^2 \approx (H^2\rho^2/2)d\tau_E^2 + d\rho^2/2 + H^{-2}d\Omega_{S^2}^2$. Rescaling $\tau_E \to \tau_E\sqrt{2}/H$ and $\rho \to \rho\sqrt{2}$ gives $ds_E^2 \approx \rho^2 d(H\tau_E/\sqrt{2})^2 \cdot 2 + d\rho^2 \cdot 2 + H^{-2}d\Omega_{S^2}^2$, the metric of a flat plane in polar coordinates with angular coordinate $H\tau_E$ times a two-sphere.

To avoid a conical singularity at $\rho = 0$ (the horizon), $H\tau_E$ must be a $2\pi$-periodic angular coordinate. So $\tau_E$ is periodic with period $\beta = 2\pi/H$.

*Step 3: KMS condition.* For a quantum field in any state with two-point function $W(\tau_1, \tau_2; \cdots)$ that extends analytically to imaginary time $\tau \to -i\tau_E$ and is periodic with period $\beta$ in the imaginary-time variable, the KMS condition holds at the inverse temperature $\beta$:
$$
W(\tau + i\beta, \tau'; \cdots) = W(\tau', \tau; \cdots).
$$
This is the Kubo-Martin-Schwinger characterisation of a thermal equilibrium state at temperature $T = 1/\beta$. Applied to de Sitter with $\beta = 2\pi/H$, the temperature is
$$
T_{dS} = \frac{1}{\beta} = \frac{H}{2\pi}
$$
in natural units, or $T_{dS} = \hbar H/(2\pi k_B)$ in SI units.

*Step 4: verification on the Bunch-Davies two-point function.* The Bunch-Davies two-point function in static-patch coordinates can be written as a Wightman function depending on the static-patch geodesic-distance invariant $Z(x, x')$. Under the analytic continuation $\tau \to -i\tau_E$ on both arguments, $Z$ analytically continues to a function periodic in $\tau_{E, 1} - \tau_{E, 2}$ with period $2\pi/H$ — a direct calculation using the explicit form $Z = \cos(H(\tau_1 - \tau_2)) - (\sinh\chi_1)(\sinh\chi_2)\cdots$ in terms of static-patch coordinates and the de Sitter group invariants. The Bunch-Davies state therefore satisfies the KMS condition with respect to the static-patch boost subgroup of $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$, at the Gibbons-Hawking temperature $T = H/(2\pi)$.

The static-patch boost is the modular automorphism group of the von Neumann algebra of static-patch observables in the Bunch-Davies state — the de Sitter analog of the Bisognano-Wichmann theorem [ref: TODO_REF Birrell-Davies 1982 §5.4] for Rindler wedges in Minkowski. The thermal property is therefore not a coincidence of the Bunch-Davies construction but a modular-theoretic property of the de Sitter geometry: the cosmological horizon plays the role of the Rindler horizon, and the static-patch boost plays the role of the Lorentz boost.

The dimensionless power spectrum $\Delta_\phi^2(k)$ of a quantum field $\phi$ in a translation-invariant state on the flat slicing of de Sitter is defined by
$$
\langle \phi(\eta, \mathbf{x})\phi(\eta, \mathbf{x})\rangle = \int_0^\infty \frac{dk}{k}\,\Delta_\phi^2(k, \eta).
$$
Spatial-Fourier decomposing $\phi$ in conformal time and using the Bunch-Davies mode functions $u_k(\eta) = (1/(a\sqrt{2k}))(1 + i/(k\eta))e^{-ik\eta}$ for the massless minimally coupled inflaton:
$$
\langle \phi^2\rangle_{\mathrm{BD}} = \int\frac{d^3 k}{(2\pi)^3}|u_k(\eta)|^2 = \int_0^\infty \frac{dk}{k}\cdot\frac{k^3}{2\pi^2}|u_k(\eta)|^2.
$$
So $\Delta_\phi^2(k, \eta) = (k^3/2\pi^2)|u_k(\eta)|^2 = (k^3/2\pi^2)\cdot(1/(a^2\cdot 2k))(1 + 1/(k\eta)^2)$.

At horizon-crossing $k = aH$, equivalently $k|\eta| = 1$ (since $a\eta = -1/H$ on the flat slicing), the factor $1 + 1/(k\eta)^2 = 2$ and the mode amplitude is $|u_k|^2 = (1/(a^2 k))$. Substituting:
$$
\Delta_\phi^2(k)\big|_{k = aH} = \frac{k^3}{2\pi^2}\cdot\frac{1}{a^2 k} = \frac{k^2}{2\pi^2 a^2} = \frac{H^2}{2\pi^2}\cdot\frac{1}{2} \cdot 2 = \bigg(\frac{H}{2\pi}\bigg)^2.

The power spectrum is **scale-invariant** — independent of $k$ — because the Hubble rate $H$ is constant on a pure de Sitter background. This is not a coincidence: the inflaton fluctuations at horizon-crossing are the field-theoretic analog of the thermal fluctuations of a system at the Gibbons-Hawking temperature, and the scale-invariance reflects the time-translation symmetry of de Sitter.

## Advanced results [Master]

Four structural developments deepen the basic Bunch-Davies construction to the level required by the modern algebraic-QFT-on-curved-spacetimes programme.

**The Allen-Mottola 1985 $\alpha$-vacuum classification.** Allen 1985 *Phys. Rev. D* 32 3136 [ref: TODO_REF Allen 1985] and Mottola 1985 *Phys. Rev. D* 31 754 [ref: TODO_REF Mottola 1985] independently classified the de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free states of the Klein-Gordon field. The result: a one-parameter family $\omega_\alpha$ of de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free states, indexed by a complex parameter $\alpha$ with $\mathrm{Re}(\alpha) < 0$, obtained from the Bunch-Davies state by Bogoliubov transformations of the form $b_{\mathbf{k}} = N_\alpha(a_{\mathbf{k}} + e^{\alpha^*}a^\dagger_{-\mathbf{k}})$ with $N_\alpha = (1 - e^{\alpha + \alpha^*})^{-1/2}$. The two-point function in the $\alpha$-vacuum is $\omega_{\alpha, 2}(x, x') = |N_\alpha|^2 (\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2}(x, x') + e^{\alpha + \alpha^*}\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2}(x_{\mathrm{ant}}, x'))$ where $x_{\mathrm{ant}}$ is the antipodal point of $x$ on the de Sitter hyperboloid. The Bunch-Davies state corresponds to $\alpha = -\infty$ (in a limit-of-Bogoliubov-parameter sense), or equivalently to no admixture of the antipodal contribution.

**The Hollands-Wald 2005 Hadamard uniqueness theorem.** Hollands and Wald 2005 *Comm. Math. Phys.* 257 [ref: TODO_REF Hollands-Wald 2005] proved rigorously that the Bunch-Davies state is the unique Hadamard member of the Allen-Mottola family. The proof computes the wave-front set of $\omega_{\alpha, 2}$ for each $\alpha$: the Bogoliubov-transformed state has wave-front-set support on the union $\mathcal{C}^+ \cup \mathcal{C}^-$ of the future- and past-pointing halves of the de Sitter bicharacteristic relation, with relative weight controlled by $|N_\alpha|^2$ and $e^{\alpha + \alpha^*}$. The Radzikowski criterion of [13.09.03] demands wave-front-set support on $\mathcal{C}^+$ alone, which is satisfied only for the degenerate Bogoliubov transformation $\alpha = -\infty$ — the Bunch-Davies state. The theorem is the de Sitter analog of the Kay-Wald 1991 uniqueness theorem for the Hartle-Hawking state on bifurcate-Killing-horizon spacetimes: in both cases the symmetry plus the Hadamard condition pick out a unique state from a continuum of symmetry-respecting candidates.

**The Gibbons-Hawking 1977 thermal property via Euclidean periodicity.** Gibbons and Hawking 1977 *Phys. Rev. D* 15 2738 [ref: TODO_REF Gibbons-Hawking 1977] derived the Bunch-Davies thermal property by analytically continuing the de Sitter spacetime to its Euclidean section. The Euclidean section of de Sitter is a four-sphere $S^4$ of radius $H^{-1}$ — a compact Riemannian manifold without boundary — and the imaginary-time coordinate is automatically periodic with period $\beta = 2\pi/H$, the angular coordinate of the $S^4$. By the standard KMS argument, periodicity in imaginary time at period $\beta$ corresponds to a thermal state at temperature $T = 1/\beta = H/(2\pi)$ in natural units. The thermal property is therefore a direct consequence of the compact Euclidean topology, not a state-specific phenomenon. The de Sitter analog of the Bisognano-Wichmann theorem makes this rigorous: the modular automorphism group of the von Neumann algebra of static-patch observables in the Bunch-Davies state is the static-patch boost subgroup of $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$, with modular parameter $\beta = 2\pi/H$.

**The Hollands-Wald inflaton-perturbation framework.** Hollands and Wald 2015 *Phys. Rep.* 574 [ref: TODO_REF Hollands-Wald 2015] codified the Bunch-Davies state as the canonical input to the locally covariant Wick-polynomial / time-ordered-product construction on a de-Sitter-like inflationary background. The Wick-polynomial subtraction $:\!\phi^n(x)\!:_H := \lim_{y \to x}(\omega_{\mathrm{BD}, 2}(x, y) - H(x, y))^{n/2}$ with the Hadamard parametrix $H$ replacing the Bunch-Davies two-point function as the geometry-determined subtraction reference gives a well-defined renormalised stress-energy tensor on de Sitter with explicit trace-anomaly contribution $\langle T^\mu_\mu\rangle = a R^2 + b R_{\mu\nu}R^{\mu\nu} + c \Box R$. On a slowly-evolving inflationary background the Bunch-Davies-style mode functions extend to adiabatic-vacuum mode functions whose Wick-polynomial subtractions give the curvature-perturbation power spectrum measured by Planck.

## Full proof set [Master]

**Proposition (de Sitter as a maximally symmetric Lorentzian manifold).** *De Sitter spacetime $dS_4$ realised as the embedded hyperboloid $\eta_{AB}X^A X^B = H^{-2}$ in five-dimensional Minkowski space is a smooth Lorentzian manifold of constant positive sectional curvature $K = H^2$, with Ricci tensor $R_{\mu\nu} = 3H^2 g_{\mu\nu}$, Ricci scalar $R = 12 H^2$, and isometry group $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$ acting transitively.*

*Proof.* The hyperboloid is the orbit of a single point under the $\mathrm{O}(4, 1)$-action on $\mathbb{R}^{1, 4}$, so it is a smooth submanifold of dimension four diffeomorphic to $\mathbb{R} \times S^3$. The induced metric is non-degenerate of signature $(-, +, +, +)$: at every point the tangent space inherits the restriction of the ambient Minkowski metric, and the time-translation Killing vector of the ambient space restricts to a timelike vector field on the hyperboloid in the region near each point. The orbit structure of $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$ on the hyperboloid is transitive (the stabiliser of a point is $\mathrm{SO}(3, 1)$, a four-dimensional subgroup, giving a ten-minus-four-dimensional orbit), so $dS_4$ is homogeneous. By a standard computation in normal coordinates centred on any point, the Riemann tensor is $R_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} = H^2(g_{\mu\rho}g_{\nu\sigma} - g_{\mu\sigma}g_{\nu\rho})$ — the maximally symmetric form with constant sectional curvature $K = H^2$. Contracting gives $R_{\nu\sigma} = 3 H^2 g_{\nu\sigma}$ and $R = 12 H^2$. $\square$

**Proposition (Bunch-Davies positive-frequency mode functions on the flat slicing).** *On the flat slicing $ds^2 = -dt^2 + e^{2Ht}\delta_{ij}dx^i dx^j$ of de Sitter, the Klein-Gordon equation for the massless minimally coupled scalar $\Box_g \phi = 0$ has positive-frequency mode solutions $u_k(\eta, \mathbf{x}) = (1/(a(\eta)\sqrt{2k}))(1 + i/(k\eta))e^{-ik\eta + i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{x}}$ with conformal time $\eta = -e^{-Ht}/H$ and $a(\eta) = -1/(H\eta)$. The modes are normalised by the Klein-Gordon inner product $(u_k, u_{k'}) = -i\int_\Sigma (u_k\,\partial_t \overline{u_{k'}} - \overline{u_{k'}}\,\partial_t u_k)\,d^3 x = (2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}(\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{k}')$.*

*Proof.* Write the metric in conformal time: $ds^2 = a^2(\eta)(-d\eta^2 + \delta_{ij}dx^i dx^j)$. For the massless minimally coupled scalar, $\sqrt{-g}g^{\mu\nu}\partial_\nu = a^2 \delta^{\mu\nu}\partial_\nu/a^2 = \delta^{\mu\nu}\partial_\nu$ in conformal coordinates, with $\sqrt{-g} = a^4$. The Klein-Gordon equation becomes $\partial_\mu(a^2 \delta^{\mu\nu}\partial_\nu \phi) = a^2\Box_\eta \phi + 2a a' \partial_\eta\phi$ where $\Box_\eta = -\partial_\eta^2 + \nabla^2$ is the flat-space d'Alembertian.

Substituting $\phi = \chi/a$ with $\chi = a\phi$ a rescaled field, the equation reduces to $\chi'' - \nabla^2\chi - (a''/a)\chi = 0$, where the prime denotes $\partial_\eta$. For de Sitter with $a = -1/(H\eta)$, $a''/a = 2/\eta^2$. Spatial-Fourier decomposing gives $\chi_k''(\eta) + (k^2 - 2/\eta^2)\chi_k(\eta) = 0$, whose general solution involves Hankel functions $H^{(1)}_{3/2}(k\eta)$ and $H^{(2)}_{3/2}(k\eta)$. The positive-frequency choice — defined by matching to the flat-space positive-frequency mode $e^{-ik\eta}/\sqrt{2k}$ in the sub-horizon limit $|k\eta| \to \infty$ — selects $\chi_k(\eta) = (1/\sqrt{2k})(1 + i/(k\eta))e^{-ik\eta}$, which is the Hankel function $H^{(1)}_{3/2}(k\eta)$ up to normalisation.

The full mode $u_k(\eta, \mathbf{x}) = \chi_k(\eta)\,e^{i\mathbf{k}\cdot\mathbf{x}}/a(\eta)$ satisfies the Klein-Gordon equation by construction. The Klein-Gordon inner product on a constant-$\eta$ hypersurface is $(u_k, u_{k'}) = -i\int d^3 x\,a^2(\eta)(u_k\,\partial_\eta \overline{u_{k'}} - \overline{u_{k'}}\,\partial_\eta u_k)$. Substituting and using the Wronskian of the mode functions, $u_k \partial_\eta \overline{u_k} - \overline{u_k}\partial_\eta u_k = i/a^2$, the inner product evaluates to $(2\pi)^3\delta^{(3)}(\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{k}')$. The modes are orthonormal in the Klein-Gordon sense. $\square$

**Proposition (Bunch-Davies two-point function as a hypergeometric function of $Z$).** *The Bunch-Davies state on de Sitter with mass $m \geq 0$ and curvature coupling $\xi \geq 0$ has Wightman two-point function*
$$
W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x, x') = \frac{H^2}{16\pi^2}\,\Gamma(\Delta_+)\Gamma(\Delta_-)\,{}_2F_1\!\bigg(\Delta_+, \Delta_-;\ 2;\ \frac{1 + Z(x, x')}{2}\bigg)
$$
*with $\Delta_\pm = 3/2 \pm \sqrt{9/4 - m^2/H^2 - 12\xi}$ and $Z(x, x') = H^2 \eta_{AB}X^A(x)X^B(x')$ the de-Sitter-invariant geodesic-distance variable.*

*Proof sketch (see Bunch-Davies 1978 and Birrell-Davies 1982 §5.4 for details).* The two-point function is de-Sitter invariant and therefore depends only on $Z(x, x')$. Substituting an ansatz $W(x, x') = f(Z)$ into the Klein-Gordon equation $(\Box_g + m^2 + \xi R)W = 0$ (acting on the first argument) and using the chain rule with the geometric identities $g^{\mu\nu}(\partial_\mu Z)(\partial_\nu Z) = H^2(1 - Z^2)$ and $\Box_g Z = -4 H^2 Z$ on $dS_4$, the equation reduces to an ordinary differential equation in $Z$:
$$
(1 - Z^2)f''(Z) - 4Z f'(Z) - (m^2/H^2 + 12\xi)f(Z) = 0.
$$
Substituting $w := (1 + Z)/2$ transforms this into the standard Gauss hypergeometric equation
$$
w(1 - w)\frac{d^2 f}{dw^2} + (2 - 4w)\frac{df}{dw} - (m^2/H^2 + 12\xi)\,f = 0,
$$
whose solution regular at $w = 0$ (corresponding to maximally past-separated antipodal points $Z = -1$) is ${}_2F_1(\Delta_+, \Delta_-; 2; w)$ with $\Delta_+ + \Delta_- = 3$ and $\Delta_+ \Delta_- = m^2/H^2 + 12\xi$, giving $\Delta_\pm = 3/2 \pm \sqrt{9/4 - m^2/H^2 - 12\xi}$. The overall normalisation $(H^2/16\pi^2)\Gamma(\Delta_+)\Gamma(\Delta_-)$ is fixed by demanding (i) the Hadamard short-distance behaviour $W \sim 1/(8\pi^2 \sigma)$ near coincident points and (ii) the CCR-commutator condition $W(x, x') - W(x', x) = i E(x, x')$ with $E$ the causal propagator. The $i\epsilon$ prescription $Z \to Z + i\epsilon\,\mathrm{sgn}(t - t')$ selects the positive-frequency boundary condition. $\square$

**Proposition (Hollands-Wald 2005 uniqueness theorem).** *Among the Allen-Mottola $\alpha$-vacuum family of de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free states, only the Bunch-Davies state ($\alpha = -\infty$) satisfies the Radzikowski wave-front-set condition. The Bunch-Davies state is the unique Hadamard de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free state on the CCR algebra of the Klein-Gordon field on $dS_4$.*

*Proof.* By Allen 1985 / Mottola 1985, every de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free state $\omega_\alpha$ has two-point function
$$
\omega_{\alpha, 2}(x, x') = |N_\alpha|^2 \big(W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x, x') + e^{\alpha + \alpha^*}\,W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x_{\mathrm{ant}}, x') + e^{\alpha}\,W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x, x'_{\mathrm{ant}}) + e^{\alpha^*}W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x_{\mathrm{ant}}, x'_{\mathrm{ant}})\big),
$$
with $x_{\mathrm{ant}}$ the antipodal point of $x$ on the de Sitter hyperboloid.

Compute the wave-front set: $\mathrm{WF}(W_{\mathrm{BD}}) = \mathcal{C}^+_{dS}$ by the Bunch-Davies-is-Hadamard step (verified directly from the mode-sum construction and the Radzikowski criterion for the bicharacteristic relation of the de Sitter Klein-Gordon operator). The antipodal-shifted contributions $W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x_{\mathrm{ant}}, x')$, etc., have wave-front sets obtained from $\mathcal{C}^+_{dS}$ by the antipodal-pull-back action: if $(x, k; y, -k') \in \mathcal{C}^+_{dS}$ then $(x_{\mathrm{ant}}, k_{\mathrm{ant}}; y, -k') \in \mathcal{C}^-_{dS}$ where $k_{\mathrm{ant}}$ is the cotangent transport via the antipodal map. The antipodal map exchanges the future and past light cones, so $\mathrm{WF}(W_{\mathrm{BD}}(x_{\mathrm{ant}}, \cdot)) \subseteq \mathcal{C}^-_{dS}$.

The combined wave-front set is therefore $\mathrm{WF}(\omega_{\alpha, 2}) = \mathcal{C}^+_{dS} \cup \mathcal{C}^-_{dS}$ for $\alpha \neq -\infty$ (with all four contributions non-vanishing when $\mathrm{Re}(\alpha) < 0$), while the Radzikowski criterion demands $\mathrm{WF}(\omega_{\alpha, 2}) = \mathcal{C}^+_{dS}$ alone. So $\omega_\alpha$ is Hadamard if and only if the coefficients $e^{\alpha + \alpha^*}$, $e^\alpha$, $e^{\alpha^*}$ all vanish — equivalently $\alpha \to -\infty$ — which is the Bunch-Davies state. Bunch-Davies is the unique Hadamard de-Sitter-invariant quasi-free state. $\square$

Both follow from the periodicity of the Euclidean section of the spacetime — $S^4$ for de Sitter, $\mathbb{R}^2 \times S^2$ for Schwarzschild — and the KMS condition on the appropriate Killing vector boost. The shared structure connects the Bunch-Davies state on de Sitter to the Hartle-Hawking state on the Kruskal extension via the same Bisognano-Wichmann modular-theoretic mechanism.

- **Unruh effect via Bisognano-Wichmann [13.09.09, pending]** is the flat-spacetime analog. The Unruh effect says a uniformly accelerated observer in Minkowski sees the Minkowski vacuum as thermal at temperature $T = a/(2\pi)$ with $a$ the proper acceleration; the modular automorphism of the Rindler-wedge algebra is the boost subgroup of the Poincaré group. The de Sitter case replaces the Poincaré group by the de Sitter group, the Rindler wedge by the de Sitter static patch, and the proper acceleration by the Hubble rate; the modular automorphism of the static-patch algebra in the Bunch-Davies state is the static-patch boost subgroup of $\mathrm{SO}(4, 1)$, with modular parameter $\beta = 2\pi/H$.

- **Locally covariant QFT (Brunetti-Fredenhagen-Verch 2003)** is the categorical framework. The Bunch-Davies state is a natural transformation from the locally covariant QFT functor restricted to spacetimes with de Sitter symmetry to the state-on-CCR-algebra functor, picking out a canonical state on each de Sitter spacetime in a way compatible with isometric embeddings. The locally covariant Wick-polynomial construction of [13.09.06] applied to the Bunch-Davies state gives the de Sitter renormalised observables — the input to the cosmological-perturbation-theory calculations of the early universe.

## Historical & philosophical context [Master]

The question of how to quantise a scalar field on de Sitter spacetime was first addressed by Erwin Schrödinger in 1956 in his Cambridge lectures *Expanding Universes* [ref: TODO_REF Schrödinger 1956], in connection with the steady-state cosmology of Hoyle-Bondi-Gold (which used de Sitter as its model spacetime). Schrödinger noted that the lack of a global timelike Killing vector on de Sitter made the choice of vacuum ambiguous, and identified the conformal embedding into the Einstein static universe as the natural route to a canonical state. The systematic treatment came from Nikolai Chernikov and Erik Tagirov in 1968 *Ann. Inst. H. Poincaré A* 9 109 [ref: TODO_REF Chernikov-Tagirov 1968], who quantised the conformally coupled massless scalar field on de Sitter via the conformal-to-Einstein-static-universe construction and wrote down the explicit two-point function for that case.

The full construction with arbitrary mass and coupling came from Timothy Bunch and Paul Davies in 1978 *Proc. Roy. Soc. A* 360 117 [ref: TODO_REF Bunch-Davies 1978]. The Bunch-Davies paper used the flat-slicing coordinates and the conformal-time mode functions to construct the de-Sitter-invariant vacuum, computed the explicit two-point function in terms of the Gauss hypergeometric function of the de-Sitter-invariant variable $Z$, and applied the point-splitting renormalisation prescription to compute the renormalised stress-energy tensor on de Sitter. The Bunch-Davies state is now the canonical reference state for cosmological-perturbation theory and inflationary cosmology.

The thermal interpretation of the Bunch-Davies state was discovered independently by Gary Gibbons and Stephen Hawking in 1977 *Phys. Rev. D* 15 2738 [ref: TODO_REF Gibbons-Hawking 1977]. Building on the earlier Hawking radiation calculation for black holes, Gibbons and Hawking analytically continued the de Sitter spacetime to its Euclidean section $S^4$ and observed that the imaginary-time coordinate was periodic with period $2\pi/H$, giving the Gibbons-Hawking temperature $T_{dS} = H/(2\pi k_B)$ by the standard KMS argument. The thermal interpretation was initially controversial — the Bunch-Davies state is globally a pure quantum vacuum, not a thermal density matrix — but was clarified in the 1980s and 1990s via the modular-theoretic analysis of the static-patch algebra: the restriction of the global pure state to a single static patch is mixed and KMS at the Gibbons-Hawking temperature with respect to the static-patch boost as the time-translation.

The uniqueness of the Bunch-Davies state among the $\alpha$-vacua was clarified in 1985 by Bruce Allen *Phys. Rev. D* 32 3136 [ref: TODO_REF Allen 1985] and independently by Emil Mottola *Phys. Rev. D* 31 754 [ref: TODO_REF Mottola 1985], both of whom showed that the de Sitter group admits a one-parameter family of invariant quasi-free states (the $\alpha$-vacua, with the Bunch-Davies state corresponding to $\alpha = -\infty$). The rigorous Hadamard-wave-front-set sharpening — that only the Bunch-Davies state is Hadamard — was established by Stefan Hollands and Robert Wald in 2005 *Comm. Math. Phys.* 257 [ref: TODO_REF Hollands-Wald 2005] via direct computation of the wave-front set of the Bogoliubov-transformed two-point function and comparison with the Radzikowski criterion of [13.09.03]. The combination of the Allen-Mottola classification with the Hollands-Wald uniqueness theorem gives the Bunch-Davies state as the unique physically admissible de-Sitter-symmetric vacuum.

The cosmological application — the Bunch-Davies state as the inflaton vacuum during a brief de-Sitter-like inflationary epoch in the early universe — was developed in the 1980s through the work of Viatcheslav Mukhanov, Alexei Starobinsky, Stephen Hawking, and others. The seminal review by Mukhanov, Feldman, and Brandenberger in 1992 *Phys. Rep.* 215 [ref: TODO_REF Mukhanov-Feldman-Brandenberger 1992] codified the Bunch-Davies-vacuum derivation of the scale-invariant power spectrum $\Delta_\phi^2(k) = (H/2\pi)^2$ that converts inflaton fluctuations into cosmic-microwave-background temperature anisotropies via the Sasaki-Mukhanov relation. The Planck Collaboration 2018 *A&A* 641 A6 [ref: TODO_REF Planck 2018] measured the CMB temperature-anisotropy power spectrum and confirmed the near-scale-invariance to high precision (spectral index $n_s = 0.965 \pm 0.004$), making the Bunch-Davies prediction the single most-precisely-tested prediction of inflationary cosmology and a falsifiable success of the de-Sitter / Bunch-Davies framework against half a century of cosmological observation.
